# Team Health Assessment

> Team health is a leading indicator. Attrition is a lagging one. By the time someone resigns, you have already lost them — usually 3-6 months before the resignation email. This template deploys quarterly health surveys, burnout detection, intervention playbooks, eNPS tracking, retention risk assessment, and structured action planning so you catch deterioration early enough to act.

---

## 1. Quarterly Team Health Survey Template

Deploy this survey quarterly. Anonymous responses are critical — named responses produce socially desirable answers, not honest ones. Use Google Forms, Typeform, Culture Amp, Lattice, or any tool that guarantees anonymity.

### Survey Questions (10 Questions, Likert Scale 1-5)

| # | Question | Dimension | Scale Anchors |
|---|----------|-----------|--------------|
| 1 | I am excited about the work I am doing right now | **Engagement** | 1=Strongly disagree, 5=Strongly agree |
| 2 | I have clarity about what is expected of me and how my work contributes to company goals | **Clarity** | 1=Strongly disagree, 5=Strongly agree |
| 3 | I feel safe raising concerns or disagreeing with my manager without fear of consequences | **Psychological Safety** | 1=Strongly disagree, 5=Strongly agree |
| 4 | My workload is sustainable — I can maintain this pace without burning out | **Sustainability** | 1=Strongly disagree, 5=Strongly agree |
| 5 | I receive regular, useful feedback that helps me grow | **Growth** | 1=Strongly disagree, 5=Strongly agree |
| 6 | I feel valued and recognized for my contributions | **Recognition** | 1=Strongly disagree, 5=Strongly agree |
| 7 | My team collaborates effectively — we help each other and resolve conflicts constructively | **Collaboration** | 1=Strongly disagree, 5=Strongly agree |
| 8 | I have the tools, resources, and information I need to do my job well | **Enablement** | 1=Strongly disagree, 5=Strongly agree |
| 9 | I trust the company's leadership to make good decisions and communicate transparently | **Trust** | 1=Strongly disagree, 5=Strongly agree |
| 10 | I see a path for growth and development at this company over the next 12 months | **Retention** | 1=Strongly disagree, 5=Strongly agree |

### Open-Ended Questions (Optional but Recommended)

| # | Question | Purpose |
|---|----------|---------|
| 11 | What is one thing we should START doing as a team? | Surface unmet needs |
| 12 | What is one thing we should STOP doing as a team? | Identify friction points |
| 13 | What is one thing we should CONTINUE doing? | Reinforce what works |
| 14 | Is there anything else you want leadership to know? | Catch-all safety valve |

### Scoring Interpretation

| Average Score | Health Level | Interpretation | Action |
|--------------|-------------|---------------|--------|
| 4.5 - 5.0 | **Thriving** | Team is highly engaged, aligned, and sustainable | Maintain and celebrate. Identify what is working and protect it. |
| 4.0 - 4.4 | **Healthy** | Team is in good shape with minor areas for improvement | Address specific dimensions that scored below 4.0 |
| 3.5 - 3.9 | **Caution** | Meaningful dissatisfaction in one or more areas | Investigate low-scoring dimensions. Manager action required within 2 weeks. |
| 3.0 - 3.4 | **At Risk** | Significant team health issues. Attrition risk is elevated. | Immediate investigation. Leadership involvement. Structured intervention plan. |
| Below 3.0 | **Critical** | Team is in crisis. Expect departures within 1-3 months if unaddressed. | Emergency response. Skip-level conversations. Potential organizational change. |

## 2. Burnout Indicators & Early Warning Signs

### Individual Burnout Signals

| Signal | What It Looks Like | Severity |
|--------|-------------------|----------|
| **Withdrawal** | Stopped participating in discussions, attending optional meetings, or socializing | Medium |
| **Cynicism** | Dismissive comments about company direction, leadership decisions, or team processes | High |
| **Productivity decline** | Missing deadlines, fewer PRs, lower quality work — from someone who was previously strong | High |
| **Presenteeism** | Online and "active" but producing very little. Going through the motions. | Medium |
| **Emotional volatility** | Uncharacteristic frustration, irritability, or emotional responses to routine feedback | High |
| **Physical symptoms** | Mentions of headaches, insomnia, back pain, or frequent sick days | Critical |
| **PTO avoidance** | Has not taken time off in 3+ months. Works on weekends regularly. | High |
| **Scope creep acceptance** | Saying yes to everything, never pushing back on unreasonable requests | Medium |
| **Isolation** | Working alone, avoiding collaboration, not asking for help | Medium |
| **Exit language** | "At my last company we..." or "If I were somewhere else..." | Critical |

### Team-Level Burnout Signals

| Signal | Measurement | Threshold |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| Sprint velocity declining for 3+ sprints | Story points completed vs committed | < 70% completion rate |
| After-hours Slack activity increasing | Messages sent outside core hours | > 20% of messages |
| PTO utilization below expectations | Days taken vs days available | < 60% utilization at mid-year |
| Incident frequency increasing | Incidents per sprint | 2x increase from baseline |
| Meeting fatigue | Survey question or hours in meetings | > 50% of work hours in meetings |
| Sick day spike | Unplanned absences per quarter | 2x increase from baseline |

## 3. Intervention Playbook (By Health Score Range)

### Thriving (4.5-5.0): Protect and Amplify

| Action | Owner | Timeline |
|--------|-------|----------|
| Celebrate in all-hands or team channel | Manager | Within 1 week of survey results |
| Identify the specific factors driving high scores | Manager + HR | 2 weeks |
| Document what is working as organizational best practices | Manager | 30 days |
| Share practices with other teams | Leadership | Next leadership meeting |
| Do NOT change anything just because you can — protect what works | Everyone | Ongoing |

### Healthy (4.0-4.4): Targeted Improvement

| Action | Owner | Timeline |
|--------|-------|----------|
| Identify dimensions scoring below 4.0 | Manager | 1 week |
| Discuss lowest-scoring areas in team retro (without breaking anonymity) | Manager | 2 weeks |
| Create 2-3 specific action items for the lowest dimension | Manager + Team | 2 weeks |
| Track improvement in next quarterly survey | Manager | Next quarter |

### Caution (3.5-3.9): Active Intervention

| Action | Owner | Timeline |
|--------|-------|----------|
| Manager has 1:1 conversations with each team member (not about survey — about how they are doing) | Manager | 1 week |
| Skip-level check-ins for the team | Manager's manager | 2 weeks |
| Identify root causes for low-scoring dimensions (workload? clarity? management? tools?) | Manager + HR | 2 weeks |
| Create intervention plan with specific actions, owners, and deadlines | Manager | 3 weeks |
| Follow up with pulse check (3-question survey) in 6 weeks | HR / Manager | 6 weeks |

### At Risk (3.0-3.4): Urgent Response

| Action | Owner | Timeline |
|--------|-------|----------|
| Manager's manager is briefed and involved | Manager | Immediately |
| Skip-level 1:1s with every team member | Manager's manager | 1 week |
| Root cause analysis with manager — is the manager part of the problem? | HR + Leadership | 1 week |
| Immediate workload reduction if sustainability scored low | Manager + PM | 1 week |
| Retention risk assessment for every team member | Manager + HR | 2 weeks |
| Structured intervention plan with weekly check-ins | Manager + HR | 2 weeks |
| Re-survey in 6 weeks (not quarterly — accelerated) | HR | 6 weeks |

### Critical (Below 3.0): Emergency Response

| Action | Owner | Timeline |
|--------|-------|----------|
| CEO / VP briefed | Manager's manager | Same day |
| All-team conversation — acknowledge the problem openly | Manager or VP | 2-3 days |
| Individual conversations with every team member to assess flight risk | Manager + HR | 1 week |
| Consider management change if manager is the root cause | VP + HR | 1-2 weeks |
| Immediate concrete improvements (workload, process, environment) | Leadership | 1 week |
| Weekly pulse surveys until score improves above 3.5 | HR | Weekly |
| Retention offers for critical flight risks | VP + HR | As needed |

## 4. eNPS Tracking

### eNPS (Employee Net Promoter Score)

**Question:** "On a scale of 0-10, how likely are you to recommend {{PROJECT_NAME}} as a place to work to a friend?"

| Response | Category | Interpretation |
|----------|----------|---------------|
| 9-10 | **Promoter** | Highly satisfied, likely to refer candidates, low flight risk |
| 7-8 | **Passive** | Satisfied but not enthusiastic. Vulnerable to outside offers. |
| 0-6 | **Detractor** | Dissatisfied. Active flight risk. May discourage others from joining. |

**eNPS Calculation:** (% Promoters - % Detractors) = eNPS

| eNPS Range | Assessment | Industry Benchmark |
|-----------|-----------|-------------------|
| +50 to +100 | Exceptional | Top 10% of companies |
| +30 to +49 | Strong | Above average |
| +10 to +29 | Good | Average |
| 0 to +9 | Concerning | Below average |
| Below 0 | Critical | Urgent intervention needed |

## 5. Retention Risk Assessment

### Individual Retention Risk Matrix

Assess each team member quarterly (manager assessment, confidential):

| Factor | Low Risk (1) | Medium Risk (2) | High Risk (3) |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|---------------|
| **Market demand** | Niche skills, limited external demand | Moderate demand, some relevant opportunities | Hot market, constant recruiter outreach |
| **Tenure** | < 1 year (still ramping) or 3-5 years (embedded) | 1-2 years (highest risk window) | 1.5-2.5 years with no recent promotion |
| **Engagement signals** | Active, enthusiastic, volunteers for projects | Steady but less proactive than before | Withdrawn, minimal participation |
| **Compensation satisfaction** | At or above market, recently reviewed | Slightly below market, not raised recently | Significantly below market, knows it |
| **Growth trajectory** | Clear path, recent promotion or skill development | Path exists but not actively pursued | Stalled, no promotion in 2+ years |
| **Manager relationship** | Strong trust, regular 1:1s, felt supported | Adequate but not remarkable | Strained, avoidance, limited communication |
| **Life circumstances** | Stable, no relocating factors | Some changes (new baby, partner's job) | Major change (relocation, family, burnout) |

**Risk Score:** Sum of all factors (7-21)

| Score | Risk Level | Action |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| 7-10 | **Low** | Continue regular management. Mention growth opportunities proactively. |
| 11-14 | **Medium** | Increase 1:1 frequency. Discuss career trajectory. Consider comp adjustment or stretch project. |
| 15-18 | **High** | Proactive retention conversation. Comp review. Create a specific stay plan (title, scope, equity refresh). |
| 19-21 | **Critical** | Immediate intervention. VP-level conversation. Retention offer. Accept that departure may happen and begin contingency planning. |

## 6. Team Retrospective on Health Trends

### Quarterly Health Retro (60 minutes)

Run this after each quarterly survey results are processed. This is not a regular sprint retro — it is specifically focused on team health trends.

**Agenda:**

1. **Results overview (10 min):** Manager presents survey results without identifying individuals. Focus on trends, not absolute numbers.

2. **Dimension deep dive (20 min):** For the 2 lowest-scoring dimensions:
   - What is causing this score?
   - What has changed since last quarter?
   - What would improve this by 1 point?

3. **Previous action review (10 min):** Review actions from the last health retro.
   - Completed? Did it help?
   - Not completed? Why? Still relevant?

4. **New actions (15 min):** Agree on 2-3 specific, measurable actions for the next quarter.

5. **Commitment (5 min):** Each action has an owner and a deadline. Manager commits to follow-up.
