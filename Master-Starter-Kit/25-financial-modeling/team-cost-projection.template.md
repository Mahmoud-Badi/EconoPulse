## Contractor vs. FTE Cost Comparison

| Factor | Full-Time Employee | Contractor / Freelancer |
|--------|-------------------|------------------------|
| Monthly cost (senior eng, US) | $18K (fully loaded) | $12K-$20K ($75-$125/hr) |
| Commitment | Ongoing | Project-based or retainer |
| Ramp-up time | 2-4 weeks | 1-2 weeks |
| IP ownership | Automatic (work-for-hire) | Requires contract clause |
| Benefits required | Yes (US: health, FICA) | No |
| Termination cost | Severance risk, notice period | Contract end, no obligation |
| Culture / retention | Builds institutional knowledge | Knowledge walks when contract ends |
| Management overhead | Lower (integrated team) | Higher (context switching, onboarding) |

**When to use contractors:**
- Specialized skills needed for <6 months (e.g., security audit, mobile app build)
- Uncertain product direction (validate before committing to FTE)
- Seasonal or burst workloads (launch campaigns, tax season features)
- Geography where employment law makes FTEs risky (Brazil, France)

**When to hire FTEs:**
- Core competency work that compounds over time
- Role needed for >6 months
- Team culture and institutional knowledge matter
- Full-time commitment and availability required

```
Runway (months) = Cash in Bank / (Monthly Burn - Monthly Revenue)

New Runway = Cash / (Current Burn + Sum of New Hire Costs - Revenue)
```

## Gotchas: Hiring Financial Mistakes

1. **Your first hire should be whoever does what you're worst at.** If you're a technical founder, your first hire is marketing or sales. If you're a business founder, your first hire is a senior engineer. Don't hire for what you enjoy — hire for what you avoid.

2. **Equity is not free.** Giving a senior hire 1% equity at a $10M valuation is a $100K commitment. Model equity dilution alongside cash compensation. Use a cap table tool (Carta, Pulley) from day one.

3. **Hiring ahead of revenue kills startups.** The most common cause of startup death is not running out of product ideas — it's running out of cash from hiring too fast. Rule of thumb: hire only when the pain of not having someone exceeds the cost of having them.

4. **The fully-loaded cost is 1.3-1.5x base salary, not 1.0x.** A $150K engineer costs $195K-$225K when you add payroll taxes, benefits, equipment, recruiting fees, and software licenses. Budget at 1.35x minimum.

5. **Contractors are not always cheaper.** A $100/hr contractor costs $16K/mo for full-time equivalent. A $150K/year FTE costs $16.9K/mo fully loaded. The FTE builds institutional knowledge and culture. Contractors make sense for specialized, time-bound work — not for ongoing core development.

6. **International hiring without an EOR is illegal in most countries.** You cannot 1099 a full-time worker in Germany or Brazil. Use an Employer of Record (Deel, Remote, Oyster) which adds $400-$600/mo per employee on top of salary costs.

7. **Severance and notice periods vary wildly.** In the US, at-will employment means near-zero termination cost. In France, mandatory notice is 1-3 months plus severance. In Brazil, FGTS and termination penalties can equal 4+ months of salary. Factor this into your risk model.
