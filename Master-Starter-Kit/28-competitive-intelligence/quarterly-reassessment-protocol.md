# Quarterly Reassessment Protocol

> The most important competitive intelligence activity you do. Every quarter, stop and systematically reassess the competitive landscape. This guide provides a 10-step protocol that takes 2-4 hours and produces a 1-page competitive landscape summary for your team.

---

## When to Run This

- **Scheduled:** First week of every quarter (January, April, July, October)
- **Triggered early by:** Competitor funding announcement, major market entrant, significant win/loss trend shift, or any event flagged as "trigger early quarterly assessment" in your alert configuration
- **Who runs it:** Product lead, founder, or designated competitive intelligence owner
- **Time required:** 2-4 hours (can be split across two sessions if needed)

---

## Pre-Assessment Preparation (15 Minutes)

Before starting the 10-step review, gather your materials:

```
[ ] Open your competitive intelligence log from the past quarter
[ ] Open the current feature parity matrix
[ ] Open current battle cards for each tracked competitor
[ ] Pull win/loss data from the past quarter (CRM, spreadsheet, or memory)
[ ] Open competitor websites in separate browser tabs
[ ] Have your RSS reader / Google Alerts history accessible
[ ] Block 2-4 hours of uninterrupted time
```

---

## The 10-Step Quarterly Review

### Step 1: Update Feature Parity Matrix (20 Minutes)

Review competitor changelogs, release notes, and product pages for the past quarter. Update the feature parity matrix in `feature-parity-tracking.template.md`.

**Process:**
1. Open each competitor's changelog or release notes page
2. List every feature they shipped in the past quarter
3. Update the feature matrix symbols (✅ 🚧 📋 ❌ ❓)
4. Reclassify Table Stakes / Differentiators / Opportunities if any features shifted categories
5. Note any features that surprised you (you did not expect them to build this)

**Output:** Updated feature parity matrix with quarterly changelog notes.

**Questions to answer:**
- Did any competitor ship a feature that our users are asking for?
- Did any competitor close a gap on our differentiators?
- Did we ship anything that created new differentiation?

---

### Step 2: Check Competitor Pricing Pages (15 Minutes)

Visit each competitor's pricing page. Compare against your last recorded pricing snapshot.

**Process:**
1. Open each competitor's pricing page
2. Compare to the pricing comparison table in your feature parity matrix
3. Note any changes: tier restructuring, price increases, price decreases, new tiers, removed tiers, changed feature bundling
4. If using Visualping, review the change history for the quarter

**Output:** Updated pricing comparison. Brief notes on any changes.

**Questions to answer:**
- Did any competitor change their pricing model (not just the price)?
- Are we still priced appropriately relative to competitors?
- Did any competitor add or remove a free tier?

---

### Step 3: Review Competitor Messaging (15 Minutes)

Check each competitor's homepage, product page, and about page for messaging shifts.

**Process:**
1. Open each competitor's homepage
2. Read their headline, subheadline, and primary CTA
3. Compare to what you remember (or what Visualping shows) from last quarter
4. Note any changes in how they describe themselves, who they target, or what they emphasize

**Output:** Brief notes on messaging changes per competitor.

**Questions to answer:**
- Are any competitors repositioning toward (or away from) our market segment?
- Has any competitor changed their primary value proposition?
- Are competitors using new terminology or framing that we should be aware of?

---

### Step 4: Search for New Market Entrants (20 Minutes)

Actively look for competitors you may have missed.

**Process:**
1. Search Product Hunt for launches in your category from the past quarter
2. Search G2 and Capterra for new listings in your product category
3. Search Google for "[your category] alternatives" and "[your category] tools 2026"
4. Check relevant subreddits for mentions of new tools
5. Search Crunchbase for recent funding in your space

**Output:** List of any new entrants. For each: name, funding status, feature overlap estimate, threat level (low/medium/high).

**Questions to answer:**
- Has anyone new entered our market that we should be tracking?
- Has anyone from an adjacent market expanded into our space?
- Are there any well-funded stealth-mode companies we should be watching?

---

### Step 5: Review Competitor Job Postings (15 Minutes)

Check competitor careers pages and LinkedIn for strategic hiring signals.

**Process:**
1. Visit each competitor's careers page
2. Search LinkedIn Jobs for each competitor
3. Categorize open roles: Engineering, Sales, Marketing, Customer Success, Executive
4. Note any unusual patterns: rapid hiring, new departments, executive departures

**Output:** Hiring signal analysis per competitor (use the signal interpretation guide in `competitive-monitoring-cadence.md`).

**Questions to answer:**
- What are competitors investing in for the next 6-12 months?
- Is any competitor hiring aggressively in a specific area?
- Is any competitor reducing headcount?

---

### Step 6: Update Competitive Battle Cards (20 Minutes)

Refresh battle cards for your top 2-3 competitors using data from Steps 1-5.

**Process:**
1. Open each battle card in `competitive-battle-cards.template.md`
2. Update the "Where WE Win" section with any new advantages
3. Update the "Where THEY Win" section honestly — have they closed any gaps?
4. Refresh objection handling with any new objections from sales conversations
5. Update the head-to-head feature comparison
6. Update the "Last Updated" date

**Output:** Refreshed battle cards.

**Questions to answer:**
- Are our claimed advantages still true?
- Have competitors neutralized any of our differentiators?
- Are there new objections we need to handle?

---

### Step 7: Review Win/Loss Data (20 Minutes)

Analyze win/loss data from the past quarter for competitive patterns.

**Process:**
1. Pull all wins and losses from the past quarter (from CRM, spreadsheet, or `win-loss-analysis.template.md`)
2. Calculate win rate overall and win rate by competitor
3. Identify the top reason for wins and top reason for losses
4. Look for trends: is win rate improving or declining? Against which competitor?
5. Flag any competitor that appears in 30%+ of losses — they need a targeted teardown

**Output:** Quarterly win/loss summary. Pattern analysis.

**Questions to answer:**
- Is our overall win rate improving or declining?
- Which competitor are we losing to most often?
- What is the most common reason we lose deals?
- Are there patterns in the types of customers we win vs. lose?

---

### Step 8: Check Competitor Traffic and SEO Trends (15 Minutes)

Use free or paid tools to assess competitor online presence trends.

**Process:**
1. Check SimilarWeb for traffic estimates (free tier gives top-level numbers)
2. Check Google Trends for competitor brand name search volume
3. If you have Ahrefs/SEMrush: check domain authority changes, new ranking keywords
4. Check social media follower counts and engagement rates (not follower count alone — look at engagement)

**Output:** Brief traffic and visibility trend notes per competitor.

**Questions to answer:**
- Is any competitor growing significantly faster than others?
- Are competitors investing in SEO in our keyword space?
- Are there traffic sources we should investigate?

**Note:** Do not over-index on traffic numbers. They are estimates and do not directly correlate with revenue. Use them as directional indicators only.

---

### Step 9: Assess Positioning Adjustment (15 Minutes)

Based on all the data from Steps 1-8, assess whether your positioning needs to change.

**Process:**
1. Re-read your current positioning statement and value propositions
2. Ask: does our positioning still accurately reflect our competitive advantages?
3. Ask: has the market shifted in a way that makes our positioning less effective?
4. Ask: is there a new positioning angle that better exploits competitor weaknesses?
5. If positioning needs adjustment, draft proposed changes

**Output:** Positioning assessment: No change needed / Minor refresh needed / Major repositioning needed.

**Questions to answer:**
- Is our positioning still differentiated?
- Has any competitor adopted positioning that is too similar to ours?
- Are we emphasizing the right things given the current competitive landscape?

---

### Step 10: Write Competitive Landscape Summary and Present (30 Minutes)

Synthesize everything into a 1-page summary and share with your team.

**Output Template:**

```

**Date:** [Date]
```

## Annual Deep Refresh Protocol

Once per year (typically Q1), replace the standard quarterly review with a comprehensive annual refresh. This takes a full day (8 hours) and produces a complete reset of your competitive intelligence.

### Annual Refresh Additions (Beyond Quarterly Steps)

| Activity | Time | What It Produces |
|----------|------|-----------------|
| Full re-run of competitor audit for top 3 | 2 hours | Refreshed competitor profiles (sign up for trials, test products) |
| New market entrant deep scan | 1 hour | Comprehensive new competitor list beyond Product Hunt |
| Market positioning map redraw | 1 hour | Fresh 2x2 positioning map with all competitors |
| Feature parity matrix rebuild from scratch | 1 hour | Clean matrix, removing accumulated errors and stale data |
| Annual win/loss report | 1 hour | Full-year patterns, not just quarterly trends |
| Strategic competitive review | 1 hour | Assessment of market evolution, category definition changes |
| Annual CI report write-up | 1 hour | Comprehensive report for stakeholders and investors |

### Market Positioning Map Template

During the annual refresh, redraw your market positioning map. Choose two axes that matter most to your market.

Common axis pairs:
- **Price vs. Feature Breadth** — where does everyone sit on price and how comprehensive is the product?
- **SMB vs. Enterprise** — who targets small teams vs. who targets large organizations?
- **Simplicity vs. Power** — who prioritizes ease of use vs. who prioritizes capability?
- **Horizontal vs. Vertical** — who serves all industries vs. who specializes?

```
                    [Axis 2 - High]
                         |
                         |
         Competitor A    |     Competitor B
              *          |          *
                         |
    [Axis 1 - Low] ------+------ [Axis 1 - High]
                         |
              *          |          *
         {{PROJECT_NAME}}|     Competitor C
                         |
                    [Axis 2 - Low]
```

**The goal is to find open space** — a quadrant where no competitor sits that aligns with market demand. If you are clustered with competitors, you need stronger differentiation. If you are alone in a quadrant, validate that customers actually want what that quadrant represents.

## Post-Assessment Actions

After completing the quarterly review:

1. **Share the summary** with your team (Slack, email, team meeting — pick one)
2. **Update all competitive artifacts** (feature matrix, battle cards, pricing comparison)
3. **File the summary** in your competitive intelligence archive for year-over-year comparison
4. **Schedule the next quarterly review** on the team calendar
5. **Create action items** in your project management tool for any strategic adjustments
6. **Communicate to sales/support** if battle cards changed significantly

## Common Mistakes in Quarterly Reviews

1. **Skipping the review because "nothing changed"** — gradual shifts are invisible without structured comparison. Run the review anyway.
2. **Spending more than 4 hours** — you are going too deep. This is a review, not a research project. Save deep dives for targeted teardowns.
3. **Not sharing the summary** — intelligence that stays in one person's head is not intelligence. Share it.
4. **Only looking at direct competitors** — check adjacent categories too. Your next competitor may come from an unexpected direction.
5. **Updating battle cards without verifying claims** — do not assume your advantages are still true. Verify.
6. **Ignoring your own changes** — the quarterly review should also assess what YOU shipped and how it changed the competitive dynamic.
7. **All analysis, no action** — every quarterly review should produce specific action items. If it does not, the review was too shallow.
