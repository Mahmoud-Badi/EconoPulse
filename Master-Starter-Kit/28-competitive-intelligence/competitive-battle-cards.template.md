# Competitive Battle Cards — {{PROJECT_NAME}}

> Battle cards are 1-page reference sheets that help your team handle competitive situations. They are used by sales reps in live calls, by marketing to craft positioning, and by founders to articulate differentiation. They must be honest, current, and actionable.

---

## Last Updated: {{DATE}}

**Review Cadence:** Every quarter or after any major competitor change
**Owner:** Product lead or sales lead
**Distribution:** Sales team, marketing team, customer success, founders
**Format:** One card per competitor. Print-friendly. Scannable in under 60 seconds.

---

## How to Use Battle Cards

1. **Sales calls:** Pull up the relevant battle card when a prospect mentions a competitor. Use the objection handling section word-for-word if needed.
2. **Marketing:** Use the "Where WE Win" section to inform landing page copy, comparison pages, and ad messaging.
3. **Product decisions:** Use the "Where THEY Win" section to identify gaps to close.
4. **Updates:** When you notice something has changed (new feature, pricing change, positioning shift), update the card immediately. Do not wait for the quarterly review.

### Battle Card Rules

- **Be honest.** If a competitor genuinely beats you in an area, say so. Your team loses credibility when prospects fact-check and find your claims are false.
- **Be specific.** "We are better" is useless. "We process reports in 2 seconds vs. their 15 seconds" is useful.
- **Use proof points.** Every advantage claim should have data, a customer quote, or a verifiable fact behind it.
- **Keep it to one page.** If you cannot summarize the competitive dynamic in one page, you do not understand it well enough.

### Knockout Questions

Questions to ask prospects that naturally highlight {{COMPETITOR_1}}'s weaknesses without directly attacking them:

1. "How important is [area where they are weak] to your workflow?"
2. "Have you had a chance to try [specific feature where we excel] in their product?"
3. "What has your experience been with [their known pain point — e.g., their support, their onboarding, their pricing at scale]?"

5. "What would it mean for your team if you could [specific outcome our differentiator enables]?"

## Adding a New Battle Card

When a new competitor enters your tracking, create a battle card using this process:

1. **Run a competitor teardown** using `competitor-teardown.template.md` (or at minimum, a quick 1-hour assessment)
2. **Copy the battle card template** for {{COMPETITOR_1}} above (it is the most detailed)
3. **Fill in all sections** — do not leave "Where THEY Win" empty; every competitor wins somewhere
4. **Have someone else review it** for honesty — confirmation bias makes us overstate our advantages
5. **Distribute to the team** with a 5-minute briefing

---

## Battle Card Maintenance Checklist

Run this checklist during every quarterly review:

```
[ ] Verified all pricing information is current (checked competitor pricing pages)
[ ] Verified all feature claims are still accurate (checked competitor product pages and changelogs)
[ ] Updated "Where WE Win" with any new advantages we have shipped
[ ] Updated "Where THEY Win" with any gaps they have closed
[ ] Refreshed objection handling based on recent sales conversations
[ ] Added any new objections that came up this quarter
[ ] Updated knockout questions based on current competitive dynamics
[ ] Updated head-to-head feature comparison for any changes
[ ] Updated quick stats (G2 ratings, review counts)
[ ] Updated "Last Updated" date
[ ] Distributed updated cards to sales and marketing
[ ] Removed or archived cards for competitors that are no longer relevant
```

## Competitor Comparison Page Strategy

Battle cards are internal. But you may also want a public comparison page on your website. If so, follow these rules:

1. **Be factual, not snarky.** Prospects respect honest comparisons. They distrust hit pieces.
2. **Lead with your value, not their flaws.** "Here is why customers choose us" beats "Here is why they are bad."
3. **Keep it updated.** A comparison page with outdated competitor pricing is worse than no comparison page.
4. **Include a clear CTA.** The page should drive sign-ups or demos, not just inform.
5. **SEO optimize for "[competitor name] alternative" keywords.** These are high-intent searches.
6. **Add customer quotes** from win/loss interviews (with permission) for credibility.
