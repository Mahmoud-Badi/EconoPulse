## Win/Loss Interview Template

### When to Interview

- **Always interview** for deals over $500 MRR (or your equivalent threshold)
- **Interview a sample** of smaller deals — aim for 2-3 win interviews and 2-3 loss interviews per month
- **Always interview** churned customers who explicitly mentioned a competitor
- **Interview within 2 weeks** of the decision — memory fades fast

### Interview Process

1. **Request the interview:** Email or call within 1 week of the win/loss event
2. **Set expectations:** "This will take 10 minutes. We want to learn, not sell."
3. **Ask the 5 core questions** below
4. **Ask 2-3 follow-up questions** based on their answers
5. **Thank them** — consider offering a small gift (gift card, account credit) for their time
6. **Log the responses** in the interview log below

### The 5 Core Questions

Ask these in order. Let the customer talk. Do not defend or sell.

#### Question 1: What problem were you trying to solve?

> *Purpose: Understand the job-to-be-done. This reveals whether they were even in our target use case.*

Prompt: "Tell me about the problem or challenge that led you to evaluate solutions like ours."

What to listen for:
- Is this a use case we designed for?
- Is the problem urgent or nice-to-have?
- How were they solving it before?

#### Question 2: What other solutions did you evaluate?

> *Purpose: Map the competitive set from the buyer's perspective. They may compare you to products you do not consider competitors.*

Prompt: "What other options did you consider? Include doing nothing or using spreadsheets."

What to listen for:
- Who is our real competitive set? (May differ from who we track)
- How many alternatives did they evaluate?
- Where did they discover each option?

#### Question 3: What was the deciding factor in your choice?

> *Purpose: Identify the single most important criterion. This is the insight that matters most.*

Prompt: "When it came down to making the final decision, what was the one thing that tipped the scales?"

What to listen for:
- Is it a feature? Price? UX? Trust? Timing?
- Is it something we can control or improve?
- Is it consistent with what other customers say?

#### Question 4: What would have changed your decision?

> *Purpose: For losses, this reveals the minimum viable improvement. For wins, this reveals your vulnerability.*

Prompt (for losses): "Was there anything we could have done differently that would have changed your decision?"
Prompt (for wins): "Was there a point where you almost chose the other option? What would have pushed you that way?"

What to listen for:
- Actionable product changes
- Sales process improvements
- Pricing adjustments
- Unmet expectations

#### Question 5: How would you describe your experience with our product?

> *Purpose: Get an honest assessment of the product experience. For wins, validate strengths. For losses, identify friction.*

Prompt: "Based on what you saw during your evaluation, how would you describe the experience of using our product?"

What to listen for:
- First impression reactions
- Onboarding friction points
- Feature quality assessments
- Support experience feedback

### Follow-Up Questions (Pick 2-3 Based on Context)

- "If you could change one thing about our product, what would it be?"
- "How did the pricing compare to what you expected?"
- "What did you think of [specific competitor]'s product?"
- "How important was [our differentiator] in your evaluation?"
- "Would you recommend us to someone in a similar situation? Why or why not?"
- "How did you first hear about us?"
- "What was your timeline from first research to final decision?"

## Reason Taxonomy

Use consistent categories so patterns are visible in the data. Every win or loss should be assigned a primary reason from this taxonomy.

| Category | Win Examples | Loss Examples |
|----------|------------|---------------|
| **Price** | More affordable than alternatives; better value per dollar; simpler pricing model | Too expensive; no free tier; per-seat pricing too high for large teams |
| **Features** | Had the specific feature they needed; more comprehensive feature set | Missing a critical feature; competitor had a feature we lack |
| **UX / Ease of Use** | Easier to use; better onboarding; more intuitive interface | Confusing UI; steep learning curve; poor onboarding experience |
| **Performance** | Faster; more reliable; better uptime; handles scale | Slow; frequent errors; downtime; cannot handle their data volume |
| **Support** | Responsive support; good documentation; helpful onboarding | Poor support response time; unhelpful documentation; no live chat |
| **Brand / Trust** | Established brand; trusted by similar companies; strong reputation | Unknown company; seemed risky; no social proof for their industry |
| **Integration** | Integrated with their existing tools; API met their needs | Missing a critical integration; API limitations |
| **Timing** | Right place, right time; they were actively looking | Bad timing; not ready to switch; contract locked with competitor |
| **Switching Cost** | Low switching cost; easy migration from current tool | High switching cost; too much effort to migrate data |
| **Security / Compliance** | Met their security requirements; had necessary certifications | Missing SOC 2; no HIPAA compliance; insufficient security features |
| **Customization** | Flexible enough for their workflow; configurable to their needs | Too rigid; could not adapt to their specific workflow |
| **Champion** | Had an internal champion who advocated for us | No internal champion; decision made by committee |

## Action Triggers

Set up automatic escalation when patterns emerge. Do not wait for the quarterly review if these thresholds are crossed.

| Trigger Condition | Action | Owner | Escalation |
|------------------|--------|-------|-----------|
| Loss rate to a specific competitor exceeds 30% in any 30-day period | Run a targeted competitor teardown. Update battle card immediately. | Product lead | Present findings to team within 1 week |
| Same loss reason appears 3+ times in any 30-day period | Escalate to product team for prioritization discussion | Product lead | Add to next sprint planning agenda |
| Win rate drops below 40% for any 30-day period | Trigger emergency competitive review (abbreviated quarterly assessment) | Founder / CEO | Strategic review meeting within 1 week |
| A new competitor appears in 3+ losses within 60 days | Add competitor to monitoring. Run competitor teardown. | Product lead | Add to tracking, create battle card |
| Price cited as loss reason 5+ times in 60 days | Pricing review meeting | Founder / Finance | Assess pricing strategy within 2 weeks |
| Feature gap cited as loss reason 5+ times in 60 days (same feature) | Feature prioritization emergency review | Product lead | Evaluate build timeline within 1 week |
| Win rate against a specific competitor improves by 20%+ | Analyze what changed. Double down on winning factors. | Product lead | Share wins with team for morale |

## Win/Loss Data Collection Methods

Not every win/loss requires a formal interview. Use the appropriate method based on deal size and strategic importance.

| Method | When to Use | Effort | Data Quality |
|--------|-----------|--------|-------------|
| **Automated churn survey** (in-app) | Every churn event | Low (automated) | Medium — biased toward simple answers |
| **Post-trial email survey** | Every trial that does not convert | Low (automated) | Low-Medium — low response rate |
| **Sales notes** (from CRM) | Every competitive deal | Low (part of sales process) | Medium — sales perspective, not buyer perspective |
| **Phone/video interview** | Deals >$500 MRR or strategic losses | High (30 min per interview) | High — nuanced, rich insights |
| **Email interview** (async) | Medium deals, busy contacts | Medium (email + follow-up) | Medium — less nuance but still valuable |
| **Support ticket analysis** | Churn mentions in support | Low (review existing data) | Medium — complaint-biased but authentic |

### Automated Churn Survey Questions

For in-app or email churn surveys, keep it to 3 questions maximum:

1. **Why are you leaving?** (multiple choice)
   - [ ] Too expensive
   - [ ] Missing a feature I need (which one? _____)
   - [ ] Switched to a competitor (which one? _____)
   - [ ] No longer need this type of product
   - [ ] Product was too complicated
   - [ ] Poor support experience
   - [ ] Other: _____

2. **What would have kept you?** (free text, optional)

3. **Would you consider coming back in the future?** (Yes / Maybe / No)

## Data Hygiene Rules

1. **Enter data within 48 hours** of the win/loss event. Memory degrades quickly.
2. **Always assign a primary reason category** from the taxonomy. "Other" is acceptable but should be less than 10% of entries.
3. **Include the competitor name** even if it is "none" or "doing nothing." No-competitor losses are still losses.
4. **Do not editorialize in the tracking table.** Record what the customer said, not your interpretation. Interpret in the quarterly analysis.
5. **Anonymize customer data** in the interview log if sharing broadly.
6. **Review taxonomy quarterly.** If a new pattern emerges that does not fit existing categories, add a new category.
