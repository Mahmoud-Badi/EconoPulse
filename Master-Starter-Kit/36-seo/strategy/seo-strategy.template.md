- [ ] North-star metric is measurable with tools you already have

## Competitive Positioning in Search

Ranking on page 1 is a zero-sum game. Ten organic slots. You need a reason Google and searchers should choose your page over what already ranks.

### SERP Differentiation Framework

For each target keyword, analyze the current page-1 results and identify your angle:

| Differentiation Strategy | When to Use | Example |
|--------------------------|-------------|---------|
| **Depth** | Top results are shallow listicles | Write the 5,000-word definitive guide |
| **Freshness** | Results are 2+ years old | Publish updated-for-{{CURRENT_YEAR}} content |
| **Original data** | No first-party research in SERPs | Conduct a survey, publish proprietary benchmarks |
| **Format** | All results are text walls | Add interactive tools, videos, comparison tables |
| **Specificity** | Results are generic | Target a sub-audience: "for startups," "for {{TARGET_AUDIENCE}}" |
| **Authority** | Results lack credible sourcing | Include expert quotes, case studies, citations |
| **Speed-to-answer** | Results bury the answer below 1,000 words | Answer immediately, then elaborate |

### Competitive Positioning Map

```
                    HIGH authority
                         │
        {{COMPETITOR_1}} │  [Your Target Position]
                         │
LOW ─────────────────────┼───────────────────── HIGH
relevance/               │               relevance/
specificity              │               specificity
                         │
        {{COMPETITOR_2}} │  {{COMPETITOR_3}}
                         │
                    LOW authority
```

**Goal**: Move {{PROJECT_NAME}} to the top-right quadrant — high authority AND high relevance for your target audience.

### SERP Feature Targeting

Identify which SERP features appear for your target keywords and build content to capture them:

| SERP Feature | How to Win It | Content Requirement |
|--------------|---------------|---------------------|
| **Featured Snippet** | Concise paragraph/list/table answering the query directly | Clear Q&A format, structured data |
| **People Also Ask** | Answer related questions within your content | FAQ sections with exact-match questions |
| **AI Overview** | Be cited as a source in AI-generated summaries | Authoritative, well-structured, factually dense |
| **Knowledge Panel** | Establish entity recognition | Wikipedia presence, structured data, consistent NAP |
| **Image Pack** | Optimized, original images | Descriptive filenames, alt text, image sitemap |
| **Video Carousel** | YouTube content targeting the keyword | Video with keyword in title, description, transcript |
| **Sitelinks** | Clear site architecture | Logical navigation, internal linking, breadcrumbs |

## Resource Allocation Model

Where you spend time and money depends on your SEO maturity. Early-stage projects waste budget on link building when technical foundations are broken. Mature sites waste time on technical fixes that yield diminishing returns.

### Time/Budget Split by Maturity Tier

| Activity | Tier 1: Foundation (0-3 mo) | Tier 2: Growth (3-9 mo) | Tier 3: Scale (9-18 mo) | Tier 4: Defend (18+ mo) |
|----------|----------------------------|------------------------|------------------------|------------------------|
| **Technical SEO** | 40% | 20% | 10% | 10% |
| **Content creation** | 25% | 40% | 35% | 30% |
| **Keyword research** | 20% | 10% | 10% | 5% |
| **Link building** | 5% | 20% | 30% | 25% |
| **Content refresh** | 0% | 5% | 10% | 20% |
| **Analytics & optimization** | 10% | 5% | 5% | 10% |

### Staffing Model

| Team Size | Roles | Output Expectation |
|-----------|-------|-------------------|

## Timeline Expectations

SEO is a compounding investment. The first months feel slow. The payoff accelerates after month 6 if you maintain consistency. Below are realistic expectations — not best-case scenarios.

### Months 1-3: Foundation

**What to expect**: Minimal organic traffic growth. Google is discovering and indexing your content. You may see impressions in Search Console with very few clicks.

**Key activities:**
- [ ] Complete technical SEO audit and fix critical issues
- [ ] Conduct keyword research for 50-100 target keywords
- [ ] Publish 6-12 keyword-targeted pages
- [ ] Set up Google Search Console, GA4, rank tracking
- [ ] Optimize existing pages (title tags, meta descriptions, headings, internal links)
- [ ] Submit XML sitemap, fix crawl errors

**Realistic benchmarks:**
- Organic sessions: 50-500/month (depending on domain age)
- Page 1 keywords: 0-3 (likely branded or very long-tail)
- Backlinks: 0-5 referring domains

### Months 3-6: Early Growth

**What to expect**: First ranking movements. Some long-tail keywords start appearing on pages 2-3. A few may break into page 1. Organic traffic begins to show consistent growth.

**Key activities:**
- [ ] Publish 8-16 additional pages, build out first topic cluster
- [ ] Begin link building outreach (guest posts, resource pages, HARO)
- [ ] Optimize pages based on Search Console impression data
- [ ] Fix any remaining technical issues flagged in crawls
- [ ] Start tracking competitors' keyword movements

**Realistic benchmarks:**
- Organic sessions: 500-3,000/month
- Page 1 keywords: 3-15
- Backlinks: 5-20 referring domains
- First organic conversions (signups/leads)

### Months 6-12: Compound Growth

**What to expect**: Meaningful traffic growth. Content library reaches critical mass. Google trusts your domain more, and new pages rank faster. Organic becomes a measurable acquisition channel.

**Key activities:**
- [ ] Scale content production (pillar pages, cluster expansion)
- [ ] Refresh and update top-performing content
- [ ] Build more backlinks systematically
- [ ] Create linkable assets (tools, research, comprehensive guides)
- [ ] Expand keyword targets based on data from Search Console

**Realistic benchmarks:**
- Organic sessions: 3,000-15,000/month
- Page 1 keywords: 15-50
- Backlinks: 20-75 referring domains
- Organic contributing 10-25% of total signups

### Months 12+: Established Authority

**What to expect**: Consistent, compounding growth. New content ranks within weeks rather than months. Organic is a top-3 acquisition channel. Defending existing rankings becomes as important as winning new ones.

**Key activities:**
- [ ] Maintain content freshness (quarterly refresh cycle)
- [ ] Protect rankings from competitor encroachment
- [ ] Expand into adjacent topic areas
- [ ] Consider international/multilingual SEO
- [ ] Regular technical audits (quarterly)
- [ ] Advanced strategies: programmatic SEO, entity optimization, AI overview targeting

**Realistic benchmarks:**
- Organic sessions: 15,000-100,000+/month
- Page 1 keywords: 50-200+
- Backlinks: 75-300+ referring domains
- Organic contributing 25-50% of total signups

### Common Reasons SEO Stalls

| Symptom | Likely Cause | Fix |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| Pages indexed but no impressions | Keyword targeting too competitive or irrelevant | Retarget to lower-difficulty keywords |
| Impressions but no clicks | Poor title tags / meta descriptions | A/B test titles, improve CTR |
| Traffic but no conversions | Wrong intent keywords or poor landing page | Audit intent alignment, improve CTA |
| Rankings plateau | Domain authority ceiling | Invest in link building |
| Rankings drop suddenly | Algorithm update or technical issue | Audit for penalties, check Search Console |

### SaaS SEO Strategy

#### Core Keyword Themes

| Theme | Keyword Pattern | Page Type | Priority |
|-------|----------------|-----------|----------|
| **Category** | "{{PRODUCT_CATEGORY}} software", "{{PRODUCT_CATEGORY}} tool" | Homepage, feature pages | Critical |

#### SaaS-Specific Page Architecture

```
Homepage — "{{PRODUCT_CATEGORY}} software"
├── Features — individual feature pages targeting feature-specific keywords
├── Pricing — "{{PROJECT_NAME}} pricing"
├── Use Cases — one page per audience/industry segment
├── Integrations — one page per integration partner
├── Comparisons — "{{PROJECT_NAME}} vs {{COMPETITOR_NAMES}}"
├── Customers — case studies optimized for "[industry] + [category] case study"
├── Blog — informational and commercial content
│   ├── Pillar pages — comprehensive guides on core topics
│   └── Cluster posts — specific subtopics linking to pillars
└── Docs — technical documentation (ranks for how-to queries)
```

#### SaaS Content Priorities

1. **Comparison and alternative pages** — highest conversion rate from organic for SaaS
2. **Feature pages** — one page per major feature, targeting feature-specific keywords
3. **Integration pages** — "[your product] [partner tool] integration" has low competition and high intent
4. **Problem-solution blog posts** — address the pain points your product solves
5. **Comprehensive guides** — build topical authority in your category

#### SaaS SEO Metrics That Matter

- Organic trial starts (not just traffic)
- Trial-to-paid conversion rate from organic visitors vs other channels
- Organic revenue attribution (LTV of organic-sourced customers)
- Brand search volume trend (leading indicator of product-market fit)

### Developer Tool SEO Strategy

#### Core Keyword Themes

| Theme | Keyword Pattern | Page Type | Priority |
|-------|----------------|-----------|----------|
| **Technology + task** | "how to [task] with [technology]" | Tutorials, docs | Critical |
| **Tool category** | "[technology] [tool type]" | Homepage, feature pages | Critical |
| **Framework-specific** | "[tool type] for [framework]" | Landing pages | High |
| **Benchmarks** | "[tool] performance", "[tool] benchmarks" | Blog posts, data pages | Medium |
| **Migration** | "migrate from [competitor] to [your tool]" | Guide pages | Medium |

#### Developer-Specific Content Strategy

Developers search differently from general audiences:

- **Documentation IS SEO** — well-structured docs rank for thousands of long-tail queries organically
- **Code examples rank** — pages with real, runnable code outrank theory-only pages
- **GitHub presence matters** — README files, starred repos, and discussions create backlinks naturally
- **Stack Overflow answers** — answering questions (genuinely) drives branded search and backlinks
- **Developer blog posts** — technical deep-dives with original benchmarks or architectural insights

#### Content Format Priorities

1. **Getting started guide** — "get started with {{PROJECT_NAME}}" (high intent, moderate volume)
2. **API reference** — ranks for every endpoint/method query
3. **Framework integration guides** — "[framework] + {{PROJECT_NAME}}" for each supported framework
4. **Performance comparison posts** — developers search for benchmarks before adopting tools
5. **Migration guides** — "migrate from [competitor]" captures users already looking to switch
6. **Troubleshooting pages** — "{{PROJECT_NAME}} [error message]" captures support queries

#### Developer Audiences Search on Multiple Platforms

| Platform | SEO Action |
|----------|-----------|
| **Google** | Standard web SEO (pages, blog posts, docs) |
| **GitHub** | Optimize README, topics, description |
| **Stack Overflow** | Answer questions, link to docs when relevant |
| **Dev.to / Hashnode** | Publish technical articles (backlinks + brand awareness) |
| **YouTube** | Tutorial videos with keyword-optimized titles |
| **Reddit** | Participate genuinely in r/programming, r/[technology] subreddits |

### E-Commerce SEO Strategy

#### Core Keyword Themes

| Theme | Keyword Pattern | Page Type | Priority |
|-------|----------------|-----------|----------|
| **Product** | "[product name]", "[product] [color/size/variant]" | Product pages | Critical |
| **Category** | "[product type]", "best [product type]" | Category pages | Critical |
| **Buying intent** | "buy [product] online", "[product] deals" | Product/category pages | High |
| **Comparison** | "[product A] vs [product B]", "[product] reviews" | Blog/comparison pages | High |
| **Informational** | "how to choose [product]", "[product] guide" | Blog/buying guides | Medium |

#### E-Commerce Page Hierarchy for SEO

```
Homepage — "[brand]" + "[main category]"
├── Category pages — "[product type]" (these are your highest-value pages)
│   ├── Subcategory pages — "[specific product type]"
│   │   └── Product pages — "[product name]" + "[product] [key attribute]"
├── Buying guides — "how to choose [product]", "best [product] for [use case]"
├── Brand pages — "shop [brand name] [product type]"
└── Blog — informational content driving top-of-funnel traffic
```

#### E-Commerce-Specific Technical SEO

- **Product schema markup** — price, availability, reviews, images (rich snippets in SERPs)
- **Faceted navigation** — prevent crawl bloat from filter combinations (use canonical tags or noindex)
- **Pagination** — use rel="next"/"prev" or load-more patterns that preserve crawlability
- **Out-of-stock pages** — do NOT 404 them; keep the page live with "notify me" or suggest alternatives
- **User-generated reviews** — unique content on product pages that targets long-tail queries naturally
- **Image SEO** — e-commerce images rank in Google Shopping and image search

#### E-Commerce Content Priorities

1. **Category page optimization** — add 200-500 words of unique content above or below product grids
2. **Product descriptions** — unique, not manufacturer copy (duplicate content kills e-commerce SEO)
3. **Buying guides** — "Best [product] for [use case]" captures comparison shoppers
4. **Product comparison content** — "[Product A] vs [Product B]" pages
5. **User reviews** — encourage reviews; each review adds unique keyword-rich content

<!-- IF {{PRODUCT_TYPE}} == "mobile-app" -->
### Mobile App SEO Strategy (ASO + Web SEO)

Mobile apps need a dual strategy: App Store Optimization (ASO) for store rankings AND web SEO for Google search visibility.

#### App Store Optimization (ASO)

| Element | Apple App Store | Google Play Store |
|---------|----------------|-------------------|
| **App name** | 30 chars, include primary keyword | 30 chars, include primary keyword |
| **Subtitle** | 30 chars, secondary keywords | N/A |
| **Short description** | N/A | 80 chars, keywords + value prop |
| **Keywords field** | 100 chars, comma-separated | N/A (uses description) |
| **Description** | Less weight for ranking, more for conversion | Full-text indexed, include keywords naturally |
| **Screenshots** | First 3 most important (visible without scrolling) | First 3 most important |
| **Ratings** | Critical ranking factor | Critical ranking factor |
| **Update frequency** | Regular updates signal active development | Same |

#### Web SEO for Mobile Apps

Your app needs a website to capture Google search traffic and drive app installs:

```
Website structure:
├── Homepage — "[app category] app" + brand
├── Features — individual feature pages
├── Use cases — "[task] app for [audience]"
├── Blog — content marketing targeting informational keywords
├── Download / Get the App — conversion page
├── Support / FAQ — ranks for "[app name] + [question]"
└── Press / Reviews — social proof, backlinks from review sites
```

#### App-Specific SEO Tactics

- **App indexing** — enable Firebase App Indexing so app content appears in Google search
- **Deep linking** — connect web URLs to in-app content
- **Web-to-app banners** — smart banners on mobile web pages drive installs
- **Review site outreach** — get listed on ProductHunt, G2, Capterra (category-dependent)
- **"Best [category] app" content** — create comparison content where your app is included
