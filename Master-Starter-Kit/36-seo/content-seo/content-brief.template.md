**CTA Rules:**
- Maximum 3 CTAs per page (more feels aggressive and hurts engagement metrics)
- CTAs must match the search intent — informational content gets soft CTAs (newsletter, download), transactional content gets direct CTAs (sign up, buy)

## How to Fill Out Each Section

### Keyword Research

Use {{SEO_TOOL_SUITE}} (Ahrefs, SEMrush, or your primary SEO tool) to gather:

1. **Search volume**: Monthly search volume for the primary keyword in your target market
2. **Keyword difficulty**: The tool's difficulty score (0-100). Below 30 is achievable for newer sites; 30-60 requires strong content and some links; 60+ requires authority and a link-building push.
3. **Secondary keywords**: Run the primary keyword through "Also rank for" or "Related keywords" — pick 5-8 that you can naturally include
4. **Long-tail variations**: Check "Questions" and "Related searches" in your SEO tool and in Google's "People Also Ask"

### SERP Analysis

For each of the top 5 results:

1. Open the page in an incognito browser (to avoid personalization)
2. Use the Ahrefs SEO Toolbar or SEMrush extension to pull DR and backlink count
3. Use the Word Counter Plus extension (or paste into a word counter) for word count
4. Read the actual content — note the angle, structure, depth, and what's missing
5. Check the page's headings using the HeadingsMap extension or browser dev tools

### Content Angle

This is where most briefs fail. "Write about {{TARGET_KEYWORD}}" is not a brief — it is a topic. The angle answers: "Why would Google rank this page above what already ranks?"

Strong angles:
- "More comprehensive: top results skip [subtopic] entirely"
- "More current: top results reference 2022 data; ours will use 2025 data"
- "Original data: no competitor has first-party research on this"
- "Better format: top results are walls of text; ours will include interactive comparisons"
- "More specific: top results are generic; ours targets [specific audience]"

### Internal Linking

Use Ahrefs or Screaming Frog to identify:
- **Link TO**: Pages on your site that are topically related and would benefit from a contextual link
- **Link FROM**: Existing pages that rank and get traffic, where a natural link to this new content would fit

This is critical for distributing link equity and ensuring new content gets crawled quickly.

| **Target publish date** | 2025-04-01 |
| **Status** | Approved |

### Keyword Targeting

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Primary keyword** | how to reduce customer churn |
| **Monthly search volume** | 2,400 |
| **Keyword difficulty** | 38 |
| **Current ranking** | Not ranking (new page) |
| **Content type** | Pillar guide (blog post) |

**Secondary keywords:**

| Secondary Keyword | Monthly Volume | Intent |
|-------------------|---------------|--------|
| customer churn rate | 3,600 | Informational |
| reduce churn rate | 880 | Informational/Commercial |
| churn prevention strategies | 480 | Informational |
| customer retention strategies | 5,400 | Informational/Commercial |
| why customers churn | 720 | Informational |

### Search Intent Classification

| Dimension | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Primary intent** | Informational |
| **Intent evidence** | All top 5 results are long-form guides (not product pages). No shopping results. PAA questions are all "how" and "what" questions. |
| **Searcher stage** | Consideration — they know they have a churn problem and are researching solutions |
| **What the searcher needs** | A comprehensive, actionable guide to identifying why customers leave and implementing retention strategies |
| **Content format** | Long-form guide with numbered strategies, real examples, and a churn calculation section |

### SERP Analysis — Position 1 (abbreviated)

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| URL | example.com/blog/reduce-customer-churn |
| Title tag | 15 Proven Strategies to Reduce Customer Churn in 2024 |
| Domain Rating | 78 |
| Word count | 4,200 |
| Content format | Numbered list of strategies with examples |
| Content angle | Breadth — covers 15 strategies but each gets only 200-300 words |
| Strengths | Comprehensive list, strong domain, good internal linking |
| Weaknesses | Outdated (2024), shallow coverage per strategy, no original data, no churn calculation examples |

### Content Angle and Differentiator

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| What do all top 5 results have in common? | All are listicles (10-20 strategies). All are generic (not industry-specific). None include original data. |
| What is missing from all of them? | Real churn calculation examples, industry benchmarks, a framework for prioritizing which strategy to implement first, and a self-assessment to diagnose the type of churn. |
| Our unique angle | A diagnostic-first approach: help the reader identify their churn type before recommending strategies, with 2025 benchmark data and a prioritization framework. |

**Differentiator statement:**

> This page will outperform existing results by leading with a churn diagnostic framework (none of the top 5 do this), including 2025 industry benchmarks from our proprietary data, and providing a strategy prioritization matrix so readers know where to start.
