## Link Quality Assessment Framework

Before pursuing any link, evaluate it against these quality signals. Not every backlink is worth your time — a single high-quality editorial link from a relevant, authoritative site outweighs 50 low-quality directory links.

### Quality Scoring Matrix

| Signal | Weight | How to Check | Good | Acceptable | Avoid |
|--------|--------|--------------|------|------------|-------|
| **Domain Authority / Domain Rating** | 25% | Ahrefs DR, Moz DA, or Majestic TF | DR 50+ | DR 20-49 | DR < 20 |
| **Topical Relevance** | 30% | Is the linking site in your industry or adjacent niche? | Same niche | Adjacent niche | Completely unrelated |
| **Organic Traffic** | 15% | Ahrefs/SEMrush estimated organic traffic to the linking domain | 10,000+/mo | 1,000-9,999/mo | < 1,000/mo |
| **Editorial Standards** | 15% | Manual review — does the site have real editors, original content, and an engaged audience? | Real editorial team | Mixed quality | Accepts anything, no editorial oversight |
| **Link Placement** | 10% | Where does the link appear? | In-content, contextual | Author bio, sidebar | Footer, sitewide, hidden |
| **Spam Signals** | 5% (disqualifier) | Thin content, excessive outbound links, PBN patterns, non-indexed pages | Clean | Minor concerns | Any spam signals = reject |

### Minimum Thresholds

Do not pursue a link unless it meets **all** of these:

- [ ] Domain has at least 1,000 monthly organic visitors (proves Google trusts it)
- [ ] Site publishes original content (not scraped or AI-generated bulk)
- [ ] The linking page is indexed by Google
- [ ] The link will be contextual (within content), not a footer/sidebar/directory listing
- [ ] The linking site is topically relevant to {{PROJECT_NAME}}

### Quick Assessment Workflow

```
1. Check DR/DA in Ahrefs or Moz           → Below 20? Skip.
2. Check organic traffic in Ahrefs         → Below 1,000/mo? Skip.
3. Visit the site manually                 → Looks spammy? Skip.
4. Check their recent posts                → All sponsored/guest? Skip.
5. Check outbound link ratio               → More outbound than inbound? Skip.
6. Verify Google indexes the target page   → site:domain.com/page — not indexed? Skip.
7. Passes all checks                       → Add to outreach list.
```

## Method 1: Guest Posting

Guest posting means writing original, high-value content for another site's audience in exchange for an editorial backlink. When done right, it builds relationships, drives referral traffic, and earns contextual links from relevant domains. When done wrong, it is indistinguishable from spam.

### Step-by-Step Process

**Step 1: Find Target Sites**

Use search operators to find sites that accept guest contributions:

```
"{{TARGET_TOPIC}}" + "write for us"
"{{TARGET_TOPIC}}" + "guest post guidelines"
"{{TARGET_TOPIC}}" + "contribute" + "article"
"{{TARGET_TOPIC}}" + "guest author"
"{{TARGET_TOPIC}}" + inurl:contribute
```

Also:
- Check where competitors have guest posted (Ahrefs → Competitor domain → Backlinks → filter by "guest" in anchor/title)
- Look at author bios on industry blogs — guest authors often write for multiple sites
- Use AllTop, Feedspot, or BuzzSumo to find active blogs in your niche

**Step 2: Qualify Targets**

Run every prospect through the Quality Scoring Matrix above. Build a spreadsheet:

| Site | DR | Monthly Traffic | Relevance (1-5) | Editorial Quality (1-5) | Contact Email | Status |
|------|-----|----------------|-----------------|------------------------|---------------|--------|
| example-blog.com | 52 | 45,000 | 5 | 4 | editor@example-blog.com | To pitch |

**Step 3: Research Before Pitching**

Before you email anyone:
- Read their last 10 posts — understand their voice, topics, and audience level
- Check what topics they have NOT covered that you could fill
- Find a content gap that aligns with your expertise
- Follow them on social media and engage genuinely for 1-2 weeks before pitching (optional but significantly improves response rates)

**Step 4: Pitch**

```
Subject: [Specific Topic Idea] for [Site Name]

Hi [Editor Name],

I've been reading [Site Name] for a while — your piece on [specific article]
really resonated, especially [specific point].

I noticed you haven't covered [specific gap] yet, and I think your audience
would find it valuable because [reason tied to their audience].

I'd love to write a piece on "[Proposed Title]" that covers:
- [Angle 1]
- [Angle 2]
- [Angle 3]

For context, I'm [brief credential — role at {{PROJECT_NAME}}, relevant
experience]. Here are a couple of my previous pieces:
- [Link to published article 1]
- [Link to published article 2]

Happy to send a draft outline if you're interested.

Best,
[Name]
```

**Step 5: Write and Deliver**

- Match the host site's style, tone, and formatting conventions exactly
- Write content that is genuinely better than what they could produce without you
- Include 1-2 natural links to {{PROJECT_NAME}} content (not the homepage — link to a genuinely useful resource page, tool, or guide)
- Include 3-5 links to the host site's own existing content (shows you are adding value to their ecosystem)
- Deliver on time, with images/screenshots if relevant

**Step 6: Promote After Publication**

- Share the published piece on your social channels and tag the host site
- Send the link to your email list (drives traffic to the host site, making them more likely to accept future pitches)
- Respond to every comment on the guest post

### Success Metrics

| Metric | Expected Range |
|--------|---------------|
| Outreach response rate | 5-15% |
| Pitch acceptance rate | 10-25% of responses |
| Time from pitch to publication | 2-8 weeks |
| Links per published post | 1-2 |
| Referral traffic per post (first 30 days) | 50-500 visitors |

### Time Investment

- Prospecting and qualifying: 3-4 hours per 50 targets
- Pitching: 20-30 minutes per personalized pitch
- Writing: 4-8 hours per article (2,000-3,000 words)
- Total per link earned: 8-15 hours

### Expected Results Timeline

- Month 1: 50 prospects identified, 20 pitches sent, 2-4 acceptances
- Month 2: First 1-2 articles published, begin second batch of pitches
- Month 3+: Steady state of 2-4 guest posts published per month

## Method 2: Resource Page Outreach

Resource pages are curated lists of links on a specific topic ("Best resources for learning Python," "Top marketing tools for startups"). These pages exist to link out — your job is to prove your resource belongs on the list.

### Step-by-Step Process

**Step 1: Find Resource Pages**

```
"{{TARGET_TOPIC}}" + "resources"
"{{TARGET_TOPIC}}" + "useful links"
"{{TARGET_TOPIC}}" + "recommended tools"
"{{TARGET_TOPIC}}" + inurl:resources
"{{TARGET_TOPIC}}" + intitle:"best" + intitle:"resources"
"{{TARGET_TOPIC}}" + "helpful links"
```

**Step 2: Qualify**

Resource pages must:
- Be on a domain with DR 20+ and real organic traffic
- Be currently indexed (site:URL in Google)
- Have been updated in the last 12 months (check Wayback Machine or page date)
- List resources genuinely relevant to what {{PROJECT_NAME}} offers

**Step 3: Identify Your Fit**

Determine which of your pages belongs on the resource list. The page you pitch must be:
- Genuinely useful as a standalone resource (not a product page or sales pitch)
- Better than at least some resources already listed
- Free to access (no login-wall for the core content)

**Step 4: Outreach**

```
Subject: Suggestion for your [Topic] resources page

Hi [Name],

I came across your [topic] resources page at [URL] — great collection,
especially [mention a specific resource they listed and why].

I recently published [description of your resource] at [URL], which covers
[specific value proposition]. I think it would be a helpful addition for
your readers because [specific reason].

Either way, thanks for maintaining such a useful list.

Best,
[Name]
```

### Success Metrics

| Metric | Expected Range |
|--------|---------------|
| Outreach response rate | 8-20% |
| Link placement rate | 15-30% of responses |
| Time from outreach to link placement | 1-4 weeks |

### Time Investment

- Prospecting: 2-3 hours per 30 resource pages
- Outreach: 10-15 minutes per email
- Total per link earned: 2-4 hours

### Expected Results Timeline

- Month 1: 30 resource pages identified, 30 outreach emails sent, 2-5 links placed
- Month 2+: Ongoing as you create new linkable resources

## Method 3: Broken Link Building

Find broken (404) outbound links on relevant sites. Offer your content as a replacement. The site owner fixes a broken link on their page and you earn a backlink — everyone wins.

### Step-by-Step Process

**Step 1: Find Broken Links on Target Sites**

Two approaches:

**Approach A — Target specific sites:**
1. Identify high-authority sites in your niche
2. Run them through Ahrefs (Site Explorer → Outgoing links → Broken links) or Screaming Frog
3. Find 404 outbound links that pointed to content similar to yours

**Approach B — Find broken links at scale:**
1. Search for resource pages in your niche (same as Method 2)
2. Install the "Check My Links" browser extension
3. Run it on each resource page to find broken outbound links
4. For each broken link, check what the page used to contain (Wayback Machine)

**Step 2: Create or Identify Your Replacement Content**

The replacement must:
- Cover the same topic as the dead page
- Be at least as comprehensive as the original (check Wayback Machine)
- Be live, functional, and free to access

If you do not have a replacement, consider creating one — but only if the topic aligns with your content strategy. Do not create throwaway content just for link building.

**Step 3: Outreach**

```
Subject: Broken link on your [topic] page

Hi [Name],

I was reading your article on [topic] at [URL] and noticed that
the link to [anchor text of broken link] appears to be broken
(it returns a 404).

I recently published a comprehensive guide on [same topic] at [your URL]
that covers [brief description]. It might work as a replacement.

Either way, thought you'd want to know about the broken link.

Best,
[Name]
```

### Success Metrics

| Metric | Expected Range |
|--------|---------------|
| Outreach response rate | 10-25% |
| Link replacement rate | 20-40% of responses |
| Time from outreach to replacement | 1-3 weeks |

### Time Investment

- Prospecting and finding broken links: 3-5 hours per 20 opportunities
- Outreach: 10-15 minutes per email
- Total per link earned: 3-5 hours

### Expected Results Timeline

- Month 1: 20 broken link opportunities found, outreach sent, 2-6 links earned
- Month 2+: Ongoing prospecting yields 3-8 links per month

## Method 4: HARO / Connectively / Qwoted

Journalists and content creators post source requests on platforms like Help A Reporter Out (HARO, now Connectively), Qwoted, SourceBottle, and Terkel. You respond with expert commentary. When they publish, you get a link.

### Step-by-Step Process

**Step 1: Set Up Accounts**

- **Connectively** (formerly HARO): connectively.us — free tier available, queries delivered via email 3x daily
- **Qwoted**: qwoted.com — free tier, direct journalist matching
- **Terkel**: terkel.io — curated expert contributions
- **SourceBottle**: sourcebottle.com — smaller but less competition
- **Featured.com**: featured.com — Q&A format, easier to get started

**Step 2: Filter Ruthlessly**

You will receive 50-100+ queries per day across platforms. Filter to only respond to queries where:
- The publication has DR 40+ and real organic traffic
- The topic directly relates to your expertise
- You can provide a genuinely expert answer (not generic filler)
- The deadline is achievable

**Step 3: Write Expert Responses**

The winning formula for HARO/Connectively responses:

```
Subject: Re: [Exact query subject line] — [Your Name], [Title]

Hi [Journalist Name],

[2-3 sentence response that directly answers the query with a specific,
actionable insight. Lead with the answer, not your credentials.]

[1-2 sentences of supporting context, data, or a brief example.]

[Optional: 1 sentence with a counterintuitive take or a common mistake
people make — journalists love unique angles.]

---
[Full Name]
[Title], {{PROJECT_NAME}}
[One-line bio or relevant credential]
[URL to your author page or company]
```

**Rules for winning responses:**
- Respond within 2 hours of the query going live (early responses get priority)
- Keep it under 200 words (journalists are skimming 50+ responses)
- Be specific — data points, percentages, and examples beat generic advice
- Do not pitch your product. Provide pure expertise.
- Include a professional headshot URL if requested

**Step 4: Track and Follow Up**

Maintain a response tracker:

| Date | Platform | Query Topic | Publication | Journalist | Response Sent | Published? | Link Earned? |
|------|----------|-------------|-------------|------------|--------------|------------|-------------|
| 2024-01-15 | HARO | Best SEO tools | Forbes | J. Smith | Yes | Yes | DR 94 dofollow |

### Success Metrics

| Metric | Expected Range |
|--------|---------------|
| Queries responded to per week | 5-15 |
| Publication rate | 5-15% of responses |
| Average link DR | 50-90 (HARO attracts high-authority publications) |
| Time from response to publication | 1-8 weeks |

### Time Investment

- Daily query review and filtering: 15-30 minutes
- Writing responses: 10-20 minutes each
- Weekly total: 3-6 hours
- Total per link earned: 4-8 hours

### Expected Results Timeline

- Month 1: 20-40 responses sent, 1-4 links earned
- Month 3: Response quality improves from feedback, 3-8 links per month
- Month 6+: Journalists begin reaching out to you directly

## Method 5: Linkable Assets

Create content so valuable that other sites link to it without you asking. This is the highest-leverage link building method — a single linkable asset can earn dozens or hundreds of links over its lifetime.

### Asset Types and Playbooks

#### Original Research and Data Studies

**What it is:** Conduct original research (surveys, data analysis, experiments) and publish the findings.

**Why it works:** Journalists, bloggers, and content creators constantly need data to cite. If you are the primary source, every citation is a backlink.

**Process:**
1. Identify a question your industry asks but nobody has answered with data
2. Design the study (survey 200+ respondents, analyze your own product data, or scrape public data)
3. Conduct the research and analyze the results
4. Publish a comprehensive report with charts, key findings, and methodology
5. Write a press release highlighting the most surprising findings
6. Outreach to journalists and bloggers who cover the topic

**Examples:**
- "State of [Industry] 2025" annual report
- "We analyzed 10,000 [things] — here's what we learned"
- "Survey: What [target audience] really thinks about [topic]"

**Time investment:** 40-80 hours for research + publication
**Expected links:** 20-200+ over the asset's lifetime
**Results timeline:** First links within 2-4 weeks of publication; long tail of citations for 1-3 years

#### Free Tools and Calculators

**What it is:** Build a free tool that solves a specific problem for your target audience.

**Process:**
1. Identify a calculation, conversion, or analysis your audience performs manually
2. Build a simple web tool that automates it (can be a single-page calculator)
3. Publish it on a clean URL (e.g., `{{DOMAIN}}/tools/[tool-name]`)
4. Outreach to resource pages and tool roundup lists
5. Submit to Product Hunt, Hacker News, and relevant communities

**Examples:**
- ROI calculators, cost estimators, readability analyzers, color palette generators
- Any tool that someone would bookmark and share with colleagues

**Time investment:** 20-60 hours for development + launch
**Expected links:** 10-100+ (tools earn links passively forever)
**Results timeline:** First links within 1-2 weeks; accelerates as the tool gets discovered

#### Comprehensive Guides (Definitive Pillar Content)

**What it is:** The most thorough guide on a topic that exists on the internet.

**Process:**
1. Choose a topic where existing content is fragmented, outdated, or shallow
2. Write the definitive resource (5,000-15,000 words, with original diagrams and examples)
3. Structure for skimmability (clear H2/H3 hierarchy, table of contents, key takeaways)
4. Outreach to sites that currently link to inferior guides on the same topic
5. Update annually to maintain freshness

**Time investment:** 20-40 hours for writing + design
**Expected links:** 10-50+ over its lifetime
**Results timeline:** 1-3 months for initial links; compounds as it ranks and gets discovered

#### Infographics and Visual Data Assets

**What it is:** Complex data or processes visualized in a shareable format.

**Process:**
1. Choose data that tells a compelling story
2. Design a professional infographic (hire a designer or use tools like Canva, Venngage, Piktochart)
3. Publish with an embed code for easy sharing
4. Outreach to bloggers in your niche with a preview + embed code
5. Submit to infographic directories (Visual.ly, Infographic Journal)

**Time investment:** 10-20 hours for data + design + outreach
**Expected links:** 5-30+ per infographic
**Results timeline:** 2-4 weeks for initial links

## Method 6: Digital PR

Earn links by getting your brand, data, or expert commentary featured in news publications and industry media. Digital PR targets authoritative publications (DR 70+) that traditional outreach rarely reaches.

> For a full Digital PR playbook, see [digital-pr-strategy.template.md](digital-pr-strategy.template.md).

### Step-by-Step Process

**Step 1: Develop a Data-Driven Story**

Journalists publish data, not pitches. Create a newsworthy data asset:
- Analyze proprietary product data for trends ("Our users saw a 47% increase in X")
- Conduct an industry survey with publishable findings
- Analyze public data in a novel way (government datasets, public APIs, academic research)
- Create a rankings list or index ("Best cities for [topic]," "Most [adjective] industries in 2025")

**Step 2: Build a Targeted Media List**

- Use Muck Rack, Cision, or manual research to find journalists who cover your topic
- Target 30-50 journalists per campaign
- Prioritize journalists who have covered similar data stories in the past 6 months
- Find their preferred contact method (most prefer email; some prefer Twitter DM)

**Step 3: Craft the Pitch**

```
Subject: Data: [Key finding — specific number or statistic]

Hi [Journalist Name],

[One sentence with the most newsworthy finding.]

We [methodology — surveyed/analyzed] [sample size] and found [2-3 key findings]:

1. [Finding with specific data point]
2. [Finding with specific data point]
3. [Finding with specific data point]

Full data and methodology available here: [link to your published report]
High-res charts available on request.

Happy to provide additional analysis or expert commentary.

Best,
[Name], [Title] at {{PROJECT_NAME}}
```

**Step 4: Follow Up (Once)**

If no response after 3-5 business days, send one follow-up:

```
Subject: Re: Data: [Key finding]

Hi [Journalist Name],

Just following up on the data below — happy to provide the raw dataset
or additional expert context if it's relevant to anything you're working on.

[Original pitch text]
```

Never follow up more than once.

### Success Metrics

| Metric | Expected Range |
|--------|---------------|
| Pitch open rate | 30-50% |
| Response rate | 5-15% |
| Coverage/link rate | 2-8% of pitches sent |
| Average link DR | 60-90+ |
| Links per successful campaign | 5-30+ |

### Time Investment

- Data asset creation: 20-40 hours
- Media list building: 3-5 hours
- Pitching and follow-up: 5-10 hours
- Total per campaign: 30-55 hours
- Total per link earned: 3-8 hours (campaigns produce multiple links)

### Expected Results Timeline

- Week 1-2: Data asset created and published
- Week 3: Pitches sent
- Week 4-6: First coverage and links appear
- Month 2-3: Long-tail coverage as the data gets discovered and cited

## Method 7: Unlinked Mention Reclamation

When someone mentions {{PROJECT_NAME}} (or your brand, product, founder, or original research) without linking to your site, you have a warm lead. They already know and reference you — you just need to ask for the link.

### Step-by-Step Process

**Step 1: Find Unlinked Mentions**

Tools for discovery:
- **Ahrefs Content Explorer**: Search for "{{PROJECT_NAME}}" → filter by "one article per domain" → filter out pages that already link to your domain
- **Google Alerts**: Set up alerts for your brand name, product names, founder names, and key content titles
- **Mention.com or Brand24**: Real-time mention monitoring across web and social
- **Manual Google search**: `"{{PROJECT_NAME}}" -site:{{DOMAIN}}` → check each result for a link

**Step 2: Qualify Mentions**

Not every unlinked mention is worth pursuing. Prioritize:
- High-DR sites (40+)
- Contextual mentions (the page discusses a topic related to what you do)
- Positive or neutral sentiment (do not ask for links from critical articles)
- Pages with organic traffic (the link will actually drive visitors)

**Step 3: Outreach**

```
Subject: Thanks for mentioning {{PROJECT_NAME}}!

Hi [Name],

I just came across your article on [topic] at [URL] — thanks for
mentioning {{PROJECT_NAME}}! Really appreciate the context you provided.

Would you mind adding a link to [specific page URL] where you mentioned
us? It would help your readers find more detail, and we'd be grateful
for the reference.

Thanks either way — great piece.

Best,
[Name]
```

### Success Metrics

| Metric | Expected Range |
|--------|---------------|
| Unlinked mentions found per month | 5-50+ (depends on brand awareness) |
| Outreach response rate | 20-40% |
| Link conversion rate | 30-50% of responses |
| Time from outreach to link | 1-2 weeks |

### Time Investment

- Monitoring and discovery: 1-2 hours per week
- Outreach: 5-10 minutes per email
- Total per link earned: 30-60 minutes

### Expected Results Timeline

- Month 1: Set up monitoring, find historical mentions, send initial outreach
- Ongoing: 2-10 links per month depending on brand awareness

## Method 8: Community Participation

Earn links and build authority by contributing genuinely helpful content in communities where your target audience participates. This is the slowest method but builds the deepest trust and produces the most natural link profile.

### Step-by-Step Process

**Step 1: Identify Communities**

| Community Type | Examples | Link Type |
|----------------|----------|-----------|
| Forums | Reddit, niche forums, Discourse communities | Nofollow (but drives traffic and brand signals) |
| Q&A sites | Stack Overflow, Quora, Stack Exchange sites | Nofollow (but drives referral traffic) |
| Open source | GitHub, GitLab — contributing code, docs, or issues | Dofollow from project pages |
| Professional communities | Indie Hackers, Hacker News, Dev.to, Product Hunt | Mixed |
| Slack/Discord groups | Industry-specific communities | No direct link, but leads to mentions |

**Step 2: Participate Before You Link**

The cardinal rule: contribute value for weeks or months before ever sharing a link. Build a reputation first.

- Answer questions thoroughly (cite sources, provide examples, share code snippets)
- Share insights from your experience that cannot be found in a Google search
- Help others debug problems, even when it has nothing to do with your product
- Upvote and comment on others' contributions

**Step 3: Share Links When Genuinely Relevant**

When someone asks a question that your content genuinely answers:
- Provide the answer directly in your comment (do not just drop a link)
- Add a link to your relevant resource as supplementary reading
- Be transparent about your affiliation ("Full disclosure: I work at {{PROJECT_NAME}}")

**Step 4: Contribute to Open Source**

- Contribute documentation improvements to tools your audience uses
- Create open-source tools, plugins, or integrations
- Write case studies or tutorials that reference open-source projects
- Sponsor community events or open-source projects (often includes a link on the project site)

### Success Metrics

| Metric | Expected Range |
|--------|---------------|
| Community reputation growth | Gradual — measured in karma, reputation score, or followers |
| Referral traffic from communities | 100-1,000+ visitors/month (after establishing presence) |
| Direct links earned | 1-5 per month (from community members referencing your content) |
| Indirect links earned | Unmeasurable but real — community presence leads to people citing you in their content |

### Time Investment

- Daily participation: 30-60 minutes
- Weekly total: 3-5 hours
- Total per link earned: High (10-20+ hours), but the compounding brand effects justify it

### Expected Results Timeline

- Month 1-2: Building presence, zero links
- Month 3-6: First organic mentions and links from community members
- Month 6+: Established authority, consistent referral traffic, inbound link-building invitations

## Toxic Link Identification and Disavow Process

Not all links help. Some actively harm your rankings. Google's algorithms can usually ignore toxic links, but in severe cases (manual penalties, negative SEO attacks), you need to intervene.

### What Makes a Link Toxic?

| Toxic Signal | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **PBN (Private Blog Network)** | Sites created solely to manipulate link equity — thin content, shared IP/hosting, interlinked with no real audience |
| **Paid links without nofollow** | Bought links that pass PageRank violate Google's guidelines |
| **Link farms** | Sites with thousands of outbound links and no editorial discretion |
| **Irrelevant foreign-language spam** | Links from sites in unrelated languages/niches with no editorial context |
| **Sitewide footer/sidebar links** | Especially if anchor text is keyword-rich |
| **Hacked sites** | Links injected into legitimate sites via security vulnerabilities |
| **Excessive exact-match anchor text** | Unnatural anchor text distribution signals manipulation |

### Identification Process

1. **Export your full backlink profile** from Ahrefs, SEMrush, or Google Search Console
2. **Sort by spam score** (Ahrefs has a built-in spam rating; Moz has Spam Score)
3. **Manually review flagged links** — automation flags false positives, so always verify
4. **Check anchor text distribution** — if more than 10% of anchor text is exact-match keyword (and not your brand name), investigate those links
5. **Cross-reference with Google Search Console** — if you have a manual action for "unnatural links," the linked pages are your starting list

### Disavow Process

**Only disavow if:**
- You have a manual penalty in Google Search Console, OR
- You have clear evidence of a negative SEO attack, OR
- You have inherited a toxic link profile from a domain purchase

**Steps:**
1. Attempt outreach first — email webmasters and request link removal. Document your attempts.
2. Wait 2-4 weeks for responses
3. For links that cannot be removed, create a disavow file:

```
# Disavow file for {{DOMAIN}}
# Generated: {{CURRENT_DATE}}
# Reason: [manual penalty / negative SEO / inherited spam]

# Individual URLs to disavow
https://spam-site-1.com/page-with-link
https://spam-site-2.com/spammy-page

# Entire domains to disavow
domain:spam-domain-1.com
domain:spam-domain-2.com
```

4. Submit via Google Search Console → Security & Manual Actions → Disavow Tool
5. Monitor Search Console for 4-8 weeks
6. If you had a manual penalty, submit a reconsideration request after disavowing

### Disavow Anti-Patterns

- **Do not disavow aggressively.** Only disavow links you are confident are toxic. Disavowing legitimate links harms your own rankings.
- **Do not disavow competitors.** The disavow tool is for your own domain's backlinks only.
- **Do not disavow and forget.** Review your disavow file every 6 months — sites change, and a previously toxic domain may have been cleaned up.

## Link Velocity Management

Link velocity is the rate at which your site acquires new backlinks over time. Google expects natural link growth — sudden spikes followed by flatlines look artificial.

### Natural Growth Patterns

| Site Stage | Natural Monthly Link Velocity | Notes |
|-----------|------------------------------|-------|
| New site (0-6 months) | 5-20 links/month | Mostly from launch PR, social, and initial outreach |
| Growing site (6-18 months) | 10-50 links/month | Mix of earned and outreach-driven |
| Established site (18+ months) | 20-100+ links/month | Most links should be earned passively from content |

### Velocity Red Flags

- **Spike-then-drop:** 200 links in one month, then 5 the next month — looks purchased
- **Perfectly consistent:** Exactly 30 links every month for 12 months — looks automated
- **All from the same domain cluster:** 50 links from 50 sites on the same hosting provider
- **Sudden anchor text shift:** Normal anchor distribution, then suddenly 80% exact-match keyword anchors

### Management Strategy

1. **Smooth out campaigns.** If a Digital PR campaign earns 50 links in week 1, don't run another campaign in week 2. Space campaigns 4-6 weeks apart.
2. **Maintain a baseline.** Use ongoing methods (community participation, HARO, unlinked mentions) to provide consistent low-volume link growth between campaigns.
3. **Diversify sources.** No single method should account for more than 40% of your monthly new links.
4. **Monitor velocity.** Check Ahrefs "New referring domains" weekly. Flag any month where new links exceed 3x your trailing 3-month average.

## Link Building Anti-Patterns

These will get you penalized. Do not do them. Do not let anyone on your team do them. Do not hire an agency that does them.

### Hard No — Immediate Penalty Risk

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails | Consequence |
|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| **Buying links** | Google's algorithms (and SpamBrain) detect paid link patterns. Manual reviewers investigate purchases. | Manual action, ranking demotion, deindexing |
| **Private Blog Networks (PBNs)** | Networks of fake sites cross-linking to manipulate PageRank. Detectable via shared hosting, WHOIS, content patterns. | Entire network deindexed; sites linking to PBNs penalized |
| **Link farms / link schemes** | Mass link exchange networks ("I'll link to you if you link to me" at scale) | Algorithmic demotion, manual penalty |
| **Comment spam** | Dropping links in blog comments, forum signatures, and user profiles | Links are nofollow anyway; gets your domain flagged as spam |
| **Automated link building tools** | Software that auto-generates links across directories, wikis, and forums | Immediate pattern detection and penalty |
| **Doorway pages** | Creating pages solely to rank for keywords and redirect to your money page | Core algorithm penalty |

### Soft No — Diminishing Returns and Risk

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Press release links** | Nofollow by default; search engines have devalued them since 2013 |
| **Low-quality directory submissions** | Only useful if the directory has real traffic and editorial standards |
| **Article spinning** | Duplicate content detection catches spun articles; sites that accept them are toxic |
| **Reciprocal link arrangements** | Small-scale and natural is fine; large-scale and organized is a scheme |
| **Sponsored post abuse** | Sponsored links must be tagged `rel="sponsored"` — using them for dofollow links violates guidelines |

### How to Spot If Your Agency Is Using Anti-Patterns

If you outsource link building, verify monthly:
- [ ] Request a full list of links earned with exact URLs
- [ ] Check that linking sites have real traffic (not just DA/DR)
- [ ] Verify that linking sites have editorial content (not auto-generated)
- [ ] Confirm links are contextual, not footers/sidebars/author bio farms
- [ ] Check anchor text distribution for unnatural exact-match patterns
- [ ] Google "[linking domain] + 'guest post'" — if the site is a guest-post mill, the link is low value

## Monthly Link Building Workflow

### Week 1: Prospecting and Monitoring

- [ ] Review new unlinked brand mentions (tools: Ahrefs Alerts, Google Alerts, Mention)
- [ ] Identify 10-15 new guest posting prospects
- [ ] Find 5-10 broken link opportunities on high-DR sites
- [ ] Review HARO/Connectively queries daily (ongoing)

### Week 2: Outreach

- [ ] Send guest post pitches (10-15 personalized emails)
- [ ] Send broken link building outreach (5-10 emails)
- [ ] Send unlinked mention reclamation emails (all found in Week 1)
- [ ] Respond to 5-10 HARO/Connectively queries (ongoing)

### Week 3: Content Creation

- [ ] Write and submit 1-2 accepted guest posts
- [ ] Create or update 1 linkable asset (tool, guide, infographic, or data study)
- [ ] Contribute to 3-5 community discussions with genuine value

### Week 4: Measurement and Planning

- [ ] Update link tracking spreadsheet with all new links earned
- [ ] Calculate monthly link velocity and compare to trailing 3-month average
- [ ] Assess link quality distribution (DR, relevance, anchor text)
- [ ] Review which methods produced the best ROI this month
- [ ] Plan next month's focus areas based on results
- [ ] Update the Digital PR story pipeline for next month's campaign

### Monthly Time Budget

| Activity | Hours/Month |
|----------|------------|
| Prospecting and monitoring | 6-8 |
| Outreach (email) | 4-6 |
| Guest post writing | 8-16 |
| HARO/Connectively responses | 12-24 |
| Linkable asset creation | 8-20 |
| Community participation | 12-20 |
| Measurement and reporting | 2-3 |
| **Total** | **52-97** |

Scale up or down based on your team size and SEO maturity level. A solo founder might spend 15-20 hours/month focusing on the highest-ROI methods (unlinked mentions, HARO, and one linkable asset). A dedicated SEO team can run the full workflow.
