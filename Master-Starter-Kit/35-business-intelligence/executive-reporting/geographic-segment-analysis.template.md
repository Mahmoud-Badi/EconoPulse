## 6. Market Penetration: TAM/SAM/SOM by Geography

### Definitions

| Term | Definition | How to Calculate |
|---|---|---|
| **TAM** (Total Addressable Market) | Total revenue opportunity if you had 100% market share globally | Industry reports, analyst estimates, or bottom-up calculation |
| **SAM** (Serviceable Addressable Market) | Portion of TAM that your product can actually serve (by geography, segment, capability) | TAM filtered by your go-to-market reach and product capability |
| **SOM** (Serviceable Obtainable Market) | Realistic share you can capture in the near term | SAM multiplied by realistic capture rate given competition and resources |

### Expansion Readiness Checklist

Before committing to a new geographic market, verify:

- [ ] **Product readiness:** Is the product localized (language, currency, date formats)?
- [ ] **Legal readiness:** Are terms of service, privacy policy, and DPA updated for the jurisdiction?
- [ ] **Tax readiness:** Is the billing system configured for local tax requirements (VAT, GST, etc.)?
- [ ] **Data residency:** Does the region require local data storage? If so, is infrastructure provisioned?
- [ ] **Support readiness:** Can we provide support in the local timezone? In the local language?
- [ ] **Payment readiness:** Are local payment methods supported (SEPA, Boleto, iDEAL, etc.)?
- [ ] **Go-to-market readiness:** Do we have localized marketing content and a channel strategy?
- [ ] **Competitive landscape:** Have we mapped local competitors and differentiation?

### Bubble Chart: Market Opportunity

**Chart type:** Bubble chart.

**Layout:**
- X-axis: Current revenue (or penetration).
- Y-axis: Growth rate.
- Bubble size: SAM (market size).
- Bubble color: LTV:CAC ratio (green = healthy, red = unhealthy).

**Interpretation:**
- **Top-right large bubbles:** High-growth, large-SAM markets where you already have traction. Invest heavily.
- **Top-left large bubbles:** High-growth, large-SAM markets where you have low penetration. Expansion opportunity.
- **Bottom-right small bubbles:** Low-growth, small markets where you have traction. Maintain but don't over-invest.
- **Bottom-left:** Low-growth, low-penetration. Deprioritize.

## 9. Data Collection Methods

### How to Capture Geographic Data

| Method | Data Captured | Accuracy | Privacy Implication |
|---|---|---|---|
| Billing address | Country, state/province, postal code | High (for paying customers) | Low (business data) |
| User profile (self-reported) | Country, timezone, language preference | Medium (depends on completion rate) | Low (explicit consent) |
| IP geolocation | Country, region, city | Medium (VPNs, proxies reduce accuracy) | Medium (may require disclosure) |
| Payment method origin | Country of card/bank | High | Low (transactional data) |
| CRM enrichment | Company HQ, employee count, industry | High (for enriched records) | Medium (third-party data) |
| Phone number prefix | Country | Low-Medium | Low |

### Recommended Approach

1. **Primary source:** Billing address (highest accuracy for paying customers).
2. **Secondary source:** User profile country (for free users or when billing address is incomplete).
3. **Fallback:** IP geolocation (only when no explicit data is available).
4. **Enrichment:** CRM data enrichment services for company size, industry, and HQ location.

### Data Quality Issues

| Issue | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Missing country data | Incomplete geographic analysis | Make country required in billing; prompt in profile |
| VPN / proxy users | IP geolocation misattribution | Use billing address as primary source |
| Multinational companies | Unclear which country to attribute | Attribute to billing entity location; track subsidiary locations separately |
| Self-reported inaccuracy | Users select wrong country | Pre-populate from IP, allow override |
| Currency ≠ geography | USD billing doesn't mean US customer | Never infer geography from currency alone |
