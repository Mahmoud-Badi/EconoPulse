## 1. Preparation Phase (Weeks 1-4)

### Pre-Fundraising Checklist

Complete every item before sending a single outreach email:

- [ ] Pitch deck finalized and reviewed by 3+ people outside the company (see `pitch-deck-framework.template.md`)
- [ ] Financial model complete with 3-year projections and defensible assumptions (Section 25)
- [ ] Cap table current and modeled for this round's dilution (see `cap-table-planning.template.md`)
- [ ] Data room organized with all due diligence documents (see `due-diligence-prep.template.md`)
- [ ] One-pager / executive summary (1 page, PDF) for cold outreach
- [ ] Investor CRM set up in {{FUNDRAISING_CRM}} (see `investor-crm.template.md`)
- [ ] Target investor list built (minimum 50-80 names for seed, 30-50 for Series A)
- [ ] Warm introduction paths mapped for top 20 targets
- [ ] Practice pitch delivered 5+ times with feedback incorporated
- [ ] Answers prepared for the 30 most common investor questions
- [ ] Term sheet preferences documented (see `term-sheet-analysis.template.md`)
- [ ] Legal counsel engaged and available for term sheet review

### Target Investor List Building

#### Research Criteria

| Criterion | Filter |
|---|---|
| Stage Focus | Investors who actively invest at {{FUNDRAISING_STAGE}} |
| Check Size | Investors whose typical check fits your round |
| Sector Focus | Investors with portfolio companies in your vertical |
| Geography | Investors who fund companies in your location (or are location-agnostic) |
| Recent Activity | Investors who have made investments in the last 6 months |
| Portfolio Conflicts | No direct competitors in their portfolio |
| Value-Add | Specific expertise, network, or resources relevant to your business |

#### Research Sources

- Crunchbase / PitchBook — filter by stage, sector, and recent deals
- AngelList — angel and micro VC profiles
- Twitter/X — follow and engage with active investors before outreach
- Portfolio company founders — best reference check and introduction source
- Accelerator alumni networks — warm paths to their investor networks
- LinkedIn — mutual connections for warm introductions

## 2. Outreach Strategy

### Warm vs. Cold Outreach

| Method | Response Rate | When to Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Warm intro from portfolio founder** | 40-60% | Always preferred — highest signal |
| **Warm intro from mutual connection** | 20-40% | Strong second option |
| **Cold email (personalized)** | 5-15% | When no warm path exists; must demonstrate research |
| **Cold email (templated)** | 1-3% | Not recommended — signals low effort |
| **Twitter/X DM** | 10-20% | For investors who are active on the platform |
| **LinkedIn message** | 3-8% | Last resort — high noise channel |

### Warm Introduction Request Template

Send this to your connector (the person making the introduction), not directly to the investor:

```
Subject: Intro request — [Investor Name] at [Fund]

Hi [Connector Name],

I'm raising a [Stage] round for [Company] and would love an
introduction to [Investor Name] at [Fund].

Quick context for your intro:
- [Company] does [one sentence description]
- We're at [key traction metric] and growing [growth rate]
- [Investor] is a great fit because [specific reason — portfolio, thesis, expertise]

I've attached a one-pager and our deck. Happy to send whatever
format works best for [Investor].

If this doesn't feel like a fit, totally understand — no pressure at all.

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

### Cold Outreach Email Template

```
Subject: [Company] — [One compelling metric or statement]

Hi [Investor First Name],

I'm [Your Name], founder of [Company]. We [one sentence description].

I'm reaching out because [specific reason this investor is relevant —
their thesis, a portfolio company, a blog post they wrote].

Quick snapshot:
- [Traction metric 1]
- [Traction metric 2]
- [Market insight or differentiator]

We're raising a [Stage] round of [Amount]. I'd love 20 minutes to
share what we're building.

[One-pager attached / Deck link]

Best,
[Your Name]
[Company] | [Title]
```

## 3. Pipeline Stages

### Stage Definitions

| Stage | Definition | Typical Duration | Actions |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1 — Identified** | Investor is on your target list, no contact yet | N/A | Research, find warm path |
| **2 — Outreach Sent** | First email/message sent | 1-7 days | Wait for response, plan follow-up |
| **3 — Responded** | Investor has replied (positive or neutral) | 1-3 days | Schedule first meeting |
| **4 — First Meeting** | Intro call or pitch meeting completed | 1 day | Send follow-up materials, log notes |
| **5 — Deep Dive** | Partner meeting, technical review, or reference calls | 1-3 weeks | Provide data room access, answer questions |
| **6 — Term Sheet** | Term sheet received | 1-2 weeks | Review with counsel, negotiate, compare offers |
| **7 — Committed** | Verbal or written commitment | 1-2 weeks | Execute documents, wire instructions |
| **8 — Closed** | Money wired, documents signed | Final | Update cap table, send confirmation |
| **X — Passed** | Investor declined at any stage | N/A | Log reason, maintain relationship |

### Pipeline Velocity Benchmarks

| Metric | Pre-Seed | Seed | Series A |
|---|---|---|---|
| Investors contacted | 30-60 | 50-80 | 30-50 |
| First meetings | 15-30 | 25-40 | 15-25 |
| Second meetings | 5-15 | 10-20 | 8-15 |
| Term sheets | 1-3 | 1-4 | 1-3 |
| Conversion (contact → close) | 3-10% | 3-8% | 5-15% |
| Average time (first contact → close) | 2-6 weeks | 4-10 weeks | 8-16 weeks |

## 4. Meeting Preparation

### First Meeting Prep Checklist

- [ ] Research the specific partner you are meeting (not just the fund)
- [ ] Review their recent investments and any public thesis statements
- [ ] Check for portfolio conflicts (competitors or adjacent companies)
- [ ] Prepare 3 questions specific to this investor's expertise
- [ ] Tailor 2-3 slides to resonate with their known interests
- [ ] Test AV setup if meeting is virtual (camera, mic, screen share, backup link)
- [ ] Have deck, one-pager, and data room links ready to share immediately after
- [ ] Prepare the "what's new" update in case they have seen a prior version of the deck
- [ ] Brief any co-presenters on who covers what — no talking over each other

## 5. Follow-Up Cadence

### Post-Meeting Follow-Up Rules

| Trigger | Action | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| After first meeting | Thank-you email + any requested materials | Same day (within 4 hours) |
| After they request data room access | Grant access + send navigation guide | Within 2 hours |
| After partner meeting | Summary email with next steps | Same day |
| No response after follow-up | Second follow-up with traction update | 5 business days |
| Still no response | Final follow-up | 10 business days |
| They pass | Thank them, ask for feedback, request referrals | Same day |
| Term sheet received | Acknowledge receipt, state review timeline | Within 24 hours |

### Post-Meeting Thank-You Template

```
Subject: Thanks for the conversation — [Company]

Hi [Investor First Name],

Thanks for taking the time today. I enjoyed the discussion about
[specific topic from the meeting].

- [Deck / Data Room / Financial Model link]
- [Specific document they requested]

To address your question about [topic]: [brief, compelling answer].

I'd love to continue the conversation. Are you available
[specific day/time] for a follow-up?

Best,
[Your Name]
```

## 6. Negotiation Framework

### Negotiation Principles

| Principle | Application |
|---|---|
| **BATNA (Best Alternative to Negotiated Agreement)** | Know your walk-away point. If you have other term sheets, your BATNA is strong. If you do not, be honest with yourself about leverage. |
| **Create Urgency Without Pressure** | Share timelines honestly: "We plan to close by [Date]." Do not manufacture fake deadlines. |
| **Separate the People from the Problem** | Investors are not adversaries. Frame negotiations as "how do we structure a deal that works for both sides?" |
| **Focus on Interests, Not Positions** | Understand why an investor wants a specific term, not just what the term is. Often there are creative alternatives. |
| **Never Negotiate Against Yourself** | If they make an offer, respond — do not preemptively lower your ask. |

### Key Negotiation Levers

| Term | Founder Priority | Investor Priority | Negotiation Space |
|---|---|---|---|
| Valuation | Higher is better (less dilution) | Lower is better (more ownership) | Moderate — market comps provide boundaries |
| Board Seats | Fewer investor seats | Board representation | High — seat count and observer rights are flexible |
| Liquidation Preference | 1x non-participating | 1x participating or higher | Moderate — 1x non-participating is standard |
| Option Pool | Smaller pool | Larger pool (pre-money) | High — size and pre/post-money placement |
| Pro-Rata Rights | Acceptable | Important to investors | Low — usually granted |
| Anti-Dilution | Broad-based weighted average | Strongest available | Low — BBWA is standard |
| Protective Provisions | Fewer and narrower | Comprehensive | Moderate — specific provisions are negotiable |
| Information Rights | Quarterly reporting | Monthly reporting | Low — usually accommodated |

### Multiple Term Sheet Scenario

If you receive multiple term sheets:

1. **Do not share specific terms** from one investor with another (unethical and damages trust)
2. **Do communicate that you have competing offers** — "We have received interest from other investors and are evaluating multiple options"
3. **Set a decision deadline** — "We plan to make a decision by [Date]"
4. **Evaluate holistically** — the highest valuation is not always the best deal
5. **Call references** — speak to 3+ founders in each investor's portfolio before deciding

### Term Sheet Evaluation Scorecard

| Factor | Weight | Investor A | Investor B | Investor C |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Valuation / Dilution | 25% | /10 | /10 | /10 |
| Investor Value-Add | 20% | /10 | /10 | /10 |
| Terms Friendliness | 20% | /10 | /10 | /10 |
| Partner Chemistry | 15% | /10 | /10 | /10 |
| Fund Stage & Follow-On | 10% | /10 | /10 | /10 |
| Portfolio Founder References | 10% | /10 | /10 | /10 |
| **Weighted Score** | **100%** | **/10** | **/10** | **/10** |

## 7. Closing Process

### Closing Checklist

| Step | Owner | Status | Deadline |
|---|---|---|---|
| Term sheet signed by all parties | Founders + Lead | ☐ | |
| Legal counsel engaged for definitive documents | Founders | ☐ | |
| Stock Purchase Agreement (SPA) drafted | Lead's counsel | ☐ | |
| Investor Rights Agreement drafted | Lead's counsel | ☐ | |
| Certificate of Incorporation amended | Company counsel | ☐ | |
| Board consent / shareholder approval obtained | Founders | ☐ | |
| Management Rights Letter (if required) | Lead's counsel | ☐ | |
| Side letters negotiated and finalized | Both counsels | ☐ | |
| Final cap table circulated and confirmed | Founders | ☐ | |
| Wire instructions sent | Company counsel | ☐ | |
| Funds received and confirmed | Founders | ☐ | |
| Post-close filings (state, federal) | Company counsel | ☐ | |
| Cap table updated in {{CAP_TABLE_TOOL}} | Founders | ☐ | |
| Investor update sent to all investors | Founders | ☐ | |
| Board meeting scheduled | Founders | ☐ | |
