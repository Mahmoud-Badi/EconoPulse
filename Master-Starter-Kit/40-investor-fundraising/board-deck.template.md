### CEO Letter (1 Page Maximum)

Write a candid, 3-5 paragraph letter covering:

1. **Overall company health** — one sentence: are we on track, ahead, or behind plan?
2. **Biggest win since last meeting** — the single most important positive development
3. **Biggest challenge** — the most significant obstacle or concern (do not hide bad news)
4. **Key decision or ask** — what you need from the board at this meeting
5. **Outlook** — your confidence level on hitting next quarter's targets and why

```
Dear Board,

[Overall health — one sentence assessment]

Since our last meeting, the most significant development has been
[biggest win with context and impact].

The primary challenge we are navigating is [biggest challenge with
specifics and what you are doing about it].

At this meeting, I am looking for the board's input on [key
decision or ask — be specific].

Looking ahead, I am [confident/cautiously optimistic/concerned]
about [next quarter's targets] because [specific reasons].

[Your Name]
CEO, {{PROJECT_NAME}}
```

## Board Deck Best Practices

### Preparation Timeline

| When | Action |
|---|---|
| Meeting - 14 days | Begin compiling data and metrics |
| Meeting - 10 days | First draft of board deck completed |
| Meeting - 7 days | Send board deck to board members for pre-read |
| Meeting - 3 days | Send meeting agenda with time allocations |
| Meeting - 1 day | Confirm logistics (location, video link, dial-in) |
| Meeting day | Arrive early, test AV, have backup materials ready |
| Meeting + 1 day | Send meeting minutes and action items |
| Meeting + 3 days | Follow up on any board commitments (intros, advice) |

### Meeting Agenda Template

| Time | Topic | Presenter | Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0:00 - 0:05 | Welcome & Agenda Review | CEO | Inform |
| 0:05 - 0:15 | CEO Letter / Executive Summary | CEO | Inform |
| 0:15 - 0:30 | Key Metrics & Financial Update | CEO / CFO | Inform + Q&A |
| 0:30 - 0:45 | Product Update | CTO/CPO | Inform + Q&A |
| 0:45 - 0:60 | Go-to-Market / Sales Update | CRO/VP Sales | Inform + Q&A |
| 0:60 - 0:90 | Discussion Topics (from Section 7) | CEO | Discuss + Decide |
| 0:90 - 0:100 | Consent Items & Formal Business | CEO | Decide |
| 0:100 - 0:110 | Action Items & Next Meeting | CEO | Inform |
| 0:110 - 0:120 | Closed Session (Board Only) | Board Chair | Discuss |

### Do

- [ ] Send the deck 7 days in advance — board members should not see it for the first time in the meeting
- [ ] Lead with the bad news — boards respect transparency
- [ ] Make specific asks — "I need help with X" beats "any advice?"
- [ ] Track action items and follow through
- [ ] Keep the meeting to 2 hours maximum
- [ ] Include a closed session (without management) at the end of every meeting

### Do Not

- [ ] Read the slides aloud — the pre-read handled that
- [ ] Hide bad news — it always surfaces eventually and trust is hard to rebuild
- [ ] Present without a clear ask — every meeting should have 2-3 specific asks
- [ ] Let the meeting run without an agenda and time allocations
- [ ] Surprise board members — if something major is happening, call them individually before the meeting
- [ ] Skip the meeting — even if things are going well, the cadence matters
