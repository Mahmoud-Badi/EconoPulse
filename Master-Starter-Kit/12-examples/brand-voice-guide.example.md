## 1. Brand Personality Definition

### Core Brand Personality

If TaskFlow were a person, they would be described as:

| # | Adjective | What It Means for Us | What It Does NOT Mean |
|---|-----------|----------------------|----------------------|
| 1 | Confident | We know our product solves real problems and we say so clearly | Arrogant, dismissive of competitors, or making unsubstantiated claims |
| 2 | Clear | We explain things simply and get to the point fast | Dumbed-down, oversimplified, or lacking depth when depth is needed |
| 3 | Empowering | We make users feel capable and in control of their work | Patronizing, hand-holding, or implying users cannot figure things out |
| 4 | Approachable | We sound like a knowledgeable teammate, not a vendor | Unprofessional, overly casual, or trying too hard to be "fun" |

### Brand Archetype

TaskFlow aligns with the **Sage** archetype.

| Archetype | Core Drive | Brand Promise | Example Brands |
|-----------|-----------|---------------|----------------|
| **Sage** | Knowledge, understanding | "We help you understand the world" | Google, TED |

**Our archetype in practice:** TaskFlow is the knowledgeable, trustworthy advisor who helps small agencies see the full picture of their work. We do not lecture. We illuminate. We give teams the clarity to make better decisions about how they spend their time, manage their projects, and grow their business. When a user reads TaskFlow content, they should feel smarter and more capable -- not sold to.

### What Our Scores Mean

**Formal/Casual: 4** -- We are professional but warm. We use contractions ("you'll", "we're") and conversational phrasing, but we do not use slang, memes, or sentence fragments in product copy. Think "smart colleague at a coffee shop," not "friend at a bar."

**Serious/Playful: 5** -- We are centered. We focus on outcomes and real value, but we are not stiff. We allow occasional light touches -- a wry headline, a positive framing -- but never forced jokes or puns.

**Technical/Accessible: 4** -- We lean accessible. Our audience is agency owners, not engineers. We use industry-standard terms (workflow, automation, integration) but avoid deep technical jargon. When a technical concept matters, we explain it in one sentence.

**Enthusiastic: 6** -- We are genuinely positive about what our product can do, but we earn that enthusiasm with specifics. "Save 4 hours a week on reporting" is enthusiastic. "Amazing game-changing tool!" is not.

## 4. Vocabulary Guide

### Words We Use

| Word/Phrase | Why We Use It | Context |
|-------------|---------------|---------|
| Streamline | Communicates efficiency without being vague | Marketing, product descriptions |
| Automate | Specific, outcome-oriented, high-value promise | Feature descriptions, landing pages |
| Clarity | Core brand value -- seeing your work clearly | Headlines, positioning, onboarding |
| Workflow | Industry-standard term our audience understands | Product UI, docs, marketing |
| Ship | Action-oriented, implies getting things done | Blog, social media, changelogs |
| Track | Specific and functional -- what the product does | Product UI, feature pages |
| Focus | Implies removing noise, which is our core value | Marketing, onboarding |
| Team | Collaborative, inclusive, matches agency culture | All customer-facing content |

### Words We Avoid

| Word/Phrase | Why We Avoid It | Use Instead |
|-------------|-----------------|-------------|
| "Synergy" | Meaningless corporate buzzword | Describe the specific collaboration benefit |
| "Leverage" (as verb) | Overused business-speak that sounds hollow | "Use," "take advantage of," or be specific |
| "Disrupt" / "Disruptive" | Overused Silicon Valley cliche, sounds grandiose | Describe the specific change we enable |
| "Utilize" | Corporate jargon; "use" is always better | "Use" |
| "Best-in-class" | Unsubstantiated superlative | Cite a specific metric or user count |
| "Seamless" | Overused, often untrue | "Works with," "connects to," or describe the integration |
| "Robust" | Vague engineering-speak | Describe the specific capability |
| "Revolutionary" | Almost never true, undermines credibility | Describe the specific improvement |
| "Empower" (overused) | Already a personality trait -- do not also use as a buzzword | Show the empowerment through specifics |

### Jargon Decisions

| Term | Decision | Guideline |
|------|----------|-----------|
| Workflow | Use freely | Our audience knows this term well |
| Kanban | Use with brief explanation | "Kanban boards (drag-and-drop task columns)" on first use |
| Sprint | Use with context | "Sprints (fixed time periods for completing work)" -- not all agencies use agile |
| API | Explain on marketing pages, use freely in docs | Marketing: "Connect TaskFlow to your tools." Docs: "Use the TaskFlow API." |
| Webhook | Use in docs only | Too technical for marketing -- say "real-time notifications" instead |

## 5. Writing Style Rules

### Sentence Structure

| Rule | Guideline | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **Average sentence length** | 12-18 words. Mix short (5-8) with medium (15-20). Never exceed 30. | "See every project at a glance. TaskFlow pulls your tasks, time logs, and deadlines into one view -- so nothing slips through the cracks." |
| **Paragraph length** | 2-3 sentences for marketing. 1-2 for UI. As needed for docs. | -- |
| **Active voice** | Use active voice at least 90% of the time | GOOD: "TaskFlow tracks your time automatically." BAD: "Time is tracked automatically by TaskFlow." |
| **Second person** | Address the reader as "you" -- aim for a 3:1 you-to-we ratio | GOOD: "You can generate a client report in two clicks." BAD: "We built a powerful reporting engine." |

### Grammar and Mechanics

| Rule | Our Standard |
|------|-------------|
| **Contractions** | Yes -- use them. "You'll" not "You will." "We're" not "We are." Exception: legal content. |
| **Oxford comma** | Always. "Tasks, time tracking, and reports." |
| **Exclamation marks** | Maximum one per page or email. Never in headlines. |
| **Emoji usage** | Social media only. Never in product UI, docs, or emails. |
| **Capitalization** | Sentence case for headlines, buttons, and navigation. "Get started today" not "Get Started Today." |
| **Numbers** | Spell out one through nine. Numerals for 10+. Always numerals for stats: "3x faster," "10 hours saved." |

### Humor Policy

**Our humor level:** Subtle

We allow clever word choices and an occasional smile, but we never force a joke. Humor appears most in social media and blog headlines. It never appears in error messages, support responses, or documentation.

### Inclusive Language

- Use "they/them" for singular generic pronouns
- "Team" instead of "guys" -- "salesperson" instead of "salesman"
- Write for a global audience: avoid US-centric sports metaphors and idioms that do not travel

## 6. Voice in Action: Examples

### Marketing Copy (Homepage Headline)

| Approach | Example |
|----------|---------|
| Benefit-driven | "See every project, deadline, and hour -- in one place" |
| Problem-driven | "Stop managing your agency from a spreadsheet" |
| Outcome-driven | "The project management tool your team will actually use" |

### Support Response

```
Hi Alex,

I can see how that would be frustrating -- losing track of logged hours
is the last thing you need during a busy week.

Here is what happened: when a task is archived, its time entries move to
the "Archived" tab in your time tracking view. They are not deleted.

To find them:
1. Go to Time Tracking in the left sidebar.
2. Click the "Archived" tab at the top.
3. You will see all time entries from archived tasks, filterable by date.

If you want those hours to appear in your active reports, you can
unarchive the task from that same view -- just click the three-dot menu
and select "Restore."

Let me know if that clears things up or if you need anything else.

Best,
Jamie — TaskFlow Support
```

### Error Message

```
We could not save your changes.

This usually means your internet connection dropped while you were
editing. Your work is saved locally -- reconnect and click "Save"
again.

Still not working? Contact support and we will sort it out.
```

## 7. Do's and Don'ts

| Category | DON'T (Before) | DO (After) | Why |
|----------|----------------|------------|-----|
| **Clarity** | "Our AI-driven platform leverages cutting-edge automation to facilitate optimized project workflows." | "TaskFlow automates the repetitive parts of project management so you can focus on client work." | Specific, no buzzwords, benefit-focused. |
| **Active voice** | "Reports are generated by the system every Friday." | "TaskFlow generates your reports every Friday." | Active voice is direct and confident. |
| **User focus** | "We built an amazing new dashboard." | "You can now see all your projects, deadlines, and hours in one view." | Focus on what the user gets. |
| **Specificity** | "Blazing fast performance." | "Reports generate in under 3 seconds." | Specific claims are believable. |
| **Empathy** | "You entered an invalid date format." | "That date does not look right -- try using MM/DD/YYYY." | Blame the situation, not the user. |
| **Honesty** | "The #1 project management tool." | "Trusted by 800+ agencies to manage their projects." | Social proof over superlatives. |

## 8. Brand Voice Checklist

Use this before publishing any content:

- [ ] **Personality match:** Does this sound like TaskFlow -- confident, clear, empowering, approachable? Read it aloud.
- [ ] **Audience appropriate:** Is the tone right for this channel? (Check the Tone Matrix in Section 3.)
- [ ] **User focused:** Does the content prioritize "you" over "we"?
- [ ] **Clear and scannable:** Can someone understand the main point in 5 seconds?
- [ ] **Active voice:** Is at least 90% in active voice?
- [ ] **Jargon check:** Has all jargon been removed, explained, or confirmed acceptable per Section 4?
- [ ] **Avoided words check:** Does the content avoid "synergy," "leverage," "disrupt," and all other words on the avoid list?
- [ ] **Specific and honest:** Are claims backed by evidence?
- [ ] **Inclusive language:** Does the content follow our inclusive language guidelines?
- [ ] **CTA clarity:** If there is a call to action, is it clear, specific, and compelling?
