# Agent Onboarding Playbook

> {{PROJECT_NAME}} — A structured {{CX_ONBOARDING_DAYS}}-day program that turns new hires into confident, independent support agents.

---

## Overview

The onboarding program determines agent quality for months. Rush it — get an agent who gives wrong answers, needs constant supervision, and damages customer trust. Invest in it — get an agent who resolves issues independently, contributes to KB quality, and represents your brand with confidence.

This playbook is designed for a 30-day program. Adjust the timeline if {{CX_ONBOARDING_DAYS}} is different, but don't compress below 14 days — the product knowledge alone takes time to absorb.

**Cross-references:**
- For canned response library to use in training, see `23-customer-support/canned-responses.template.md`
- For QA scoring rubric used in Week 4 evaluation, see `qa-scoring-and-coaching.template.md`
- For role definitions and expectations, see `support-team-hiring.template.md`

## 30-Day Timeline Overview

| Week | Focus | Goal | Ticket Handling |
|------|-------|------|-----------------|
| **Week 1** | Product Immersion | Learn the product as a user. Understand what customers experience. | None — observation only |
| **Week 2** | Shadow + Guided | Observe real support, then handle tickets with supervision. | 5-10 supervised tickets/day |
| **Week 3** | Independent with Review | Handle tickets independently, every response reviewed before sending. | 15-25 tickets/day (reviewed) |
| **Week 4** | Full Autonomy + First QA | Independent work with post-send QA sampling. Formal quality evaluation. | 25-35 tickets/day (independent) |

## Week 1: Product Immersion

### Day 1: Welcome & Setup

**Morning (4 hours):**
- [ ] Welcome meeting with manager: team overview, culture, expectations, 30-day roadmap
- [ ] Account provisioning: email, Slack, {{SUPPORT_PLATFORM}}, internal admin panel, KB CMS
- [ ] Team introductions (schedule 15-min 1:1s with each team member throughout the week)
- [ ] Read team handbook: working hours, communication norms, escalation contacts

**Afternoon (4 hours):**
- [ ] Product overview walkthrough with manager or senior agent (1 hour — demo, not slides)
- [ ] Create personal test account on {{PROJECT_NAME}} — use a real email, go through actual signup
- [ ] Complete the onboarding flow as a new user — take notes on every moment of confusion
- [ ] Explore the product freely for 1 hour — click everything, try to break things

**End-of-day deliverable:** Write a 1-paragraph summary of what {{PROJECT_NAME}} does and who it's for (in your own words).

### Day 2: Deep Product Exploration

- [ ] Go through the Getting Started guide in the KB as if you're a new customer
- [ ] Complete all Tier 1 product workflows (see Product Knowledge Curriculum below)
- [ ] Document 5 things that confused you — these are potential KB improvement opportunities
- [ ] Set up 2-3 test scenarios that cover common customer use cases
- [ ] Read the product changelog for the last 3 months — understand what's changed recently

**End-of-day deliverable:** List 5 confusion points and whether existing KB articles address them.

### Day 3: Knowledge Base Deep Dive

- [ ] Read all Tier 1 KB articles (see curriculum below) — highlight anything unclear
- [ ] Read the top 10 most-viewed KB articles — understand what customers search for most
- [ ] Read the canned response library (`23-customer-support/canned-responses.template.md`)
- [ ] Take the Tier 1 Knowledge Check quiz
- [ ] Identify 3 KB articles that could be improved — write specific suggestions

**End-of-day deliverable:** Tier 1 quiz score (target: ≥ 80%).

### Day 4: Shadow Day

- [ ] Shadow a senior agent for 2 hours of live support (sit next to them or screen-share)
- [ ] Observe: how do they read tickets? How do they search for answers? How do they write responses?
- [ ] Take notes on: patterns in questions, tools used, shortcuts, escalation decisions
- [ ] After shadowing: debrief with the agent (30 min) — ask your questions
- [ ] Read 20 recent resolved tickets — study the question-response pattern

**End-of-day deliverable:** Write a 5-bullet list of techniques you observed from the senior agent.

### Day 5: Practice & Quiz

- [ ] Respond to 5 mock tickets (manager provides realistic scenarios) — get feedback on each
- [ ] Review the escalation workflow: when to handle, when to escalate, who to escalate to
- [ ] Take the Tier 1 Knowledge Check quiz (retake if Day 3 score was < 80%)
- [ ] Set up your {{SUPPORT_PLATFORM}} workspace: macros, views, notification preferences
- [ ] Week 1 check-in with manager: review quiz score, discuss observations, set Week 2 goals

**End-of-day deliverable:** 5 mock ticket responses graded by manager.

## Week 2: Shadow + Guided Tickets

### Daily Structure (Days 6–10)

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 9:00–9:15 | Daily check-in with buddy (15 min) |
| 9:15–11:00 | Handle "green" tickets with buddy review (5-10 tickets) |
| 11:00–12:00 | Product knowledge study (Tier 2 topics) |
| 12:00–1:00 | Lunch |
| 1:00–3:00 | Handle "green" tickets with buddy review |
| 3:00–4:00 | Shadow senior agent on 2-3 complex tickets |
| 4:00–4:30 | End-of-day review: buddy reviews all sent responses, provides feedback |
| 4:30–5:00 | Self-study: review KB articles related to today's tickets |

### "Green" Tickets (Week 2 Only)

New agents handle only straightforward tickets during Week 2:

| Green (Handle) | Yellow (Observe) | Red (Escalate Immediately) |
|----------------|-------------------|---------------------------|
| Password resets | Bug reports | Production outages |
| How-to questions | Integration issues | Data loss reports |
| Feature inquiries | Account access problems | Security concerns |
| General feedback | Billing disputes | Legal/compliance issues |
| KB article requests | Complex troubleshooting | Angry/threatening users |

### Week 2 Milestones

- [ ] Day 6: Handle first 5 real tickets (buddy reviews before sending)
- [ ] Day 7: Handle 8-10 tickets, buddy reviews 100% of responses
- [ ] Day 8: Take Tier 2 Knowledge Check quiz (target: ≥ 80%)
- [ ] Day 9: Handle 10 tickets, buddy reviews 50% of responses (trust building)
- [ ] Day 10: Week 2 check-in with manager — review ticket quality, quiz scores, discuss challenges

## Week 3: Independent with Review

### Daily Structure (Days 11–15)

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 9:00–9:15 | Check-in with buddy (15 min — questions from yesterday) |
| 9:15–12:00 | Handle tickets independently (green + yellow) — all responses held in "draft" |
| 12:00–12:30 | Team lead reviews and approves drafted responses (batch review) |
| 12:30–1:30 | Lunch |
| 1:30–4:30 | Handle tickets independently — responses sent after brief self-review |
| 4:30–5:00 | End-of-day: buddy reviews 5 random sent responses, provides feedback |

### Progression Through Week 3

- **Days 11–12:** All responses reviewed before sending (draft → review → send)
- **Days 13–14:** Morning responses reviewed before sending; afternoon responses sent directly, reviewed post-send
- **Day 15:** All responses sent directly, 30% reviewed post-send

### Week 3 Milestones

- [ ] Handle 15-25 tickets/day independently
- [ ] First "yellow" tickets handled (with buddy available for questions)
- [ ] Take Tier 3 Knowledge Check quiz (target: ≥ 70%)
- [ ] Write first KB article draft (based on a recurring question encountered this week)
- [ ] Week 3 check-in: review quality metrics, discuss readiness for full autonomy

## Week 4: Full Autonomy + First QA Review

### Daily Structure (Days 16–20+)

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 9:00–9:15 | Queue review and prioritization |
| 9:15–12:00 | Handle all ticket types independently (25-35 tickets/day target) |
| 12:00–1:00 | Lunch |
| 1:00–4:30 | Handle tickets + 30 min knowledge study (edge cases, Tier 3 topics) |
| 4:30–5:00 | Self-review: re-read 3 of today's responses, note improvements |

### Week 4 Milestones

- [ ] Handle "red" tickets for the first time (with team lead as backup)
- [ ] Complete first formal QA review (10 tickets scored using `qa-scoring-and-coaching.template.md` rubric)
- [ ] Personal CSAT ≥ {{CX_CSAT_TARGET}} minus 0.3 (slight grace for new agents)
- [ ] Publish first KB article (reviewed and approved by team lead)
- [ ] Onboarding completion check-in with manager (see Completion Criteria below)

## Product Knowledge Curriculum

### Tier 1: Core (Must Know by End of Week 1)

| Topic | Study Material | Verification |
|-------|---------------|-------------|
| Account creation and setup | KB: Getting Started section + hands-on | Quiz Q1-3 |
| Core feature workflows ({{ENTITY_NAME}} CRUD) | KB: Features section + hands-on | Quiz Q4-6 |
| User management (invite, roles, permissions) | KB: Account section + hands-on | Quiz Q7-8 |
| Billing and plan overview | KB: Billing section + pricing page | Quiz Q9-10 |
| Password reset and login troubleshooting | KB: Troubleshooting section | Quiz Q11-12 |

### Tier 2: Advanced (Must Know by End of Week 2)

| Topic | Study Material | Verification |
|-------|---------------|-------------|
| All product features (not just core) | KB: full Features section + hands-on | Quiz Q1-5 |
| Integrations and API basics | KB: Integrations section + test integration | Quiz Q6-8 |
| Billing details (invoices, upgrades, downgrades, refunds) | KB: Billing section + billing admin panel | Quiz Q9-10 |
| Common error messages and their causes | Internal wiki: error code reference | Quiz Q11-13 |
| Product roadmap awareness (what's coming, what's deprecated) | Changelog + internal roadmap doc | Quiz Q14-15 |

### Tier 3: Expert (Learn by Exposure, Verify by End of Week 4)

| Topic | Study Material | Verification |
|-------|---------------|-------------|
| Edge cases and known issues | Internal wiki: known issues list | Quiz Q1-3 |
| Legacy features and migration paths | Internal wiki + senior agent knowledge | Quiz Q4-5 |
| API troubleshooting (auth, rate limits, common errors) | API docs + test API calls | Quiz Q6-8 |
| Multi-tenant / team scenarios | Hands-on with test accounts | Quiz Q9-10 |
| Competitive landscape (how to handle "does it do X like [competitor]?") | Battle cards (if available) | Quiz Q11-12 |

### Knowledge Check Quiz Format

- 10-15 multiple choice + short answer questions per tier
- Open-book for Tier 1 (learning to find answers), closed-book for Tier 2-3 (retention)
- Passing score: 80% for Tier 1 and 2, 70% for Tier 3
- If failed: review weak areas, retake after 2 days
- Quiz bank maintained by team lead — updated when product changes

## Buddy System

### Structure

- **Buddy assignment:** Experienced agent (6+ months tenure), not the new hire's manager
- **Why not the manager:** Buddies create a safe space for "dumb questions" without judgment anxiety

### Check-in Schedule

| Period | Frequency | Duration | Focus |
|--------|-----------|----------|-------|
| Week 1 | Daily | 15 min | Product questions, tool help, general orientation |
| Week 2 | Daily | 15 min | Ticket handling questions, response review |
| Week 3 | Every other day | 15 min | Edge cases, escalation decisions, confidence building |
| Week 4 | Weekly | 30 min | Independence check, remaining knowledge gaps |

### Buddy Responsibilities

- Answer questions without judgment (there are no dumb questions during onboarding)
- Review ticket responses and provide constructive feedback
- Share tribal knowledge that isn't in the KB
- Flag concerns to manager if new hire is struggling (privately, not punitively)
- Celebrate milestones (first ticket resolved, first KB article, first QA review)

## Onboarding Completion Criteria

All criteria must be met to "graduate" from onboarding:

| Criterion | Target | Measurement |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Tier 1 Knowledge Quiz | ≥ 80% | Quiz score |
| Tier 2 Knowledge Quiz | ≥ 80% | Quiz score |
| Tier 3 Knowledge Quiz | ≥ 70% | Quiz score |
| First QA Review (10 tickets) | ≥ {{CX_QA_PASS_SCORE}} | QA rubric score |
| Independent tickets handled | ≥ 50 | Ticket count |
| Personal CSAT | ≥ {{CX_CSAT_TARGET}} minus 0.3 | CSAT average |
| KB article published | ≥ 1 | Published article |

### If Criteria Not Met

- Extend onboarding by 1 week with focused improvement plan:
  - Identify specific weak areas (quiz results, QA feedback)
  - Assign targeted study and practice
  - Daily check-in with manager
  - Re-evaluate at end of extension
- If criteria still not met after 1-week extension: performance conversation with manager — discuss fit, additional support needed, or role adjustment
