## 4. Advancement Rules

### 4.1 Tier Advancement Process

| Step | Description | Timeline |
|------|-------------|----------|
| 1. Criteria met | Partner meets ALL quantitative thresholds for next tier | Monitored continuously |
| 2. System notification | Portal alerts partner and channel manager | Automated |
| 3. Qualitative review | Channel manager evaluates qualitative criteria | 5 business days |
| 4. Approval | Channel manager approves or defers advancement | Within review period |
| 5. Notification | Partner notified of advancement with new benefits summary | Same day as approval |
| 6. Benefits activation | New tier benefits activated in portal and systems | Within 24 hours |
| 7. Recognition | Announcement in partner newsletter, badge update | Within 1 week |

### 4.2 Fast-Track Advancement

Partners can be fast-tracked to a higher tier under exceptional circumstances:

| Circumstance | Requirement | Approval |
|-------------|-------------|----------|
| Strategic acquisition | Partner acquires company already at higher tier | VP Partnerships |
| Major deal | Single deal exceeds next tier's annual revenue threshold | Channel manager |
| Market entry | Partner provides access to critical new market | VP Partnerships |
| Competitive win | Partner switches from competitor's program with established book | Channel manager |

## 6. Demotion Policy

### 6.1 Demotion Triggers

| Trigger | Grace Period | Action |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| Revenue drops below tier threshold | 2 consecutive quarters | Warning → demotion if not recovered |
| Customer retention drops below minimum | 1 quarter | Investigation → improvement plan → demotion |
| Certification lapsed (all reps) | 60 days | Warning → suspend deal registration → demotion |
| Brand violation (MAP, trademark) | Immediate warning | 30-day cure → demotion on repeat |
| Inactivity (no deals, no logins) | 90 days | Outreach → 30-day reactivation window → demotion |
| Partnership agreement violation | Severity-dependent | Investigation → cure period → demotion or termination |

### 6.2 Demotion Process

1. **Warning:** Partner and channel manager notified of risk (email + portal alert)
2. **Improvement plan:** 60-day improvement plan co-created with channel manager
3. **Progress check:** 30-day check-in during improvement period
4. **Decision:** At 60 days, partner either recovers or is demoted
5. **Demotion notification:** Formal notification with new tier, adjusted benefits, and path back
6. **Benefits adjustment:** Reduced benefits activated within 24 hours
7. **Retention outreach:** Channel manager schedules call to discuss recovery path

### 6.3 Demotion Communication Template

```
Subject: Important update to your {{PROJECT_NAME}} Partner status

Dear [Partner Name],

Following our improvement plan discussion on [date], we are updating
your partner tier from [Current Tier] to [New Tier] effective [date].

Reason: [specific metrics that triggered demotion]

What changes:
  - Revenue share: [old]% → [new]%
  - Support SLA: [old] → [new]
  - [Other benefit changes]

What does NOT change:
  - Your existing customers remain unaffected
  - Your pending commissions will be paid as scheduled
  - Your access to the partner portal continues

Path back to [Current Tier]:
  - Achieve $[amount] in quarterly revenue
  - Maintain [percentage]% customer retention
  - [Other specific criteria]

Your channel manager [Name] is available to discuss a recovery plan.

[Schedule a Call]
```
