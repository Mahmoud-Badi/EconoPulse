### 1.3 Data Collection Sources

Use these methods to gather competitive pricing data:

- [ ] **Pricing pages:** Screenshot and record current pricing (prices change)
- [ ] **Web Archive (Wayback Machine):** Check historical pricing at web.archive.org
- [ ] **Review sites:** G2, Capterra, TrustRadius often list pricing information
- [ ] **Social media:** Search Twitter/X, Reddit, and forums for pricing discussions
- [ ] **Sales calls:** Sign up for competitor demos to learn enterprise pricing
- [ ] **Customer interviews:** Ask your target customers what they currently pay
- [ ] **Job postings:** Competitor job postings sometimes reveal product direction and pricing strategy
- [ ] **SEC filings:** Public companies disclose revenue metrics in quarterly reports
- [ ] **PriceIntelligently / Profitwell:** Third-party pricing research tools
- [ ] **Competitor blogs/announcements:** Watch for pricing change announcements

- You must match or exceed their free tier to compete for signups
- Differentiate on upgrade value (paid features that competitors do not offer)
- Focus on activation and conversion rate, not just signup volume

**If competitors have no free tier:**

- Be cautious about training the market to expect free (hard to remove later)
- Consider a generous free trial instead of a permanent free tier

**If competitors have restrictive free tiers:**
- Opportunity to offer a more generous free tier and capture market share
- Focus on including enough value that free users become advocates
- Design the free tier to create natural upgrade moments
