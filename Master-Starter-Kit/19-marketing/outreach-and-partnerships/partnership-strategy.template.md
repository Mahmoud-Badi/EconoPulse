## Partnership Types

### 1. Co-Marketing Partnerships

**What it is**: Two companies jointly market to each other's audiences. No product integration required — just coordinated marketing activities.

**Examples:**
- Joint webinars on a shared topic
- Co-authored blog posts or guides
- Shared email blasts to combined audiences
- Bundle deals or cross-promotional discounts
- Joint social media campaigns

**Best for**: Early-stage companies with limited marketing resources. Low commitment, high potential reach.

**Requirements**: Similar target audience, compatible brand values, roughly equal effort from both parties.

### 2. Integration Partnerships

**What it is**: Building a technical integration between {{PROJECT_NAME}} and another product, so they work together seamlessly.

**Examples:**
- Zapier/Make integration enabling workflow automation
- Native API integration between {{PROJECT_NAME}} and a popular tool
- Plugin or extension for a platform (Slack, Shopify, WordPress)
- Data sync between {{PROJECT_NAME}} and a CRM, analytics tool, etc.

**Best for**: Products that become more valuable when connected to other tools. Developer tools, B2B SaaS, productivity tools.

**Requirements**: API capability, development resources, partner's marketplace or integration directory listing.

### 3. Affiliate/Referral Partnerships

**What it is**: Partners earn a commission for referring customers to {{PROJECT_NAME}}.

**Examples:**
- Blogger/YouTuber earns 20% recurring commission for referred customers
- Consultants/agencies recommend {{PROJECT_NAME}} to clients for a referral fee
- Complementary SaaS products recommend each other for mutual commissions

**Best for**: Scaling customer acquisition through a distributed network of promoters.

**Requirements**: Affiliate tracking system, clear commission structure, partner portal.

### 4. Technology Partnerships

**What it is**: Joining a larger platform's partner ecosystem as a certified/recognized solution.

**Examples:**
- Salesforce AppExchange partner
- Shopify Partner Program
- HubSpot Solutions Partner
- AWS/Google Cloud/Azure technology partner
- Stripe Verified Partner

**Best for**: Products that serve users of a major platform. Provides credibility, marketplace visibility, and co-selling opportunities.

**Requirements**: Integration with the platform, meeting partner program requirements, often a certification or review process.

### 5. Channel Partnerships

**What it is**: Third parties (resellers, agencies, consultants) sell or implement {{PROJECT_NAME}} on your behalf.

**Examples:**
- Marketing agencies recommend and implement {{PROJECT_NAME}} for their clients
- IT consultants include {{PROJECT_NAME}} in their solution stack
- Regional distributors sell {{PROJECT_NAME}} in specific geographies

**Best for**: Scaling beyond direct sales, entering new markets, serving enterprise customers who buy through channels.

**Requirements**: Partner training, sales materials, deal registration system, margin structure.

### 6. Content Partnerships

**What it is**: Ongoing content collaboration — regular co-produced content, shared editorial calendars, content syndication.

**Examples:**
- Monthly guest column on a partner's blog
- Content syndication agreement
- Co-produced podcast series
- Joint research reports with shared data

**Best for**: Building thought leadership, SEO value, and audience growth through content.

**Requirements**: Aligned editorial standards, consistent publishing schedule, shared promotion commitments.

## Finding the Right Partners

### The Complementary Partner Framework

The best partners are **complementary, not competitive**. They serve the same audience with a different (non-competing) product.

**Framework for identifying partners:**

```
Your customer uses {{PROJECT_NAME}} to {{YOUR_USE_CASE}}.

Before using {{PROJECT_NAME}}, they use _______________ to _______________. (upstream partner)
After using {{PROJECT_NAME}}, they use _______________ to _______________. (downstream partner)
While using {{PROJECT_NAME}}, they also use _______________ to _______________. (parallel partner)
```

**Examples:**
- If {{PROJECT_NAME}} is project management: upstream = CRM, downstream = invoicing, parallel = communication
- If {{PROJECT_NAME}} is email marketing: upstream = CRM, downstream = analytics, parallel = landing page builder
- If {{PROJECT_NAME}} is code review: upstream = IDE, downstream = CI/CD, parallel = project management

4. **Apply for marketplace listing** (each has a review process)
5. **Create documentation** for end users
6. **Launch the integration** with a co-marketing push
7. **Maintain the integration** as both products evolve

## Affiliate Program Design

### Commission Structure

<!-- IF {{PRODUCT_TYPE}} == "saas" -->
**For SaaS products:**
| Model | Rate | When to Use |
|-------|------|------------|
| **Recurring commission** | 15-30% of monthly/annual revenue | Most common for SaaS; aligns incentives long-term |
| **One-time commission** | 50-100% of first month | Simpler to manage; good for low-priced products |
| **Tiered commission** | 15% (0-10 referrals), 20% (11-50), 25% (51+) | Incentivizes top-performing affiliates |
| **Hybrid** | $50 flat + 10% recurring | Combines upfront incentive with long-term alignment |
<!-- ENDIF -->

### Cookie Duration
- **30 days**: Industry minimum (too short for B2B)
- **60-90 days**: Standard for B2B SaaS
- **120+ days**: Generous; attracts more affiliates
- **Recommendation**: 90 days for {{PROJECT_NAME}}

### Affiliate Program Setup

| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Tracking platform** | PartnerStack, Rewardful, FirstPromoter, Post Affiliate Pro, or custom |
| **Signup page** | Dedicated page explaining the program, commission rates, and how to join |
| **Partner portal** | Dashboard where affiliates can see clicks, conversions, earnings, and get links |
| **Marketing materials** | Provide banners, email swipe copy, social media copy, product screenshots |
| **Payment terms** | Monthly or bi-monthly payouts, 30-day hold for refunds |
| **Payment methods** | PayPal, bank transfer, Wise |
| **Terms and conditions** | No bidding on brand keywords, no misleading claims, FTC disclosure required |

### Affiliate Recruitment

- Invite your most enthusiastic customers
- Reach out to bloggers/YouTubers who cover your category
- List your program on affiliate directories (ShareASale, CJ Affiliate, Impact)
- Include "Become a partner" link in your website footer
- Mention the program in your email newsletter

## Partnership Agreement Essentials

**Note**: This is not legal advice. Use these as discussion points and have your legal team draft the actual agreement.

### Key Terms to Agree On

| Term | What to Discuss |
|------|----------------|
| **Goals** | What does success look like for both parties? Specific metrics. |
| **Responsibilities** | Who does what? Be specific: who writes the content, who promotes, who builds the integration. |
| **Timeline** | Start date, key milestones, duration, renewal terms. |
| **Metrics** | How will you measure success? What data will be shared? |
| **Resources** | What resources does each party commit (time, budget, people)? |
| **Branding** | How will each brand be represented? Logo usage, naming conventions. |
| **Exclusivity** | Is this exclusive? Can either party pursue similar partnerships with competitors? |
| **Communication** | How often will you sync? Weekly/monthly check-ins? |
| **Termination** | Under what conditions can either party end the partnership? Notice period? |
| **IP ownership** | Who owns co-created content? Can both parties use it? |
| **Revenue sharing** | If applicable: commission rates, tracking, payment terms, reporting. |
| **Confidentiality** | What information is shared and what must remain confidential? |

## Common Partnership Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It Fails | What to Do Instead |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| Partnering with a competitor (even a "friendly" one) | Confuses customers, risks losing users | Only partner with complementary products |
| Unequal effort | One party does all the work, builds resentment | Agree on specific, equal contributions upfront |
| No measurement | Can't tell if partnership is worth continuing | Define metrics and review quarterly |
| Too many partnerships at once | Spreads resources too thin, none get adequate attention | Focus on 2-3 partnerships at a time |
| Vague agreements | Misaligned expectations lead to disappointment | Document everything: goals, responsibilities, timeline |
| Neglecting the relationship | Partnership atrophies without regular communication | Monthly check-in calls, quarterly reviews |
| Not promoting the partnership | Building an integration but not telling anyone | Every partnership needs a launch and ongoing promotion |
