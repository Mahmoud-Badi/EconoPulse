## When PR Makes Sense (and When It Doesn't)

PR is not a silver bullet. It works well for specific situations and poorly for others. Before investing time in media outreach, determine if your situation warrants it.

### PR Makes Sense When...

| Situation | Why PR Works Here |
|-----------|-------------------|
| **Product launch** | New products are newsworthy; journalists cover launches |
| **Significant funding round** | Funding is a standard news story in tech media |
| **Major milestone** (100K users, profitability, etc.) | Numbers tell a story; journalists love concrete data |
| **Original data or research** | Proprietary data gives journalists exclusive angles |
| **Industry trend alignment** | Your product connects to a hot trend (AI, sustainability, etc.) |
| **Notable customer or partnership** | Big names lend credibility and newsworthiness |
| **Contrarian perspective** | A strong, well-reasoned opinion that challenges conventional wisdom |
| **Founder story** (compelling) | Human interest adds depth to product stories |

### PR Does NOT Make Sense When...

| Situation | Why PR Won't Work |
|-----------|-------------------|
| **Minor feature update** | Not newsworthy; use your own channels instead |
| **No unique angle** | "We launched a product" is not a story by itself |
| **Not ready for attention** | If your product has bugs or the website is rough, don't invite scrutiny |
| **Expecting direct sales** | PR builds awareness, not direct conversions |
| **No capacity to handle traffic** | A press mention can crash your site if you're not prepared |
| **Competitor just got major coverage** | Journalists don't want to cover the same story twice in a row |

## Step 1: Prepare Your Press Kit

Before pitching any journalist, have your press kit ready. Journalists work on tight deadlines — if they cover you, they need assets immediately.

### Press Kit Components

#### 1. Company Overview (Fact Sheet)
A one-page document with essential information:

```
## {{PROJECT_NAME}} — Company Fact Sheet

**What we do**: {{ONE_SENTENCE_DESCRIPTION}}

**Founded**: {{FOUNDING_DATE}}
**Headquarters**: {{LOCATION}}
**Team size**: {{TEAM_SIZE}}
**Funding**: {{FUNDING_STATUS}} (e.g., "Bootstrapped" or "$2M Seed led by {{INVESTOR}}")
**Users/Customers**: {{USER_COUNT}}
**Key metrics**: {{KEY_METRIC}} (e.g., "Processing 1M transactions/month")

**Product**: {{PRODUCT_DESCRIPTION}} (2-3 sentences)

**Target audience**: {{TARGET_AUDIENCE}}

**Key differentiators**:
- {{DIFFERENTIATOR_1}}
- {{DIFFERENTIATOR_2}}
- {{DIFFERENTIATOR_3}}

**Website**: {{WEBSITE_URL}}
**Press contact**: {{PRESS_EMAIL}}
**Social**: Twitter: {{TWITTER}} | LinkedIn: {{LINKEDIN}}
```

#### 2. Founder Bio
A short biography for each founder (3-5 sentences):

```
## {{FOUNDER_NAME}}, {{FOUNDER_TITLE}}

{{FOUNDER_NAME}} is the {{FOUNDER_TITLE}} of {{PROJECT_NAME}}.
{{BACKGROUND_SENTENCE}} (e.g., "Previously, they led engineering at
Company X where they...").
{{MOTIVATION_SENTENCE}} (e.g., "They founded {{PROJECT_NAME}} after
experiencing {{PROBLEM}} firsthand and realizing existing solutions
fell short.").
{{CREDENTIAL_SENTENCE}} (e.g., "They hold a degree in X from Y and
have been featured in Z.").

Connect: {{LINKEDIN_URL}} | {{TWITTER_URL}}
```

#### 3. Product Screenshots
- 5-8 high-resolution product screenshots (1920x1080px minimum)
- PNG format, no compression artifacts
- Show the product in action with realistic data
- Include both desktop and mobile views if applicable
- Label each screenshot with a brief caption

#### 4. Logo Pack
Provide your logo in multiple formats:
- Full color on transparent background (PNG, SVG)
- White on transparent background (for dark backgrounds)
- Black on transparent background (for light backgrounds)
- Square version (for social media)
- Horizontal version (for article headers)
- Minimum size guidance (don't scale below X pixels)

#### 5. Media Coverage Links
If you have been covered before, list links:
```
## Previous Coverage
- "[Headline]" — Publication Name, Date (URL)
- "[Headline]" — Publication Name, Date (URL)
```

If this is your first PR push, skip this section.

#### 6. Boilerplate
A standardized paragraph about {{PROJECT_NAME}} used at the end of press releases:

```
## About {{PROJECT_NAME}}

{{PROJECT_NAME}} is a {{PRODUCT_CATEGORY}} that helps {{TARGET_AUDIENCE}}
{{ACHIEVE_OUTCOME}}. Founded in {{YEAR}} by {{FOUNDER_NAME}},
{{PROJECT_NAME}} {{KEY_FACT}} (e.g., "serves over 5,000 teams across
40 countries"). {{PROJECT_NAME}} is {{FUNDING_STATUS}} and headquartered

```

### Where to Host Your Press Kit
- Dedicated page on your website: `{{WEBSITE_URL}}/press` or `{{WEBSITE_URL}}/media`
- Include a downloadable ZIP file with all assets
- Make it easy to find (link from your footer)
- Keep it updated (stale press kits signal an inactive company)

## Step 2: Find Relevant Journalists

### Identifying the Right Journalists

The biggest mistake in PR is pitching the wrong journalist. A technology reporter at TechCrunch doesn't want your HR software pitch. A fintech reporter doesn't want your design tool pitch. Targeting matters more than anything.

### How to Find Journalists

#### Method 1: Publication Research
1. Identify publications relevant to {{PROJECT_NAME}} (see target list below)
2. Browse their recent articles in your category
3. Note which journalists wrote those articles
4. Check their social media profiles for beat description and recent coverage

#### Method 2: Twitter/X Lists
- Many journalists are active on Twitter/X and list their beat in their bio
- Search for journalists using keywords: "reporter {{CATEGORY}}" or "journalist {{INDUSTRY}}"
- Follow relevant journalists and engage with their content before pitching
- Many journalists explicitly tweet what stories they're looking for

#### Method 3: HARO / Connectively (Formerly HARO)
- **What it is**: A platform connecting journalists with expert sources
- **URL**: https://www.connectively.us (formerly helpareporter.com)
- **How it works**: Journalists post queries; you respond as a source
- **Strategy**: Set up alerts for keywords related to {{PROJECT_NAME}}'s space
- **Response speed matters**: Respond within 2-3 hours of a query posting
- **Quality over quantity**: Only respond when you genuinely have expertise

#### Method 4: Competitor Coverage
- Search for press coverage of your competitors
- Note which journalists covered them
- Those journalists cover your space and may be interested in {{PROJECT_NAME}}

## Step 3: Pitch Journalists

### Pitch Email Template

```
Subject: {{NEWSWORTHY_ANGLE}} — {{PROJECT_NAME}}

Hi {{JOURNALIST_FIRST_NAME}},

{{PERSONALIZATION_SENTENCE}} (e.g., "I read your piece on {{THEIR_RECENT_ARTICLE}}
and thought you might be interested in a related angle.")

{{THE_HOOK}}: {{ONE_SENTENCE_THAT_EXPLAINS_WHY_THIS_IS_NEWS}} (e.g., "We just
crossed 50,000 users in 6 months, making us the fastest-growing {{CATEGORY}}
tool this year." or "We're launching the first {{NOVEL_APPROACH}} for
{{USE_CASE}}, which our early users say cuts their {{METRIC}} in half.")

{{PROJECT_NAME}} is a {{BRIEF_DESCRIPTION}}. {{ONE_SENTENCE_DIFFERENTIATOR}}.

{{SUPPORTING_DATA_OR_SOCIAL_PROOF}} (e.g., "We're already used by teams at
{{NOTABLE_CUSTOMER_1}}, {{NOTABLE_CUSTOMER_2}}, and {{NOTABLE_CUSTOMER_3}}."
or "We're backed by {{INVESTOR}} and just closed a {{FUNDING_AMOUNT}} round.")

I'd be happy to provide:
- An exclusive angle or early access
- Founder interview
- Customer quotes and case studies
- Product demo

Press kit: {{PRESS_KIT_URL}}

Let me know if this is on your radar. Thanks for your time,

{{YOUR_NAME}}
{{YOUR_TITLE}}, {{PROJECT_NAME}}
{{YOUR_EMAIL}} | {{YOUR_PHONE}}
```

### What Makes a Good Pitch

| Element | Good | Bad |
|---------|------|-----|
| **Length** | 150-200 words maximum | Long email (500+ words) |
| **Subject line** | Specific, newsworthy angle | Generic ("Check out our product!") |
| **Personalization** | Reference their recent work specifically | "Dear journalist" or no personalization |
| **Hook** | Data, novelty, timeliness, or impact | "We launched a new product" (so what?) |
| **Ask** | Clear: interview, demo, early access, exclusive | Vague: "Would love to chat sometime" |
| **Attachments** | None (link to press kit instead) | Attached PDFs, images, etc. (spam filters) |
| **Tone** | Concise, professional, respectful of their time | Overly enthusiastic, hyperbolic, salesy |

### What NOT to Do When Pitching

| Don't | Why |
|-------|-----|
| Mass BCC journalists | Shows you don't value them individually; will be ignored or blacklisted |
| Attach files | Triggers spam filters; journalists won't open attachments from unknown senders |
| Send a long pitch (500+ words) | Journalists receive 100+ pitches daily; they won't read a long email |
| Lead with your product features | Lead with why the reader (journalist's audience) should care |
| Send multiple follow-ups | One follow-up after 5-7 days is acceptable; more is harassment |
| Pitch on Monday morning or Friday afternoon | Monday inbox is full; Friday they're wrapping up for the week |
| Use empty superlatives | "Revolutionary," "disruptive," "game-changing" are meaningless without proof |
| Pitch the same story to competing journalists at the same publication | Pick one journalist per publication |

### Follow-Up Email (Once, After 5-7 Days)

```
Subject: Re: {{ORIGINAL_SUBJECT_LINE}}

Hi {{JOURNALIST_FIRST_NAME}},

Just wanted to float this back up — I know your inbox is busy. If
{{NEWSWORTHY_ANGLE}} is relevant to something you're working on, I'm
happy to share more details or set up a quick call.

If it's not a fit, totally understand. Thanks either way.

{{YOUR_NAME}}
```

**Important**: Send only ONE follow-up. If they don't respond after that, move on. They are not interested, and additional follow-ups will annoy them and burn the relationship.

## Step 4: Press Release (When Actually Needed)

Press releases are not needed for every announcement. They are appropriate for:
- Funding announcements
- Major product launches
- Significant partnerships
- Acquisitions
- Major milestones with hard data

### Press Release Template

```
FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE

{{HEADLINE: CLEAR, FACTUAL, UNDER 80 CHARACTERS}}
{{SUBHEADLINE: ADDITIONAL CONTEXT (OPTIONAL)}}

{{CITY}}, {{STATE}} — {{DATE}} — {{PROJECT_NAME}}, a {{BRIEF_DESCRIPTION}},
today announced {{THE_NEWS}}.

{{LEAD PARAGRAPH}}: Expand on the news. Who, what, when, where, why.
Include the most important facts in the first paragraph. (3-4 sentences)

{{CONTEXT PARAGRAPH}}: Why this matters. Market context, problem being
solved, industry trend this connects to. (2-3 sentences)

{{QUOTE FROM FOUNDER}}:
"{{QUOTE_ABOUT_THE_SIGNIFICANCE_OF_THE_NEWS}}," said {{FOUNDER_NAME}},
{{FOUNDER_TITLE}} of {{PROJECT_NAME}}. "{{SECOND_SENTENCE_OF_QUOTE}}."

{{DETAILS PARAGRAPH}}: Product details, specific features, availability,
pricing if relevant. (2-3 sentences)

{{CUSTOMER OR PARTNER QUOTE}} (if applicable):
"{{QUOTE_FROM_CUSTOMER_OR_PARTNER}}," said {{THEIR_NAME}}, {{THEIR_TITLE}}
at {{THEIR_COMPANY}}.

{{AVAILABILITY}}: {{PROJECT_NAME}} is available at {{WEBSITE_URL}}.
{{PRICING_IF_RELEVANT}}.

###

About {{PROJECT_NAME}}
{{BOILERPLATE_PARAGRAPH}}

Media Contact:
{{CONTACT_NAME}}
{{CONTACT_EMAIL}}
{{CONTACT_PHONE}}
```

### Press Release Distribution

| Channel | Cost | Value |
|---------|------|-------|
| **Direct email to targeted journalists** | Free | Highest value (personalized) |
| **PR Newswire** | $350-800+ per release | Wide distribution, SEO, picked up by news aggregators |
| **Business Wire** | $400-1,000+ per release | Enterprise-grade distribution |
| **EIN Presswire** | $99-499 per release | Budget-friendly distribution |
| **Your own blog** | Free | Permanent record, SEO, social sharing |
| **Social media** | Free | Immediate reach to your audience |

**Recommendation for early-stage**: Skip paid wire services. Send personalized pitches to targeted journalists and publish the press release on your blog. Wire services add minimal value for most startups.

## Step 6: HARO/Connectively Strategy

### What It Is
HARO (now Connectively) connects journalists with expert sources. Journalists post queries, and you respond as a source. This is one of the most accessible ways to get press coverage without pitching.

### How to Use It

1. **Sign up** at https://www.connectively.us as a "Source"
2. **Set up alerts** for keywords related to {{PROJECT_NAME}}'s space:
   - `{{KEYWORD_1}}` (your category)
   - `{{KEYWORD_2}}` (your industry)
   - `{{KEYWORD_3}}` (your technology)
   - "startup," "founder," "technology"
3. **Review daily queries** (sent by email, 3x per day)
4. **Respond quickly**: Journalists often choose the first quality responses
5. **Provide value**: Answer their question thoroughly, not just "here's my product"

### Response Template

```
Subject: Re: [HARO Query] — {{QUERY_TOPIC}}

Hi {{JOURNALIST_NAME}},

I'm {{YOUR_NAME}}, {{YOUR_TITLE}} at {{PROJECT_NAME}}
({{WEBSITE_URL}}).

{{DIRECT_ANSWER_TO_THEIR_QUERY}}

{{SUPPORTING_DETAIL_OR_DATA}}

{{OPTIONAL_PERSONAL_ANECDOTE_OR_EXAMPLE}}

I'm happy to provide additional details or expand on any of these points.

Best,
{{YOUR_NAME}}
{{YOUR_TITLE}}, {{PROJECT_NAME}}
{{YOUR_EMAIL}} | {{YOUR_PHONE}}
```

### HARO Success Tips
- Respond within 2-3 hours of the query being published
- Be concise (200-300 words maximum)
- Include credentials that establish your expertise
- Provide specific data, quotes, or examples (not generic advice)
- Include a headshot URL (journalists sometimes need it for the article)
- Don't pitch your product — answer their question. They may mention your product in the article if your response is good.

## Step 7: Timing and Coordination

### Embargo Strategy

An embargo means you share news with journalists before the announcement date, under an agreement that they won't publish until the embargo lifts. This gives journalists time to prepare their story and allows you to coordinate coverage.

**When to use embargoes:**
- Major announcements (funding, product launch, major partnership)
- You want multiple publications to cover you on the same day

**How it works:**
1. Pitch journalists with "under embargo until {{DATE}}"
2. Provide all materials (press release, assets, access)
3. On embargo day, all coverage publishes simultaneously
4. Amplify across your channels

**Risks:**
- Embargoes can be broken (rare but happens)
- Only use with trusted journalists
- Small publications are less likely to respect embargoes

### Avoiding Bad Timing

| Avoid Pitching During | Why |
|----------------------|-----|
| Major tech company announcements (Apple WWDC, Google I/O, etc.) | Your story will be drowned out |
| Major news events | Journalists are focused elsewhere |
| Friday afternoon or weekend | Will be buried by Monday |
| Year-end holidays (mid-Dec to early Jan) | Reduced editorial staff, lower readership |
| Same week as a competitor's major announcement | You'll look reactive |

### Best Times to Pitch

| Day | Time | Notes |
|-----|------|-------|
| **Tuesday** | 9-11am (journalist's timezone) | Best day for pitching; journalists have cleared Monday backlogs |
| **Wednesday** | 9-11am | Second best day |
| **Thursday** | 9-11am | Still good; avoid afternoon (they're preparing for end of week) |

## Step 8: Amplify Press Coverage

Getting press coverage is only half the battle. Amplifying it extends its reach and longevity.

### Immediate Actions (Within 24 Hours)

1. **Share on social media**: Post the article link on Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Facebook with a brief commentary
2. **Email your list**: Send a brief email to your subscriber list: "{{PROJECT_NAME}} in the news"
3. **Update your website**: Add a "As seen in" or "Press" section with the publication logo and link
4. **Internal Slack/team channel**: Share with the team for them to amplify on personal social media
5. **Thank the journalist publicly**: Tag them on social media with a thank you (builds the relationship)

### Ongoing Amplification

- **Add to email signature**: "Recently featured in {{PUBLICATION}}"
- **Include in investor updates**: Press coverage is a strong signal to investors
- **Reference in sales materials**: "As covered by {{PUBLICATION}}..."
- **Add to press kit**: Update your media coverage links
- **Social proof**: Add publication logos to your homepage "as seen in" section
- **Retarget article readers**: If the article drives traffic to your site, retarget those visitors with ads

### "As Seen In" Section

Add a section to your homepage or a dedicated press page:

```
As featured in:
[TechCrunch logo] [Hacker News logo] [Product Hunt logo] [dev.to logo]
```

**Rules:**
- Only include publications that actually covered you (not just directories you're listed on)
- Use grayscale logos for a cleaner look
- Link each logo to the actual article
- Update as you get new coverage

## Step 9: Measuring PR Success

PR is harder to measure than paid advertising but not impossible.

| Metric | How to Measure | Target |
|--------|---------------|--------|
| **Media mentions** | Google Alerts, Mention.com, manual tracking | Track each mention |
| **Quality of publications** | Publication tier (Tier 1, 2, 3) | At least 1 Tier 1 mention per quarter |
| **Backlinks from coverage** | Google Search Console, Ahrefs, Moz | Each article should provide a dofollow backlink |
| **Referral traffic** | Google Analytics > Referrals | Track traffic from each press article |
| **Social shares of articles** | Social media monitoring | Amplification beyond the article itself |
| **Domain authority impact** | Moz, Ahrefs | Track DA changes after quality backlinks |
| **Inbound inquiries** | Track "how did you hear about us" | "Press/article" as a response option |
| **Share of voice** | Mention tracking vs competitors | What % of industry press mentions include {{PROJECT_NAME}}? |

### Google Alerts Setup

- `"{{PROJECT_NAME}}"` (in quotes for exact match)
- `{{FOUNDER_NAME}}`
- `{{COMPETITOR_1}}` (track competitor coverage)
- `{{PRODUCT_CATEGORY}} software` (track industry coverage)
