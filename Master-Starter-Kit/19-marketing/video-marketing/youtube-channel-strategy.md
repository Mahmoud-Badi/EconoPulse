## Channel Setup

### Channel Name

Your channel name should be one of:

- **Product name:** Use this if the channel exists solely to support the product. Example: "Figma" or "Notion."
- **Brand + descriptor:** If the product name is obscure, add context. Example: "Supabase - Open Source Firebase Alternative."
- **Personal name:** If you (the founder) are the face of the product. Example: "Pieter Levels" rather than "Nomad List." Works well for indie hackers and solo founders.

**Keep it simple, searchable, and memorable.** Don't add "Official Channel" or "HQ" — it looks dated.

### Channel Description

Write a description that tells viewers:
1. What the channel is about (what topics you cover).
2. Who it's for (your target audience).
3. What they'll get (the value of subscribing).
4. Upload schedule (sets expectations).

**Template:**
```
[Product/Channel Name] helps [target audience] [achieve outcome].

Every [frequency], we publish [content types] covering [topics].

Whether you're [persona 1] or [persona 2], you'll find [value proposition].

Subscribe for [specific content promise].

🔗 Try [Product Name]: [URL]
🐦 Twitter: [URL]
📧 Newsletter: [URL]
```

### Branding Assets

| Asset | Specs | Tips |
|-------|-------|------|
| **Profile picture** | 800x800px | Use your logo or face. Must be recognizable at tiny sizes (it appears as a 40px circle). |
| **Banner** | 2560x1440px (safe area: 1546x423px center) | Show what the channel is about. Include upload schedule. Keep text in the safe zone — it crops differently on TV, desktop, and mobile. |
| **Watermark** | 150x150px transparent PNG | Your logo, appears in bottom-right of videos. Subtle branding, doubles as subscribe button. |
| **Video intro** | 3-5 seconds max | Short animated logo or branded bumper. Do NOT use a 15-second intro — viewers will click away. |
| **End screen template** | Last 20 seconds of video | Standard layout: subscribe button + 2 video recommendations. Design a background for this. |

### Channel Settings

- **Set your channel URL** once you hit 100 subscribers (custom URL).
- **Add channel keywords** in YouTube Studio > Settings > Channel > Basic Info. Include your product name, niche, and related terms.
- **Set your default upload settings** (description template, tags, category, language) to save time.
- **Enable the Community tab** (available after 500 subscribers, or sometimes earlier).
- **Verify your channel** to unlock custom thumbnails, longer videos, and live streaming.

## Content Pillars

A content pillar is a recurring theme or category. For tech/dev product channels, these five pillars cover most needs:

### 1. Tutorials (40-50% of content)

- **What:** Step-by-step guides teaching people how to use your product or related skills.
- **Why:** Tutorials are the bread and butter of tech YouTube. They have long shelf life (evergreen) and get found through search.
- **Examples:**
  - "How to Build a REST API with [Product]"
  - "Getting Started with [Product] in 10 Minutes"
  - "[Product] + [Popular Tool] Integration Tutorial"
- **Format:** Screen recording with voiceover. 5-15 minutes. Clear steps.

### 2. Product Updates (10-15% of content)

- **What:** Announcements of new features, improvements, or releases.
- **Why:** Keeps existing users informed and shows prospects the product is actively developed.
- **Examples:**
  - "What's New in [Product] v2.0"
  - "5 New Features You Missed This Month"
  - "[Product] Changelog — January 2025"
- **Format:** Screen recording showing new features. 3-8 minutes. Enthusiasm without hype.

### 3. Industry Commentary (15-20% of content)

- **What:** Your takes on industry news, trends, and debates.
- **Why:** Positions you as a thought leader. Gets shared and discussed. Attracts new audiences beyond existing users.
- **Examples:**
  - "The Future of [Industry/Technology]"
  - "Why [Trend] Changes Everything for Developers"
  - "My Honest Take on [Competitor/Technology]"
- **Format:** Talking head or screen share. 5-12 minutes. Opinionated but fair.

### 4. Behind-the-Scenes (10-15% of content)

- **What:** Show how you build the product, run the company, or solve problems.
- **Why:** Builds authentic connection. People love seeing how things are made. Especially powerful for indie hackers and small teams.
- **Examples:**
  - "How We Scaled to 10K Users"
  - "A Day in the Life of a Solo Founder"
  - "Building [Feature] Live — Full Process"
- **Format:** Vlog style, screen recording, or live stream. 8-20 minutes. Authentic and unpolished is fine.

### 5. Comparisons and Reviews (10-15% of content)

- **What:** Comparing tools, approaches, or technologies — including your own product versus alternatives.
- **Why:** Captures high-intent search traffic ("X vs Y"). Builds trust by being honest.
- **Examples:**
  - "[Your Product] vs [Competitor] — Honest Comparison"
  - "Best [Category] Tools in 2025"
  - "When NOT to Use [Your Product]"
- **Format:** Screen recording with side-by-side demos. 5-12 minutes. Balanced and factual.

### Content Calendar Template

| Week | Monday | Wednesday | Friday |
|------|--------|-----------|--------|
| 1 | Tutorial | - | Industry commentary |
| 2 | Tutorial | Product update | - |
| 3 | Tutorial | - | Behind-the-scenes |
| 4 | Tutorial | Comparison/review | - |

Adjust based on your publishing frequency. One video per week is the minimum for growth.

## YouTube SEO

YouTube is the world's second-largest search engine. SEO matters here.

### Keyword Research

**Free tools:**
- **YouTube search autocomplete:** Type your topic and see what YouTube suggests. These are real searches people make.
- **TubeBuddy (free tier):** Browser extension showing search volume and competition for keywords.
- **vidIQ (free tier):** Similar to TubeBuddy. Shows keyword scores, trending topics, competitor analytics.
- **Google Trends (YouTube search filter):** Compare keyword popularity over time. Filter by "YouTube Search."
- **Competitor analysis:** Look at successful videos in your niche. What titles and tags do they use?

**Process:**
1. Brainstorm 5-10 topic ideas.
2. Search each on YouTube — are there videos with 10K+ views? (Demand exists.)
3. Check TubeBuddy/vidIQ keyword score. Aim for "good" or "excellent" score.
4. Pick topics with high demand and moderate competition.
5. Note exact search phrases to use in your title and description.

### Title Optimization

- **Include your primary keyword near the beginning of the title.** "React Tutorial for Beginners" not "Beginners Guide to Learning React."
- **Keep titles under 60 characters** so they don't get cut off.
- **Use numbers when applicable.** "5 Ways to..." or "Build X in 10 Minutes."
- **Create curiosity without clickbait.** "The Feature Nobody Talks About" is curiosity. "YOU WON'T BELIEVE THIS" is clickbait.
- **Match search intent.** If people search "how to," start with "How to."
- **A/B test titles.** TubeBuddy Pro lets you test two titles and measure CTR.

### Description Template

```
[1-2 sentence summary of what the video covers and why it matters]

In this video, I'll show you [what the viewer will learn or accomplish].

⏱ Timestamps:
0:00 - Intro
0:45 - [Section 1]
2:30 - [Section 2]
5:15 - [Section 3]
8:00 - Summary

🔗 Resources mentioned:
- [Resource 1]: [URL]
- [Resource 2]: [URL]

🚀 Try [Product Name] free: [URL]

📱 Follow us:
- Twitter: [URL]
- Newsletter: [URL]
- Discord: [URL]

#[keyword1] #[keyword2] #[keyword3]
```

**Description SEO tips:**
- Put the most important keywords in the first 2-3 lines (visible before "Show More").
- Include 3-5 relevant hashtags (YouTube shows up to 3 above the title).
- Add timestamps — YouTube uses these for key moments in search results and chapters.
- Link to related videos on your channel (keeps viewers in your ecosystem).

### Tags

- Add 5-10 tags per video.
- Include: exact title, primary keyword, variations, your channel name, related topics.
- Tags matter less than they used to, but they still help YouTube understand your content.
- Don't keyword-stuff — irrelevant tags can hurt performance.

### Chapters

- Add timestamps in your description in the format `0:00 - Chapter Title`.
- YouTube automatically creates chapters from these timestamps.
- Chapters improve user experience and can appear in search results as key moments.
- First chapter MUST start at `0:00`.
- Each chapter must be at least 10 seconds long.

### Cards and End Screens

- **Cards:** Add 2-3 cards per video linking to related content. Place them at natural transition points, not randomly.
- **End screens:** Always add an end screen in the last 20 seconds. Include a subscribe element and 1-2 video/playlist suggestions.

## Thumbnail Design

Thumbnails are your video's billboard. A great video with a bad thumbnail will underperform.

### Design Principles

1. **High contrast.** Use bold colors that pop against YouTube's white/dark backgrounds. Yellow, red, and blue perform well.
2. **Minimal text.** 3-5 words maximum. The title already has your full description — the thumbnail should complement, not repeat it.
3. **Large text.** If you use text, it must be readable on a phone screen (where 70%+ of viewers are). Test by shrinking your thumbnail to 120px wide.
4. **Faces.** Human faces with clear expressions get more clicks. Show surprise, excitement, confusion, or concentration.
5. **Curiosity gap.** Show a result without explaining how. Show a problem without the solution. Make viewers want to click to find out.
6. **Brand consistency.** Use consistent colors, fonts, and layout across your thumbnails. Viewers should recognize your videos instantly.
7. **No clutter.** One clear focal point per thumbnail. If you can't tell what it's about in 1 second, simplify.

### Thumbnail Specs

- **Resolution:** 1280x720px (16:9 aspect ratio).
- **File size:** Under 2MB.
- **Format:** JPG, GIF, BMP, or PNG.
- **Minimum width:** 640px.

### Templates by Content Type

| Content Type | Thumbnail Approach |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Tutorial | Screenshot of the result + text of what you'll build |
| Product update | New feature screenshot + "NEW" badge |
| Commentary | Your face with expression + topic text |
| Comparison | Side-by-side logos/screenshots + "vs" |
| Behind-the-scenes | Candid/authentic photo + descriptive text |

### Tools for Thumbnails

- **Canva (free):** Templates, easy drag-and-drop. Good enough for most creators.
- **Figma (free):** More control, better for consistent branding systems.
- **Photoshop:** Full creative control (if you have it).
- **Remove.bg:** Remove backgrounds from face photos for cleaner composition.

## Video Structure

Every video should follow a structure that respects the viewer's time and optimizes for retention.

### The YouTube Video Framework

```
HOOK (0:00 - 0:10)
  "In this video, I'll show you how to [outcome] in [timeframe]."
  OR: "Why is everyone talking about [topic]? Let's find out."
  OR: Start with the end result: "This is what we're building today."

INTRO (0:10 - 0:30)
  Brief context. Who this is for. What they'll learn.
  DO NOT do a long channel intro. No "Hey guys, welcome back to my channel..."
  Get to the value fast.

VALUE DELIVERY (0:30 - end minus 30s)
  The actual content. Structured in clear sections.
  Use pattern interrupts every 2-3 minutes: change angle, show something new, ask a question.
  Signpost: "Now that we've covered X, let's move on to Y."

CTA (end minus 30s - end minus 20s)
  "If this was helpful, subscribe for more [topic] content."
  OR: "Try [Product] free — link in the description."
  Keep it brief and genuine. One CTA, not five.

END SCREEN (last 20 seconds)
  Point to another video. "If you liked this, watch [related video] next."
  This is designed for the end screen overlay with clickable elements.
```

### Retention Optimization

- **Eliminate dead time.** Cut pauses, "ums," and slow segments ruthlessly.
- **Use jump cuts.** Quick cuts between sentences keep energy high and pace tight.
- **Change visual every 10-15 seconds.** New angle, zoom, graphic, or screen change.
- **Preview what's coming.** "In a minute, I'll show you the surprising part..." keeps viewers watching.
- **Open loops.** Mention something early that you'll address later. Viewers stay to see the payoff.
- **Pattern interrupt.** Every 2-3 minutes, change something: camera angle, location, graphic style, energy level.

## Publishing Schedule

### Consistency Over Frequency

- **One video per week is the minimum** for meaningful growth on YouTube.
- YouTube rewards consistency. The algorithm learns your schedule and promotes accordingly.
- Better to publish 1 great video per week than 3 mediocre ones.
- Pick a day and time and stick to it. Tell subscribers when to expect new videos.

### Recommended Schedule by Resource Level

| Resources | Frequency | Strategy |
|-----------|-----------|----------|
| Solo founder (limited time) | 1 video/week | Focus on tutorials with long shelf life. Batch record. |
| Small team (some help) | 2 videos/week | Mix tutorials and commentary. Repurpose into Shorts. |
| Dedicated content person | 3+ videos/week | Full content calendar across all pillars. |

### Batch Recording

- **Set aside one day for recording multiple videos.** This is far more efficient than recording one at a time.
- Record 2-4 videos in a single session.
- Edit throughout the week and schedule releases.
- Keep a running list of video ideas so you never sit down without knowing what to record.

### Best Publishing Times

- **General best times for tech audiences:** Tuesday-Thursday, 8-11 AM in your target audience's timezone.
- **Check YouTube Studio > Analytics > Audience > When your viewers are on YouTube** for data specific to your channel.
- **Schedule uploads 2-3 hours before peak viewing time** to allow YouTube to index and start recommending the video.

## YouTube Shorts Strategy

YouTube Shorts (vertical videos under 60 seconds) are YouTube's answer to TikTok and Reels.

### Why Shorts Matter

- Shorts get significantly more impressions than long-form content, especially for smaller channels.
- They introduce your channel to new audiences who may then watch your longer content.
- YouTube is actively promoting Shorts in the feed and search results.
- They require less production effort — you can create 5 Shorts in the time it takes to make 1 long-form video.

### Content Ideas for Tech Shorts

1. **Quick tips:** "One [tool/feature] tip in 30 seconds."
2. **Before/after:** Show a transformation or improvement.
3. **Code snippets:** Solve a common problem in 30 seconds.
4. **Hot takes:** 30-second opinion on an industry topic.
5. **Tool reveals:** "Wait until you see what [tool] can do..."
6. **Long-form clips:** Take the best 30-60 seconds from a full video and post as a Short.
7. **Speed builds:** Speed up a process to fit in 60 seconds.
8. **Mistakes to avoid:** "Stop doing [common mistake]. Do [better approach] instead."

### Repurposing Long-Form Into Shorts

- After editing a long-form video, identify 2-3 "highlight moments" — moments that stand alone as a clip.
- Crop to 9:16 vertical (or re-record vertically if the original doesn't crop well).
- Add captions (required for Shorts — most are watched without sound).
- Add a hook in the first frame: on-screen text that makes people stop scrolling.
- End with "Full video on my channel" to drive traffic to long-form content.

### Shorts Best Practices

- **Hook in the first 1-2 seconds.** Even more critical than long-form. Viewers decide instantly.
- **Captions always.** Use large, centered text captions.
- **Loop potential.** If the video loops seamlessly, it gets replayed (counts as multiple views).
- **Post 3-5 Shorts per week** for meaningful growth. Daily is ideal.
- **Don't use #Shorts in the title** — it's no longer necessary.

## Playlists

### Why Playlists Matter

- Playlists auto-play the next video, dramatically increasing watch time.
- They appear as their own result in YouTube search.
- Well-organized playlists improve the viewer experience and help people find content.
- YouTube recommends playlist videos together, creating a binge-watching loop.

### Playlist Strategy

1. **Create a playlist for each content pillar:** "Tutorials," "Product Updates," "Industry Insights."
2. **Create topic-based playlists:** "[Product] + React," "[Product] + Python," "Getting Started Series."
3. **Create a "Start Here" playlist** with your best introductory content. Feature it on your channel page.
4. **Order videos intentionally.** Put the best/most introductory video first, then logical progression.
5. **Use SEO-optimized playlist titles and descriptions.** They rank in search independently.
6. **Link to playlists in video descriptions and end screens** rather than individual videos when possible.

### Playlist SEO

- Title your playlist with a keyword: "React Authentication Tutorials" not "My React Videos."
- Write a 2-3 sentence description with keywords.
- Choose an engaging thumbnail for the playlist (the first video's thumbnail becomes the default).

## Community Tab

The Community tab lets you post text, images, polls, and links directly on your channel — like a social media feed for your YouTube audience.

### Available After 500 Subscribers

- **Polls:** Great for engagement. Ask about future content, preferences, or opinions. Polls consistently get high engagement.
- **Images:** Share behind-the-scenes photos, infographics, or upcoming video previews.
- **Text posts:** Share updates, ask questions, or start discussions.
- **Video links:** Promote older videos that are relevant to current trends.

### Community Tab Strategy

- **Post 2-3 times per week** between video uploads to stay visible.
- **Use polls frequently.** They drive the most engagement and YouTube promotes them in subscriber feeds.
- **Preview upcoming content.** "Working on a video about [topic] — what questions do you want answered?"
- **Celebrate milestones.** Subscriber counts, product launches, personal updates.
- **Promote older videos.** "If you missed this one, it's perfect for [current trend]."
- **Ask for feedback.** "What videos do you want to see next?" (and actually use the responses).

## Analytics

### Key Metrics to Monitor

| Metric | What It Means | Benchmark |
|--------|--------------|-----------|
| **Watch time** | Total minutes viewed. YouTube's #1 ranking factor. | Growing month over month |
| **Click-through rate (CTR)** | % of impressions that result in a click. Measures thumbnail + title effectiveness. | 4-10% is good. Above 10% is excellent. |
| **Average view duration** | How long viewers watch before leaving. | 40-60% of video length is good. |
| **Audience retention** | Graph showing exactly where viewers drop off. | Look for steep drops — fix those sections in future videos. |
| **Subscriber growth** | New subscribers per video and overall. | Positive trend. Each video should earn subscribers. |
| **Traffic sources** | Where views come from: search, suggested, browse, external. | Diversified sources are healthiest. |
| **Impressions** | How many times YouTube showed your thumbnail. | Growing means YouTube is distributing your content more. |

### How to Use Analytics

1. **Weekly:** Check CTR and average view duration for each new video. Identify top performers.
2. **Monthly:** Review overall watch time trend, subscriber growth, and best-performing content.
3. **Quarterly:** Analyze which content pillars drive the most growth. Adjust your content mix.

### Audience Retention Analysis

- Open YouTube Studio > Analytics > select a video > Engagement tab.
- Look at the retention graph. Every video has a natural drop in the first 30 seconds — that's normal.
- **Red flags:** Steep drops at specific moments mean viewers lost interest. Analyze what happened at that timestamp and avoid it in future videos.
- **Green flags:** Flat or rising sections mean high engagement. Do more of whatever's working there.
- **Spikes:** Viewers re-watching a section means it was valuable or confusing. Either way, it's notable.

## Monetization

### YouTube Partner Program Requirements

- 1,000 subscribers AND
- 4,000 watch hours in the past 12 months OR 10 million Shorts views in 90 days.
- Once accepted: ads, memberships, Super Chat, and merchandise shelf become available.

### Realistic Timeline

- Most tech channels hit monetization eligibility in **6-18 months** of consistent weekly uploads.
- Early revenue is modest: $100-500/month for channels with 5K-20K subscribers.
- Revenue varies wildly by niche. Developer/tech content typically has high CPMs ($8-25) due to advertiser demand.

### Beyond Ad Revenue

For product channels, YouTube monetization is a bonus — the real value is:
- **Product signups:** Every video is a marketing asset driving users to your product.
- **Brand authority:** Being on YouTube positions you as a credible, established player.
- **SEO backlinks:** YouTube videos rank in Google search results, driving organic traffic.
- **Sales tool:** Sales reps can share your YouTube tutorials with prospects.
- **Community building:** YouTube comments and Community tab create an engaged audience.

## Cross-Promotion

### Driving YouTube Viewers to Other Platforms

- **Product:** "Try [product] free — link in the description" at the end of every relevant video.
- **Newsletter:** "I share weekly behind-the-scenes content in my newsletter. Link below."
- **Twitter/X:** Share clips, polls, and commentary. Different audience behavior.
- **Discord/Community:** "Join our Discord to discuss this video with other developers."

### Driving Other Platforms to YouTube

- **Email list:** "New video: [Title]" with a direct link. Include a thumbnail image.
- **Twitter/X:** Post a clip or thread summarizing the video. Link to the full video.
- **Blog:** Embed your YouTube video in relevant blog posts. Helps YouTube SEO and blog engagement.
- **Reddit:** Share in relevant subreddits (following their rules). Focus on value, not self-promotion.
- **Product itself:** Link to tutorial videos from your docs, help center, and onboarding flow.

### Repurposing YouTube Content

| YouTube Content | Repurpose As |
|----------------|-------------|
| Long-form tutorial | Blog post + 3 Shorts + Twitter thread |
| Product update | Email newsletter + social post + changelog entry |
| Industry commentary | LinkedIn article + Twitter thread + podcast episode |
| Behind-the-scenes | Instagram/TikTok stories + newsletter section |

**Rule of thumb:** Every YouTube video should become at least 3 pieces of content on other platforms.

## Getting Started Checklist

If you're starting a YouTube channel from scratch for your tech product:

- [ ] Create and brand the channel (name, avatar, banner, description)
- [ ] Set up default upload settings (description template, tags)
- [ ] Install TubeBuddy or vidIQ (free tier)
- [ ] Plan your first 10 video topics using keyword research
- [ ] Record and publish your first video (don't wait for perfection)
- [ ] Create a "Start Here" playlist
- [ ] Set a consistent publishing schedule (minimum 1/week)
- [ ] Cross-promote every video on at least 2 other platforms
- [ ] Review analytics weekly and adjust
- [ ] Batch record to stay ahead of schedule
