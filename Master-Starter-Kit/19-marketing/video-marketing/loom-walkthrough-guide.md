## What Are Loom-Style Walkthrough Videos

A walkthrough video is a short, informal screen recording — often with a talking-head webcam overlay in the corner — where you walk someone through something visually. Think of it as "showing, not telling."

Unlike polished marketing videos or YouTube content, walkthrough videos are:

- **Informal.** No scripts, no editing, no production value needed. Raw and real.
- **Personalized.** Often recorded for a specific person or situation.
- **Async.** Recorded at your convenience, watched at theirs. No scheduling required.
- **Visual.** You literally show what you mean by sharing your screen.
- **Fast.** A 2-minute walkthrough video replaces a 10-minute email or a 30-minute call.

### Why They Convert Better Than Text

- **Clarity:** A 60-second screen recording showing "click here, then here, then configure this" eliminates the ambiguity of written instructions.
- **Personal connection:** Seeing and hearing a real person builds trust. Your face in the webcam bubble makes it human.
- **Effort signal:** A personalized video tells the recipient "I cared enough to record this for you." That effort builds rapport.
- **Consumption rate:** People are far more likely to watch a 2-minute video than read a 500-word email. The information actually gets consumed.
- **Retention:** Viewers remember visual demonstrations better than written instructions. They can also rewatch specific parts.

### Where Walkthrough Videos Fit in Your Business

```
SALES
├── Prospecting: Personalized product walkthroughs for cold outreach
├── Follow-ups: Post-call summaries showing what you discussed
├── Proposals: Walk through your proposal/pricing visually
└── Objection handling: Address specific concerns with a demo

CUSTOMER SUCCESS
├── Onboarding: Personalized setup walkthroughs for new users
├── Feature adoption: "Here's a feature I think you'd love"
├── Check-ins: Quarterly review of their usage/results
└── Upselling: Show them what they're missing on a higher tier

SUPPORT
├── Bug explanations: "Here's what happened and how we fixed it"
├── How-to responses: Show them how instead of writing instructions
├── Escalation context: Visual reproduction of issues for engineering
└── Proactive tips: "I noticed you might benefit from..."

INTERNAL
├── Team updates: Record instead of meeting
├── Code reviews: Walk through your PR visually
├── Handoffs: Record context for the next person
└── Training: Create reusable onboarding videos for new hires
```

## Use Cases

### 1. Personalized Sales Demos

**Scenario:** You've identified a prospect who could benefit from your product. Instead of a generic email, you record a personalized walkthrough.

**How it works:**
1. Research the prospect: visit their website, understand their role, identify their likely pain points.
2. Open your product and set up a scenario relevant to their use case.
3. Record a 60-120 second walkthrough: "Hi [Name], I noticed [observation about their business]. Let me show you how [Product] could help with [specific use case]..."
4. Share the video link in your outreach email.

**Why it works:** The prospect sees their exact use case demonstrated, by a real person who did their homework. This converts 3-5x better than a generic email.

**Example script:**
```
"Hi Sarah, I was checking out Acme Corp's website and noticed you're doing
[specific thing]. A lot of [their role type] tell us that [pain point] is
a big challenge. Let me show you something quick —

[Share screen, demonstrate the relevant feature]

This would let your team [specific benefit]. It takes about [time] to set up.

If you're curious, I'd love to chat for 15 minutes. I'll drop a calendar link
below. Either way, hope this was useful!"
```

### 2. Post-Call Follow-Ups

**Scenario:** After a sales call or demo, record a summary walkthrough.

**How it works:**
1. Immediately after the call, record a 2-3 minute recap.
2. Revisit the features they were most interested in.
3. Address any questions that came up.
4. Include next steps.

**Why it works:** The prospect can share the video with other decision-makers who weren't on the call. It keeps momentum going.

### 3. Proposal Walkthroughs

**Scenario:** Instead of emailing a PDF proposal, record yourself walking through it.

**How it works:**
1. Open the proposal document on your screen.
2. Walk through each section: what you're offering, pricing, timeline, expectations.
3. Highlight the parts most relevant to their specific needs.

**Why it works:** Proposals are skimmed. Videos are watched. You control the narrative and can emphasize value.

### 4. Customer Onboarding

**Scenario:** A new customer just signed up. Send them a personalized onboarding walkthrough.

**How it works:**
1. Log into their account (or a demo account configured for their use case).
2. Record: "Welcome to [Product]! Let me show you how to get set up for [their specific goal]..."
3. Walk through the 3-5 most important first steps.
4. End with: "Any questions? Reply to this email or hit the chat button inside the app."

**Why it works:** Personalized onboarding dramatically improves activation. The new user feels supported, not abandoned after signup.

### 5. Feature Adoption

**Scenario:** A user has been active but hasn't tried a feature that would benefit them.

**How it works:**
1. Identify underused features from usage data.
2. Record: "Hey [Name], I noticed you've been doing great with [feature they use]. Did you know about [underused feature]? Let me show you..."
3. Quick 60-second demo of the feature applied to their use case.

**Why it works:** Proactive feature education reduces churn and increases expansion revenue.

### 6. Support Responses

**Scenario:** A customer asks "how do I do X?" in a support ticket.

**How it works:**
1. Instead of writing a multi-step text response, open your product.
2. Record: "Great question! Let me show you exactly how to do that..."
3. Walk through the solution step by step on screen.
4. Include the video link in your ticket response alongside brief written steps.

**Why it works:** Customers resolve their issue faster. Support reps save time (recording is faster than writing detailed instructions). Customer satisfaction scores improve.

### 7. Bug Reproduction

**Scenario:** A customer reports a bug. You need engineering to see exactly what happened.

**How it works:**
1. Ask the customer to record their screen showing the bug (send a Loom link).
2. Or reproduce the bug yourself and record it for the engineering team.
3. Include the video in the bug report alongside technical details.

**Why it works:** "Steps to reproduce" in text are ambiguous. A screen recording eliminates guesswork. Engineers fix bugs faster when they can see them.

## Sales Prospecting With Async Video

This is the highest-ROI use of walkthrough videos. A personalized video in a cold email can transform your response rates.

### The Process

```
Step 1: RESEARCH (2-3 minutes)
   │  Visit their website, LinkedIn, recent posts.
   │  Identify their role, industry, likely pain points.
   │  Note something specific you can reference.
   │
Step 2: PREPARE (1 minute)
   │  Open your product.
   │  Set up a scenario relevant to their use case.
   │  Have their website open in another tab (to reference).
   │
Step 3: RECORD (60-120 seconds)
   │  Hi [Name]. Quick introduction.
   │  Reference something specific about them (shows research).
   │  "Let me show you something..." → share screen.
   │  Walk through the product applied to their use case.
   │  Light CTA: "Would love to chat if you're curious."
   │
Step 4: SEND (1 minute)
   │  Drop video link in email.
   │  Subject line: "Quick video for you, [Name]"
   │  2-3 sentence email body + video link.
   │  Include calendar link for booking.
   │
Step 5: FOLLOW UP (if no response after 3 days)
      "Did you get a chance to watch the video?"
      Some tools show if/when the video was viewed.
```

### Prospecting Email Template

```
Subject: Made you a quick video, {{PROSPECT_NAME}}

Hi {{PROSPECT_NAME}},

I was looking at {{PROSPECT_COMPANY}}'s {{SPECIFIC_OBSERVATION}} and thought
of a way you might {{BENEFIT}}.

I recorded a quick 90-second walkthrough showing exactly what I mean:
👉 [VIDEO LINK]

No pitch — just wanted to show you something I think you'd find interesting.

If you'd like to chat, here's my calendar: [CALENDAR LINK]

Best,
{{YOUR_NAME}}
```

### Tips for Sales Videos

- **Keep it under 2 minutes.** Respect their time. If they're interested, they'll reply.
- **Show their website or product on screen.** This proves you did research and personalized the video.
- **Don't ask for a meeting in the video.** Ask in the email. The video should deliver value, not make demands.
- **Record in batches.** Do 5-10 prospect videos in one session. You'll get into a flow.
- **Track views.** Tools like Vidyard and Loom show when and how much of the video was watched. Follow up when they watch.
- **Accept low response rates.** Even personalized video outreach gets 10-30% response rates for cold outreach. That's 3-5x better than text-only cold email, but most people still won't respond. That's normal.

## Customer Onboarding

### The Personalized Onboarding Walkthrough

**When to send:** Within 24 hours of a new customer signing up.

**Structure:**
```
WELCOME (0:00 - 0:15)
  "Hey [Name], welcome to [Product]! I'm [Your Name], and I'll be
  helping you get set up. Let me show you the quickest way to get started."

STEP 1 (0:15 - 0:45)
  Show the first thing they need to do. Walk through it on screen.
  "First, you'll want to [action]..."

STEP 2 (0:45 - 1:30)
  Show the core feature that delivers value.
  "This is where the magic happens..."

STEP 3 (1:30 - 2:00)
  Show how to get help and next steps.
  "If you have any questions, [how to reach support]."

WRAP-UP (2:00 - 2:30)
  "That should get you up and running. I'll check in next week to see
  how things are going. Welcome aboard!"
```

### Scaling Onboarding Videos

For high-touch (enterprise) customers, record a unique video for each one. For lower-touch (self-serve) customers, use a hybrid approach:

| Customer Tier | Approach |
|--------------|----------|
| Enterprise / high-value | Fully personalized video for each customer |
| Mid-market | Semi-personalized: generic walkthrough + 30 seconds of personalized intro |
| Self-serve / free | Pre-recorded generic onboarding video in welcome email |

### Onboarding Video Series

Instead of one long video, create a sequence:

1. **Day 0:** Welcome + first 3 steps (2-3 minutes).
2. **Day 3:** "How to [core feature]" (2 minutes).
3. **Day 7:** "3 tips for getting the most out of [Product]" (2 minutes).
4. **Day 14:** "Have you tried [advanced feature]?" (90 seconds).
5. **Day 30:** "Checking in — how's it going?" (60 seconds, with feedback request).

## Support Escalation

### Using Video in Support

**Instead of this text response:**
```
To export your data:
1. Go to Settings > Data > Export
2. Select the date range
3. Choose CSV format
4. Click "Generate Export"
5. The file will appear in your Downloads folder within 5 minutes.
   If it takes longer, check your notification settings under
   Preferences > Notifications > Email...
```

**Record this:**
60-second video walking through the exact steps, showing each click and result.

### When to Use Video in Support

| Situation | Use Video? | Why |
|-----------|-----------|-----|
| Simple one-step answer | No | Text is faster: "Yes, you can do that in Settings > Privacy." |
| Multi-step instructions | Yes | Video prevents confusion about which buttons to click. |
| Visual feature explanation | Yes | Some things are nearly impossible to explain in text. |
| Bug reproduction | Yes | "Here's what I see on my end" — shows the issue clearly. |
| Angry/frustrated customer | Yes | Video is personal. It de-escalates because they see a real person who cares. |
| Complex configuration | Yes | Walking through config options visually prevents errors. |
| "I can't find it" | Yes | Just show them where it is. 10-second video beats 5 paragraphs. |

### Support Video Template

```
"Hi [Name], great question! Let me show you exactly how to do that.

[Share screen — navigate to the relevant section]

So here in [section], you'll see [element]. Click on that, and...

[Walk through the steps]

And that's it! [Confirm the result on screen]

If you run into any issues, just reply to this ticket and I'll help out.
Have a great day!"
```

### Pro tip: Save and Reuse

If you record a support video for one customer and the question is common, save it. The next time someone asks the same question, you already have a video ready. Over time, this builds a video knowledge base.

## Recording Best Practices

### Before You Hit Record

- **Close everything irrelevant.** Slack, email, personal tabs — everything. Your screen should only show what the viewer needs to see.
- **Prepare your flow.** Know what you're going to show, in what order. Don't wing it completely — have 3-4 bullet points in mind.
- **Set up the product state.** If you're demoing a feature, make sure the demo data is ready and the feature is in the right state.
- **Check your audio.** Do a 5-second test recording. Listen back. Make sure your mic is clear.
- **Notifications OFF.** System notifications, Slack, email — disable all of them. Nothing ruins a walkthrough like a personal notification popping up.

### Webcam Best Practices

- **Use the webcam overlay (talking head bubble).** This is what makes walkthrough videos feel personal. Your face in the corner builds trust.
- **Position the webcam bubble in the bottom-left or bottom-right corner.** Don't cover important UI elements.
- **Look at the camera lens, not the screen.** This creates the feeling of eye contact.
- **Good lighting on your face.** Sit facing a window or use a simple ring light.
- **Clean background.** Blur or use a plain background if your environment is distracting.
- **When to hide webcam:** When showing detailed UI that needs the full screen. Toggle webcam off for complex screen demonstrations, back on for introductions and CTAs.

### Screen Recording Tips

- **Zoom in on important elements.** Don't make viewers squint at a tiny button.
- **Move your cursor slowly and deliberately.** Pause on each element before clicking.
- **Use cursor highlighting.** Most tools offer this — a colored circle around your cursor makes it easy to follow.
- **Draw/annotate on screen.** Some tools let you circle or underline elements in real-time. Use this sparingly to highlight key areas.
- **Narrate as you go.** "Now I'm clicking on Settings... and here you'll see the Export option..." — sync your words with your actions.

### Audio Tips

- **Use a decent microphone.** Your laptop's built-in mic is acceptable. A $30 USB mic or your earbuds' mic is noticeably better.
- **Speak at a natural pace.** Not too fast (they'll miss things), not too slow (they'll get bored).
- **It's okay to make small mistakes.** A natural "oops, let me go back" is fine and actually makes the video feel more authentic. Don't re-record for minor stumbles.
- **Avoid filler words.** Occasional "um" is fine. Constant "um, uh, so, basically" is distracting. Practice reducing these, but don't obsess.

## Video Length Guidelines

| Use Case | Recommended Length | Notes |
|----------|-------------------|-------|
| Sales prospecting (cold) | 60-90 seconds | Keep it tight. They don't know you yet. Respect their time. |
| Sales follow-up (warm) | 90-120 seconds | Slightly longer okay — they're already interested. |
| Proposal walkthrough | 2-4 minutes | As long as needed to cover the proposal, but trim the fat. |
| Customer onboarding | 2-5 minutes per video | Break into a series. No single video over 5 minutes. |
| Feature education | 60-120 seconds | One feature per video. Don't bundle. |
| Support response | 30-120 seconds | As short as possible while fully solving the problem. |
| Bug reproduction | 30-60 seconds | Show the exact steps and result. Nothing else. |
| Internal team update | 2-5 minutes | Replace meetings, not create longer ones. |
| Knowledge base article | 1-3 minutes | Supplement written docs with visual walkthrough. |

### The Golden Rule

**If your video would be faster as a text message, don't record a video.** Video is for things that benefit from being shown visually. "Yes, that feature is available" doesn't need a video. "Here's how to configure that feature" does.

## Integration and Distribution

### Embedding in Emails

Most email clients don't support embedded video playback. Instead:

1. **Take a screenshot or thumbnail** of the video (Loom and Vidyard auto-generate these).
2. **Add a play button overlay** on the thumbnail image.
3. **Link the image to the video URL.**
4. The recipient sees what looks like an embedded video, clicks, and is taken to the video.

**Email template:**
```
Hi [Name],

I recorded a quick walkthrough showing [what it covers]:

[THUMBNAIL IMAGE WITH PLAY BUTTON — linked to video URL]

Takes about [X] minutes to watch. Let me know if you have questions!

[Your Name]
```

### CRM Integration

| CRM | Tool | Integration |
|-----|------|-------------|
| HubSpot | Vidyard | Native integration. Videos auto-log in contact timeline. View notifications trigger workflows. |
| Salesforce | Vidyard | Activity logging, video views tracked in Salesforce. |
| HubSpot | Loom | Integration available. Video links tracked. |
| Any CRM | Any tool | Copy-paste video link into notes. Manual but works. |

### Help Docs and Knowledge Base

- **Embed walkthrough videos** at the top of help articles. Video-first, text as supplementary.
- **Tools that support video embeds:** Notion, GitBook, Zendesk, Intercom, Help Scout, Confluence.
- **Create a "Video Tutorials" section** in your help center with organized playlists.
- **Update videos when the UI changes.** Outdated videos are worse than no videos. Track which videos need re-recording when you ship UI updates.

### In-App Video

- **Onboarding tooltips:** Trigger short walkthrough videos at key moments in the user journey.
- **Feature announcements:** When launching a new feature, show a 30-second walkthrough in-app.
- **Help widget:** Replace "Read the docs" with "Watch a quick walkthrough."
- **Tools for in-app video:** Appcues, Chameleon, Pendo, UserGuiding (most support video embeds).

## Scaling With Reusable Libraries

As you record more walkthroughs, you'll notice the same questions and scenarios coming up repeatedly. Build a library.

### Building a Walkthrough Library

1. **Track common scenarios.** What questions does support get most often? What do sales reps demo repeatedly? What onboarding steps confuse users?
2. **Record one high-quality video for each common scenario.** Take a few extra minutes to make it clear and well-paced.
3. **Organize by category:**
   - Getting Started (onboarding)
   - Features (one video per major feature)
   - Integrations (one video per integration)
   - Troubleshooting (common issues)
   - Sales Demos (by use case or persona)
4. **Store in a central, searchable location.** Loom workspace, Notion page, or your help center.
5. **Make the library accessible to your whole team.** Support, sales, and success should all know what's available.

### Hybrid Personalization

The most efficient approach combines reusable and personalized video:

```
REUSABLE LIBRARY VIDEO (pre-recorded)
  ↓
+ PERSONALIZED INTRO/OUTRO (recorded fresh)
  ↓
= FEELS PERSONALIZED, BUT 80% LESS WORK
```

**Example:**
- Record a 15-second personalized intro: "Hi Sarah, welcome to [Product]! I put together a quick walkthrough to help you get started."
- Link to the reusable onboarding walkthrough video.
- The recipient feels it's personalized. You saved 5 minutes of recording time.

### Library Maintenance

- **Review your library quarterly.** Remove or re-record videos that reference outdated UI or features.
- **Tag videos with the product version** so you know which ones need updating when the product changes.
- **Track usage.** Which library videos get watched most? Which get shared by your team? Double down on high-usage content.
- **Gather feedback.** Ask your team: "Is there a walkthrough video you wish existed?" Add it to the library.

## Metrics and Optimization

### Key Metrics

| Metric | What It Tells You | Where to Find It |
|--------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **View rate** | % of recipients who watched | Loom/Vidyard analytics dashboard |
| **Watch-through rate** | % of the video that was watched on average | Loom/Vidyard analytics |
| **Drop-off point** | Where viewers stop watching | Vidyard (detailed engagement graphs) |
| **CTA click rate** | % of viewers who clicked your CTA button | Loom/Vidyard (if using CTA feature) |
| **Reply rate** | % of video recipients who replied to your email | Your email tool / CRM |
| **Meeting booked rate** | % who booked a meeting after watching | Calendar tool / CRM |
| **Support resolution time** | Time to resolve tickets with vs. without video | Help desk analytics |
| **Onboarding activation rate** | % of users who complete onboarding with vs. without video | Product analytics |

### Benchmarks

| Metric | Good | Excellent |
|--------|------|-----------|
| View rate (sales outreach) | 40-50% | 60%+ |
| View rate (customer onboarding) | 60-70% | 80%+ |
| Watch-through rate | 50-60% | 70%+ |
| CTA click rate | 5-10% | 15%+ |
| Reply rate (sales prospecting) | 15-25% | 30%+ |

### Optimization Tips

- **If view rate is low:** Your email subject line or thumbnail isn't compelling. Test different approaches.
- **If watch-through rate is low:** Your video is too long or loses the viewer's interest. Check the drop-off point — is there a boring section?
- **If CTA click rate is low:** Your CTA isn't clear or compelling. Test different button text and placement.
- **If reply rate is low but view rate is high:** They watched but weren't convinced. Make your video more relevant to their specific situation.

### A/B Testing for Walkthrough Videos

Test these variables to improve performance:

1. **Video length:** 60s vs. 120s for sales outreach. Which gets more replies?
2. **With vs. without webcam:** Some audiences prefer screen-only. Test it.
3. **Email subject lines:** "Quick video for you" vs. "Saw [observation], had an idea" vs. "[Name], check this out."
4. **CTA placement:** In the video vs. in the email vs. both.
5. **Personalization level:** Fully personalized vs. semi-personalized (generic video + personalized email).

## Getting Started Checklist

### Day 1: Setup

- [ ] Choose a recording tool (Loom free tier is recommended to start)
- [ ] Set up your profile (photo, name, company)
- [ ] Test recording: audio, webcam, screen share
- [ ] Clean up your desktop and browser for recording sessions
- [ ] Practice recording 2-3 throwaway videos to get comfortable

### Week 1: First Videos

- [ ] Record one personalized sales prospecting video and send it
- [ ] Record one support response as a walkthrough video
- [ ] Record a generic onboarding walkthrough for new users
- [ ] Track results: views, replies, feedback

### Month 1: Build the Habit

- [ ] Record 3-5 sales prospecting videos per week
- [ ] Use video for all multi-step support responses
- [ ] Start building your reusable video library (aim for 10 videos)
- [ ] Measure: compare email response rates with video vs. without

### Month 2+: Optimize and Scale

- [ ] Review metrics and optimize (length, personalization, timing)
- [ ] Expand library to 25+ reusable walkthroughs
- [ ] Train your team on walkthrough video best practices
- [ ] Integrate with CRM for tracking
- [ ] Establish video recording as a standard part of sales and support workflows
