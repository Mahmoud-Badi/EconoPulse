## Types of Demo Videos

Not every demo video serves the same purpose. Choose the type based on where your audience is in the buyer journey.

### 1. Full Product Tour (3-5 minutes)

- **Purpose:** Give prospects a comprehensive overview of what {{PROJECT_NAME}} does.
- **Audience:** Warm leads who already know what you do, evaluating whether to sign up.
- **Structure:** Walk through the main workflows in order. Show the complete user experience from signup to core value.
- **Where to use:** Homepage, pricing page, "How it works" page.

### 2. Feature Spotlight (60-90 seconds)

- **Purpose:** Highlight a single feature in depth.
- **Audience:** Existing users who may not know about the feature, or prospects comparing features.
- **Structure:** Problem → How this one feature solves it → Quick walkthrough → Result.
- **Where to use:** Blog posts, changelog, email announcements, social media.

### 3. Use-Case Demo (2-3 minutes)

- **Purpose:** Show how a specific type of user or industry uses {{PROJECT_NAME}}.
- **Audience:** Prospects who see themselves in the use case.
- **Structure:** "If you're a [role] who struggles with [problem], here's how {{PROJECT_NAME}} helps..."
- **Where to use:** Landing pages, targeted ads, sales outreach, case study pages.

### 4. Comparison Demo (2-4 minutes)

- **Purpose:** Show how {{PROJECT_NAME}} compares to a competitor or manual process.
- **Audience:** Prospects actively evaluating alternatives.
- **Structure:** Side-by-side or before/after. Show the old way, then the {{PROJECT_NAME}} way.
- **Where to use:** Comparison landing pages, sales calls, competitive blog posts.
- **Caution:** Be factual and fair. Never trash competitors — just show your strengths.

### 5. Quick-Start Tutorial (1-2 minutes)

- **Purpose:** Get new users from zero to first success as fast as possible.
- **Audience:** Brand new users or trial users who need onboarding.
- **Structure:** Step 1 → Step 2 → Step 3 → "You just did the thing!" celebration.
- **Where to use:** Welcome email, onboarding flow, help center, in-app.

## Demo Video Structure

Every effective demo video follows this arc, regardless of length:

```
HOOK (0:00 - 0:15)
  ↓ State the problem your audience has. Make them nod.
SOLUTION INTRO (0:15 - 0:30)
  ↓ Introduce {{PROJECT_NAME}} as the answer. One sentence.
WALKTHROUGH (0:30 - 2:00)
  ↓ Show the product in action. Focus on outcomes, not clicks.
RESULTS (2:00 - 2:30)
  ↓ Show the end result. Before/after. Metrics if possible.
CTA (2:30 - 3:00)
  ↓ Tell the viewer exactly what to do next.
```

### The Hook — Most Critical 10 Seconds

Your hook determines whether people watch or click away. Use one of these formulas:

| Hook Type | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| Pain point | "Tired of spending 3 hours every week on {{PAIN_POINT}}?" |
| Bold claim | "I'm going to show you how to {{KEY_BENEFIT}} in under 2 minutes." |
| Question | "What if you could {{DESIRED_OUTCOME}} without {{COMMON_FRICTION}}?" |
| Statistic | "{{STATISTIC_OR_PROOF_POINT}} — here's how {{PROJECT_NAME}} makes it possible." |
| Demonstration | Start with the end result, then rewind: "Let me show you how I got here." |

## Screen Recording Best Practices

### Resolution and Display Settings

- **Record at 1920x1080 (1080p) minimum.** This is the standard for YouTube and most platforms.
- **Use 4K (3840x2160) if your product has fine UI details** and your audience watches on desktop.
- **Set your display scaling to 100%** — scaled-up interfaces look blurry when recorded.
- **Increase font size in your product** if text is small. Viewers watch on phones too.
- **Use a clean browser profile** — no personal bookmarks, extensions, or embarrassing tabs.

### Cursor and Interaction

- **Slow down your mouse movements.** You know the product; viewers don't. Move 50% slower than normal.
- **Use cursor highlighting** so viewers can track where you're clicking. Most recording tools offer this.
- **Pause briefly after each click** to let the viewer see the result before moving on.
- **Avoid scrolling fast.** Smooth, deliberate scrolls only.
- **Click precisely.** Hesitant cursor wandering looks unprofessional.

### Zoom and Focus

- **Zoom in on important UI elements.** Don't make viewers squint at a tiny button.
- **Use callouts or annotations** to draw attention to key areas.
- **Blur or crop out irrelevant parts** of the screen when showing a specific feature.
- **Pan smoothly** between different parts of the interface.

### Clean Desktop Setup

- **Hide your bookmarks bar** in the browser.
- **Use a neutral desktop wallpaper** if your desktop will be visible.
- **Close all notifications** — Slack, email, OS notifications, everything.
- **Use demo/sample data** that looks realistic. No "test123" or "asdf" entries.
- **Pre-load all pages** you'll navigate to so there's no awkward loading time.

## Narration Tips

### Pacing

- **Speak slower than you think you should.** Most people rush when recording.
- **Target 130-150 words per minute.** Normal conversation is 150-170, so dial it back.
- **Pause after important points.** Give viewers a beat to process what they just saw.
- **Match your narration to screen actions.** Say "click here" as you click, not before.

### Tone

- **Conversational, not corporate.** Imagine explaining the product to a smart friend.
- **Enthusiastic but not hypey.** Be genuinely excited, not infomercial-excited.
- **Confident but not arrogant.** "This is really useful for X" beats "This is the best tool ever."
- **Acknowledge pain points honestly.** "We've all been there" builds rapport.

### Script vs. Improvised

| Approach | Best For | Tips |
|----------|----------|------|
| Fully scripted | Homepage demos, ads, explainers | Write it out. Read it 3x before recording. Don't sound like you're reading. |
| Bullet points | Feature spotlights, tutorials | Write key points. Speak naturally between them. More authentic feel. |
| Improvised | Quick social content, Loom-style | Know your start and end point. Accept small imperfections. |

**Recommendation for {{PROJECT_NAME}}:** Start with a fully scripted approach for your main product demo (homepage, landing page). Use bullet points for feature spotlights and tutorials.

### Background Music

- **Use it for polished demos** (homepage, ads). Skip it for tutorials and quick walkthroughs.
- **Keep it subtle.** Music should be barely noticeable — 10-15% volume compared to voice.
- **Choose upbeat but unobtrusive tracks.** No lyrics. Lo-fi, ambient, or light electronic works well.
- **Royalty-free sources:** Epidemic Sound, Artlist, YouTube Audio Library (free), Uppbeat (free tier).
- **Match the energy.** Fast-paced product = slightly upbeat music. Thoughtful tool = calm music.

## Explainer Video Script Formula

For animated explainers (motion graphics style), use this proven structure:

```
[SECTION 1: THE PROBLEM — 15-20 seconds]
"{{TARGET_AUDIENCE}} deal with {{PAIN_POINT}} every day.
They try {{CURRENT_SOLUTION_1}} and {{CURRENT_SOLUTION_2}}, but
these approaches are {{LIMITATION_1}}, {{LIMITATION_2}}, and {{LIMITATION_3}}."

[SECTION 2: THE SOLUTION — 10-15 seconds]
"Introducing {{PROJECT_NAME}} — {{ONE_LINE_DESCRIPTION}}.
{{PROJECT_NAME}} lets you {{KEY_BENEFIT}} without {{KEY_FRICTION}}."

[SECTION 3: HOW IT WORKS — 30-40 seconds]
"Here's how it works:
Step 1: {{HOW_STEP_1}}
Step 2: {{HOW_STEP_2}}
Step 3: {{HOW_STEP_3}}
That's it. {{SIMPLICITY_STATEMENT}}."

[SECTION 4: BENEFITS — 15-20 seconds]
"With {{PROJECT_NAME}}, you get:
✓ {{BENEFIT_1}}
✓ {{BENEFIT_2}}
✓ {{BENEFIT_3}}
{{SOCIAL_PROOF_POINT}}"

[SECTION 5: CTA — 5-10 seconds]
"{{CTA_ACTION}} at {{WEBSITE_URL}}.
{{OFFER_DETAIL}}."
```

**Total: 75-105 seconds (ideal for explainers)**

### Animated Explainer Production

- **DIY options:** Vyond ($49/mo), Animaker (free tier), Powtoon (free tier), Canva animations.
- **Professional production:** $2,000-$10,000 for a 60-90 second explainer from a studio.
- **Middle ground:** Hire a freelance motion designer on Upwork ($500-$2,000).

## Video Length Recommendations

The right length depends on where the video lives and what it's supposed to do.

| Context | Recommended Length | Reasoning |
|---------|-------------------|-----------|
| Social media (feed) | 30-60 seconds | Attention spans are shortest here. Hook fast, deliver value, get out. |
| Social media (stories/reels) | 15-30 seconds | Even shorter. One idea per video. |
| Landing page | 60-120 seconds | Visitors are semi-interested. Don't lose them with a 5-minute video. |
| Homepage | 60-90 seconds | Overview only. Link to longer content for those who want more. |
| Email embed | 30-60 seconds | Keep it short. Thumbnail + play button > autoplay. |
| Sales outreach | 60-120 seconds | Personalized and direct. Respect their time. |
| Onboarding | 60-180 seconds per step | Break into multiple short videos rather than one long one. |
| Full product demo | 3-5 minutes | For engaged prospects. Used in sales calls or "watch full demo" CTAs. |
| Tutorial | 3-10 minutes | As long as needed, but get to the point quickly. |
| YouTube content | 8-15 minutes | YouTube algorithm favors watch time. Longer is fine if valuable. |

**Rule of thumb:** Make it as short as possible while still delivering the full message. If you can say it in 60 seconds, don't take 120.

## A/B Testing Videos

### What to Test

1. **Thumbnails** — The single biggest lever for click-through rate.
   - Test: faces vs. no faces, text vs. no text, different colors, different expressions.
   - Use YouTube's built-in thumbnail A/B test or tools like TubeBuddy.

2. **CTAs** — What you ask viewers to do at the end.
   - Test: "Start free trial" vs. "See pricing" vs. "Watch next video."
   - Test: CTA at the end vs. mid-video CTA vs. both.

3. **Hook/Opening** — The first 5-10 seconds.
   - Test: problem-first vs. result-first vs. question-first.
   - Measure: audience retention drop-off in the first 30 seconds.

4. **Video Length** — Shorter vs. longer versions of the same demo.
   - Test: 60-second cut vs. 2-minute full version on landing pages.
   - Measure: conversion rate, not just watch time.

5. **Video vs. No Video** — Does video actually improve your landing page?
   - Test: landing page with video vs. without.
   - Measure: overall conversion rate, time on page.

### How to Test

- **Landing pages:** Use your A/B testing tool (Google Optimize, Optimizely, VWO, or built-in tools like Unbounce).
- **YouTube:** Use the built-in thumbnail test feature (requires 1,000+ subscribers).
- **Email:** Send two versions to split segments. Measure click rate and conversion.
- **Social media:** Post two versions at different times or to different audiences. Compare engagement.

### Minimum Sample Size

Don't make decisions on 50 views. Aim for at least 200-500 views per variant before drawing conclusions for landing page videos. For social media, look at 1,000+ impressions per variant.

## Product-Type-Specific Strategies

<!-- IF {{PRODUCT_TYPE}} == "saas" -->
### SaaS Product Demo Strategy

**Primary demo video:** 2-minute product tour showing the core workflow from signup to first value.

**Key focus areas:**
- Show the dashboard and main interface early — SaaS buyers want to see the UI.
- Demonstrate a complete workflow, not isolated features.
- Include real data (or realistic sample data) — empty-state demos feel underwhelming.
- Show integration points with tools your audience already uses.
- End with pricing clarity: "Free to start" or "Plans start at $X/month."

**Video funnel for SaaS:**
1. **Awareness:** 30-60s social clips showing the problem + quick solution.
2. **Consideration:** 2-3 minute use-case demo on landing page.
3. **Decision:** Full 5-minute product tour with all features.
4. **Onboarding:** Step-by-step tutorials for each core feature.
5. **Retention:** Monthly "what's new" feature announcement videos.
<!-- ENDIF -->

<!-- IF {{PRODUCT_TYPE}} == "mobile_app" -->
### Mobile App Demo Strategy

**Primary demo video:** 60-90 second app preview showing the core user experience.

**Key focus areas:**
- Record on a real device (not simulator) for authenticity.
- Show the app in context — a hand holding a phone, or a phone mockup frame.
- Focus on speed and simplicity. Mobile users expect instant gratification.
- Include App Store / Play Store preview requirements (15-30 seconds, specific dimensions).
- Show notifications, widgets, and offline functionality if applicable.

**Video funnel for mobile apps:**
1. **Awareness:** 15-30s vertical video showing the "wow moment."
2. **Consideration:** 60-90s app walkthrough on landing page.
3. **Decision:** Side-by-side comparison with competitors.
4. **Onboarding:** In-app tutorial videos (keep under 30 seconds each).
5. **Retention:** Push notification linking to new feature preview videos.
<!-- ENDIF -->

<!-- IF {{PRODUCT_TYPE}} == "api" -->
### API / Developer Tool Demo Strategy

**Primary demo video:** 3-5 minute technical walkthrough showing integration from scratch.

**Key focus areas:**
- Show real code, not slides about code. Developers want to see it work.
- Use a clean IDE/terminal setup with readable font size (16px+).
- Start with `npm install` or equivalent — show how easy setup is.
- Build something real in the demo. "Let's build X in 3 minutes" format works great.
- Show error handling and edge cases — developers appreciate honesty.
- Include API response times and performance metrics.

**Video funnel for APIs/dev tools:**
1. **Awareness:** 60s "build X in 60 seconds" challenge on social media.
2. **Consideration:** 3-5 minute "getting started" tutorial.
3. **Decision:** Full integration walkthrough with a real project.
4. **Onboarding:** Video-enhanced documentation (embedded in docs).
5. **Retention:** Monthly technical deep-dives on advanced features.
<!-- ENDIF -->

<!-- IF {{PRODUCT_TYPE}} == "ecommerce" -->
### E-commerce Product Demo Strategy

**Primary demo video:** 30-90 second product showcase with lifestyle context.

**Key focus areas:**
- Show the product being used in real life, not just studio shots.
- Include unboxing if it's a physical product — unboxing videos drive purchase intent.
- Show scale and dimensions clearly. Buyers want to know what they're getting.
- Include customer testimonial clips in product demos.
- UGC (user-generated content) style videos outperform polished studio content for ads.

**Video funnel for e-commerce:**
1. **Awareness:** 15-30s scroll-stopping product reveal on social.
2. **Consideration:** 60-90s product demo with features and use cases.
3. **Decision:** Customer testimonials and unboxing videos.
4. **Onboarding:** Setup/assembly/getting-started guides.
5. **Retention:** Styling tips, advanced uses, complementary products.
<!-- ENDIF -->

<!-- IF {{PRODUCT_TYPE}} == "marketplace" -->
### Marketplace Demo Strategy

**Primary demo video:** Two videos — one for buyers/users, one for sellers/providers.

**Key focus areas:**
- Separate your messaging by audience. Buyers care about selection and ease. Sellers care about reach and revenue.
- Show both sides of the marketplace in action.
- Highlight trust and safety features (reviews, verification, payments).
- Demonstrate the transaction flow end-to-end.
- Use real examples (with permission) to show marketplace activity.

**Video funnel for marketplaces:**
1. **Awareness (buyers):** 30-60s "Find the perfect X" discovery video.
2. **Awareness (sellers):** 60-90s "Start earning on {{PROJECT_NAME}}" recruitment video.
3. **Consideration:** Full marketplace walkthrough showing search, compare, transact.
4. **Onboarding:** Separate tutorial tracks for buyers and sellers.
5. **Retention:** Success stories from both sides of the marketplace.
<!-- ENDIF -->

## Production Checklist

Before recording your next demo video for {{PROJECT_NAME}}, run through this checklist:

### Pre-Recording
- [ ] Script or bullet points written and reviewed
- [ ] Demo data/account prepared with realistic content
- [ ] Screen resolution set correctly (1920x1080 minimum)
- [ ] Notifications disabled (Slack, email, OS, phone)
- [ ] Browser cleaned (no personal bookmarks, extensions, tabs)
- [ ] Recording software tested (audio levels, screen capture area)
- [ ] Quiet recording environment secured
- [ ] Water nearby (dry mouth is real during recording sessions)

### During Recording
- [ ] Record in sections, not one continuous take (easier to edit)
- [ ] Pause 2-3 seconds between sections (gives editing room)
- [ ] Speak slowly and clearly
- [ ] Move cursor deliberately — no frantic clicking
- [ ] Zoom in on small UI elements
- [ ] Record multiple takes of the hook (the hardest part)

### Post-Recording
- [ ] Edit out mistakes, long pauses, and awkward moments
- [ ] Add intro/outro slides with {{PROJECT_NAME}} branding
- [ ] Add background music (if appropriate)
- [ ] Add captions/subtitles (85% of social video is watched without sound)
- [ ] Create thumbnail (bright, clear, readable on mobile)
- [ ] Export in correct format for target platform
- [ ] Upload and add metadata (title, description, tags)
- [ ] Test video embed on target page before publishing

## Key Metrics to Track

| Metric | What It Tells You | Target |
|--------|-------------------|--------|
| **View count** | Reach and distribution | Growing month over month |
| **Play rate** | How many page visitors click play | 30-50% on landing pages |
| **Watch-through rate** | How much of the video people watch | 50%+ average |
| **Drop-off point** | Where viewers stop watching | Identify and fix weak sections |
| **Click-through rate** | How many click your CTA | 3-7% for embedded CTAs |
| **Conversion impact** | Does video improve signup/purchase rate | A/B test video vs. no video |
