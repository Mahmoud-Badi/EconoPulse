## 1. Messaging Hierarchy

Every piece of messaging should be traceable back to these four levels. When creating new content, start with the level that matches your available space, then expand or condense from there.

### Level 1: One-Line Description (10 words or fewer)

The shortest possible description of what {{PROJECT_NAME}} does. Use this for:
- Social media bios
- Directory listings
- Elevator intros at networking events
- Browser tab titles

> **{{ONE_LINE_DESCRIPTION}}**

**Formula:** [Action verb] + [what you do] + [for whom or with what outcome]

**Examples by product type:**

<!-- IF {{PRODUCT_TYPE}} == "saas" -->
- "Automate your team's reporting in minutes, not hours."
- "The dashboard your whole team actually understands."
- "Turn customer data into decisions. Automatically."
<!-- ENDIF -->

<!-- IF {{PRODUCT_TYPE}} == "dev_tool" -->
- "Ship reliable code faster with automated testing."
- "Debug production issues before your users notice."
- "The CLI that makes infrastructure feel simple."
<!-- ENDIF -->

<!-- IF {{PRODUCT_TYPE}} == "mobile_app" -->
- "Track your habits. Build your best life."
- "Restaurant recommendations from people you trust."
- "Learn any language in five minutes a day."
<!-- ENDIF -->

<!-- IF {{PRODUCT_TYPE}} == "ecommerce" -->
- "Curated essentials for the modern home office."
- "Sustainable fashion that does not cost the earth."
- "Premium ingredients delivered to your door weekly."
<!-- ENDIF -->

### Level 2: Elevator Pitch (30 seconds / 2-3 sentences)

Use this for:
- Networking conversations
- Email introductions
- Partnership inquiries
- Website hero section (combined with headline)

> **{{ELEVATOR_PITCH}}**

**Formula:**
> [Customer segment] struggle with [problem]. {{PROJECT_NAME}} [solution] so they can [outcome]. Unlike [alternatives], we [differentiator].

### Level 3: Detailed Description (1 paragraph / 50-80 words)

Use this for:
- About page
- Product Hunt description
- Conference talk introductions
- Press mentions
- App store descriptions

> **{{DETAILED_DESCRIPTION}}**

**Formula:**
> {{PROJECT_NAME}} is a [category] that helps [audience] [accomplish goal]. By [how it works / key mechanism], [product name] [primary benefit]. [Secondary benefit]. [Social proof or traction statement]. [Forward-looking statement or CTA].

### Level 4: Full Story (1 page / 250-400 words)

Use this for:
- Investor pitch narrative section
- "Our Story" page
- Long-form press pitches
- Conference keynote introduction

> **{{FULL_STORY}}**

**Structure:**
1. **The problem** (2-3 sentences): Paint the world as it is. Make the reader feel the pain.
2. **Why it matters** (1-2 sentences): Quantify the impact. What is at stake?
3. **The insight** (1-2 sentences): What did you realize that others missed?
4. **The solution** (2-3 sentences): Introduce {{PROJECT_NAME}}. What does it do?
5. **How it works** (2-3 sentences): Briefly explain the mechanism. Keep it accessible.
6. **The results** (2-3 sentences): What has happened since launch? Traction, outcomes, testimonials.
7. **The vision** (1-2 sentences): Where is this going? What does the future look like?
8. **The ask** (1 sentence): What should the reader do next?

## 4. Sub-Headline Formulas

Sub-headlines support and expand the headline. They provide the "how" or "proof" after the headline makes the promise.

### Sub-Headline Pairing Rules

| Headline Type | Sub-Headline Should... | Example Pair |
|---------------|----------------------|--------------|
| **Benefit-driven** | Explain how or add proof | H: "Deploy 5x faster." SH: "Automated CI/CD that catches errors before they reach production." |
| **Curiosity** | Partially resolve the curiosity | H: "The secret behind Stripe's reliability." SH: "It starts with how they test. Now you can do the same." |
| **Social proof** | Expand with specifics | H: "10,000 teams trust us." SH: "From solo founders to Fortune 500 engineering teams." |
| **Urgency** | Reduce risk of action | H: "Stop losing customers to slow load times." SH: "Set up in 5 minutes. See results by tomorrow. No credit card required." |
| **How-to** | Preview the value | H: "How to cut your AWS bill in half." SH: "Three changes that saved our customers $2M last year." |
| **Question** | Answer with a teaser | H: "What would you do with 10 extra hours a week?" SH: "Our customers spend them on strategy, not spreadsheets." |
| **Contrarian** | Provide the evidence | H: "More features is not the answer." SH: "We built less and grew faster. Here's the framework." |

## 6. Messaging by Stage of Awareness

Based on Eugene Schwartz's five levels of awareness. Your messaging must match where the prospect is in their journey -- mismatched messaging fails regardless of quality.

### Stage 1: Unaware

The prospect does not know they have a problem. Your job is to make them feel the problem.

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Their mindset** | "Everything is fine." / "This is just how it is." |
| **Your goal** | Create awareness of the problem, not your product |
| **Headline approach** | Lead with a story, statistic, or provocative question about their world |
| **Example headline** | "{{UNAWARE_HEADLINE}}" |
| **Example body** | "{{UNAWARE_BODY}}" |
| **CTA** | Educational content, not product. "Read the report" / "See the data" |
| **Channels** | Content marketing, social media, PR, thought leadership |

### Stage 2: Problem-Aware

They know the problem exists but do not know solutions exist. Your job is to validate their pain and hint at a solution category.

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Their mindset** | "This is a real problem, but I don't know what to do about it." |
| **Your goal** | Agitate the problem and introduce the solution category |
| **Headline approach** | Name the pain directly. Show you understand it deeply. |
| **Example headline** | "{{PROBLEM_AWARE_HEADLINE}}" |
| **Example body** | "{{PROBLEM_AWARE_BODY}}" |
| **CTA** | Solution-education. "Learn how teams solve this" / "See the guide" |
| **Channels** | SEO (problem-keyword targeting), communities, comparison content |

### Stage 3: Solution-Aware

They know solutions exist but have not found yours yet. Your job is to position your category and differentiate.

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Their mindset** | "I know there are tools for this -- which one is right for me?" |
| **Your goal** | Position {{PROJECT_NAME}} as the best fit for their specific situation |
| **Headline approach** | Lead with differentiators and specific outcomes |
| **Example headline** | "{{SOLUTION_AWARE_HEADLINE}}" |
| **Example body** | "{{SOLUTION_AWARE_BODY}}" |
| **CTA** | Product-oriented. "See how it works" / "Compare features" |
| **Channels** | Comparison pages, review sites, landing pages, retargeting ads |

### Stage 4: Product-Aware

They know about {{PROJECT_NAME}} but have not decided yet. Your job is to overcome objections and prove value.

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Their mindset** | "I know about {{PROJECT_NAME}} -- is it worth it?" |
| **Your goal** | Build conviction through proof and risk reduction |
| **Headline approach** | Lead with social proof, specific results, and risk reversal |
| **Example headline** | "{{PRODUCT_AWARE_HEADLINE}}" |
| **Example body** | "{{PRODUCT_AWARE_BODY}}" |
| **CTA** | Trial or demo. "Start free trial" / "See it in action" |
| **Channels** | Email nurture, retargeting, case studies, demos, webinars |

### Stage 5: Most Aware

They know {{PROJECT_NAME}} and are ready to act. They just need a reason to act now. Your job is to make the next step easy and urgent.

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Their mindset** | "I want this. What's the deal?" |
| **Your goal** | Remove the final friction. Give them a reason to act today. |
| **Headline approach** | Lead with offer, ease, or urgency |
| **Example headline** | "{{MOST_AWARE_HEADLINE}}" |
| **Example body** | "{{MOST_AWARE_BODY}}" |
| **CTA** | Direct action. "Start now" / "Get {{PROJECT_NAME}} today" / "Claim your discount" |
| **Channels** | Pricing page, sales follow-up, limited-time offers, onboarding page |

### Awareness Stage Quick Reference

```
UNAWARE         --> Content that reveals the problem
PROBLEM-AWARE   --> Content that validates the pain
SOLUTION-AWARE  --> Content that differentiates your approach
PRODUCT-AWARE   --> Content that proves your value
MOST-AWARE      --> Content that triggers action
```

## 8. Comparison Messaging

Use when prospects are evaluating {{PROJECT_NAME}} against alternatives. Always be factual, fair, and focused on fit -- not on tearing down competitors.

### Comparison Framework

**Formula:** "Unlike [alternative], {{PROJECT_NAME}} [differentiator] so you can [outcome]."

| Alternative | Their Strength | Their Weakness (Our Opportunity) | Our Positioning |
|-------------|---------------|----------------------------------|-----------------|
| {{COMPETITOR_1}} | {{COMP_1_STRENGTH}} | {{COMP_1_WEAKNESS}} | "{{COMP_1_POSITIONING}}" |
| {{COMPETITOR_2}} | {{COMP_2_STRENGTH}} | {{COMP_2_WEAKNESS}} | "{{COMP_2_POSITIONING}}" |
| {{COMPETITOR_3}} | {{COMP_3_STRENGTH}} | {{COMP_3_WEAKNESS}} | "{{COMP_3_POSITIONING}}" |
| DIY / In-house | Full control | High cost, slow, maintenance burden | "{{DIY_POSITIONING}}" |
| Status quo / Do nothing | No effort | Problem persists, compounds over time | "{{STATUS_QUO_POSITIONING}}" |

### Comparison Page Messaging Rules

1. **Acknowledge competitor strengths honestly** -- credibility comes from fairness
2. **Focus on fit, not superiority** -- "{{PROJECT_NAME}} is better for X" not "{{PROJECT_NAME}} is better"
3. **Use specific, verifiable claims** -- features and metrics, not adjectives
4. **Include a migration path** -- "Switching takes [time]. Here's how."
5. **Let customers speak** -- include quotes from people who switched
6. **Update regularly** -- competitor features change; stale comparisons damage credibility

## 10. CTA Copy Library

The right CTA depends on context: what the user knows, what you are asking them to do, and how much commitment is involved.

### Primary CTAs (High commitment -- signup, purchase, demo)

| # | CTA | Best For |
|---|-----|----------|
| 1 | "Start your free trial" | SaaS with free trial |
| 2 | "Get started free" | Freemium products |
| 3 | "Get {{PROJECT_NAME}} now" | Direct purchase |
| 4 | "Create your account" | Sign-up focused |
| 5 | "Start building" | Developer tools |
| 6 | "Request a demo" | Enterprise / high-touch sales |
| 7 | "Talk to sales" | Complex buying process |
| 8 | "Buy now" | E-commerce, simple purchase |
| 9 | "Download the app" | Mobile apps |
| 10 | "Join the waitlist" | Pre-launch |

### Secondary CTAs (Low commitment -- learn more)

| # | CTA | Best For |
|---|-----|----------|
| 11 | "See how it works" | Product tour / demo video |
| 12 | "View pricing" | Pricing page link |
| 13 | "Read the case study" | Social proof content |
| 14 | "Explore features" | Feature page link |
| 15 | "Watch the demo" | Video demonstration |
| 16 | "Learn more" | Generic fallback (try to be more specific) |
| 17 | "See examples" | Portfolio or gallery |
| 18 | "Compare plans" | Multi-tier pricing |
| 19 | "Read the docs" | Developer documentation |
| 20 | "Browse templates" | Template-driven products |

### Engagement CTAs (Community, content, sharing)

| # | CTA | Best For |
|---|-----|----------|
| 21 | "Join the community" | Discord/Slack/forum |
| 22 | "Subscribe to updates" | Newsletter |
| 23 | "Follow us on {{SOCIAL_PLATFORM}}" | Social growth |
| 24 | "Share with your team" | Viral/referral |
| 25 | "Star us on GitHub" | Open source projects |

### Urgency / Risk-Reversal CTAs

| # | CTA | Best For |
|---|-----|----------|
| 26 | "Start free -- no credit card required" | Reducing signup friction |
| 27 | "Try it free for {{TRIAL_DAYS}} days" | Time-limited trial |
| 28 | "Get started in under {{SETUP_TIME}}" | Fast time-to-value |
| 29 | "Cancel anytime" | Reducing commitment anxiety |
| 30 | "Money-back guarantee" | Purchase risk reversal |
| 31 | "Limited spots available" | Scarcity (only if true) |
| 32 | "{{DISCOUNT_PERCENTAGE}}% off -- ends {{DEADLINE}}" | Time-limited offer |

### CTA Best Practices

- **Be specific:** "Start your free 14-day trial" beats "Sign up"
- **Use first person when appropriate:** "Start my free trial" can outperform "Start your free trial"
- **Match commitment level:** Do not ask for a credit card on a blog post CTA
- **One primary CTA per page:** Multiple equal CTAs create decision paralysis
- **Action verbs only:** Start, Get, Join, Try, Build, Create, Discover -- never "Submit" or "Click here"
- **Add a micro-commitment CTA:** When the main CTA feels too big, offer a smaller step: "See a 2-minute demo" before "Start free trial"

- Always include a free tier or trial to reduce risk
