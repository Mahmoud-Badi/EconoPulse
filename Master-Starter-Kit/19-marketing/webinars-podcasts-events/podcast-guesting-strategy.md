## Why Podcast Guesting Works

Podcast guesting is one of the highest-ROI marketing activities for founders and product builders. Here is why:

### Authority Building
- Appearing on podcasts positions you as an expert in {{TARGET_MARKET}}.
- Hosts introduce you with credibility ("Today's guest is the founder of {{PROJECT_NAME}}...").
- Listeners develop trust through long-form conversation in ways that short-form content cannot replicate.
- Podcast interviews become permanent portfolio pieces you can reference for years.

### Backlinks and SEO
- Most podcasts publish show notes with links to guests' websites.
- These backlinks from podcast domains improve {{PROJECT_NAME}}'s domain authority.
- Show notes often rank in search results for your name and brand.
- Some podcast sites have high domain authority (DA 40-80+), making their links valuable.

### New Audience Access
- You reach the host's established audience without building your own from scratch.
- Podcast listeners are highly engaged (average listen time is 80%+ of episode length).
- Niche podcasts deliver highly targeted, qualified audiences.
- Cross-promotion means the host actively promotes your episode to their subscribers.

### Long-Form Storytelling
- Podcast format (30-90 minutes) allows you to tell your story in depth.
- Listeners hear your voice, personality, and passion — creating real connection.
- Complex topics can be explored fully, unlike social media's character limits.
- Conversations feel authentic, not scripted or salesy.

### Compounding Returns
- Episodes stay online forever. People discover them months or years later.
- Each appearance builds on the last — you get better at interviewing.
- One good appearance leads to other hosts inviting you on their shows.

## Finding Relevant Podcasts

### Search Strategies

**Apple Podcasts and Spotify:**
- Search for keywords related to {{TARGET_MARKET}} and {{INDUSTRY}}.
- Look at "Related Shows" on podcasts you already know.
- Check the charts in relevant categories.
- Filter by recency — active podcasts post at least monthly.

**ListenNotes (listennotes.com):**
- The "Google of Podcasts" — search by keyword, filter by audience size.
- Use advanced search to filter by language, region, and episode frequency.
- Check estimated audience size (gives a rough range).
- Search for competitors by name to find podcasts that have covered similar topics.

**Podchaser (podchaser.com):**
- Search by topic, guest, or podcast.
- Look at guest lists for podcasts — who else has been on the show?
- Use "Creators" search to find hosts in your niche.
- Check ratings and review counts as a quality signal.

### Competitor Analysis

Research what podcasts your competitors or similar products have appeared on:

1. Google: `"{{COMPETITOR_NAME}}" podcast interview`
2. Google: `"{{COMPETITOR_NAME}}" site:listennotes.com`
3. Check competitors' press/media pages — many list podcast appearances.
4. Search YouTube for `"{{COMPETITOR_NAME}}" podcast` for video podcast appearances.
5. Look at competitor founders' LinkedIn activity — they often share podcast episodes.

### Community Recommendations

- Ask in {{COMMUNITY_PLATFORMS}}: "What podcasts do you listen to about {{TOPIC}}?"
- Check community forums and Slack channels for podcast recommendations.
- Survey your existing audience/customers: "What podcasts are you subscribed to?"
- Look at what podcasts community leaders and influencers in your space have appeared on.

### Size Targeting Strategy

Start small and work your way up. This is not a compromise — it is the optimal strategy.

**Tier 1: Small Niche Podcasts (under 1,000 listeners per episode)**
- Easiest to book — hosts are actively looking for guests.
- More engaged audience — listeners feel like they know the host.
- Excellent practice for refining your talking points.
- Often the highest conversion rate despite smaller audience.

**Tier 2: Mid-Size Podcasts (1,000-10,000 listeners per episode)**
- Require a pitch but are receptive to interesting guests.
- Good balance of reach and engagement.
- Often have more professional production.

**Tier 3: Large Podcasts (10,000+ listeners per episode)**
- Competitive to book — you need a strong pitch and proven track record.
- Reference your previous podcast appearances to show you are a good guest.
- May require PR or agent connections for the largest shows.
- Higher reach but potentially lower conversion rate (broader audience).

### Podcast Evaluation Criteria

Before pitching, evaluate each podcast against these criteria:

| Criteria | What to Check | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Audience overlap | Does the audience match {{TARGET_AUDIENCE}}? | Ensures listeners are potential users |
| Active posting | When was the last episode? | Dead podcasts waste your time |
| Episode format | Interview, solo, panel? | Verify they take guests |
| Episode length | Short (15 min) or long (90 min)? | Plan your content depth |
| Production quality | Audio quality, editing, show notes? | Reflects professionalism |
| Guest caliber | Who else has been a guest? | Indicates your fit |
| Engagement | Reviews, social shares, comments? | Signals active audience |

## Pitching Podcast Hosts

### Before You Pitch

Do your homework. Hosts can immediately tell when a pitch is generic.

1. **Listen to 2-3 full episodes:** Understand the format, tone, typical guest profile, and audience.
2. **Note specific details:** Reference a topic they covered, a question they asked, or a point they made.
3. **Verify they accept guests:** Some podcasts are solo shows or only invite guests they know.
4. **Find the right contact:** Check the podcast website for a guest submission form, or email the host directly.

### Pitch Email Template

**Subject line options:**
- "Podcast guest idea: {{TOPIC_ANGLE}}"
- "Story idea for {{PODCAST_NAME}}: {{HOOK}}"
- "Quick pitch — {{YOUR_NAME}} on {{TOPIC}}"

```
Hi {{HOST_NAME}},

I'm a regular listener of {{PODCAST_NAME}} — really enjoyed your
recent episode on {{SPECIFIC_EPISODE_TOPIC}}. Your point about
{{SPECIFIC_DETAIL}} resonated with me because {{BRIEF_REASON}}.

I'm {{YOUR_NAME}}, founder of {{PROJECT_NAME}}, which helps
{{TARGET_AUDIENCE}} {{ACHIEVE_OUTCOME}}. We've {{TRACTION_POINT}}
(e.g., "helped 5,000+ users" or "grown to $X ARR bootstrapped").

I think your audience would find value in a conversation about:

1. {{TOPIC_IDEA_1}}: {{ONE_LINE_DESCRIPTION}}
2. {{TOPIC_IDEA_2}}: {{ONE_LINE_DESCRIPTION}}
3. {{TOPIC_IDEA_3}}: {{ONE_LINE_DESCRIPTION}}

A bit about me:
- {{CREDIBILITY_POINT_1}}
- {{CREDIBILITY_POINT_2}}
- Previous podcasts: {{PAST_APPEARANCE_1}}, {{PAST_APPEARANCE_2}}

Happy to adjust the angle to fit what works best for your show.
Would any of these topics be interesting for your audience?

Best,
{{YOUR_NAME}}
{{YOUR_WEBSITE}}
{{YOUR_TWITTER/LINKEDIN}}
```

### Follow-Up Protocol

- **Follow up once** after 7 days if you haven't heard back.
- Keep the follow-up short: "Hi {{HOST_NAME}}, just bumping this in case it got buried. Would love to chat about {{TOPIC}} for {{PODCAST_NAME}}. No worries either way!"
- After two emails with no response, move on. Do not follow up again.
- Never pitch the same host more than once per quarter.

### What NOT to Do

| Mistake | Why It Hurts |
|---|---|
| Generic pitch with no podcast-specific details | Shows you didn't do research — instant delete |
| Overly promotional angle ("I want to promote my product") | Hosts want value for their audience, not your ad |
| Long, rambling pitch (more than 200 words) | Hosts are busy — get to the point |
| Pitching a topic unrelated to the podcast's focus | Proves you haven't listened to the show |
| CC'ing multiple hosts in one email | Impersonal and often visible to recipients |
| Following up more than once | Becomes spam and damages your reputation |
| Attaching large files or media kits unsolicited | Clogs inbox, looks corporate and impersonal |

## Preparation

### Talking Points Document

Prepare 3-5 key stories and points you want to make in every interview. These form your "core content" that you adapt per podcast.

**Story 1: Origin story of {{PROJECT_NAME}}**
- The problem you experienced personally
- The moment you decided to build a solution
- Key insight or aha moment
- What makes your approach unique

**Story 2: Customer success story**
- Specific customer (anonymized if needed)
- Their situation before {{PROJECT_NAME}}
- How they used it and what changed
- Quantifiable results if possible

**Story 3: Hard-won lesson**
- A mistake you made building {{PROJECT_NAME}}
- What it cost you (time, money, users)
- What you learned
- Actionable advice for listeners

**Story 4: Industry insight or contrarian take**
- A common belief in {{INDUSTRY}} that you disagree with
- Evidence or experience backing your position
- What you recommend instead
- Why this matters for listeners

**Story 5: Future vision**
- Where you see {{INDUSTRY}} heading
- How {{PROJECT_NAME}} fits into that future
- What listeners should be thinking about now
- Predictions they can act on

### Product Mentions: Weaving Naturally

- DO: Mention {{PROJECT_NAME}} when it's genuinely relevant to the topic.
- DO: Share how building {{PROJECT_NAME}} taught you something.
- DO: Offer the audience something valuable (free resource, trial, tool).
- DO NOT: Force a product pitch into every answer.
- DO NOT: Start sentences with "At {{PROJECT_NAME}}, we..."
- DO NOT: Turn every question into a sales opportunity.
- Rule of thumb: mention {{PROJECT_NAME}} by name 2-3 times maximum in a 45-minute episode.

**Links to share:**
- Website: {{WEBSITE_URL}}
- CTA URL: {{SPECIAL_LANDING_PAGE}} (create a /podcast or /welcome page)
- Social: {{TWITTER_URL}}, {{LINKEDIN_URL}}
- Headshot: hosted URL for high-resolution photo

### Technical Setup

Your audio quality reflects your professionalism. Invest in the basics:

| Item | Budget Option | Better Option |
|---|---|---|
| Microphone | Wired earbuds (not laptop mic) | USB mic: Samson Q2U ($70), Audio-Technica ATR2100x ($100) |
| Environment | Quiet room, close door | Room with soft furnishings (absorbs echo) |
| Internet | Stable WiFi | Wired ethernet connection |
| Backup | Phone hotspot ready | Backup recording via phone |
| Software | Whatever the host uses | Riverside or SquadCast for local recording |

**Pre-interview checklist:**
- [ ] Close all unnecessary applications (reduce CPU load)
- [ ] Turn off notifications (phone and computer)
- [ ] Test microphone and audio levels
- [ ] Have water within reach
- [ ] Have talking points document visible (not on screen if video)
- [ ] Confirm recording start with the host

## During the Interview

### How to Be a Great Podcast Guest

**Be genuinely helpful:**
- Answer questions thoroughly with actionable advice.
- Share specific examples, not abstract theories.
- Give the audience something they can do immediately after listening.
- Don't gatekeep — share your best insights freely.

**Tell stories:**
- Stories are memorable; facts are forgettable.
- Use the structure: situation → challenge → action → result.
- Include emotions and details that make stories relatable.
- Practice your key stories so they flow naturally.

**Be concise:**
- Answer the question that was asked (don't ramble).
- If you're going long, check: "I could go deeper on this — would that be helpful?"
- Short, punchy answers are often more impactful than lengthy ones.

**Engage with the host:**
- Ask the host follow-up questions ("Have you experienced that?").
- Reference things the host has said earlier in the conversation.
- Match the host's energy and conversational style.

**Mention your product naturally:**
- When the conversation naturally leads to your product: "That's actually why I built {{PROJECT_NAME}} — to solve exactly that problem."
- When sharing a customer story: "One of our users at {{PROJECT_NAME}}..."
- At the end when asked: "People can find {{PROJECT_NAME}} at {{WEBSITE}} — we have a free {{TRIAL/PLAN}} if you want to try it."

## After the Episode Airs

### Promotion Checklist

Within 24-48 hours of episode publication:

- [ ] Share on Twitter/X with host tagged and podcast handle mentioned
- [ ] Share on LinkedIn with a personal comment about the experience
- [ ] Share in relevant communities ({{COMMUNITY_PLATFORMS}})
- [ ] Email your list: "I was on {{PODCAST_NAME}} — here's what we discussed"
- [ ] Add to your website's press/media page
- [ ] Add to your LinkedIn profile's "Featured" section
- [ ] Pin to social profiles (temporarily)

### Thank the Host Publicly

- Tag the host and podcast in your social shares.
- Write something specific: "Had a great conversation with {{HOST_NAME}} about {{TOPIC}}. We went deep on {{SPECIFIC_POINT}}."
- Engage with any comments or shares from the podcast's audience.
- Leave a review for the podcast on Apple Podcasts (genuine, not transactional).

### Create Content from the Appearance

| Content Type | Source | Distribution |
|---|---|---|
| Quote graphics | Pull 3-5 quotable moments | Instagram, LinkedIn, Twitter |
| Blog post | Expand on one key topic discussed | Your blog |
| Short video clips | Extract 60-90 second highlights (if video) | TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts |
| Twitter thread | Key takeaways from the conversation | Twitter/X |
| Email content | Use insights from the interview | Email newsletter |
| Social proof | Screenshot of the episode listing | Website testimonials section |

### Build the Relationship

- Send a personal thank-you message to the host (email or DM).
- Share the episode again when it reaches milestones (100 plays, etc.).
- Refer other potential guests to the host if appropriate.
- Invite the host to your events, webinars, or community.
- Consider the host for future collaborations (joint webinars, content, partnerships).
- Keep the host updated on your journey — future milestones can warrant a return appearance.

## Measuring Impact

### Tracking Setup

Before your first podcast appearance, set up tracking:

1. **UTM-tagged CTA URL:** Create a unique landing page or UTM link for podcast appearances.
   - Example: `{{WEBSITE_URL}}/welcome?utm_source=podcast&utm_medium=audio&utm_campaign={{PODCAST_SLUG}}`
   - Or a simpler redirect: `{{WEBSITE_URL}}/podcast` → tracked redirect.

2. **Google Analytics:** Monitor traffic from podcast referral domains.

3. **"How did you hear about us?"** survey question on signup — include "Podcast" as an option.

### Metrics to Track

| Metric | How to Measure | What It Tells You |
|---|---|---|
| Referral traffic | Google Analytics referral report | Direct website visits from show notes |
| CTA link clicks | UTM tracking, link shortener stats | How many listeners took action |
| Social mentions | Brand mentions on social after episode | Reach and audience engagement |
| Backlinks | Ahrefs, Moz, or Google Search Console | SEO value from show notes |
| New signups | "How did you hear about us?" survey | Direct acquisition attribution |
| New social followers | Compare before/after episode air date | Audience growth |
| Podcast inquiries | Inbound requests from other podcasts | Compounding visibility |
| Email list growth | Segment signups by source | Lead generation |
