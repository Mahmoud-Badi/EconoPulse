# Launch Mistakes

> What kills most product launches — and how to avoid each failure mode.

---

## Mistake 1: Launching Without an Audience

**The mistake:** You finish building, click "deploy," tweet about it once to your 47 followers, and wait.

**Why it happens:** You spent all your time building and none building an audience. "Marketing starts after the product is ready" is the most dangerous assumption in startups.

**The fix:**
- Start building an audience 3-6 months before launch (minimum 30 days)
- Channels: email waitlist, Twitter/X building in public, blog posts, community presence
- Target: 500+ email subscribers or 2,000+ engaged social followers before launch
- Your launch audience IS your launch. No audience = no launch, just a deployment.

---

## Mistake 2: No Pre-Launch Buildup

**The mistake:** The first time anyone hears about your product is the day it launches. No teasing, no waitlist, no anticipation.

**Why it happens:** You're heads-down building and don't think about launch until it's time to ship. Launch feels like a single event, not a campaign.

**The fix:**
- 30 days out: Announce what you're building, start a waitlist
- 14 days out: Share sneak peeks, behind-the-scenes content
- 7 days out: Announce the launch date publicly, DM your network
- 3 days out: "Launching in 3 days" countdown, ask supporters to save the date
- Launch day: Execute the plan (see launch-day-checklist.template.md)

---

## Mistake 3: Launching Everywhere at Once

**The mistake:** Simultaneously posting on Product Hunt, Hacker News, Reddit, Twitter, LinkedIn, Indie Hackers, and 10 directories on the same day.

**Why it happens:** You want maximum exposure. More platforms = more visibility, right?

**The fix:**
- Stagger launches across platforms over 2-4 weeks
- Each platform has different audiences, norms, and optimal content formats
- Focus your energy: Product Hunt on Day 1, Hacker News on Day 3, Reddit on Day 7
- A great launch on one platform beats a mediocre presence on ten

---

## Mistake 4: Poor Timing

**The mistake:** Launching on a Friday afternoon, during a holiday weekend, or the same day as a major industry event.

**Why it happens:** You're excited and want to ship as soon as it's ready, regardless of calendar.

**The fix:**
- Best days to launch: Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday
- Best time for Product Hunt: 12:01 AM PT (Pacific Time)
- Avoid: Fridays, weekends, major holidays, big tech conference days, Apple keynote days
- Check Product Hunt's upcoming launches page to avoid competing with well-known products
- Weather check: Is anything dominating the news cycle today?

---

## Mistake 5: No Launch Day Plan

**The mistake:** You have no hour-by-hour plan for launch day. You wing it, forget to post on key platforms, and miss your window.

**Why it happens:** Launch day feels like a single action ("push the button") not a full-day operation.

**The fix:**
- Create an hour-by-hour launch day schedule (see `launch-day-checklist.template.md`)
- Pre-write all social posts, emails, and community messages
- Assign tasks if you have a team (who monitors comments, who handles support, who posts where)
- Have a "launch kit" ready: screenshots, GIFs, demo video, one-liner, key links

---

## Mistake 6: Not Having Social Proof Ready

**The mistake:** You launch with zero testimonials, no user count, no logos, no reviews. The product looks brand new and unproven.

**Why it happens:** You didn't run a beta, or you did but didn't collect feedback in a usable format.

**The fix:**
- Run a beta with 10-50 users for 2-4 weeks before public launch
- Collect testimonials during beta (ask specific questions: "What would you tell a friend about this?")
- Get permission to use real names and photos
- Even 3-5 genuine testimonials dramatically increase conversion
- Screenshot positive feedback from beta testers, DMs, or emails

---

## Mistake 7: Broken Onboarding on Launch Day

**The mistake:** New users sign up and immediately hit friction: confusing onboarding, broken features, empty states, or no guidance on what to do first.

**Why it happens:** You tested the product as a power user, not as a first-time visitor. What's obvious to you is invisible to them.

**The fix:**
- Test onboarding with 5 people who've never seen the product
- Watch them use it without helping — note every point of confusion
- Fix critical onboarding issues before launch
- Have a "getting started" guide, tooltip tour, or onboarding checklist ready
- Ensure the product has meaningful content/data on first login (not empty states)

---

## Mistake 8: Not Monitoring During Launch

**The mistake:** You launch and then step away from the computer. You miss critical feedback, bug reports, server issues, and engagement opportunities.

**Why it happens:** Launch day is exhausting. You think the hard part is over once you ship.

**The fix:**
- Block your entire launch day — no meetings, no other work
- Monitor: server health, error logs, support inbox, Product Hunt comments, social mentions
- Respond to every comment and question within 30 minutes
- Have a fast-fix process ready — you WILL find bugs on launch day
- Set up alerts for downtime, error rate spikes, and social mentions

---

## Mistake 9: Ignoring Feedback Loops

**The mistake:** You get tons of feedback on launch day and do nothing with it. No follow-up, no acknowledgment, no iteration.

**Why it happens:** You're overwhelmed with the volume of feedback and don't have a system to capture and act on it.

**The fix:**
- Set up a feedback capture system before launch (spreadsheet, Notion board, or tool like Canny)
- Categorize feedback: bugs (fix now), quick wins (fix this week), feature requests (evaluate), praise (share as social proof)
- Publicly acknowledge feedback: "Great suggestion — added to our roadmap"
- Ship 2-3 quick improvements within the first week based on feedback

---

## Mistake 10: No Post-Launch Plan

**The mistake:** Launch day is the climax. After that? Nothing. No follow-up content, no retention plan, no momentum strategy.

**Why it happens:** You planned for launch as an event, not as the start of a marketing campaign. All energy went into the big day.

**The fix:**
- Plan 30 days of post-launch activities before launch day
- Week 1: Follow up with early users, collect testimonials, fix bugs
- Week 2: Publish "lessons learned" content, share milestones
- Week 3: Start directory submissions, begin content marketing cadence
- Week 4: Analyze metrics, plan next growth experiments
- See `post-launch-playbook.template.md` for full plan

---

## Mistake 11: Celebrating Too Early

**The mistake:** You get 500 upvotes on Product Hunt, celebrate for a week, and then realize none of those people actually signed up or stayed.

**Why it happens:** Vanity metrics feel great. Upvotes, likes, and congratulatory DMs are dopamine hits. Actual activation and retention numbers are less exciting.

**The fix:**
- Track real metrics, not vanity metrics: signups → activated users → retained users → paying customers
- The launch is the beginning, not the end
- A "successful" launch with 1,000 signups and 10% activation means only 100 real users
- Focus on activation rate and 7-day retention as your true launch success metrics

---

## Mistake 12: Not Following Up with Early Users

**The mistake:** People sign up on launch day and never hear from you again until they churn.

**Why it happens:** You don't have email sequences set up. You're too busy fixing bugs to think about user engagement.

**The fix:**
- Have a welcome email sequence ready before launch (see `email-marketing/welcome-sequence.template.md`)
- Personally email your first 50-100 users (doesn't scale, but the insights are invaluable)
- Ask: "What brought you here? What are you hoping to accomplish?"
- Schedule 1-on-1 calls with power users in the first two weeks

## Launch Timeline Template

| Timeframe | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| T-60 days | Start building audience, create waitlist |
| T-30 days | Begin beta program, collect early feedback |
| T-14 days | Announce launch date, prepare launch assets |
| T-7 days | Brief supporters, finalize launch checklist |
| T-3 days | Final testing, pre-write all launch day content |
| T-1 day | Send "launching tomorrow" email to waitlist |
| Launch day | Execute hour-by-hour plan, engage everywhere |
| T+1 day | Follow up, share results, thank supporters |
| T+7 days | First improvements shipped, collect testimonials |
| T+14 days | Start content marketing cadence |
| T+30 days | Full post-launch review, plan next growth phase |

## The "Are You Ready to Launch?" Checklist

- [ ] 500+ people know this product exists (waitlist, followers, beta users)
- [ ] 3+ testimonials or social proof elements ready
- [ ] Onboarding tested with 5+ first-time users
- [ ] Welcome email sequence configured
- [ ] Hour-by-hour launch day plan written
- [ ] All launch assets prepared (screenshots, copy, demo)
- [ ] Monitoring and alerting set up
- [ ] Post-launch 30-day plan written
- [ ] Support system ready (FAQ, help docs, response templates)
- [ ] Feedback capture system in place
