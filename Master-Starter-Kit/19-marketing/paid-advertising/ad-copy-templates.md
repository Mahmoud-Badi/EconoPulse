## Headlines by Awareness Stage

Different people need different messages depending on how aware they are of their problem and your solution. Use this framework to match ad copy to audience awareness level.

### Stage 1: Unaware (Does not know they have a problem)

The audience does not realize they have an issue. Your ad must make them feel the problem.

**Headlines:**
```
"Are You Making These {{TASK}} Mistakes?"
"{{PERCENTAGE}}% of {{TARGET_AUDIENCE}} Get This Wrong"
"The Hidden Cost of Manual {{TASK}}"
"What's Really Slowing Your {{TEAM/BUSINESS}} Down?"
"You're Spending {{HOURS_WASTED}} Hours on This Every Week"
```

**Ad approach:** Lead with education. Blog posts, statistics, industry insights. Do NOT pitch your product yet.

### Stage 2: Problem-Aware (Knows they have a problem, not sure what to do)

The audience feels the pain but has not started looking for solutions.

**Headlines:**
```
"Tired of {{PAIN_POINT}}? You're Not Alone"
"The {{TASK}} Problem Every {{TARGET_AUDIENCE_ROLE}} Faces"
"Why {{TASK}} Takes So Long (And How to Fix It)"
"{{PAIN_POINT}} Is Costing You ${{COST}} per Month"
"Ready to Solve {{PAIN_POINT}} Once and For All?"
```

**Ad approach:** Validate the pain. Show you understand it. Introduce the idea that a solution exists.

### Stage 3: Solution-Aware (Knows solutions exist, evaluating options)

The audience is actively looking at different approaches or tools.

**Headlines:**
```
"The Best {{PRODUCT_CATEGORY}} for {{TARGET_AUDIENCE}}"
"{{PRODUCT_CATEGORY}} Comparison: What Actually Works"
"How {{TARGET_AUDIENCE}} Should Choose a {{PRODUCT_CATEGORY}}"
"Not All {{PRODUCT_CATEGORY}} Tools Are Created Equal"
"What to Look for in a {{PRODUCT_CATEGORY}} Tool"
```

**Ad approach:** Position your category. Educate on what a good solution looks like (criteria your product meets).

### Stage 4: Product-Aware (Knows about your product, needs to be convinced)

The audience knows {{PROJECT_NAME}} exists but has not converted yet.

**Headlines:**
```
"Why {{USER_COUNT}}+ Teams Chose {{PROJECT_NAME}}"
"{{PROJECT_NAME}}: {{KEY_BENEFIT}} Made Simple"
"See Why {{TARGET_AUDIENCE}} Love {{PROJECT_NAME}}"
"{{PROJECT_NAME}} vs {{COMPETITOR}}: The Real Difference"
"What Makes {{PROJECT_NAME}} Different"
```

**Ad approach:** Social proof, testimonials, case studies, detailed feature benefits. Overcome specific objections.

### Stage 5: Most Aware (Ready to buy, needs a final push)

The audience is convinced but needs a trigger to act.

**Headlines:**
```
"Start Your Free Trial of {{PROJECT_NAME}} Today"
"{{PROJECT_NAME}}: Free {{TRIAL_LENGTH}}-Day Trial"
"Your {{PROJECT_NAME}} Account Is Ready"
"Limited Time: {{OFFER_DETAILS}}"
"Join {{USER_COUNT}}+ Teams Using {{PROJECT_NAME}}"
```

**Ad approach:** Direct CTA. Remove friction. Urgency if appropriate. Make the next step obvious and easy.

## Ad Copy Testing Prioritization

### What to Test First (Highest Impact)

1. **Headline angle** - Problem vs benefit vs social proof vs competitive
2. **CTA** - "Start free trial" vs "See demo" vs "Learn more"
3. **Copy length** - Short (1-2 lines) vs medium (3-5 lines) vs long (story)
4. **Social proof type** - Numbers vs testimonials vs logos vs ratings
5. **Visual approach** - Product screenshot vs lifestyle vs illustration vs video

### Testing Template

| Test # | Platform | Variable | Variant A | Variant B | Spend | Winner | CPA Diff |
|--------|----------|----------|-----------|-----------|-------|--------|----------|
| 1 | | Headline angle | Problem-focused | Benefit-focused | $____ | | |
| 2 | | CTA | "Start free trial" | "See demo" | $____ | | |
| 3 | | Copy length | Short (2 lines) | Medium (5 lines) | $____ | | |
| 4 | | Social proof | User count | Testimonial quote | $____ | | |
| 5 | | Visual | Screenshot | Video | $____ | | |

Run each test for at least 7 days or 100 clicks per variant before declaring a winner.
