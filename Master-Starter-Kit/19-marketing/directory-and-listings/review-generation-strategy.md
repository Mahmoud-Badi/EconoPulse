## Why Reviews Matter

Reviews are the most powerful form of social proof for software products. They influence purchasing decisions, affect directory rankings, improve SEO, and build trust.

|------|--------|
| **93%** of B2B buyers say online reviews influence their purchasing decision | Reviews are not optional |

| **4.0-4.5 stars** is the ideal range (perfect 5.0 seems suspicious) | Authenticity matters |
| Listings with reviews get **270% more clicks** than those without | Direct traffic impact |
| Each additional review increases conversion by approximately **1%** | Cumulative impact |

### What Reviews Do For {{PROJECT_NAME}}

1. **Trust signals**: Strangers vouch for your product to other strangers
2. **Directory ranking**: More and better reviews = higher placement in directory search results
3. **SEO benefit**: Review content adds keyword-rich, unique text associated with your brand
4. **Conversion lift**: Social proof on your website (quoted reviews) increases signup rates
5. **Product feedback**: Genuine reviews reveal what customers love and what needs improvement
6. **Competitive advantage**: If competitors have 5 reviews and you have 50, you win the comparison

## Ethical Review Collection: Non-Negotiable Rules

Before anything else, understand the boundaries. Violating these rules can get your listings removed, your brand damaged, and in some cases, result in legal consequences.

### NEVER Do These Things

| Practice | Why It's Wrong | Consequence |
|----------|---------------|-------------|
| **Write fake reviews** (yourself or via a service) | Fraud, deceptive | Listing removal, brand damage, potential legal action |
| **Pay for positive reviews** | Incentivized reviews violate every platform's ToS | Listing removal, reviews deleted |
| **Incentivize specific ratings** ("leave a 5-star review for a discount") | Biases the review and violates ToS | Reviews removed, account flagged |
| **Review gate** (only show the review form to happy users) | Deceptive practice | Some platforms explicitly prohibit this |
| **Threaten or pressure users** for reviews | Unethical, will backfire | Negative reviews, PR disaster |
| **Ask employees to write reviews** pretending to be customers | Fake reviews, even if opinions are honest | Flagged accounts, reviews removed |

### ALWAYS Do These Things

| Practice | Why It's Right |
|----------|---------------|
| Ask all users for honest reviews (not just happy ones) | Authentic feedback improves your product and builds genuine trust |
| Make it easy to leave a review (direct link, clear instructions) | Reduces friction; more responses |
| Accept negative reviews gracefully | Shows maturity; respond constructively |
| Thank reviewers publicly | Encourages future reviews |
| Wait for value delivery before asking | Only people who have experienced your product can give meaningful reviews |

### What IS Acceptable

- Asking customers to leave an honest review (without specifying a rating)
- Providing a direct link to make the review process easier
- G2's gift card program (G2-funded, not vendor-funded, platform-sanctioned)
- Timing your ask after a positive experience (as long as you don't exclude negative experiences)
- Following up once if someone did not respond to your first ask
- Including a review request in your email signature or product interface

## When to Ask for Reviews

Timing is everything. Ask too early and the user has not experienced value. Ask at the wrong moment and you interrupt their workflow. Ask at the right moment and they are happy to share.

### Optimal Timing Triggers

| Trigger | Why It Works | How to Implement |
|---------|-------------|-----------------|
| **After value delivery** | User just experienced the core benefit | Track when users complete key workflows |
| **After positive support interaction** | User feels helped and grateful | Support team flags resolved tickets for follow-up |
| **After milestone completion** | User achieved something meaningful | Track milestones: first project, 100th task, etc. |
| **At NPS 9-10 response** | User already indicated high satisfaction | Automated follow-up for NPS promoters |
| **After X days of active use** | User has enough experience to review | 14-30 days of active use is ideal |
| **After successful onboarding** | User has completed setup and seen value | Track onboarding completion |
| **After renewal or upgrade** | User chose to continue/expand | Indicates satisfaction |

### When NOT to Ask

- During onboarding (too early, no experience yet)
- When the user just submitted a support ticket (bad timing)
- When the user has not logged in for 2+ weeks (disengaged)
- More than once per quarter (avoid review fatigue)
- Immediately after a bug or outage (terrible timing)

## How to Ask for Reviews

### Channel 1: Email Request

The most effective channel for review solicitation. Personal emails from founders or customer success managers convert best.

#### Email Template 1: Casual/Founder (Best for Early-Stage)

```
Subject: Got 2 minutes, {{CUSTOMER_FIRST_NAME}}?

Hey {{CUSTOMER_FIRST_NAME}},

It's {{FOUNDER_NAME}} from {{PROJECT_NAME}}. I noticed you've been using
{{FEATURE_THEY_USE_MOST}} pretty regularly — glad to see it's been useful!

Quick favor: would you mind leaving a short review on {{PLATFORM_NAME}}? It
takes about 2 minutes and it really helps other {{TARGET_AUDIENCE}} find us.

Here's the direct link: {{REVIEW_LINK}}

Totally honest review — we value all feedback, positive or constructive.
No pressure either way.

Cheers,
{{FOUNDER_NAME}}

P.S. If something isn't working well, I'd rather hear about it directly so
we can fix it. Just reply to this email.
```

**When to use**: Early-stage products (under 100 customers), founder-led relationships

### Channel 2: In-App Review Prompt

An in-app prompt catches users at the moment of engagement. Done well, it converts at 3-5x the rate of email. Done poorly, it annoys users.

#### UX Best Practices for In-App Prompts

**Timing rules:**
- Do NOT show on first visit (user has no basis for a review)
- Show after completing a key workflow or achieving a milestone
- Show after minimum 14 days of active use
- Show maximum once per 90 days
- Show only to users who have been active in the last 7 days

**Design rules:**
- Use a subtle banner or modal (not a blocking popup)
- Make it easy to dismiss ("Not now" and "Don't ask again" options)
- Include the direct review link
- Keep the message to 1-2 sentences
- Match your product's UI style

**Example prompt copy:**
```
Enjoying {{PROJECT_NAME}}?
Share your experience on {{PLATFORM_NAME}} — it takes 2 minutes and helps
other {{TARGET_AUDIENCE}} discover us.

[Leave a Review]  [Not Now]  [Don't Ask Again]
```

**Conditional logic for the prompt:**
```
IF user.active_days >= 14
AND user.key_action_completed == true
AND user.last_review_prompt > 90_days_ago
AND user.review_dismissed_permanently != true
AND user.active_in_last_7_days == true
THEN show_review_prompt()
```

### Channel 3: Personal Message (Highest Conversion)

For your first 10-20 reviews, nothing beats a personal, 1-on-1 message.

- **LinkedIn DM**: If you have a connection with the customer
- **Slack/Discord DM**: If you have a community where customers are active
- **Text message**: Only if you have a personal relationship with the customer
- **Video message** (Loom): Record a brief personal video asking for a review

**Personal message template:**
```
Hey {{CUSTOMER_FIRST_NAME}}! Quick question — would you be up for leaving
a 2-minute review for {{PROJECT_NAME}} on {{PLATFORM_NAME}}? You've been
one of our most engaged users and your perspective would mean a lot.
Here's the link: {{REVIEW_LINK}}. No worries if not!
```

### Channel 4: Automated Trigger (Scale)

Once you have a review collection process that works, automate it:

1. **After NPS survey (score 9-10)**: Automatically send review request email
2. **After support ticket resolution (positive CSAT)**: Trigger review request
3. **After milestone completion**: Trigger in-app prompt
4. **After X days of use**: Trigger email sequence

**Important**: Even automated requests should feel personal. Use the customer's name, reference their specific usage, and come from a real person's email address.

## Handling Negative Reviews

Negative reviews will happen. How you respond to them matters more than the review itself. Buyers often check negative reviews first to see how the company handles criticism.

### Response Framework for Negative Reviews

**Step 1: Pause (don't respond emotionally)**
Wait at least 1 hour before responding. Never respond defensively.

**Step 2: Acknowledge the experience**
Validate the reviewer's frustration. They took time to share feedback.

**Step 3: Apologize (for their experience, not necessarily fault)**
"I'm sorry you had this experience" is different from "I'm sorry we made a mistake."

**Step 4: Explain (briefly, if there is context)**
If there is a reason (bug that has been fixed, misunderstanding), briefly explain. Do not make excuses.

**Step 5: Offer resolution**
Provide a specific next step and a direct contact for follow-up.

**Step 6: Follow up privately**
Reach out directly to resolve the issue. If resolved, ask if they would consider updating their review (never demand this).

### Response Templates

#### Template: Bug/Technical Issue
```
Thank you for this feedback, {{REVIEWER_NAME}}. I'm sorry you experienced
issues with {{SPECIFIC_ISSUE}}. This was caused by {{BRIEF_EXPLANATION}},
and our team has since resolved it as of {{DATE/VERSION}}.

I'd love to personally ensure things are working well for you now. Please
reach out to me at {{PERSONAL_EMAIL}} and I'll make sure you're taken care of.
```

#### Template: Unmet Expectations
```
Thank you for sharing your experience, {{REVIEWER_NAME}}. I understand
{{PROJECT_NAME}} didn't meet your expectations for {{USE_CASE}}, and I
appreciate your candor.

We're actively working on improvements in {{RELATED_AREA}}, and your feedback
is informing our roadmap. If you're open to it, I'd love to discuss your needs
in more detail — please reach out at {{PERSONAL_EMAIL}}.
```

#### Template: Competitive Comparison
```
Thank you for the review, {{REVIEWER_NAME}}. You raise fair points about
{{FEATURE_COMPARISON}}. We approach {{AREA}} differently than {{COMPETITOR}}
because {{DESIGN_REASON}}, but I understand that may not be the right fit
for every team.

We're continuously improving based on feedback like yours. If you'd like to
revisit {{PROJECT_NAME}} after our upcoming {{IMPROVEMENT}}, I'd be happy to
set you up — just email {{PERSONAL_EMAIL}}.
```

## Review Velocity: Patterns and Pacing

Review platforms and potential buyers can detect suspicious review patterns. Natural review collection looks organic; manufactured reviews look suspicious.

### What Looks Natural

| Pattern | Natural | Suspicious |
|---------|---------|------------|
| **Timing** | Spread over weeks/months | 20 reviews in one day |
| **Reviewer profiles** | Varied industries, roles, company sizes | All from same company or similar profiles |
| **Review length** | Varies (short, medium, long) | All same length or template-based |
| **Ratings** | Mix of 3, 4, and 5 stars | All 5 stars |
| **Language** | Diverse, personal voice | Similar phrasing, marketing language |

### Recommended Review Velocity

| Stage | Target Reviews Per Month | Strategy |
|-------|------------------------|----------|
| Month 1-3 | 3-5 per month | Personal outreach to best customers |
| Month 4-6 | 5-10 per month | Mix of personal outreach and automated triggers |
| Month 7-12 | 10-15 per month | Automated + in-app prompts + email campaigns |
| Year 2+ | 15-25+ per month | Fully automated with periodic manual campaigns |

### Avoiding Suspicious Patterns
- Never send a mass email to all customers at once asking for reviews
- Stagger review requests over weeks
- Ask customers across different platforms (not all on G2 in one week)
- Accept that some months will have more reviews than others (natural variance)
- A mix of ratings (3-5 stars) is actually better than all 5-star reviews

## Leveraging Reviews: From Reviews to Marketing Assets

Reviews are not just for directory rankings. They are a gold mine of marketing content.

### Quoting Reviews in Marketing

- **Website**: Add a "What our customers say" section with review quotes
- **Landing pages**: Place relevant review quotes near your CTA
- **Email campaigns**: Include a customer quote in onboarding emails
- **Social media**: Share positive reviews as social posts
- **Sales decks**: Include review quotes in presentation materials
- **Ads**: Use review quotes in ad copy (with permission and attribution)

### Social Proof Section Best Practices

When displaying reviews on your website:
- Include the reviewer's name, role, and company (with permission)
- Show the star rating from the platform (with platform logo)
- Link to the full review on the platform (increases platform profile traffic)
- Rotate featured reviews periodically
- Include reviews that mention specific outcomes or metrics

### Case Study Seeds

Reviews that mention specific results or detailed experiences are seeds for case studies:
- If a reviewer mentions "{{PROJECT_NAME}} saved us 10 hours per week," follow up for a full case study
- Reviews that describe specific use cases can be expanded into customer stories
- Ask permission to feature the reviewer in a more detailed format

### Review-Based Content Ideas

| Review Theme | Content to Create |
|-------------|-------------------|
| "Easy to set up" | Blog post: "How to get started with {{PROJECT_NAME}} in 5 minutes" |
| "Great customer support" | Case study focusing on support experience |
| "Replaced {{COMPETITOR}}" | Comparison page: "{{PROJECT_NAME}} vs {{COMPETITOR}}" |
| "Saved us X hours/dollars" | ROI calculator or value-based landing page |
| "Missing feature X" | Roadmap blog post addressing planned features |

## App Store Review Management

<!-- IF {{PRODUCT_TYPE}} == "mobile-app" -->

### iOS App Store Review Strategy

**In-app review prompt (Apple's API):**
- Use Apple's `SKStoreReviewController` API
- Apple limits prompt display to 3 times per 365-day period
- Time it after a positive experience (completed task, reached goal)
- You have no control over when Apple actually shows the prompt (they may suppress it)

**Responding to App Store reviews:**
- Go to App Store Connect > My Apps > {{PROJECT_NAME}} > Ratings and Reviews
- Respond within 24 hours for negative reviews
- Keep responses concise (visible to all potential users)
- Track common complaints to inform product development

**Star rating optimization:**
- Current average: {{CURRENT_RATING}} (target: 4.5+)
- Ask happy users for reviews at the right moment
- Fix bugs that cause 1-star reviews
- Respond to negative reviews and resolve issues
- Update your app regularly (stale apps lose rating over time)

### Google Play Store Review Strategy

**In-app review prompt (Google's API):**
- Use Google's In-App Review API
- Google allows the prompt once every few months per user (exact timing is Google-controlled)
- Show after the user has been using the app for at least several minutes
- Cannot customize the prompt UI (system-controlled)

**Responding to Play Store reviews:**
- Go to Google Play Console > User feedback > Reviews
- Respond to all reviews (Google tracks your response rate)
- Google notifies users when you respond (opportunity to re-engage)
- Updated reviews (after resolution) are common on Play Store

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It's Bad | What to Do Instead |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| Asking for reviews too early | User has no experience to review | Wait for value delivery (14+ days active) |
| Asking only happy users | Creates bias, looks suspicious | Ask all engaged users for honest feedback |
| Not responding to reviews | Looks like you don't care | Respond to every review within 48 hours |
| Ignoring negative reviews | Misses improvement opportunities | Respond, resolve, and learn from every negative review |
| Reviewing your own product | Fraud, will get caught | Never do this under any circumstances |
| Stopping after 10 reviews | Competitors will surpass you | Review collection is an ongoing activity, not a one-time task |
| Same response to every review | Looks automated and impersonal | Personalize every response |
| Arguing with negative reviews | Makes you look defensive and petty | Acknowledge, apologize, offer resolution |
