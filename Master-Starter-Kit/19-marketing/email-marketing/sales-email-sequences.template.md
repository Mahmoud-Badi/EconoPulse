## Sequence Overview

This document contains ready-to-customize email sequences for every stage of the sales funnel. Each sequence includes subject lines, body copy, timing, and A/B testing suggestions.

| Sequence | Purpose | Emails | Total Duration |
|----------|---------|--------|---------------|
| Cold Outreach | Reach prospects who don't know you | 5 emails | 18-22 days |
| Warm Lead Nurture | Convert demo requesters / trial signups | 5 emails | 14 days |
| Re-Engagement | Win back inactive subscribers | 4 emails | 12 days |
| Post-Purchase Onboarding | Activate new customers | 5 emails | 14 days |
| Referral Request | Ask happy customers for referrals | 3 emails | 10 days |
| Annual Renewal / Upgrade | Retain and expand existing customers | 4 emails | 30 days |

## Sequence 1: Cold Outreach (5 Emails)

**When to use:** Reaching out to prospects who have never heard of {{PROJECT_NAME}}.
**Pre-requisite:** Research EACH prospect before emailing. Generic cold emails have a 1% response rate. Personalized ones get 5-15%.

### Email 1: Problem-Aware Introduction
**Send:** Day 1
**Goal:** Get a reply. NOT a sale. Just start a conversation.

**Subject Line Options:**
- A: "Quick question about {{THEIR_COMPANY}}'s {{PROBLEM_AREA}}"
- B: "{{FIRST_NAME}}, noticed something about {{THEIR_COMPANY}}"
- C: "Idea for {{THEIR_COMPANY}}'s {{SPECIFIC_CHALLENGE}}"

**Body:**
```
Hi {{FIRST_NAME}},

I noticed {{SPECIFIC_OBSERVATION_ABOUT_THEIR_COMPANY}}.

{{PERSONALIZED_CONTEXT}} -- for example:
- "I saw your team posted about needing to {{GOAL}} on LinkedIn"
- "I noticed {{THEIR_COMPANY}} recently launched {{PRODUCT/FEATURE}}"
- "Your job posting for a {{ROLE}} mentions {{RELEVANT_SKILL}}"

At {{PROJECT_NAME}}, we help {{TARGET_AUDIENCE}} {{ACHIEVE_OUTCOME}}
-- specifically when they're dealing with {{PROBLEM_THEY_LIKELY_HAVE}}.

I'm curious: is {{PROBLEM}} something your team is actively
working on?

No pitch, just genuinely curious.

{{YOUR_NAME}}
Founder, {{PROJECT_NAME}}
```

**Key principles:**
- Reference something SPECIFIC about their company (not a template fill-in-the-blank)
- Lead with their problem, not your solution
- End with a question (easy to reply to)
- Keep it under 100 words
- No attachments, no links, no images in the first email

---

### Email 2: Value Add Follow-Up
**Send:** Day 4 (3 days after Email 1)
**Goal:** Provide value without asking for anything.

**Subject Line Options:**
- A: "Re: Quick question about {{THEIR_COMPANY}}'s {{PROBLEM_AREA}}" (reply thread)
- B: "Thought this might be useful for {{THEIR_COMPANY}}"
- C: "{{RESOURCE_TITLE}} -- relevant to your team"

**Body:**
```
Hi {{FIRST_NAME}},

Following up on my note from earlier this week.

I wanted to share something that might be useful regardless of
whether we ever talk:

{{VALUE_ADD}} -- for example:
- "We just published a guide on {{TOPIC}}: {{LINK}}"
- "Here's a framework we use to {{SOLVE_PROBLEM}}: {{BRIEF_DESCRIPTION}}"
- "I came across this data about {{INDUSTRY}}: {{INSIGHT}}"

Companies like {{SIMILAR_COMPANY}} and {{SIMILAR_COMPANY_2}} have
found this approach helpful for {{SPECIFIC_OUTCOME}}.

Worth a look if {{CONDITION_THAT_APPLIES_TO_THEM}}.

{{YOUR_NAME}}
```

**Key principles:**
- Provide genuine value (a resource, insight, or data they can use NOW)
- Do not pitch your product directly
- Mention similar companies to build credibility
- Keep it brief

---

### Email 3: Social Proof
**Send:** Day 8 (4 days after Email 2)
**Goal:** Show that companies like theirs get results.

**Subject Line Options:**
- A: "How {{SIMILAR_COMPANY}} solved {{PROBLEM}}"
- B: "{{METRIC_IMPROVEMENT}} -- is this interesting?"
- C: "Case study: {{SIMILAR_COMPANY}} + {{PROBLEM_DOMAIN}}"

**Body:**
```
Hi {{FIRST_NAME}},

Quick story that might resonate:

{{SIMILAR_COMPANY}} was dealing with {{SAME_PROBLEM_PROSPECT_HAS}}.

They tried {{PREVIOUS_APPROACH}} but kept running into
{{LIMITATION}}.

After implementing {{PROJECT_NAME}}, they:

- {{RESULT_1}} (e.g., "Reduced {{METRIC}} by {{PERCENTAGE}}")
- {{RESULT_2}} (e.g., "Saved {{HOURS}} hours per week")
- {{RESULT_3}} (e.g., "Increased {{METRIC}} by {{NUMBER}}")

Here's the full case study if you're interested: {{CASE_STUDY_URL}}

{{THEIR_COMPANY}} seems like it could benefit from a similar
approach. Would it make sense to explore this in a quick call?

{{YOUR_NAME}}
```

**Key principles:**
- Use a company similar to theirs in size, industry, or problem
- Include specific, measurable results
- The case study link is optional (some prefer no links in cold emails for deliverability)
- The ask is soft: "Would it make sense..." not "Let's schedule..."

---

### Email 4: Direct Ask
**Send:** Day 13 (5 days after Email 3)
**Goal:** Request a specific meeting or demo.

**Subject Line Options:**
- A: "15 minutes this week?"
- B: "{{FIRST_NAME}} -- worth a quick conversation?"
- C: "Can I show you something?"

**Body:**
```
Hi {{FIRST_NAME}},

I've reached out a couple of times about {{PROBLEM_AREA}} at
{{THEIR_COMPANY}}.

I'll be direct: I think {{PROJECT_NAME}} could help your team
{{SPECIFIC_OUTCOME}} based on what I know about your
{{COMPANY/SITUATION}}.

Would you be open to a 15-minute call to see if there's a fit?

I'm available {{DAY_1}} at {{TIME_1}} or {{DAY_2}} at {{TIME_2}},
but happy to work around your schedule.

If now isn't the right time, no worries at all -- just let me know
and I'll follow up later.

{{YOUR_NAME}}

P.S. Here's a 2-minute demo of what {{PROJECT_NAME}} does in
case that's easier than a call: {{DEMO_VIDEO_URL}}
```

**Key principles:**
- Be direct about what you want (a meeting)
- Offer specific times (reduces friction vs. "let me know when you're free")
- Give an out ("if now isn't the right time") -- this actually increases response rates
- The P.S. video is a low-commitment alternative

---

### Email 5: Breakup Email
**Send:** Day 20 (7 days after Email 4)
**Goal:** Final touch. Elicit a response or gracefully close.

**Subject Line Options:**
- A: "Should I close your file?"
- B: "One last thing, {{FIRST_NAME}}"
- C: "Not the right time?"

**Body:**
```
Hi {{FIRST_NAME}},

I've reached out a few times and haven't heard back -- which is
totally fine. I know you're busy.

I don't want to be that person who keeps emailing, so this will
be my last note.

If {{PROBLEM_DOMAIN}} becomes a priority at {{THEIR_COMPANY}} down
the road, I'm here. You can always reach me at {{YOUR_EMAIL}} or
check out {{PROJECT_NAME}} at {{PROJECT_URL}}.

Wishing you and the {{THEIR_COMPANY}} team all the best.

{{YOUR_NAME}}
```

**Key principles:**
- Short, respectful, no guilt-tripping
- Breakup emails paradoxically have the highest response rate in cold sequences (15-25%)
- Leave the door open without being pushy
- This is your last email -- respect that commitment. If they do not reply, they go back to the marketing funnel, not another sales sequence.

## Sequence 2: Warm Lead Nurture (5 Emails)

**When to use:** For leads who requested a demo, started a free trial, or showed buying intent.
**Timing note:** These people already know about you. Move faster and be more direct.

### Email 1: Welcome + Immediate Value
**Send:** Immediately upon trial signup or demo request
**Subject:** "Welcome to {{PROJECT_NAME}} -- let's get you started"

```

Welcome to {{PROJECT_NAME}}! I'm excited to have you.

1. {{QUICK_WIN_STEP_1}} (takes 2 minutes)
2. {{QUICK_WIN_STEP_2}} (takes 3 minutes)
3. {{QUICK_WIN_STEP_3}} (takes 5 minutes)

Once you complete these, you'll have {{FIRST_RESULT}}.

Here's a quick walkthrough if you prefer video: {{VIDEO_URL}}

monitor these and respond within {{RESPONSE_TIME}}.

{{YOUR_NAME}}
```

## Sequence 3: Re-Engagement (4 Emails)

**When to use:** Subscribers who have not opened or clicked in 60-90 days.
**Goal:** Wake them up or clean them off your list.

### Email 1: The "Still There?" Email
**Send:** Day 1
**Subject:** "{{FIRST_NAME}}, are we still a thing?"

```
Hi {{FIRST_NAME}},

I noticed you haven't opened my emails in a while.

No judgment -- inboxes are overwhelming.

But I want to make sure I'm sending you stuff you actually want
to read.

Quick question: Are you still interested in {{TOPIC}}?

- **Yes, keep sending!** -- Just click here: {{RECONFIRM_LINK}}
- **Nah, I'm good** -- Click here and I'll stop emailing:
  {{UNSUBSCRIBE_LINK}}

If I don't hear from you, I'll clean up your subscription in
a couple of weeks. No hard feelings either way.

{{YOUR_NAME}}
```

### Email 2: Value Reminder
**Send:** Day 4
**Subject:** "In case you missed it: {{BEST_RECENT_CONTENT}}"

```
Hi {{FIRST_NAME}},

Since we last connected, here are the 3 things our community
found most valuable:

1. {{RESOURCE_1_TITLE}} -- {{BRIEF_DESCRIPTION}} ({{LINK}})
2. {{RESOURCE_2_TITLE}} -- {{BRIEF_DESCRIPTION}} ({{LINK}})
3. {{RESOURCE_3_TITLE}} -- {{BRIEF_DESCRIPTION}} ({{LINK}})

Also, {{PROJECT_NAME}} has some exciting updates:
- {{UPDATE_1}}
- {{UPDATE_2}}

Worth checking out if {{PROBLEM_DOMAIN}} is still relevant to you.

{{YOUR_NAME}}
```

### Email 3: Special Offer
**Send:** Day 8
**Subject:** "A gift for you, {{FIRST_NAME}}"

```
Hi {{FIRST_NAME}},

I want to give you a reason to come back.

Here's {{OFFER}} exclusively for you:

{{OFFER_DETAILS}} -- for example:
- "50% off your first 3 months of {{PROJECT_NAME}}"
- "Free access to our premium tier for 30 days"
- "Our brand new {{RESOURCE}} -- not available anywhere else"

[CLAIM YOUR OFFER] ({{OFFER_URL}})

This expires {{EXPIRY_DATE}}.

Whether you take the offer or not, I'd love to know: what would
make {{PROJECT_NAME}} more useful for you?

{{YOUR_NAME}}
```

### Email 4: Final Goodbye
**Send:** Day 12
**Subject:** "Goodbye, {{FIRST_NAME}} (for now)"

```
Hi {{FIRST_NAME}},

This is my last email.

I'm removing inactive subscribers to keep my list healthy (and
to respect your inbox).

If you want to stay, just click here: {{RECONFIRM_LINK}}

If not, I understand. You can always resubscribe at
{{SUBSCRIBE_URL}} if things change.

It was great having you. Wishing you the best.

{{YOUR_NAME}}

P.S. If you do nothing, you'll be unsubscribed automatically
in 48 hours.
```

**After Email 4:** If no engagement, REMOVE THEM. Do not keep inactive subscribers -- they hurt your deliverability.

## Sequence 4: Post-Purchase Onboarding (5 Emails)

**When to use:** Immediately after a customer pays for the first time.

### Email 1: Purchase Confirmation + Quick Start
**Send:** Immediately
**Subject:** "You're in! Here's how to get started"
- Thank them, confirm the purchase, and provide immediate next steps

### Email 2: Day 2 -- Feature Discovery
**Subject:** "3 features you'll want to try this week"
- Highlight the 3 most valuable features for new customers

### Email 3: Day 5 -- First Milestone
**Subject:** "You're closer than you think to {{FIRST_MILESTONE}}"
- Guide them toward their first meaningful result

### Email 4: Day 9 -- Resource Pack
**Subject:** "Resources to become a {{PROJECT_NAME}} power user"
- Share documentation, video tutorials, community access, and support channels

### Email 5: Day 14 -- Feedback Request
**Subject:** "How's your first 2 weeks with {{PROJECT_NAME}}?"
- Ask for feedback, NPS score, and offer help with any issues

## Sequence 5: Referral Request (3 Emails)

**When to use:** After a customer has been active for 30+ days and shows signs of satisfaction (high usage, positive support interactions, NPS score of 9-10).

### Email 1: The Ask
**Send:** After satisfaction signal
**Subject:** "Know anyone who'd benefit from {{PROJECT_NAME}}?"

```
Hi {{FIRST_NAME}},

I hope you're enjoying {{PROJECT_NAME}}!

I have a quick favor to ask.

If you know anyone who's dealing with {{PROBLEM}}, I'd be grateful
if you'd share {{PROJECT_NAME}} with them.

Here's your personal referral link: {{REFERRAL_LINK}}

When someone signs up through your link:
- **They get:** {{REFERRAL_INCENTIVE_FOR_FRIEND}} (e.g., 20% off first month)
- **You get:** {{REFERRAL_INCENTIVE_FOR_REFERRER}} (e.g., ${{AMOUNT}} credit)

No pressure at all. But if someone comes to mind, it means the
world to a small team like ours.

Thank you for being part of this,
{{YOUR_NAME}}
```

### Email 2: Make It Easy
**Send:** Day 5
**Subject:** "Share {{PROJECT_NAME}} in 10 seconds"

```
Hi {{FIRST_NAME}},

In case it's helpful, here's a message you can forward to anyone
who might benefit from {{PROJECT_NAME}}:

---

"Hey, I've been using {{PROJECT_NAME}} for {{PROBLEM_DOMAIN}}
and it's been great. {{ONE_SENTENCE_VALUE_PROP}}. You can check
it out here and get {{REFERRAL_BENEFIT}}: {{REFERRAL_LINK}}"

---

Just copy, paste, and send to anyone who comes to mind.

Your referral link: {{REFERRAL_LINK}}

{{YOUR_NAME}}
```

## Sequence 6: Annual Renewal / Upgrade (4 Emails)

**When to use:** 30 days before annual renewal or when a customer is on a lower tier with potential to upgrade.

### Email 1: Value Recap (30 days before renewal)
**Subject:** "Your year with {{PROJECT_NAME}} -- by the numbers"

```
Hi {{FIRST_NAME}},

Your annual {{PROJECT_NAME}} subscription renews on {{RENEWAL_DATE}}.

Before that, I wanted to share what you've accomplished this year:

- {{USAGE_METRIC_1}} (e.g., "{{NUMBER}} projects created")
- {{USAGE_METRIC_2}} (e.g., "Approximately {{HOURS}} hours saved")
- {{USAGE_METRIC_3}} (e.g., "{{NUMBER}} team members collaborating")

That's impressive.

Your subscription will auto-renew at {{PRICE}}/year on
{{RENEWAL_DATE}}. No action needed if you want to continue.

If you have questions about your plan or want to explore
upgrades, just reply.

{{YOUR_NAME}}
```

### Email 2: Upgrade Opportunity (21 days before renewal)
**Subject:** "Unlock more with {{HIGHER_PLAN}}"
- Highlight features on the next tier that match their usage patterns
- Show the ROI of upgrading

### Email 3: Renewal Reminder (7 days before)
**Subject:** "Your renewal is coming up on {{DATE}}"
- Confirm details, address any concerns, make renewal easy

### Email 4: Confirmation / Thank You (Day of renewal)
**Subject:** "You're renewed! Here's what's new"
- Confirm renewal, share what is new or coming soon, thank them for their loyalty

## Personalization Variables

### Variables to Use in Every Sequence

| Variable | Source | Impact |
|----------|--------|--------|
| `{{FIRST_NAME}}` | Signup form, CRM | Basic personalization, use in subject + greeting |
| `{{THEIR_COMPANY}}` | CRM, LinkedIn research | Shows you know who you are emailing |
| `{{THEIR_ROLE}}` | CRM, LinkedIn | Tailor messaging to their responsibilities |
| `{{INDUSTRY}}` | CRM | Reference industry-specific problems and benchmarks |
| `{{COMPANY_SIZE}}` | CRM, research | Adjust messaging for solo vs. team vs. enterprise |
| `{{USAGE_DATA}}` | Product analytics | Reference specific actions they have taken |
| `{{PLAN}}` | Billing system | Know their current subscription level |
| `{{DAYS_SINCE_SIGNUP}}` | CRM | Track where they are in their journey |

### When to Use Personalization
- **Always:** First name, company name (in sales sequences)
- **When available:** Usage data, plan info, industry
- **Carefully:** Deep personalization (referencing specific posts, events) -- only in cold outreach where the extra effort drives higher response rates
- **Never:** Fake personalization ("I loved your recent blog post" when you did not read it)

## A/B Testing Across Sequences

### What to Test (Priority Order)
1. **Subject lines** -- highest impact, easiest to test
2. **Send timing** -- morning vs. afternoon, different days of the week
3. **CTA placement** -- beginning vs. end, button vs. text link
4. **Email length** -- short (under 100 words) vs. medium (100-200 words)
5. **Tone** -- formal vs. casual, first person vs. third person
6. **Social proof** -- with testimonial vs. without

### Testing Methodology
- Minimum 100 recipients per variant for meaningful results
- Test ONE variable at a time
- Run for at least 48 hours before evaluating (some people check email irregularly)
- Track the metric that matters for that sequence (open rate for subject lines, reply rate for body copy, click rate for CTAs)
- Document every test and result

## Quick-Start Checklist

- [ ] Choose which sequences to implement first (start with welcome/nurture and cold outreach)
- [ ] Customize email templates with your product details, voice, and offers
- [ ] Set up sequences in your email platform or CRM
- [ ] Gather at least 2-3 customer testimonials for social proof emails
- [ ] Create a case study for the social proof email in cold outreach
- [ ] Set up tracking: UTM parameters on all links
- [ ] Test each sequence with internal emails before going live
- [ ] Define A/B tests for your first 30 days
- [ ] Set up a review cadence: analyze sequence performance monthly
- [ ] Document response rates, reply rates, and conversion rates per sequence
