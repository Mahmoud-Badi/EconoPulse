## 4. Customer Interview Script

Conduct 15-20 interviews with people in your target segments. These conversations are the single most valuable marketing research you can do.

### Interview Setup

- **Duration:** 30-45 minutes
- **Format:** Video call (with permission to record) or in-person
- **Incentive:** $25-50 gift card, free product access, or mutual value exchange
- **Recording:** Always ask permission. Use transcription tools (Otter.ai, Grain, Fireflies).
- **Goal:** Understand their problem, current solutions, decision-making process, and language.

### Recruitment Message Template

```
Subject: Quick chat about [problem area]? (${{INCENTIVE_AMOUNT}} gift card)

Hi [Name],

I'm researching how [audience type] handle [problem area]. I'd love to hear about
your experience -- what works, what doesn't, and what you wish existed.

It's a casual 30-minute conversation, not a sales pitch. As a thank you, I'll send
you a ${{INCENTIVE_AMOUNT}} [Amazon/Starbucks] gift card.

Would you have time this week or next?

[Your name]
```

### Interview Questions (15 Questions)

**Opening (2 minutes) -- Build rapport:**

1. "Tell me about your role and what you do day-to-day."
   - *Purpose: Context and warm-up. Note their job title and responsibilities.*

**Problem Discovery (10 minutes) -- Understand the pain:**

2. "Walk me through the last time you had to [do the task your product addresses]. What happened?"
   - *Purpose: Get a specific, concrete story -- not hypotheticals.*

3. "What was the most frustrating part of that experience?"
   - *Purpose: Identify the emotional pain point.*

4. "How often do you encounter this problem? Daily? Weekly? Monthly?"
   - *Purpose: Quantify frequency -- higher frequency = more urgency.*

5. "On a scale of 1-10, how much of a priority is solving this problem for you right now? Why?"
   - *Purpose: Gauge urgency and willingness to act.*

**Current Solutions (8 minutes) -- Understand the status quo:**

6. "What are you currently using to solve this problem?"
   - *Purpose: Identify direct and indirect competitors, including manual workarounds.*

7. "What do you like about your current approach?"
   - *Purpose: Understand what to preserve or improve upon. Do not dismiss their current solution.*

8. "What are the biggest limitations of your current approach?"
   - *Purpose: Identify specific gaps you can fill.*

9. "Have you tried other solutions in the past? What happened?"
   - *Purpose: Understand their evaluation history and why alternatives failed.*

**Decision-Making Process (8 minutes) -- Understand how they buy:**

10. "When you last evaluated a new tool or solution in this area, how did you find it?"
    - *Purpose: Identify discovery channels (Google, recommendations, reviews, ads).*

11. "Who else was involved in that decision? Did you need approval from anyone?"
    - *Purpose: Understand the buying committee and decision dynamics.*

12. "What were the top 3 factors in your decision?"
    - *Purpose: Identify decision criteria to emphasize in marketing.*

13. "What would make you switch from your current solution to something new?"
    - *Purpose: Identify switching triggers and barriers.*

**Value and Willingness to Pay (5 minutes):**

14. "If a solution could [describe your key benefit], what would that be worth to you?"
    - *Purpose: Anchor value before discussing price. Let them describe value in their terms.*

15. "What would you expect to pay for something like this? What would feel too expensive?"
    - *Purpose: Price sensitivity data. Note the range, not just a single number.*

### Survey Questions (12 Questions)

**Screening (2 questions):**

1. "Which best describes your role?" (Multiple choice)
   - Options: {{ROLE_OPTIONS}}
   - *Purpose: Filter to target audience.*

2. "How often do you [task related to your product]?" (Multiple choice)
   - Daily / Several times a week / Weekly / Monthly / Rarely / Never
   - *Purpose: Filter for frequency -- exclude "Never."*

**Problem Validation (3 questions):**

3. "How satisfied are you with your current approach to [task]?" (Scale 1-5)
   - Very dissatisfied / Dissatisfied / Neutral / Satisfied / Very satisfied
   - *Purpose: Quantify dissatisfaction.*

4. "What is the biggest challenge you face with [task]?" (Multiple choice, select up to 3)
   - Options based on interview findings: {{CHALLENGE_OPTIONS}}
   - *Purpose: Rank pain points by frequency.*

5. "How much time do you spend per week on [task]?" (Multiple choice)
   - Less than 1 hour / 1-3 hours / 3-5 hours / 5-10 hours / 10+ hours
   - *Purpose: Quantify time investment (correlates with willingness to pay).*

**Solution Interest (3 questions):**

6. "If a tool could [primary benefit], how interested would you be?" (Scale 1-5)
   - Not at all / Slightly / Moderately / Very / Extremely interested
   - *Purpose: Validate demand for your core value proposition.*

7. "Which features would be most important to you?" (Rank top 5 from list)
   - Options: {{FEATURE_OPTIONS}}
   - *Purpose: Prioritize product and marketing focus.*

8. "How would you prefer to access this solution?" (Multiple choice)
   - Web app / Mobile app / Desktop software / Browser extension / API / Other

**Pricing (2 questions):**

9. "What would you expect to pay monthly for a solution like this?" (Multiple choice)
   - Free / $1-10 / $11-25 / $26-50 / $51-100 / $101-250 / $250+ / Not sure
   - *Purpose: Price range validation.*

10. "Which pricing model do you prefer?" (Multiple choice)
    - Monthly subscription / Annual subscription / One-time purchase / Usage-based / Freemium
    - *Purpose: Pricing model preference.*

**Discovery (2 questions):**

11. "How do you typically discover new tools for your work?" (Select all that apply)
    - Google search / Social media / Colleague recommendation / Online reviews / Blog posts / YouTube / Newsletter / Podcast / App Store / Conference / Reddit / Other

12. "Is there anything else you would want from a tool like this?" (Open text)
    - *Purpose: Capture ideas you may have missed.*

## 10. Product-Type-Specific Audience Insights

<!-- IF {{PRODUCT_TYPE}} == "saas" -->
### SaaS Audience Considerations

**B2B SaaS Buying Dynamics:**
- Average B2B SaaS buying committee: 6-10 people
- Average evaluation cycle: 1-6 months (depends on ACV)
- Champion (user) vs Decision-maker (manager/exec) -- you need to market to both
- Technical evaluator may have veto power (security, IT compatibility)

**Key Questions for SaaS Audience Research:**
- Who is the champion (the user who will push for adoption)?
- Who is the economic buyer (the person who approves the budget)?
- Who is the technical evaluator (IT/security review)?
- What is the trigger event that starts evaluation (contract renewal, team growth, pain threshold)?
- How do they currently evaluate SaaS tools (free trial, demo call, peer recommendation)?

**SaaS-Specific Channels:**
- G2 and Capterra reviews are critical for mid-funnel credibility
- LinkedIn is the primary B2B channel for awareness and thought leadership
- Product-led growth (free tier) can bypass the buying committee for bottom-up adoption
- Webinars and demos are high-intent lead generation
- Case studies and ROI calculators close enterprise deals
<!-- ENDIF -->

<!-- IF {{PRODUCT_TYPE}} == "mobile_app" -->
### Mobile App Audience Considerations

**Mobile User Behavior:**
- Average user has 80+ apps installed but uses only 9 daily
- 77% of users never use an app again 72 hours after install
- App Store discovery is dominated by search (65%) and browsing (25%)
- Social proof (ratings, reviews, download count) drives install decisions

**Key Questions for Mobile Audience Research:**
- What apps do they check first thing in the morning and last at night?
- What category does your app belong to in their mental model?
- Are they willing to pay upfront, subscribe, or do they expect free with ads?
- What is the viral loop potential (do they share content or invite friends)?
- What is their phone OS split (iOS vs Android) in your target geography?

**Mobile-Specific Channels:**
- App Store Optimization (ASO) is your most important channel
- TikTok and Instagram Reels for awareness and install campaigns
- Influencer marketing drives installs for consumer apps
- Push notifications are your primary retention tool
- In-app referral programs for viral growth
- Apple Search Ads and Google App Campaigns for paid acquisition
<!-- ENDIF -->

<!-- IF {{PRODUCT_TYPE}} == "marketplace" -->
### Marketplace Audience Considerations

**Two-Sided Audience:**
You have two audiences: supply (providers) and demand (consumers). Research both.

**Supply-side audience research:**
- Where do providers currently find clients? (Upwork, referrals, social media?)
- What is their biggest pain point? (Finding clients, getting paid, managing projects?)
- What take rate are they accustomed to paying?
- What makes them loyal to a platform vs multi-homing?

**Demand-side audience research:**
- How do they currently find providers? (Google, referrals, existing platforms?)
- What is their biggest concern? (Quality, reliability, price, speed?)
- How do they evaluate and compare providers?
- What trust signals do they need? (Reviews, portfolios, guarantees?)

**Marketplace-Specific Channels:**
- SEO for demand-side acquisition ("find a [provider type] near me")
- LinkedIn and industry communities for supply-side acquisition
- Referral programs for both sides
- Content marketing establishing category authority
- Local marketing (if geographically constrained)
<!-- ENDIF -->

<!-- IF {{PRODUCT_TYPE}} == "ecommerce" -->
### E-Commerce Audience Considerations

**Online Shopping Behavior:**
- 81% of consumers research online before buying
- Average cart abandonment rate: 70%
- Reviews influence 93% of purchase decisions
- Free shipping expectations: 75% of consumers expect free shipping over a threshold

**Key Questions for E-Commerce Audience Research:**
- What triggers the purchase need (seasonal, event-based, impulse, replacement)?
- Where do they discover products (Instagram, Google, TikTok, Amazon)?
- What drives their purchase decision (price, reviews, brand, aesthetics)?
- How important is brand loyalty vs price comparison?
- What is their return and exchange expectation?

**E-Commerce-Specific Channels:**
- Instagram and Pinterest for visual product discovery
- Google Shopping for high-intent search
- Email marketing for retention and repeat purchase
- Influencer marketing for social proof and awareness
- TikTok Shop for younger demographics
- Retargeting ads for cart abandonment recovery
- Amazon (if applicable) for marketplace presence
<!-- ENDIF -->

## 11. Research Action Plan

- [ ] Complete demographic segmentation using LinkedIn, Census data, industry reports
- [ ] Map online communities and forums where audience congregates
- [ ] Analyze competitor reviews for audience language and pain points
- [ ] Run Google Trends analysis for relevant keywords
- [ ] Document audience's existing tool stack
- [ ] Identify top publications, podcasts, and newsletters for the audience

- [ ] Recruit 15-20 interview candidates across target segments
- [ ] Conduct and transcribe all interviews
- [ ] Analyze interviews for patterns (pain points, language, decision factors)
- [ ] Complete Jobs-to-be-Done framework
- [ ] Build enriched buyer personas
- [ ] Document objections and trust requirements

### Phase 3: Quantitative Validation (Week 4-6)

- [ ] Design survey based on interview findings
- [ ] Distribute survey to 100+ respondents
- [ ] Analyze survey results
- [ ] Validate or revise segment prioritization
- [ ] Estimate audience size per channel
- [ ] Finalize engagement patterns and content format preferences

### Phase 4: Ongoing Research (Continuous)

- [ ] Monthly: Monitor community conversations for new language and pain points
- [ ] Quarterly: Re-survey a sample for shifting preferences
- [ ] Quarterly: Update persona documents with new insights
- [ ] Continuously: Feed customer support interactions into audience intelligence
- [ ] Continuously: Track which content formats and channels perform best
