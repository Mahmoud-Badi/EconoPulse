## 1. How to Use This Template

### Step-by-Step Process

1. **Brainstorm broadly** -- list every item you can think of in each quadrant without filtering.
2. **Prioritize ruthlessly** -- narrow each quadrant to the 5-8 most significant items.
3. **Be honest** -- weaknesses are only useful if you acknowledge them truthfully.
4. **Focus on marketing** -- this is not a product SWOT. Ask "how does this affect our ability to acquire and retain customers?" for each item.
5. **Use evidence** -- back up each item with data or specific observations, not gut feelings.
6. **Generate strategies** -- the TOWS matrix in Section 7 converts your SWOT into actionable strategies.
7. **Prioritize and assign** -- in Section 8, turn strategies into action items with owners and deadlines.
8. **Review quarterly** -- the marketing landscape changes fast. Revisit every 3 months.

### Who Should Participate

| Role | Why They Should Be Involved |
|------|-----------------------------|
| Founder / CEO | Strategic context, vision, resource allocation |
| Marketing lead | Channel expertise, campaign performance data |
| Sales (if applicable) | Customer objection insights, competitive intel |
| Product lead | Feature roadmap, positioning opportunities |
| Customer support | Customer language, pain points, common questions |
| External advisor / mentor | Objective perspective, industry benchmarks |

### Internal vs External

| | Positive | Negative |
|---|----------|----------|
| **Internal** (within your control) | **Strengths** | **Weaknesses** |
| **External** (outside your control) | **Opportunities** | **Threats** |
