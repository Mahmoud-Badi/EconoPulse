## Core Copywriting Formulas

### 1. AIDA — Attention, Interest, Desire, Action

The oldest and most widely used copywriting formula. Works everywhere: landing pages, emails, ads, product descriptions.

**How it works:**
- **Attention**: Grab attention with a bold claim, surprising fact, or question
- **Interest**: Build interest by explaining the relevance to the reader
- **Desire**: Create desire by showing the outcome/transformation
- **Action**: Tell them exactly what to do next

**Fill-in Template:**

```
[ATTENTION]
Did you know that [surprising statistic or bold claim]?

[INTEREST]
If you're a [target audience] who struggles with [problem],
you know how frustrating it is to [specific pain].

[DESIRE]
Imagine if you could [desired outcome] without [undesirable effort].
With [product], you can [specific benefit] and [secondary benefit].
[Customer name] used [product] to [specific result].

[ACTION]
Start your free trial today — no credit card required.
[CTA Button]
```

**Example:**

```
[ATTENTION]
The average developer spends 6 hours a week on deployment tasks.

[INTEREST]
If you're shipping code manually, you know the drill: SSH into servers,
run scripts, cross your fingers, and hope nothing breaks.

[DESIRE]
Imagine pushing to main and having your code live in under 2 minutes —
automatically tested, automatically deployed, with instant rollback
if anything goes wrong. ShipFast customers deploy 12x more frequently
with 90% fewer incidents.

[ACTION]
Try ShipFast free for 14 days. Setup takes 5 minutes.
[Start Deploying →]
```

### 2. PAS — Problem, Agitation, Solution

The most powerful formula for pain-point-driven copy. It works because it makes the reader feel the problem deeply before presenting the solution.

**How it works:**
- **Problem**: State the problem the reader faces
- **Agitation**: Twist the knife — make the problem feel urgent, expensive, or emotionally painful
- **Solution**: Present your product as the answer

**Fill-in Template:**

```
[PROBLEM]
[Target audience] waste [time/money/effort] every [time period] on [painful task].

[AGITATION]
And it's getting worse. [Consequence 1]. Meanwhile, [consequence 2].
You've tried [alternative 1] — too [drawback]. You've tried [alternative 2] —
too [drawback]. Every [time period], the problem costs you [specific cost].

[SOLUTION]
[Product] eliminates [problem] entirely. Instead of [old painful way],
you [new easy way] — and [key benefit]. That's why [social proof].
[CTA]
```

**Example:**

```
[PROBLEM]
Marketing teams spend 15+ hours per week creating content for
multiple platforms — writing blog posts, then manually reformatting
for social media, email, and ads.

[AGITATION]
That's 780 hours per year. Per person. And the content still looks
inconsistent across channels. Your brand voice gets diluted. Your
team burns out. And competitors who publish more frequently are
stealing your audience.

[SOLUTION]
ContentFlow turns one blog post into 10+ pieces of content —
automatically formatted for Twitter, LinkedIn, email, and more.
Your team saves 12 hours per week while publishing 5x more content.
500+ marketing teams already use ContentFlow.
[Start Free — Reclaim Your Week →]
```

### 3. BAB — Before, After, Bridge

A storytelling formula that paints a picture of life before and after your product, then bridges the gap with your solution.

**How it works:**
- **Before**: Describe the reader's current painful reality
- **After**: Paint the picture of life after using your product
- **Bridge**: Explain how your product gets them from Before to After

**Fill-in Template:**

```
[BEFORE]
Right now, you [current painful reality]. Every day, you [painful daily task].
You feel [negative emotion] because [reason].

[AFTER]
But imagine: [positive future state]. You [positive daily experience].
You feel [positive emotion] because [reason]. Your [metric] has [improved].

[BRIDGE]
[Product] is the bridge. It [core functionality] so you can go from
[before state] to [after state] in [timeframe].

Here's how:
1. [Step 1]
2. [Step 2]
3. [Step 3]

[CTA]
```

**Example:**

```
[BEFORE]
Right now, your customer support inbox is chaos. Tickets pile up.
Response times stretch to hours. Customers churn because they feel ignored.

[AFTER]
But imagine: every ticket auto-categorized, auto-prioritized, and routed
to the right agent. Response time drops to under 5 minutes. CSAT scores
hit 95%+. Customers stay because they feel heard.

[BRIDGE]
HelpDesk AI is the bridge. It uses AI to triage, categorize, and suggest
responses for every ticket — so your team handles 3x the volume with
half the stress.
[See It In Action — Free Demo →]
```

### 4. The 4 Ps — Picture, Promise, Prove, Push

A formula that paints a vivid picture, makes a promise, proves it, and pushes the reader to act.

**How it works:**
- **Picture**: Paint a vivid image of the reader's situation
- **Promise**: Make a specific promise about what your product delivers
- **Prove**: Back up the promise with evidence
- **Push**: Push them toward action with urgency or incentive

**Fill-in Template:**

```
[PICTURE]
Picture this: it's [time of day]. You're [scenario related to their world].
[Vivid description of their situation].

[PROMISE]
What if [product] could [specific promise]? Not someday — [timeframe].

[PROVE]
Don't just take our word for it:
- [Customer] achieved [result] in [timeframe]
- [Metric]: [specific number]
- [Third-party validation]

[PUSH]
[Urgency or incentive]. [CTA with clear action].
```

---

### 5. ACCA — Awareness, Comprehension, Conviction, Action

A formula focused on educating the reader before asking for action. Best for complex products or skeptical audiences.

**How it works:**
- **Awareness**: Make the reader aware of a problem or opportunity they may not fully understand
- **Comprehension**: Help them understand the implications and the available solutions
- **Conviction**: Build conviction that your solution is the right one (through proof)
- **Action**: Direct them to take the next step

**Fill-in Template:**

```
[AWARENESS]
Most [target audience] don't realize that [non-obvious problem or opportunity].
[Surprising fact or statistic].

[COMPREHENSION]
Here's why this matters: [explanation of implications].
The traditional approach is to [old way], but this leads to [negative outcome].
There's a better approach: [new approach category].

[CONVICTION]
[Product] is the [product category] built specifically for [target audience].
Here's what makes it different:
- [Differentiator 1] → [Outcome 1]
- [Differentiator 2] → [Outcome 2]
- [Differentiator 3] → [Outcome 3]

[Evidence: testimonial, case study, or data]

[ACTION]
See for yourself. [CTA — low commitment, matches the educational tone].
```

### 6. The "So What?" Test

Not a formula, but a critical technique. After writing any claim, ask "So what?" until you reach the actual benefit.

```
Claim: "Our platform uses AI-powered analytics."
So what? → "It identifies trends in your data automatically."
So what? → "You spot opportunities 10x faster than manual analysis."
So what? → "You make better decisions and grow revenue faster."
So what? → "You hit your quarterly targets without working weekends."

Final copy: "Hit your quarterly targets without working weekends.
Our AI finds the opportunities hiding in your data — before
your competitors do."
```

**Rule**: The real benefit is usually 3-4 "So what?"s deep. Most marketing copy stops at level 1.

## Headline Formulas

### The 20+ Headline Patterns

Every headline should do at least one of: promise a benefit, create curiosity, provide specificity, or speak to the reader's identity.

#### Category: How-To Headlines

| # | Pattern | Example |
|---|---------|---------|

| 3 | "How to [achieve outcome] in [timeframe]" | "How to set up CI/CD in under 10 minutes" |
| 4 | "How [audience] can [achieve outcome]" | "How solo founders can compete with funded startups" |

#### Category: Number/List Headlines

| # | Pattern | Example |
|---|---------|---------|

| 7 | "[X] reasons why [claim]" | "3 reasons why your landing page isn't converting" |
| 8 | "[X] [things] that [outcome]" | "12 email subject lines that get 50%+ open rates" |

#### Category: Secret/Revelation Headlines

| # | Pattern | Example |
|---|---------|---------|
| 9 | "The secret to [desired outcome]" | "The secret to shipping code without breaking production" |
| 10 | "What [successful people] know about [topic]" | "What top-performing sales teams know about follow-ups" |
| 11 | "Why [thing] doesn't work (and what to do instead)" | "Why cold outreach doesn't work (and what to do instead)" |

#### Category: Question Headlines

| # | Pattern | Example |
|---|---------|---------|
| 12 | "Are you making these [X] [mistakes]?" | "Are you making these 5 onboarding mistakes?" |
| 13 | "What if you could [desired outcome]?" | "What if you could deploy 10x a day without fear?" |

| # | Pattern | Example |
|---|---------|---------|

#### Category: Direct/Bold Headlines

| # | Pattern | Example |
|---|---------|---------|
| 18 | "[Outcome]. [Outcome]. [Short timeframe]." | "Faster deployments. Zero downtime. 5-minute setup." |
| 19 | "The [category] that [differentiator]" | "The project management tool that developers actually use" |

#### Category: Social Proof Headlines

| # | Pattern | Example |
|---|---------|---------|
| 23 | "Join [X] [audience] who [outcome]" | "Join 10,000+ teams who ship with confidence" |
| 24 | "Why [X] [audience] switched to [product]" | "Why 500 agencies switched to our platform this quarter" |
| 25 | "Rated #1 by [source] for [category]" | "Rated #1 by G2 for developer experience" |

### Headline Writing Rules

1. **Specificity beats vagueness**: "Save 10 hours/week" beats "Save time"
2. **Odd numbers outperform even numbers** in list headlines (7 beats 8)
3. **Negative headlines can outperform positive**: "5 mistakes" beats "5 tips"
4. **Keep it under 12 words** for landing pages
5. **Test 5-10 headlines** before committing — the headline is the most impactful element

## Feature-Benefit-Proof Chain

### The Methodology

Every product claim should follow the Feature → Benefit → Proof chain. Features tell, benefits sell, proof convinces.

| Layer | What It Is | Example |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| **Feature** | What the product has/does | "Real-time analytics dashboard" |
| **Benefit** | Why that matters to the user | "See what's working and what's not — instantly" |
| **Proof** | Evidence that it delivers | "Teams using our dashboard make decisions 3x faster" |

### Fill-in Template

```
Feature: [Product] includes [feature name].
Benefit: This means you can [outcome] without [pain].
Proof: [Customer/metric] [specific evidence].
```

### Example Chain

```
Feature: Automated deployment pipeline
Benefit: Ship code to production in minutes, not hours — with zero manual steps
Proof: Acme Corp reduced their deployment time from 4 hours to 3 minutes

Feature: Built-in error monitoring
Benefit: Know about issues before your customers do
Proof: Our monitoring catches 99.7% of errors within 30 seconds of occurrence

Feature: One-click rollback
Benefit: Sleep soundly knowing any bad deploy can be undone instantly
Proof: "The rollback feature saved us during Black Friday." — CTO, ShopCo
```

### Applying the Chain to Your Copy

**Bad copy (feature-only):**
"We offer real-time analytics, automated reports, and team dashboards."

**Good copy (feature → benefit):**
"Track what matters in real time. Get automated reports that highlight what's working. Share dashboards that keep your whole team aligned."

**Best copy (feature → benefit → proof):**
"Track what matters in real time — teams using our analytics make decisions 3x faster. Get automated weekly reports that highlight what's working — saving you 5 hours per week. Share dashboards that keep your whole team aligned — rated 4.9/5 for ease of use on G2."

## Power Words That Convert

Words that trigger emotional responses and increase engagement. Use them in headlines, CTAs, and body copy.

### Urgency & Scarcity Words
now, today, instant, immediately, hurry, limited, last chance, deadline, before, expires, only, remaining, closing, final, act

### Trust & Safety Words
guaranteed, proven, certified, trusted, secure, protected, verified, backed, official, authentic, reliable, tested, endorsed, risk-free, no-obligation

### Exclusivity Words
exclusive, members-only, insider, invitation, limited, private, VIP, early access, first, premium, elite, handpicked, curated, behind-the-scenes

### Value Words
free, bonus, save, discount, deal, bargain, value, included, complimentary, gift, reward, extra, unlimited, forever, lifetime

### Curiosity Words
secret, revealed, discover, hidden, surprising, unexpected, little-known, untold, behind, truth, myth, mistake, overlooked, forgotten

### Power & Control Words
dominate, master, control, command, unleash, transform, revolutionize, breakthrough, unlock, accelerate, supercharge, amplify, maximize, skyrocket

### Simplicity Words
easy, simple, quick, effortless, automatic, instant, one-click, no-code, seamless, straightforward, intuitive, hassle-free, painless, smooth

### Emotional Words
love, hate, fear, joy, freedom, peace, confidence, pride, relief, excitement, amazement, delight, frustration, overwhelm, anxiety, hope

## Words to Avoid

### Vague Words That Mean Nothing
- "innovative" (everyone says it — nobody believes it)
- "cutting-edge" (same as above)
- "best-in-class" (says who?)
- "world-class" (unverifiable)
- "synergy" (corporate jargon)
- "leverage" (use "use" instead)
- "utilize" (use "use" instead)
- "robust" (what does this actually mean to the user?)
- "scalable" (unless you provide specific scale numbers)
- "seamless" (every product claims this)
- "next-generation" (compared to what?)
- "state-of-the-art" (prove it or drop it)
- "turnkey" (outdated and vague)
- "holistic" (meaningless in tech)
- "paradigm-shifting" (seriously, do not)

### Fixing Vague Copy

| Vague (avoid) | Specific (use instead) |
|---------------|----------------------|
| "Our innovative solution" | "We reduced deploy time from 4 hours to 3 minutes" |
| "Best-in-class support" | "Average response time: 4 minutes" |
| "Scalable platform" | "Handles 10 to 10 million requests with the same config" |
| "Seamless integration" | "Connect to Slack in 2 clicks — no code required" |
| "Robust analytics" | "Track 50+ metrics across all channels in real time" |
| "Cutting-edge AI" | "GPT-4-powered suggestions that save 3 hours per report" |

### Words That Trigger Skepticism
- "guaranteed results" (without specifics)
- "no risk" (everything has risk — say "30-day money-back guarantee" instead)
- "the best" (unverifiable superlative)
- "never" / "always" (absolute claims feel untrustworthy)
- "breakthrough" (overused, triggers skepticism)

## Readability Guidelines

### Target Reading Level

**Aim for 8th grade reading level** (age 13-14). This is not about "dumbing down" — it is about clarity. The most successful companies write at this level:

| Company | Approximate Reading Level |
|---------|--------------------------|
| Apple | 5th-6th grade |
| Stripe | 8th-9th grade |
| Mailchimp | 7th-8th grade |
| Basecamp | 6th-7th grade |

### Sentence Length

| Length | Perception |
|--------|-----------|
| Under 10 words | Punchy, impactful — use for emphasis |
| 10-20 words | Clear, comfortable — your standard |
| 20-30 words | Complex but manageable — use sparingly |
| 30+ words | Confusing — rewrite or split |

**Mix sentence lengths** for rhythm. Three short sentences in a row feel choppy. Three long sentences in a row feel exhausting. Alternate.

### Paragraph Length

- **Web copy**: 1-3 sentences per paragraph
- **Email copy**: 1-2 sentences per paragraph
- **Blog posts**: 2-4 sentences per paragraph
- **Never**: Paragraphs longer than 5 sentences on any digital medium

### Formatting for Scanners

Most visitors scan, they do not read. Format your copy for scanning:

1. **Bold key phrases** so scanners catch the important bits
2. **Use bullet points** for lists of 3 or more items
3. **Use subheadings** every 2-3 paragraphs
4. **Use white space** generously — do not fill every inch of the page
5. **Left-align text** (centered text is harder to scan, except for headings)
6. **One idea per paragraph** — if you switch topics, start a new paragraph

### Readability Testing Tools

| Tool | Cost | Features |
|------|------|----------|
| Hemingway App (hemingwayapp.com) | Free (web), $20 (desktop) | Grade level, sentence complexity, passive voice |
| Grammarly | Free tier | Grammar, clarity, tone, readability score |
| Readable.com | Free analysis | Flesch-Kincaid score, sentence analysis |
| Yoast SEO (WordPress) | Free | Readability analysis built into editor |

## Before/After Examples

### Example 1: SaaS Landing Page Headline

**Before (feature-focused, vague):**
"An innovative, AI-powered, next-generation analytics platform for modern businesses"

**After (benefit-focused, specific):**
"Know exactly what's working in your business — updated every second"

**Why it's better**: The "after" version tells the reader what they GET (knowledge, real-time), not what the product IS (AI-powered, next-gen).

---

### Example 2: CTA Button

**Before (generic):**
"Submit"

**After (value-driven):**
"Start My Free Trial"

**Why it's better**: "Submit" tells the user what they DO. "Start My Free Trial" tells them what they GET.

---

### Example 3: Feature Description

**Before (technical):**
"Our platform offers a distributed, fault-tolerant message queue with configurable retry policies and dead letter queues."

**After (benefit-first):**
"Never lose a message. Our message queue retries failed deliveries automatically and stores undeliverable messages for review. Your data is safe even when services go down."

**Why it's better**: The "after" version leads with the outcome ("Never lose a message") and explains the technical features in terms of what they mean for the user.

---

### Example 4: Pricing Page Copy

**Before:**
"Our Enterprise plan provides advanced features and dedicated support."

**After:**
"Enterprise: For teams that need SSO, audit logs, 99.9% uptime SLA, and a dedicated account manager who knows your name. Custom pricing based on your team size."

**Why it's better**: The "after" version lists specific, tangible benefits instead of vague promises.

---

### Example 5: Email Subject Line

**Before:**
"Monthly Newsletter — February 2026"

**After:**
"The 3 deployment mistakes that cost teams 10 hours/week"

**Why it's better**: The "after" version uses specificity (3, 10 hours/week) and curiosity (what are the mistakes?) to drive opens. Nobody opens "Monthly Newsletter."

---

### Example 6: Testimonial Copy

**Before:**
"Great product! Really helped our team."

**After:**
"ShipFast reduced our deployment time from 4 hours to 3 minutes. We went from deploying once a week to deploying 5 times a day. It paid for itself in the first week."

**Why it's better**: Specific numbers (4 hours → 3 minutes, 1x → 5x/day) are infinitely more credible than "Great product."

---

### Example 7: Problem Statement

**Before:**
"Many businesses struggle with data management."

**After:**
"You have customer data in Salesforce, product data in Amplitude, financial data in QuickBooks, and support data in Zendesk. When your CEO asks 'how are we doing?' — you spend 3 hours pulling spreadsheets together before you can answer."

**Why it's better**: The "after" version is vivid and specific. The reader thinks "that is exactly my situation."

## Quick Reference Card

**When writing ANY piece of marketing copy, check these 10 things:**

1. [ ] Does it lead with a benefit (not a feature)?
2. [ ] Does it specify who it is for (not "everyone")?
3. [ ] Does it include a specific number or metric?
4. [ ] Is the CTA action-oriented and value-driven?
5. [ ] Is it readable at an 8th grade level?
6. [ ] Are paragraphs 3 sentences or fewer?
7. [ ] Does every claim pass the "So what?" test?
8. [ ] Is there proof to back up the main claim?
9. [ ] Have you eliminated vague words (innovative, robust, seamless)?
10. [ ] Would you click/sign up/buy based on this copy? Be honest.
