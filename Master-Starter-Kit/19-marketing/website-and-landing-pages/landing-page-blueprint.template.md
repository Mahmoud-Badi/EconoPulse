## Pre-Build Strategy

Before writing a single line of copy or designing a single element, answer these questions:

| Question | Your Answer |
|----------|-------------|
| **Primary goal** (one only) | {{PRIMARY_GOAL}} (e.g., sign-up, purchase, demo request, waitlist) |
| **Target audience** | {{TARGET_AUDIENCE}} |
| **Their #1 pain point** | {{PRIMARY_PAIN_POINT}} |
| **Your unique mechanism** | {{UNIQUE_MECHANISM}} (how you solve it differently) |
| **Key objections** | {{OBJECTION_1}}, {{OBJECTION_2}}, {{OBJECTION_3}} |
| **Desired emotional shift** | From {{BEFORE_EMOTION}} to {{AFTER_EMOTION}} |
| **One metric that proves value** | {{PROOF_METRIC}} (e.g., "saves 10 hours/week") |
| **Traffic source** | {{TRAFFIC_SOURCE}} (ads, organic, referral — affects messaging) |

### Landing Page vs. Homepage

A landing page has ONE goal and ONE CTA. It removes navigation and distractions. A homepage serves many audiences. Do NOT treat them the same.

- **Landing page**: Single conversion goal, no navigation bar, no external links, every element supports the CTA
- **Homepage**: Multiple goals, navigation to sub-pages, brand storytelling, serves returning visitors

## Section 1: Hero Section

### Purpose
The hero section has 5 seconds to communicate what you do, who it is for, and why they should care. If this fails, nothing below matters because visitors will leave.

### Copy Guidelines

**Headline (H1):**
- 6-12 words maximum
- Communicate the primary benefit, not the feature
- Be specific — avoid vague claims like "the best solution"
- Include the outcome the visitor wants

**Sub-headline:**
- 15-25 words
- Expand on the headline with supporting detail
- Address "how" or "for whom"
- Can include a secondary benefit

**CTA Button:**
- 2-5 words, action-oriented
- Start with a verb
- Communicate value, not action (e.g., "Start Saving Time" not "Submit")
- Use first person if natural: "Start My Free Trial"

### Template Copy

```
Headline:
{{PRIMARY_BENEFIT}} for {{TARGET_AUDIENCE}}

Sub-headline:
{{PROJECT_NAME}} helps {{TARGET_AUDIENCE}} {{ACHIEVE_OUTCOME}}
without {{PAIN_THEY_AVOID}}. {{PROOF_METRIC}}.

CTA: {{CTA_TEXT}} — Free for {{TRIAL_PERIOD}}
Secondary CTA: Watch Demo (2 min) | See How It Works
```

**Example variations:**

| Style | Headline |
|-------|----------|
| Outcome-first | "Ship features 3x faster with zero deployment headaches" |
| Audience-first | "The deployment tool backend teams actually enjoy using" |
| Curiosity | "Why 500+ teams stopped dreading deploy day" |
| Direct | "Automated deployments. Zero downtime. 5-minute setup." |

### Design Recommendations

- **Hero image/visual**: Show the product in use (screenshot, short demo GIF, or embedded video). Avoid generic stock photos.
- **Layout**: Headline left, visual right (or headline centered with visual below on mobile)
- **Whitespace**: Generous padding — do not crowd the hero
- **Background**: Clean, light background or subtle gradient. Avoid busy patterns.
- **CTA button**: High contrast color, large enough to be obvious (minimum 48px height)
- **Trust indicator**: Small line below CTA — "No credit card required" or "Free 14-day trial" or "Used by 1,000+ teams"
- **Above the fold**: Headline, sub-headline, CTA, and a visual must ALL be visible without scrolling

## Section 2: Social Proof Bar

### Purpose
Immediately after the hero, a social proof bar reduces skepticism. It answers: "Do real people/companies use this?" before the visitor even forms the question.

### Copy Guidelines
- Minimal text — logos and numbers speak louder than words
- If using a label, keep it short: "Trusted by" or "Used by teams at"
- Display 4-6 logos in a single row (more looks cluttered, fewer looks unimpressive)
- If no logos yet, use metrics: user count, items processed, uptime, satisfaction rate

### Template Copy

```
Trusted by {{USER_COUNT}}+ {{USER_TYPE}} at companies like:
[Logo 1] [Logo 2] [Logo 3] [Logo 4] [Logo 5]

OR (if early stage):
"{{EARLY_TRACTION_METRIC}}" — {{METRIC_CONTEXT}}
Example: "500+ projects deployed in the first month of launch"
```

### Design Recommendations

- Grayscale logos (full color distracts from your brand)
- Horizontal row, evenly spaced
- Subtle background color change (light gray or slight tint) to visually separate from hero
- Logo height: 24-40px, consistent across all logos
- On mobile: 2 rows of 3 or a horizontally scrolling strip

## Section 3: Problem Statement

### Purpose
Agitate the pain the visitor already feels. This section creates emotional resonance — the visitor should think "yes, that is exactly my problem." This builds desire for the solution you will introduce next.

### Copy Guidelines

- Use the visitor's own language (pull from customer interviews, support tickets, forums, Reddit)
- Be specific about the pain — vague problems get vague attention
- Use "you" language, not "we" language
- Do NOT introduce your product here — this section is about THEM
- 3-5 short paragraphs or a list of pain points

### Template Copy

```
Section heading: "{{PAIN_POINT_CATEGORY}} is broken"
OR: "Sound familiar?"
OR: "If you're like most {{TARGET_AUDIENCE}}..."

You spend {{TIME_WASTED}} every {{TIME_PERIOD}} on {{PAINFUL_TASK}}.

Meanwhile, {{CONSEQUENCE_1}}. And {{CONSEQUENCE_2}}.

You've tried {{EXISTING_SOLUTION_1}} — too {{DRAWBACK_1}}.
You've tried {{EXISTING_SOLUTION_2}} — too {{DRAWBACK_2}}.

What you need is {{DESIRED_STATE}} — without {{UNACCEPTABLE_TRADEOFF}}.
```

### Design Recommendations

- Dark or contrasting background to create visual weight (this section should feel serious)
- Short paragraphs, large readable text
- Optional: icons or small illustrations for each pain point
- Can use a two-column layout: pain points on left, frustrated illustration on right

## Section 4: Solution Introduction

### Purpose
Transition from pain to hope. Introduce your product as the answer to the problem you just described. This is the "bridge" — keep it concise and aspirational.

### Copy Guidelines

- Bridge directly from the problem: "That is why we built {{PROJECT_NAME}}"
- Communicate the core value proposition in one sentence
- Focus on the OUTCOME, not the mechanics
- This is a summary, not a feature list — details come later

### Template Copy

```
Heading: "Meet {{PROJECT_NAME}}"
OR: "There's a better way"
OR: "{{PROJECT_NAME}}: {{ONE_LINE_VALUE_PROP}}"

{{PROJECT_NAME}} is a {{PRODUCT_CATEGORY}} that helps {{TARGET_AUDIENCE}}
{{PRIMARY_OUTCOME}}.

Instead of {{OLD_WAY}}, you can {{NEW_WAY}} — in {{TIME_TO_VALUE}}.

[Product screenshot or short demo video]
```

### Design Recommendations

- Return to light, clean background (contrast with the dark problem section)
- Large product screenshot or 30-60 second demo video (autoplay muted if GIF/video)
- Screenshot should show the product in its best light — real data, clean UI
- Centered layout works well here

## Section 5: Feature Showcase (3 Key Features)

### Purpose
Highlight the 3 most compelling features — the ones that differentiate you from alternatives and directly address the pains from Section 3. Three is the magic number: memorable, scannable, complete.

### Copy Guidelines

- Each feature: icon + headline (3-5 words) + description (1-2 sentences) + visual
- Headlines should be benefit-oriented, not feature-oriented
  - Bad: "Dashboard analytics"
  - Good: "See what's working in seconds"
- Description should connect feature to outcome
- Include a mini visual for each (screenshot, icon, illustration)

### Template Copy

```
Feature 1:
Icon: {{FEATURE_1_ICON}}
Headline: "{{FEATURE_1_BENEFIT}}"
Description: "{{FEATURE_1_DESCRIPTION}}. This means you can {{FEATURE_1_OUTCOME}}."
[Screenshot of feature 1]

Feature 2:
Icon: {{FEATURE_2_ICON}}
Headline: "{{FEATURE_2_BENEFIT}}"
Description: "{{FEATURE_2_DESCRIPTION}}. So you never have to {{PAIN_REMOVED_2}}."
[Screenshot of feature 2]

Feature 3:
Icon: {{FEATURE_3_ICON}}
Headline: "{{FEATURE_3_BENEFIT}}"
Description: "{{FEATURE_3_DESCRIPTION}}. Which means {{FEATURE_3_OUTCOME}}."
[Screenshot of feature 3]
```

### Design Recommendations

- Three-column layout on desktop, stacked on mobile
- Alternating left/right layout also works well (feature text + screenshot, alternating sides)
- Icons: simple, consistent style (line icons or filled icons — do not mix)
- Each feature block should be visually equal weight
- Optional: subtle animation on scroll (fade-in, not distracting)

## Section 6: How It Works

### Purpose
Reduce perceived complexity. Show that getting value from your product is simple. A 3-step process makes anything feel achievable, even if the product is sophisticated.

### Copy Guidelines

- Exactly 3 steps (occasionally 4, never more)
- Each step: number + action headline + one-sentence description
- Step 1 should be low-friction (sign up, connect, install)
- Step 2 should be the core action (configure, import, create)
- Step 3 should be the outcome/value (launch, deploy, see results)
- Use active voice and simple verbs

### Template Copy

```
Heading: "Get started in 3 simple steps"
OR: "How {{PROJECT_NAME}} works"
OR: "From {{STARTING_POINT}} to {{END_RESULT}} in minutes"

Step 1: {{STEP_1_ACTION}}
"{{STEP_1_DESCRIPTION}}. Takes {{STEP_1_TIME}}."

Step 2: {{STEP_2_ACTION}}
"{{STEP_2_DESCRIPTION}}. {{STEP_2_BENEFIT}}."

Step 3: {{STEP_3_ACTION}}
"{{STEP_3_DESCRIPTION}}. {{STEP_3_OUTCOME}}."
```

**Example:**
```
Step 1: Connect your repo
"Link your GitHub repository with one click. Takes 30 seconds."

Step 2: Configure your pipeline
"Use our visual editor or drop in a config file. Preview before you commit."

Step 3: Ship with confidence
"Push to deploy. Automatic rollbacks if anything goes wrong."
```

### Design Recommendations

- Numbered steps (1, 2, 3) with connecting lines or arrows
- Horizontal layout on desktop, vertical on mobile
- Each step gets a small illustration or screenshot
- Progress indicator feel (steps flowing left to right)
- Background: clean, light — this section should feel easy and inviting

## Section 7: Detailed Features & Benefits Grid

### Purpose
For visitors who need more detail before converting. This is the deep dive — every important feature gets a row. Power users and comparison shoppers read this section carefully.

### Copy Guidelines

- 6-12 features in a grid or list format
- Each: icon + feature name + benefit-driven description (2-3 sentences)
- Group features by category if you have many (e.g., "Collaboration," "Security," "Integration")
- Every feature description must answer: "So what? Why does this matter to me?"

### Template Copy

```
Heading: "Everything you need to {{CORE_OUTCOME}}"
OR: "Built for {{TARGET_AUDIENCE}} who {{CHARACTERISTIC}}"

Category: {{CATEGORY_1}}
- {{FEATURE_A}}: {{BENEFIT_DESCRIPTION_A}}
- {{FEATURE_B}}: {{BENEFIT_DESCRIPTION_B}}
- {{FEATURE_C}}: {{BENEFIT_DESCRIPTION_C}}

Category: {{CATEGORY_2}}
- {{FEATURE_D}}: {{BENEFIT_DESCRIPTION_D}}
- {{FEATURE_E}}: {{BENEFIT_DESCRIPTION_E}}
- {{FEATURE_F}}: {{BENEFIT_DESCRIPTION_F}}
```

### Design Recommendations

- Grid layout: 2 or 3 columns on desktop
- Each grid item: icon (top), feature name (bold), description (regular)
- Consistent icon style across all features
- Adequate spacing between grid items (breathing room)
- Optional: category labels as section dividers within the grid
- On mobile: single column, each feature as a card

### Purpose
Provide detailed, credible proof that your product delivers results. This goes beyond the logo bar — full testimonials with faces, names, titles, and specific outcomes.

### Copy Guidelines

- 3-6 testimonials (3 minimum for credibility, 6 maximum before diminishing returns)
- Each testimonial must include: quote, full name, job title, company, headshot
- Best testimonials include SPECIFIC results: numbers, time saved, money saved, outcomes achieved
- Organize testimonials to address different concerns: ease of use, ROI, support quality, reliability

- Quote marks: large decorative quotation marks add visual weight

- Carousel on mobile (swipeable), grid on desktop
- Optional: video testimonials (embed thumbnails with play button)
- Highlight key phrases in the quote with bold text

## Section 9: Pricing Section

### Purpose
If this is a landing page with a single conversion goal (e.g., free trial), this section either shows simplified pricing or a strong CTA to the full pricing page. If the landing page IS the pricing page, provide full tier details.

### Copy Guidelines

- Lead with the value, not the price: "Start {{PRIMARY_OUTCOME}} for {{PRICE}}"
- If free tier exists, lead with it: "Free forever for {{FREE_TIER_LIMITS}}"
- Address the money question directly — hiding pricing increases bounce rate
- If complex pricing, link to dedicated pricing page

- Trust elements directly under pricing: guarantee, security badges, payment logos
- On mobile: stack cards vertically, recommended tier first

## Section 10: FAQ Section

### Purpose
Address remaining objections that prevent conversion. Every unanswered question is a reason NOT to buy. The FAQ anticipates and resolves these.

### Copy Guidelines

- 6-10 questions maximum
- Prioritize by objection strength (most common doubts first)
- Write conversational answers (not legal-speak)
- Answer the actual question in the first sentence, then elaborate
- Include questions about: pricing, security, support, getting started, migration, cancellation

### Template Copy

```
Heading: "Frequently Asked Questions"
OR: "Got questions? We've got answers."

Q: "Is {{PROJECT_NAME}} really free to start?"
A: "{{FREE_TIER_ANSWER}}"

Q: "How long does setup take?"
A: "{{SETUP_TIME_ANSWER}}"

Q: "Can I migrate from {{COMPETITOR}}?"
A: "{{MIGRATION_ANSWER}}"

Q: "Is my data secure?"
A: "{{SECURITY_ANSWER}}"

Q: "What if I need help?"
A: "{{SUPPORT_ANSWER}}"

Q: "Can I cancel anytime?"
A: "{{CANCELLATION_ANSWER}}"

Q: "Do you offer refunds?"
A: "{{REFUND_ANSWER}}"

Q: "Does {{PROJECT_NAME}} integrate with {{POPULAR_TOOL}}?"
A: "{{INTEGRATION_ANSWER}}"
```

### Design Recommendations

- Accordion/expandable format (click to expand answers) — reduces visual overwhelm
- Clean, simple layout — no imagery needed
- Questions in bold or semi-bold, answers in regular weight
- Group by category if more than 8 questions
- "Still have questions? Contact us" link at the bottom
- Structured data: implement FAQ schema (JSON-LD) for Google rich results

## Section 11: Final CTA

### Purpose
The last conversion opportunity before the footer. This catches visitors who scrolled through the entire page, consumed all the information, and are now ready to act. Make it easy and compelling.

### Copy Guidelines

- Restate the primary value proposition (not a new pitch)
- Create mild urgency or reinforce the offer
- Repeat the same CTA from the hero section (consistency reduces confusion)
- Optionally add a secondary CTA for those not quite ready (newsletter, demo, content download)
- Keep it short — 2-3 lines max plus the CTA button

### Template Copy

```
Heading: "Ready to {{PRIMARY_OUTCOME}}?"
OR: "Start {{BENEFIT_VERB}}ing today"
OR: "Join {{USER_COUNT}}+ {{USER_TYPE}} already using {{PROJECT_NAME}}"

Sub-line: "{{SETUP_EASE_STATEMENT}}. {{RISK_REVERSAL_STATEMENT}}."

[Primary CTA Button: {{CTA_TEXT}}]
Trust line: "Free for {{TRIAL_PERIOD}}" | "No credit card required"
Secondary link: "Schedule a demo" or "Talk to sales"
```

### Design Recommendations

- Full-width section with contrasting background (brand color or dark)
- Large heading, centered
- CTA button: prominent, high-contrast, same style as hero CTA
- Minimal text — no paragraphs, just heading + sub-line + button
- Optional: subtle background pattern or gradient
- This is NOT the place for new information — it is a decision point

## Section 12: Footer

### Purpose
Provide navigation, legal compliance, and trust signals. The footer catches visitors looking for specific information (legal, contact, about) and provides a last layer of credibility.

### Copy Guidelines

- Organized in columns: Product, Company, Resources, Legal
- Include social media links
- Copyright notice with current year
- Trust badges: security certifications, compliance logos, payment processors

### Template Copy

```
Column 1 — Product:
- Features
- Pricing
- Integrations
- Changelog
- API Docs

Column 2 — Company:
- About
- Blog
- Careers
- Contact
- Press

Column 3 — Resources:
- Documentation
- Community
- Status Page
- Support

Column 4 — Legal:
- Privacy Policy
- Terms of Service
- Cookie Policy
- Security

Bottom bar:
(c) {{CURRENT_YEAR}} {{COMPANY_NAME}}. All rights reserved.
[Social icons: Twitter, LinkedIn, GitHub, Discord]
```

- Social icons: consistent style, reasonable size (24-32px)
- Trust badges: bottom of footer, grayscale or muted colors
- Newsletter signup form (optional): email input + subscribe button in footer

## Mobile-First Design Considerations

### Layout Adjustments

| Desktop | Mobile |
|---------|--------|
| Multi-column grids | Single column stack |
| Side-by-side hero (text + image) | Stacked: heading, then image, then CTA |
| Horizontal feature showcase | Vertical cards |
| Pricing cards side by side | Stacked, recommended tier first |
| Logo bar in one row | Two rows or horizontal scroll |
| FAQ visible | Accordion (collapsed by default) |

### Mobile-Specific Rules

1. **CTA buttons**: Full width on mobile, minimum 48px height, minimum 16px font
2. **Text size**: Body text minimum 16px (prevents auto-zoom on iOS)
3. **Tap targets**: Minimum 44x44px with 8px spacing between targets
4. **Images**: Responsive, load appropriate size (use srcset), compress aggressively
5. **Videos**: Do NOT autoplay with sound on mobile. Use poster images.
6. **Forms**: Use appropriate input types (email, tel, number) for better mobile keyboards
7. **Navigation**: Hamburger menu or no nav at all on landing pages
8. **Sticky CTA**: Consider a sticky CTA bar at the bottom of mobile screens
9. **Load time**: Target under 3 seconds on 3G. Mobile users are less patient.
10. **Thumb zone**: Place primary CTAs in the bottom-center of the screen (easy thumb reach)

### Testing Checklist

- [ ] Test on iPhone SE (small screen)
- [ ] Test on iPhone 14/15 (standard)
- [ ] Test on Samsung Galaxy (Android)
- [ ] Test on iPad (tablet)
- [ ] Check all CTAs are tappable
- [ ] Check no horizontal scroll
- [ ] Check images load correctly
- [ ] Check forms are usable
- [ ] Check page speed on mobile (Google PageSpeed Insights)

## Above-the-Fold Optimization

### What Must Be Visible Without Scrolling

1. **Headline** (H1) — what you do and for whom
2. **Sub-headline** — supporting detail
3. **Primary CTA** — the action you want them to take
4. **Visual** — product screenshot, demo, or hero image
5. **Trust indicator** — one trust element (user count, "no credit card required," logo)

### What Must NOT Be Above the Fold

- Long paragraphs of text
- Multiple CTAs competing for attention
- Navigation that dominates the space
- Large unoptimized images that push content down
- Auto-playing videos with sound

### Testing Above-the-Fold

- **Desktop**: Assume 1366x768 viewport (most common)
- **Mobile**: Assume 375x667 viewport (iPhone SE/8 size)
- **Tools**: Use Chrome DevTools device toolbar to simulate viewports
- **5-second test**: Show someone the page for 5 seconds, then ask: "What does this product do?" If they cannot answer, your above-the-fold content is failing.

## Landing Page Performance Benchmarks

Use these benchmarks to evaluate your landing page effectiveness:

| Metric | Poor | Average | Good | Excellent |
|--------|------|---------|------|-----------|
| **Conversion rate** | < 1% | 2-3% | 4-5% | > 7% |
| **Bounce rate** | > 70% | 50-70% | 35-50% | < 35% |
| **Time on page** | < 30s | 30-60s | 1-3 min | > 3 min |
| **Scroll depth** | < 25% | 25-50% | 50-75% | > 75% |
| **Page load time** | > 5s | 3-5s | 1-3s | < 1s |
| **Mobile conversion** | < 0.5% | 1-2% | 2-3% | > 4% |

### Tracking Setup Checklist

- [ ] Google Analytics 4 (or privacy-friendly alternative: Plausible, Fathom)
- [ ] CTA click tracking (events)
- [ ] Scroll depth tracking
- [ ] Form submission tracking
- [ ] Heatmap tool installed (Hotjar free tier or Microsoft Clarity)
- [ ] A/B testing tool ready (Google Optimize sunset — use PostHog, VWO, or Optimizely)
