## Content Category Framework

Every piece of social media content should fall into one of these six categories. The key insight: most of your content should NOT be promotional. People follow accounts that provide value, not advertisements.

### The Six Content Categories

| Category | Purpose | % of Content | Examples |
|----------|---------|-------------|----------|
| **Educational** | Teach something useful, establish authority | 30-35% | Tips, how-tos, tutorials, explainers, frameworks, industry insights |
| **Entertaining** | Make people smile, stop scrolling, share | 15-20% | Memes, relatable humor, hot takes, behind-the-scenes bloopers, industry jokes |
| **Promotional** | Drive signups, sales, or awareness of your product | 10-15% | Product features, launches, demos, testimonials, pricing announcements |
| **Community** | Build relationships, start conversations | 15-20% | Questions, polls, replies, shoutouts, collaborations, asking for opinions |
| **Behind-the-Scenes** | Build trust through transparency | 10-15% | Build-in-public updates, team intros, office/workspace, decision-making process, mistakes and lessons |
| **User Spotlight** | Amplify your users, create social proof | 5-10% | Customer stories, user-generated content, testimonial highlights, case studies, feature requests shipped |

## Content Ratio Rules

### The 80/20 Rule
- **80% value** (educational, entertaining, community, behind-the-scenes, user spotlight)
- **20% promotion** (direct product pitches, CTAs, sales content)

If someone looks at your last 10 posts and 5 of them are "check out my product," they will unfollow. If 8 of them are genuinely useful and 2 mention your product, they will stay AND be more receptive to those 2 promotional posts.

### The 4-1-1 Rule (Alternative)
For every 6 pieces of content:
- **4** pieces of relevant content from others (curated, reshared, or inspired by)
- **1** piece of original educational/entertaining content
- **1** piece of promotional content

This works especially well on Twitter/X and LinkedIn where sharing others' content builds goodwill.

### The Give-Give-Give-Ask Framework
- Post 1: Give value (tip, insight, resource)
- Post 2: Give value (story, lesson learned)
- Post 3: Give value (respond to community, share others' work)
- Post 4: Ask (promote your product, request a signup, share a launch)

## Posting Frequency by Platform

| Platform | Minimum Frequency | Optimal Frequency | Best Times (General) | Content Lifespan |
|----------|-------------------|--------------------|-----------------------|-----------------|
| Twitter/X | 1x/day | 3-5x/day (including replies) | 8-10am, 12-1pm, 5-6pm (audience timezone) | 15-30 minutes |
| LinkedIn | 3x/week | 1x/day (weekdays) | Tue-Thu, 7-9am or 12pm | 24-48 hours |
| Reddit | 2-3x/week | 1x/day (comments count) | Varies by subreddit, generally mornings | Days to weeks |
| YouTube | 1x/week | 2x/week + Shorts daily | Tue/Thu/Sat, 12-3pm | Months to years |
| TikTok | 3x/week | 1-3x/day | 7-9am, 12-3pm, 7-11pm | 24-48 hours (but can resurface) |
| Instagram | 3x/week | 1x/day (Reels) + Stories daily | Mon-Fri, 11am-1pm, 7-9pm | 24-48 hours (Reels longer) |
| Facebook | 3x/week | 1x/day | Wed-Fri, 1-4pm | 5-6 hours |
| Threads | 3x/week | 1-2x/day | Still being determined, test your audience | 6-12 hours |

### Why Timing Matters
The first 30-60 minutes after posting are critical on most platforms. This is when the algorithm tests your content with a small audience. If it gets engagement (likes, comments, shares, saves), it gets shown to more people. Post when your audience is most active.

**Find YOUR best times:** Use platform analytics after 2-4 weeks of posting to see when YOUR audience is online. General best times are starting points, not rules.

## Hashtag Strategy by Platform

### Twitter/X
- Use 1-3 hashtags maximum (more looks spammy)
- Place at the end of the tweet or naturally within text
- Focus on community hashtags: #buildinpublic #indiehackers #saas #devtools #{{PRODUCT_HASHTAG}}
- Create a branded hashtag: #{{PROJECT_NAME}} for tracking mentions

### LinkedIn
- Use 3-5 hashtags at the end of posts
- Mix broad (#marketing #saas #startup) with niche (#{{INDUSTRY_HASHTAG}})
- Follow hashtags to see trending content in your space
- Do not use hashtags in the first line (they become clickable links that pull people away)

### Instagram
- Use 5-15 relevant hashtags (Instagram has reduced hashtag effectiveness -- quality over quantity)
- Mix popularity levels: 2-3 large (1M+ posts), 5-7 medium (10K-1M), 3-5 small/niche (<10K)
- Place in first comment or at the bottom of caption
- Research competitor hashtags and niche-specific tags

### TikTok
- Use 3-5 hashtags
- Include 1-2 trending hashtags + 2-3 niche-specific ones
- #fyp and #foryou are debated -- they are extremely broad, may not help
- Focus on descriptive hashtags that match your content topic

### Reddit
- Reddit does not use hashtags. Use post flair instead (subreddit-specific categories).
- Choose the most accurate flair for your post type.

## Monthly Theme Planning

Each month, choose an overarching theme that connects your content. This creates narrative coherence and makes content creation easier.

### Month 1: {{MONTH_1_THEME}}
**Focus:** Introduction and awareness. Who are you? What problem do you solve?
- Week 1: Origin story -- why {{PROJECT_NAME}} exists
- Week 2: The problem deep dive -- what your audience struggles with
- Week 3: Your approach -- how you solve it differently
- Week 4: Social proof -- early results, beta user feedback, metrics

### Month 2: {{MONTH_2_THEME}}
**Focus:** Education and authority. Position yourself as the expert.
- Week 1: Industry trends and predictions
- Week 2: How-to tutorial series (part 1-3)
- Week 3: Common mistakes and how to avoid them
- Week 4: Tools and resources roundup (including your product naturally)

### Month 3: {{MONTH_3_THEME}}
**Focus:** Community and engagement. Shift from broadcasting to conversation.
- Week 1: Ask your audience -- polls, questions, what they want to learn
- Week 2: User spotlight series -- feature 3-5 users/customers
- Week 3: Collaboration -- co-create content with another creator, founder, or brand
- Week 4: Challenge or event -- launch a mini-challenge related to your product

### Month 4+: Rotate and Refine
Repeat themes with new angles based on what performed best. Double down on content types that drove the most engagement and conversions.

## Engagement Time Blocks

Posting is only 40% of social media. The other 60% is engagement. Schedule dedicated time for:

### Daily Engagement Block (30-45 minutes)

**Morning (15 min):**
- Check notifications and reply to all comments on your posts
- Reply to 3-5 DMs
- Like and comment on 5-10 posts from people in your niche

**Afternoon (15 min):**
- Search for conversations about {{PROBLEM_DOMAIN}} or {{INDUSTRY}}
- Provide helpful replies (without pitching your product)
- Share 1-2 pieces of others' content with thoughtful commentary

**Evening (15 min):**
- Reply to any remaining comments
- Engage with any new followers (welcome DM, check their profile)
- Note content ideas inspired by conversations

### Weekly Engagement Activities
- [ ] Identify 5 new accounts to follow and engage with consistently
- [ ] Participate in 2-3 relevant Twitter Spaces, LinkedIn Live events, or community discussions
- [ ] Send 5 genuine DMs (not pitches -- congratulations, compliments, questions)
- [ ] Share 3 pieces of content from others with your own commentary
- [ ] Reply to at least 10 comments/posts from people in your target audience

## Content Batching Workflow

Creating content one post at a time is inefficient. Batch your content creation into focused sessions.

### The 2-Hour Batch Session (Do This Weekly)

**Step 1: Research & Inspiration (20 min)**
- Review top-performing posts from the past week (yours and competitors)
- Save 5-10 ideas from trending topics, conversations, and questions you've seen
- Check your content calendar themes for the week

**Step 2: Write All Posts (60 min)**
- Write all 5-7 posts for the week using your calendar template
- Focus on writing first, editing second
- Use the prompts above as starting points, then customize
- Write long-form content first (threads, carousels), then short posts

**Step 3: Create Visuals (20 min)**
- Design any graphics, carousels, or images needed
- Use templates in Canva, Figma, or your design tool of choice
- Resize for each platform's specifications

**Step 4: Schedule (15 min)**
- Load all posts into your scheduling tool (Buffer, Hootsuite, Typefully, etc.)
- Set times based on optimal posting times for each platform
- Double-check that each post is formatted correctly for its platform

**Step 5: Review (5 min)**
- Read through all scheduled posts one more time
- Check for typos, broken links, missing images
- Ensure the content ratio is balanced (not too promotional)

## Metrics to Track Per Platform

### Universal Metrics (Track on ALL Platforms)

| Metric | What It Tells You | Target Trend |
|--------|-------------------|-------------|
| **Follower Growth Rate** | Are you attracting the right audience? | Steady increase |
| **Engagement Rate** | Is your content resonating? (likes + comments + shares / impressions) | 2-5% for most platforms |
| **Click-Through Rate** | Are people taking action? | Depends on CTA type |
| **Impressions/Reach** | How many people see your content? | Increasing over time |
| **Profile Visits** | Is your content driving curiosity? | Increasing over time |
| **Saves/Bookmarks** | Is your content valuable enough to save? | Indicator of high-quality content |

### Platform-Specific Metrics

**Twitter/X:** Impressions per tweet, profile visits, link clicks, quote tweets, reply depth
**LinkedIn:** Post views, engagement rate, connection acceptance rate, profile views, search appearances
**Reddit:** Upvote ratio, comment quality, karma growth, subreddit standing
**YouTube:** Watch time (most important), CTR on thumbnails, subscriber conversion rate, audience retention curve
**TikTok:** Average watch time, completion rate, shares, profile visits, follower conversion
**Instagram:** Reach (not impressions), saves, shares to Stories, Reel plays, follower growth rate

## Scheduling Tools Comparison

| Tool | Free Tier | Best For | Key Features |
|------|-----------|----------|-------------|
| **Buffer** | 3 channels, 10 posts/channel | Beginners, simple scheduling | Clean UI, basic analytics, link shortener |
| **Typefully** | Limited | Twitter/X threads and scheduling | Thread editor, analytics, cross-posting |
| **Hootsuite** | Limited free | Teams, multi-platform | Comprehensive dashboard, team collaboration |
| **Later** | 1 profile/platform | Instagram-first | Visual planning, link-in-bio, auto-publish |
| **Native scheduling** | Free | Each platform individually | No cost, built into Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Facebook |

**Recommendation for solo founders:** Start with native scheduling (free) or Buffer's free tier. Upgrade only when you are posting consistently across 2+ platforms.
