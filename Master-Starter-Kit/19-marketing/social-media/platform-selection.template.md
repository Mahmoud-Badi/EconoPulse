## Philosophy: Start With Two

Before diving into platform profiles, internalize this: **pick two platforms and dominate them before adding a third.** Spreading across five or six platforms with mediocre content is worse than owning two with consistent, high-quality presence.

Why two?
- You can realistically produce quality content for two platforms with limited resources
- Each platform has its own culture, format, and algorithm -- learning two deeply beats dabbling in six
- You will burn out trying to be everywhere at once
- Compounding growth on one platform beats scattered impressions across many

**Your two platforms should be:** one where your audience already hangs out, and one where your content format shines.

## Platform Profiles

### 1. Twitter / X

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Monthly Active Users** | ~550M+ |
| **Primary Demographics** | 25-49 year olds, skews male, tech-savvy, urban, professional |
| **Content Formats** | Short text (up to 25K chars for premium), threads, images, polls, Spaces (audio), video clips |
| **Organic Reach** | Moderate -- algorithm favors engagement and recency. Threads and replies can go viral. Non-followers see your content if it gets engagement. |
| **Ad Options** | Promoted tweets, follower campaigns, trend takeovers, conversation ads. Minimum budgets are flexible (~$1/day). Self-serve ads platform. |
| **Best For** | Tech products, SaaS, developer tools, B2B, thought leadership, build-in-public, real-time engagement, community building among professionals |
| **Tone** | Conversational, witty, opinionated, authentic. Corporate-speak gets ignored. |

**Strengths:** Fastest feedback loop of any platform. You can have a conversation with potential users within minutes. Tech/dev community is massive and active. Build-in-public culture is strong.

**Weaknesses:** Noise level is extreme. Content lifespan is ~18 minutes. Algorithm changes frequently. Platform stability concerns.

### 2. LinkedIn

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Monthly Active Users** | ~1B+ |
| **Primary Demographics** | 25-54 year olds, professionals, decision-makers, B2B buyers, recruiters, managers |
| **Content Formats** | Text posts (up to 3,000 chars), articles, carousels (PDF), video, polls, newsletters, documents |
| **Organic Reach** | High -- one of the best organic platforms right now. Posts regularly reach 5-20x your follower count. |
| **Ad Options** | Sponsored content, InMail, lead gen forms, dynamic ads. Expensive ($5-15+ CPC) but high-intent B2B targeting. |
| **Best For** | B2B SaaS, enterprise tools, professional services, recruiting, thought leadership, fundraising visibility |
| **Tone** | Professional but increasingly personal. Storytelling and vulnerability perform well. Avoid jargon. |

**Strengths:** Highest organic reach of any major platform. Decision-makers actually use it. Content lifespan is 24-48 hours. Great for B2B lead generation.

**Weaknesses:** Can feel performative. Not ideal for consumer products. Audience expects professional context. Ads are expensive.

---

### 3. Reddit

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Monthly Active Users** | ~1.7B+ |
| **Primary Demographics** | 18-40 year olds, tech-savvy, skeptical, research-oriented, value authenticity |
| **Content Formats** | Text posts, links, images, video, AMAs, polls, comments |
| **Organic Reach** | High within relevant subreddits. Front page posts can reach millions. Subreddit-specific reach depends on community size. |
| **Ad Options** | Promoted posts, display ads. Targeting by subreddit and interest. Relatively affordable. Users are hostile to obvious ads. |
| **Best For** | Developer tools, open-source projects, niche products, market research, authentic community engagement, SEO (Reddit posts rank on Google) |
| **Tone** | Authentic, helpful, humble. Marketing-speak will get you destroyed. Self-deprecating humor works. |

**Strengths:** Incredibly engaged communities. High-intent users (they are literally searching for solutions). Great for market research. Posts have long lifespan via search. Reddit results show up prominently in Google.

**Weaknesses:** Anti-marketing culture. One wrong move and you are banned or publicly shamed. Requires genuine participation over time. Cannot automate or fake it.

---

### 4. YouTube

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Monthly Active Users** | ~2.5B+ |
| **Primary Demographics** | All ages but strong 18-49, global, seeking education and entertainment |
| **Content Formats** | Long-form video, Shorts (under 60s), live streams, community posts |
| **Organic Reach** | Strong -- algorithm recommends content to non-subscribers. Evergreen content compounds over months/years. |
| **Ad Options** | Pre-roll, mid-roll, bumper ads, discovery ads. Google Ads integration. Highly targetable. |
| **Best For** | Tutorials, product demos, educational content, explainer videos, developer tools, any product that benefits from "show don't tell" |
| **Tone** | Educational, entertaining, authentic. Production quality matters but authenticity matters more. |

**Strengths:** Second-largest search engine. Content is evergreen -- a good tutorial gets views for years. Builds deep trust through long-form content. Shorts provide discoverability.

**Weaknesses:** Highest production effort of any platform. Slow to build an audience. Requires consistent publishing (weekly minimum). Steep learning curve for video editing.

---

### 5. TikTok

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Monthly Active Users** | ~1.5B+ |
| **Primary Demographics** | 16-34 year olds, increasingly older. Global, entertainment-first, short attention spans. |
| **Content Formats** | Short video (15s-10min), live streams, photo carousels |
| **Organic Reach** | Extremely high -- the algorithm shows your content to non-followers aggressively. Small accounts can go viral on their first post. |
| **Ad Options** | In-feed ads, branded hashtag challenges, top-view ads, spark ads (boosting organic). Affordable CPMs. |
| **Best For** | Consumer products, mobile apps, any product targeting younger demographics, visual products, brand awareness |
| **Tone** | Casual, entertaining, trend-aware, raw/unpolished. Over-produced content underperforms. |

**Strengths:** Highest organic reach of any platform. Algorithm does not care about follower count. Great for brand awareness. Forces you to communicate value quickly.

**Weaknesses:** Audience skews young. Content lifespan is short. Trends move fast. Regulatory uncertainty in some markets. Hard to drive direct conversions.

---

### 6. Instagram

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Monthly Active Users** | ~2B+ |
| **Primary Demographics** | 18-44 year olds, slight female skew, visual-first, lifestyle-oriented |
| **Content Formats** | Photos, Reels (short video), Stories (24hr), carousels, live streams, DMs |
| **Organic Reach** | Low for photos, moderate-high for Reels. Algorithm heavily favors video content (Reels). |
| **Ad Options** | Feed ads, Story ads, Reel ads, shopping tags, influencer partnerships. Meta Ads Manager (shared with Facebook). |
| **Best For** | Visual products, design tools, consumer apps, lifestyle brands, influencer marketing, D2C products |
| **Tone** | Aesthetic, aspirational, authentic, visual-first. Carousels work well for educational content. |

**Strengths:** Strong visual storytelling. Reels can reach massive audiences. Shopping integration for e-commerce. Good for brand building.

**Weaknesses:** Organic reach for static posts is declining. Algorithm changes constantly. Hard for non-visual products. Links only in bio (no clickable links in posts).

---

### 7. Facebook

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Monthly Active Users** | ~3B+ |
| **Primary Demographics** | 25-65+, broadest demographic range, increasingly older skew |
| **Content Formats** | Text, photos, video, live streams, groups, events, marketplace |
| **Organic Reach** | Very low for pages (<2% of followers). Groups have better reach. |
| **Ad Options** | Most sophisticated ad platform. Detailed targeting, lookalike audiences, retargeting. Shared with Instagram via Meta Ads Manager. |
| **Best For** | Paid advertising (especially retargeting), Facebook Groups for community, local businesses, products targeting 35+ audience, e-commerce |
| **Tone** | Varies by audience. Groups are conversational. Page content needs to be highly engaging to beat the algorithm. |

**Strengths:** Largest user base. Best advertising platform for targeting and retargeting. Facebook Groups are excellent for community. Events feature is useful.

**Weaknesses:** Organic reach is nearly dead for business pages. Younger demographics are leaving. Pay-to-play platform. Privacy concerns affect ad targeting.

---

### 8. Discord

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Monthly Active Users** | ~200M+ |
| **Primary Demographics** | 16-35 year olds, gamers, tech-savvy, developer communities, crypto/web3 |
| **Content Formats** | Text chat, voice channels, video, forums, stage channels (events), bots |
| **Organic Reach** | Not applicable -- Discord is a closed community platform. Growth comes from inviting members. |
| **Ad Options** | No native ads. Growth is organic through external promotion. |
| **Best For** | Developer tools, gaming products, open-source communities, power-user engagement, real-time support, beta testing |
| **Tone** | Casual, real-time, community-driven. More like a group chat than a broadcast platform. |

**Strengths:** Deep engagement. Real-time feedback. Strong sense of community. Great for support and beta testing. Bot integration for automation.

**Weaknesses:** Not a discovery platform -- you need to drive members from elsewhere. Moderation is time-intensive. Can be overwhelming for non-technical users. Not indexed by search engines.

---

### 9. Threads

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Monthly Active Users** | ~200M+ |
| **Primary Demographics** | Instagram's existing user base transitioning to text. 25-44, slightly more mainstream than Twitter/X. |
| **Content Formats** | Short text (500 chars), images, carousels, polls |
| **Organic Reach** | Currently high -- Meta is boosting content to grow the platform. |
| **Ad Options** | Limited -- ads are being rolled out. Will integrate with Meta Ads Manager. |
| **Best For** | Brands with existing Instagram audiences, conversational content, early adopters wanting to establish presence |
| **Tone** | Casual, conversational, lighter than Twitter/X. Less political, more lifestyle. |

**Strengths:** Connected to Instagram (easy cross-posting). Currently boosted reach. Less hostile environment than Twitter/X. Growing user base.

**Weaknesses:** Still maturing. Algorithm and culture are still forming. Limited features. Uncertain long-term viability. No hashtag search (yet). Less tech/dev community compared to Twitter/X.

---

### 10. Mastodon / Fediverse

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Monthly Active Users** | ~10M+ (across the Fediverse) |
| **Primary Demographics** | Tech-savvy, privacy-conscious, developers, open-source advocates, 25-50 |
| **Content Formats** | Text (500+ chars depending on instance), images, polls, video |
| **Organic Reach** | Moderate within federated instances. No algorithm -- chronological timeline. Boosts (retweets) are the main discovery mechanism. |
| **Ad Options** | None. The platform is philosophically opposed to advertising. |
| **Best For** | Open-source projects, privacy-focused tools, developer tools, building credibility in the open-source community |
| **Tone** | Thoughtful, technical, anti-corporate. Genuine conversations valued. |

**Strengths:** Highly engaged tech/FOSS audience. No algorithm manipulation. Authentic community. Great for open-source credibility.

**Weaknesses:** Tiny user base. Fragmented across instances. Steep onboarding curve for new users. Limited growth potential. No ads or paid promotion options.

3. **Discord** -- Build a community server for support, feedback, and power users.
4. **YouTube** -- Technical tutorials and integration guides have long-tail search value.
