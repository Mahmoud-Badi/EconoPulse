## Why LinkedIn Matters

LinkedIn has the highest organic reach of any major social platform right now. A post from an account with 500 connections can regularly reach 5,000-50,000 people. For B2B products, there is no better platform. Decision-makers -- the people who approve budgets and sign contracts -- actually use LinkedIn daily.

Key stats:
- 4 out of 5 LinkedIn members drive business decisions
- LinkedIn generates 80% of B2B social media leads
- Content on LinkedIn has a 24-48 hour lifespan (vs. 18 minutes on Twitter)
- The platform is still under-saturated with quality content creators

## Personal Profile Optimization

On LinkedIn, personal profiles outperform company pages by 5-10x in reach and engagement. Your personal profile is your most powerful marketing asset.

### Headline Formula
Your headline appears everywhere -- in search results, comments, connection requests. It should communicate value, not just your title.

**Formula:** [Role] at [Company] | [What you help people do] | [Keyword for search]

**Examples:**
- "Founder at {{PROJECT_NAME}} | Helping {{TARGET_AUDIENCE}} {{ACHIEVE_OUTCOME}} | {{INDUSTRY}} | {{KEYWORD}}"
- "Building {{PROJECT_NAME}} | {{VALUE_PROP}} for {{TARGET_AUDIENCE}} | Former {{PREVIOUS_ROLE}}"
- "CEO & Co-founder, {{PROJECT_NAME}} | Making {{PROBLEM_DOMAIN}} easier for {{TARGET_AUDIENCE}}"

### Profile Photo
- Professional headshot, facing camera, smiling or approachable
- Plain or blurred background
- High resolution (minimum 400x400)
- Same photo as other social profiles for cross-platform recognition

### Banner Image
- Show your product, value proposition, or a key visual
- Include your product URL or tagline
- 1584x396 pixels recommended
- Update for launches and milestones

### About Section (Summary)
This is your 2,600-character pitch. Write it in first person. Tell a story.

**Structure:**
1. **Hook** (first 2 lines -- this is what shows before "see more"): Bold statement about the problem you solve
2. **The problem** (2-3 sentences): What your target audience struggles with
3. **Your story** (3-4 sentences): Why you care about this problem, your journey
4. **The solution** (2-3 sentences): What {{PROJECT_NAME}} does and how it helps
5. **Social proof** (1-2 sentences): Numbers, testimonials, logos
6. **CTA** (1 sentence): What you want the reader to do next

**Template:**
```

I know because I lived it. As a {{YOUR_PREVIOUS_ROLE}}, I spent {{PAINFUL_EXPERIENCE}}.

That's why I built {{PROJECT_NAME}} -- {{VALUE_PROP_ONE_SENTENCE}}.

Today, {{PROJECT_NAME}} helps {{NUMBER}} {{TARGET_AUDIENCE}} {{ACHIEVE_OUTCOME}}.

{{SOCIAL_PROOF_STATEMENT}}
```

## Content Types on LinkedIn

### 1. Text-Only Posts (Highest Organic Reach)
Plain text posts consistently outperform other formats in reach. The algorithm favors content that keeps people on the platform (no external links).

**Best for:** Stories, lessons, opinions, frameworks, lists

### 2. Carousel Posts (PDF Documents)
Upload a PDF and it becomes a swipeable carousel. These drive high dwell time (the algorithm loves dwell time).

**Best for:** Step-by-step tutorials, frameworks, checklists, data presentations, before/after comparisons

**Tool:** Create carousels in Canva (use LinkedIn carousel template), export as PDF

### 3. Native Video
Video uploaded directly to LinkedIn (not YouTube links). Keep it under 3 minutes for best performance.

**Best for:** Product demos, talking-head insights, customer testimonials, behind-the-scenes

### 4. Polls
Simple polls with 2-4 options. Great for engagement and sparking conversation in comments.

**Best for:** Market research, conversation starters, gauging audience opinions

### 5. Articles / Newsletters
Long-form content published on LinkedIn. Newsletters allow subscribers to get notifications.

**Best for:** Thought leadership, comprehensive guides, industry analysis. Lower reach than posts but deeper engagement.

### 6. Documents
Upload PDFs, slides, or documents. Different from carousels -- more formal content.

**Best for:** White papers, research reports, presentations, detailed guides

## Post Writing Formula

### The LinkedIn Post Structure

**Line 1-2: The Hook**
This is the ONLY thing people see before they click "see more." It must stop the scroll.

**Hook formulas:**
- "I [did something unexpected]. Here's what happened."
- "Most people think [common belief]. They're wrong."
- "I almost [bad outcome]. Then I discovered [insight]."
- "[Strong statistic or number]. Let that sink in."
- "Stop [common mistake]. Do this instead."
- "I spent [time] studying [topic]. Here's what I found."

**Body: Story or Value**
- Keep paragraphs to 1-2 sentences
- Use line breaks liberally (dense text gets skipped)
- Include a personal story or specific example
- Be concrete -- use numbers, names, dates (not vague generalities)
- One idea per post (not three half-developed ideas)

**Ending: CTA or Question**
End with something that drives engagement:
- Ask a question (this dramatically increases comments)
- Request agreement/disagreement
- Ask people to share their own experience
- Direct CTA (link in comments, follow for more, check out X)

### The Algorithm Signals
LinkedIn's algorithm prioritizes these signals:

1. **Dwell time:** How long people spend reading your post. Write long enough to be valuable, formatted for easy reading.
2. **Early engagement:** The first 60-90 minutes matter most. If your post gets quick engagement, it gets shown to more people.
3. **Comments > Reactions:** Comments (especially long ones and conversations) signal high quality to the algorithm.
4. **Connection signals:** Content from people you interact with regularly appears more often. Engage with your target audience consistently.
5. **Native content:** Posts without external links perform better. Put links in the first comment instead.

### Formatting Tips
- Use single-sentence paragraphs
- Add white space between every 1-2 lines
- Use emojis sparingly as bullet points (not decoratively)
- Bold key phrases using Unicode bold text generators if needed
- Keep posts between 800-1,500 characters for optimal engagement (not too short, not too long)

## Posting Frequency and Best Times

### Frequency
- **Minimum:** 3 posts per week (Tue, Wed, Thu)
- **Optimal:** 5 posts per week (weekdays)
- **Maximum useful:** 1-2 posts per day (more than this and you compete with yourself)

### Best Posting Times
| Day | Best Times (Your Audience's Timezone) | Notes |
|-----|---------------------------------------|-------|
| Monday | 7:30-8:30 AM | People catching up, lower competition |
| Tuesday | 7:00-9:00 AM, 12:00 PM | Strong engagement day |
| Wednesday | 7:00-9:00 AM, 12:00 PM | Peak LinkedIn day |
| Thursday | 7:00-9:00 AM, 12:00 PM | Second-best day |
| Friday | 7:00-8:00 AM | Lower engagement but less competition |
| Saturday-Sunday | Avoid (or test) | 70-80% lower engagement |

### The First-Hour Strategy
When you post, stay active on LinkedIn for 60 minutes:
- Reply to every comment immediately (this triggers the algorithm)
- Engage with 5-10 other posts (increases your visibility)
- Reply to comments with questions (starts deeper threads that boost the post)

## Engagement Strategy

### Commenting on Others' Posts
This is the #1 underrated growth strategy on LinkedIn. A thoughtful comment on a high-reach post exposes you to thousands of people.

**Rules for effective commenting:**
- Add genuine value (not "Great post!" or emoji-only reactions)
- Share your own perspective or experience related to the topic
- Ask a thoughtful follow-up question
- Keep comments 3-5 sentences (long enough to be valuable, short enough to be read)
- Comment on posts from people your target audience follows

**Target:** 10-15 genuine comments per day on other people's posts.

### DM Networking Strategy
LinkedIn DMs are powerful for B2B -- but most people do it wrong (immediate pitch = blocked).

**The Right Approach:**
1. Engage with their content publicly for 1-2 weeks first
2. Send a connection request with a personalized note (reference a specific post or shared interest)
3. After they accept, send a warm DM: "Hey [Name], loved your post about [Topic]. I'm building something in the same space -- would love to connect."
4. Build rapport over 2-3 exchanges before ever mentioning your product
5. When you do mention it, frame it as relevance: "Given your interest in [Topic], you might find [Feature] interesting"

**Connection Request Template:**
```
Hi {{CONTACT_NAME}},

I've been following your posts about {{TOPIC}} -- your recent piece on {{SPECIFIC_POST}} really resonated.

I'm building {{PROJECT_NAME}} ({{SHORT_DESCRIPTION}}) and would love to connect with others in the {{INDUSTRY}} space.

Looking forward to exchanging ideas.

{{YOUR_NAME}}
```

## LinkedIn Outreach Strategy

### Warm Outreach Sequence
For leads who have engaged with your content or visited your profile:

1. **Connect** with a personalized note referencing their engagement
2. **Thank them** for connecting (genuine, no pitch)
3. **Share value** -- send a relevant article, resource, or insight (3-5 days later)
4. **Soft mention** -- reference your product in context of a problem they posted about (1 week later)
5. **Direct ask** -- propose a call, demo, or trial (only after establishing rapport)

### Cold Outreach (Use Sparingly)
Cold outreach on LinkedIn works when ultra-targeted and personalized:
- Research the person's profile, posts, and company
- Reference something specific about them (not generic)
- Lead with value, not a pitch
- Keep the first message to 3-4 sentences max
- Expect a 10-20% response rate at best

## LinkedIn Ads Overview

### When to Invest in LinkedIn Ads
- You have validated product-market fit organically
- Your average deal size is $1,000+/year (LinkedIn CPCs are $5-15+)
- You have a clear ICP that maps to LinkedIn targeting criteria (job title, company size, industry)
- You have a content library or landing pages to drive traffic to

### Ad Formats
| Format | Best For | Min Budget |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| Sponsored Content (feed ads) | Awareness, content promotion | $10-50/day |
| Lead Gen Forms | Collecting leads without landing page | $20-50/day |
| Message Ads (InMail) | Direct outreach at scale | $25-50/day |
| Dynamic Ads | Personalized ads | $10-25/day |
| Text Ads | Low-budget awareness | $5-10/day |

### LinkedIn Ads Tips
- Start with Sponsored Content to promote your best-performing organic posts
- Use Lead Gen Forms (pre-filled with LinkedIn data) for highest conversion rates
- Target by job title + company size + industry (not just one dimension)
- A/B test ad creative and audience segments
- Retarget website visitors with LinkedIn Insight Tag

## LinkedIn Newsletter Feature

LinkedIn Newsletters allow you to publish recurring content that subscribers get notified about. This is essentially a free email list built into LinkedIn.

### Setting Up
- Go to your profile > "Write article" > Create newsletter
- Choose a name, description, and publishing cadence
- Your connections are automatically invited to subscribe

### Newsletter Strategy
- Publish weekly or bi-weekly (consistency matters)
- Use the newsletter for deeper content than regular posts
- Each newsletter edition should be 800-2,000 words
- Include a CTA to {{PROJECT_NAME}} in every edition
- Cross-promote: mention the newsletter in regular posts

## Employee Advocacy

If you have a team, their combined reach likely exceeds your company page's reach by 10x.

### How to Enable Employee Sharing
1. Create shareable content and send it to the team (with suggested copy they can customize)
2. Make it easy -- do not expect employees to write from scratch
3. Celebrate team members who share (recognition, not mandates)
4. Lead by example -- if the founder shares consistently, the team follows
5. Use tools like Shield, Hootsuite Amplify, or simply a Slack channel with pre-written posts

### What Employees Can Share
- Company milestones and achievements
- Their own work and contributions (with company context)
- Industry insights from their expertise area
- Job openings (personal referrals convert better)
- Customer success stories they were involved in
