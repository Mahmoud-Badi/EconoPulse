# Terms of Service — Marketing Claims

> Guide to marketing-related terms, refund policies, trial terms, and promotional compliance.

---

## Overview

Marketing claims made on your website, in ads, and in emails are legally binding. This guide covers the terms and disclosures that protect both you and your customers, and ensures your marketing claims are defensible.

---

## Truthful Advertising

### What You Can Claim
- Factual product capabilities that are verifiable
- Performance metrics you can substantiate with data
- Customer results that are documented and typical (or disclosed as atypical)
- Comparison claims that are specific, accurate, and verifiable

### What You Cannot Claim
- Unsubstantiated superlatives ("the best," "the fastest," "#1") without evidence
- Implied guarantees you can't deliver
- Results that aren't typical unless disclosed ("Results not typical. Individual results may vary.")
- Features that don't exist yet as if they're currently available

### Safe Practices
- Use "up to" rather than absolute claims ("saves up to 10 hours/week" vs "saves 10 hours/week")
- Qualify comparative claims ("50% faster than manual methods based on [study/data]")
- Date claims that may change ("As of [date], we serve 10,000+ users")

## Refund Policies

### What to Include
- Refund window (7 days, 14 days, 30 days, or none)
- Process for requesting a refund (email, form, automated)
- What's refundable (subscription fees, one-time purchases, credits)
- What's NOT refundable (setup fees, consumed usage, custom work)
- Timeline for receiving the refund (5-10 business days)
- Partial refund policy for mid-cycle cancellations

### How to Present Refund Policies in Marketing
- "30-day money-back guarantee" must actually be no-questions-asked for 30 days
- "Risk-free" implies a full refund option — don't use it if refunds are conditional
- "Satisfaction guaranteed" is vague — specify what happens if they're not satisfied
- Place refund policy link near any purchase CTA

### Template Language
```
Money-Back Guarantee: If you're not satisfied within [X] days of purchase,
contact us at [email] for a full refund. No questions asked.

Refund processing time: 5-10 business days to your original payment method.
```

## Trial Terms

### Auto-Renewal Disclosure (Required)
If a free trial converts to a paid subscription automatically, you MUST disclose:
- That billing will begin automatically at trial end
- The exact amount that will be charged
- The billing frequency (monthly, annual)
- How to cancel before being charged
- This must be visible BEFORE the user enters payment information

### Free Trial Best Practices
- Clearly state trial length ("14-day free trial")
- Send reminder email 2-3 days before trial ends
- Make cancellation easy and obvious
- Don't hide the cancel button
- State whether payment info is required upfront

### Template Language
```
Start your [X]-day free trial. No credit card required.

— OR —

Start your [X]-day free trial. Your card will be charged $[X]/month
after the trial ends. Cancel anytime before [date] to avoid charges.
```

## Subscription Terms

### What to Disclose
- Billing frequency (monthly, annual, custom)
- Auto-renewal terms (subscriptions renew unless canceled)
- Price change notice requirements (30 days notice minimum recommended)
- Cancellation process and timing (effective immediately or end of period)
- What happens to data after cancellation (retention period, export options)

### Pricing Display Requirements
- Show the per-month price even for annual plans
- Clearly distinguish between promotional and regular pricing
- If showing "per user" pricing, clarify minimum seats if applicable
- Display currency and indicate if taxes are additional

## Money-Back Guarantees

### Making a Guarantee
- Specify exact terms: duration, conditions, process
- "Money-back guarantee" with no details is legally risky
- Honor guarantee requests promptly — delays erode trust and invite chargebacks
- Track guarantee claims as a product quality signal

### Guarantee Types
| Type | Marketing Copy | Legal Requirement |
|------|---------------|-------------------|
| Unconditional | "100% money-back, no questions asked" | Must refund for any reason within window |
| Conditional | "Full refund if [condition] within 30 days" | Must refund if condition is met |
| Satisfaction | "Satisfaction guaranteed or your money back" | Must refund if customer claims dissatisfaction |
| Performance | "Get [result] or your money back" | Must refund if result not achieved |

## Promotional Terms

### Discount Offers
- Specify expiration date for limited-time offers
- "50% off" must reference a genuine regular price (not an inflated "original" price)
- Clearly state what's discounted (first month, first year, forever)
- "Lifetime deal" means the lifetime of the product or the customer's account — define which

### Contest and Sweepstakes
- Require official rules (even for small giveaways)
- "No purchase necessary" is required in the US for sweepstakes
- State eligibility requirements (age, geography)
- Clearly state prize, odds, and selection method
- Different rules apply per state/country — consult local requirements

### Urgency and Scarcity
- "Limited time" must actually be limited — don't run "limited time" sales permanently
- "Only X spots left" must be truthful — false scarcity is deceptive
- Countdown timers must reflect real deadlines, not reset for each visitor
- "Early bird pricing" should have a genuine end date

## Testimonial and Review Disclaimers

- Testimonials must be from real customers (not fabricated)
- If results are atypical, add: "Results may vary. These testimonials reflect individual experiences."
- If the testimonial provider received compensation (free product, payment), disclose it
- Don't edit testimonials to change meaning — grammatical fixes are fine
- See `advertising-compliance.md` for full FTC testimonial requirements

## International Considerations

### EU Consumer Rights
- 14-day right of withdrawal for digital purchases (with specific exceptions for digital content)
- Clear cancellation process required
- Pre-contractual information must be provided before purchase

### UK Consumer Rights
- Similar to EU with 14-day cooling-off period
- Digital content exceptions apply when consumer gives explicit consent

### Australian Consumer Law
- Refunds required for products not of acceptable quality
- "No refund" signs are illegal for faulty products
- Consumer guarantees cannot be excluded

## Checklist for Marketing Terms Compliance

- [ ] Pricing page shows clear total cost, billing frequency, and auto-renewal terms
- [ ] Free trial terms state exactly when and how much will be charged
- [ ] Refund policy is linked from pricing page and checkout flow
- [ ] Promotional offers have clear start/end dates and conditions
- [ ] Testimonials are from real customers with appropriate disclaimers
- [ ] "Lifetime deal" has clear definition of "lifetime"
- [ ] Cancellation process is easy to find and execute
- [ ] Money-back guarantee terms are specific and honored
- [ ] Email reminder sent before trial-to-paid conversion
- [ ] International consumer rights addressed if selling globally
