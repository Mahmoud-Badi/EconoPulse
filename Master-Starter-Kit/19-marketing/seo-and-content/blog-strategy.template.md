| Positioning | Description | Best For |
|-------------|-------------|----------|
| **Product blog** | Focused on your product, features, updates, tutorials | Customer education, product-led growth |

## Post Type Selection

### Post Types and When to Use Each

#### 1. How-To Guides

**What**: Step-by-step instructions for completing a task.
**SEO value**: Very high — targets "how to" queries, which have massive search volume.
**Lead gen value**: Medium — readers are seeking information, not necessarily a product.
**Example**: "How to Set Up Automated Deployments in 10 Minutes"

**Best practices:**
- Number the steps clearly
- Include screenshots or code examples for each step
- Show the expected output/result at each step
- Include troubleshooting tips for common issues
- End with a clear "what's next" section

#### 2. Listicles

**What**: Numbered lists of tips, tools, examples, or resources.
**SEO value**: High — targets "best X" and "top X" queries.
**Lead gen value**: Medium — good for awareness, less for direct conversion.
**Example**: "12 Developer Productivity Tools You Need in 2026"

**Best practices:**
- Use odd numbers (7, 9, 11 outperform even numbers in click-through rates)
- Start with the most valuable items (not the weakest)
- Include your product naturally (if relevant) — do not force it
- Add a brief explanation for each item (not just a list of names)

#### 3. Comparison Posts (X vs Y)

**What**: Head-to-head comparison of two or more products/approaches.
**SEO value**: Very high — targets commercial-intent "vs" queries.
**Lead gen value**: Very high — readers are actively evaluating options.
**Example**: "GitHub Actions vs CircleCI: Which CI/CD Tool Should You Choose?"

**Best practices:**
- Be genuinely fair and balanced (biased comparisons lose trust)
- Use a comparison table with clear criteria
- Declare your affiliation transparently
- Address the specific scenarios where each option wins
- Include a "who should use which" recommendation

#### 4. Case Studies

**What**: Detailed customer success stories with specific results.
**SEO value**: Medium — more effective for direct conversion than search traffic.
**Lead gen value**: Very high — the most persuasive content type for mid-funnel prospects.
**Example**: "How Acme Corp Reduced Deploy Time by 90% with {{PROJECT_NAME}}"

**Best practices:**
- Follow the Challenge → Solution → Results structure
- Include specific numbers (time saved, money saved, % improvement)
- Use direct quotes from the customer
- Include the customer's name, title, photo, and company logo
- End with a CTA that connects to the reader's situation

#### 5. Data & Research Posts

**What**: Original research, surveys, data analysis, or industry reports.
**SEO value**: Very high — attracts backlinks because journalists and bloggers cite original data.
**Lead gen value**: High — can be gated as downloadable reports.
**Example**: "We Analyzed 10,000 Deployments: Here's What the Best Teams Do Differently"

**Best practices:**
- Use real data (do not fabricate or exaggerate)
- Visualize data with charts and graphs
- Highlight surprising or counterintuitive findings
- Make the data citable (specific numbers, clear methodology)
- Offer a downloadable PDF version (lead magnet)

#### 6. Opinion & Thought Leadership

**What**: Your unique perspective on industry trends, practices, or debates.
**SEO value**: Low-Medium — less keyword-targeted, but can go viral.
**Lead gen value**: Low — but builds brand authority and attracts talent.
**Example**: "Why We Believe Infrastructure Should Be Boring"

**Best practices:**
- Have a genuine, defensible opinion (not lukewarm consensus)
- Support your opinion with evidence or experience
- Acknowledge counterarguments
- Keep it concise (800-1,500 words — opinion pieces should be tight)
- Write in first person (personal, authentic tone)

#### 7. Industry Analysis & Trends

**What**: Analysis of industry developments, market shifts, or emerging trends.
**SEO value**: Medium — can rank for "[industry] trends [year]" queries.
**Lead gen value**: Medium — positions you as an informed player.
**Example**: "The State of DevOps in 2026: 5 Trends That Will Shape the Industry"

**Best practices:**
- Back up claims with data sources
- Provide actionable takeaways (not just observations)
- Update annually for evergreen SEO value
- Include quotes from industry experts if possible

## Blog Post Structure Template

### Universal Post Structure

Every blog post should follow this skeleton:

```markdown
# [Post Title — Include Primary Keyword]

[Hook: 2-3 sentences that grab attention and state why this matters]

[Transition: Brief overview of what the post covers]

**In this post, you'll learn:**
- [Key takeaway 1]
- [Key takeaway 2]
- [Key takeaway 3]

---

## [H2: First Major Section]

[Content — 200-400 words per H2 section]

### [H3: Subsection if needed]

[Supporting detail, examples, data]

---

## [H2: Second Major Section]

[Content]

---

## [H2: Third Major Section]

[Content]

---

## [H2: Additional Sections as Needed]

[Content]

---

## Conclusion / Key Takeaways

[Summary: Restate the 2-3 main points]

[CTA: What should the reader do next?]

---

*[Author bio with photo, name, and brief credentials]*
```

### Hook Intro Patterns

The first 2-3 sentences determine whether someone reads or bounces. Use one of these patterns:

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| **Surprising stat** | "The average developer wastes 6 hours per week on deployment tasks. Here's how to get those hours back." |
| **Pain point** | "If you've ever deployed on a Friday and spent your weekend fixing it, this post is for you." |
| **Story** | "Last month, our team shipped a bug to production that cost us $12,000 in lost revenue. Here's what we changed." |
| **Bold claim** | "Most CI/CD tutorials are wrong. They teach you the syntax without teaching you the strategy." |
| **Question** | "What if your deployments took 3 minutes instead of 3 hours?" |
| **Contrarian** | "Everyone says you need microservices. We disagree — and here's why." |

### CTA Templates for Blog Posts

End every post with a clear call to action:

```
[For product-related posts:]
"Ready to try this for yourself? Get started with {{PROJECT_NAME}}
— free for {{TRIAL_PERIOD}}."
[CTA Button]

[For educational posts:]

{{PROJECT_NAME}} and see the difference."

[For thought leadership:]

[Twitter thread link] or [LinkedIn post link]."
```

## Publishing Frequency Recommendations

### By Team Size

| Team Size | Recommended Frequency | Content Types |
|-----------|----------------------|---------------|
| **Solo founder** | 1 post/week (or biweekly) | Focus on SEO-driven how-to guides and comparisons |
| **1-2 people** | 1-2 posts/week | How-to guides, comparisons, occasional thought leadership |
| **Small team (3-5)** | 2-3 posts/week | Full content calendar with all post types |
| **Content team (5+)** | 4-5 posts/week | Daily publishing with heavy distribution |

### Quality vs. Quantity

**The golden rule**: One great post per week beats five mediocre posts. If you cannot publish weekly, publish biweekly — but make every post count.

**What "great" means:**
- More comprehensive than competing articles on the same topic
- Well-structured with clear headings and visual breaks
- Includes specific examples, data, or code samples
- Properly optimized for SEO
- Promoted across multiple channels

### Consistency Matters More Than Frequency

Publishing 1 post every week for a year (52 posts) beats publishing 5 posts per week for 2 months then stopping (40 posts). Search engines and audiences reward consistency.

**Sustainability test**: Can you maintain this frequency for 12 months without burning out? If not, reduce frequency.

## Blog Post SEO Optimization Checklist

Run this checklist before publishing EVERY post.

### Pre-Write
- [ ] Primary keyword identified (with search volume and difficulty data)
- [ ] Search intent analyzed (informational, commercial, etc.)
- [ ] Top 5 competing articles reviewed (understand what to beat)
- [ ] Content angle determined (what makes your post unique/better)

### Writing
- [ ] Primary keyword in the title (H1)
- [ ] Primary keyword in the first 100 words
- [ ] Primary keyword in at least one H2
- [ ] Secondary keywords included in H2s and body text naturally
- [ ] Content is longer and more comprehensive than top competitors
- [ ] Unique value added (original insights, data, examples, code)

### On-Page SEO
- [ ] Title tag: Under 60 characters, includes primary keyword
- [ ] Meta description: Under 155 characters, includes primary keyword, compelling
- [ ] URL slug: Short, includes primary keyword, uses hyphens
- [ ] Heading hierarchy: H1 → H2 → H3 (no skipping)
- [ ] Internal links: 3-5 links to related pages on your site
- [ ] External links: 2-5 links to authoritative sources (builds trust)
- [ ] Image alt text: Descriptive, includes keyword where natural
- [ ] Image file names: Descriptive (not IMG_12345.jpg)

### Technical
- [ ] Canonical URL set (prevents duplicate content issues)
- [ ] Open Graph tags configured (title, description, image for social sharing)
- [ ] Twitter Card meta tags configured
- [ ] Schema markup applied (Article, HowTo, FAQ — whatever fits)
- [ ] Page loads in under 3 seconds
- [ ] Mobile responsive

### Readability
- [ ] Reading level: 8th grade or below (check with Hemingway App)
- [ ] Paragraphs: 3 sentences or fewer
- [ ] Subheadings: Every 200-300 words
- [ ] Bullet points: Used for lists of 3+ items
- [ ] Bold text: Key phrases highlighted for scanners
- [ ] Images/visuals: Break up text every 300-400 words

## Blog Promotion Checklist

Publishing is only 50% of the work. Promotion is the other 50%.

### Owned Channels
- [ ] Share on Twitter/X (create a thread or highlight key takeaway)
- [ ] Share on LinkedIn (personal profile AND company page)
- [ ] Send to email subscriber list
- [ ] Share in your community (Discord, Slack, forum)
- [ ] Post on your company's social media accounts
- [ ] Add to your email signature (link to latest post)

### Earned Channels
- [ ] Cross-post on dev.to (with canonical URL back to your blog)
- [ ] Cross-post on Medium (with canonical URL back to your blog)
- [ ] Submit to Hacker News (if technically interesting)
- [ ] Post on relevant Reddit subreddits (add genuine value, not just a link)
- [ ] Answer related questions on Quora with a link to your post
- [ ] Share in relevant Facebook groups (if your audience uses them)
- [ ] Share in relevant Slack/Discord communities (where allowed)
- [ ] Email influencers/experts mentioned in the post (they may share it)

### Amplification
- [ ] Reshare on social media with a different angle (1 week later)
- [ ] Include in weekly/monthly newsletter roundup
- [ ] Internal link from older related blog posts
- [ ] Repurpose into other formats (video, infographic, carousel)
- [ ] Promote in webinars or podcasts (reference the post)

### Paid Promotion (Optional)
- [ ] Boost on LinkedIn (target your ideal audience)
- [ ] Promote on Twitter/X (promote tweet to target audience)
- [ ] Facebook/Instagram ads (retarget website visitors with content)
- [ ] Consider Quuu Promote or similar content promotion platforms

## Guest Posting Strategy

### Why Guest Post?

1. **Backlinks**: Guest posts include a link back to your site (SEO value)
2. **Audience exposure**: Reach an established audience you do not have
3. **Authority**: Being published on respected sites builds credibility
4. **Relationships**: Build connections with editors and other writers

### Finding Guest Posting Opportunities

**Search queries:**
- "[your industry] guest post"
- "[your industry] write for us"
- "[your industry] contribute"
- "[your industry] submit article"
- "[your industry] guest author"

**Evaluate opportunities by:**
- Domain authority (DA 30+ is worth pursuing)
- Audience overlap with your target audience
- Engagement on existing posts (comments, shares)
- Whether they include dofollow backlinks
- Publication quality and reputation

### Pitching Template

```
Subject: Guest Post Idea: [Specific, Compelling Title]

Hi [Editor Name],

I'm [Your Name], [Your Role] at [Your Company]. I've been reading
[Publication Name] for [time period] and particularly enjoyed your
recent piece on [specific article].

I'd love to contribute an article on [topic]. Here's my pitch:

**Title**: [Proposed Title]
**Angle**: [2-3 sentences on your unique angle]
**Key takeaways for readers**:
1. [Takeaway 1]
2. [Takeaway 2]
3. [Takeaway 3]

**Why I'm qualified**: [1-2 sentences on your expertise]

**Previous bylines**: [Links to 2-3 published articles]

I'll follow your editorial guidelines and am happy to revise
based on feedback. Would this be of interest?

Best,
[Your Name]
[Your website]
```

### Guest Post Best Practices

1. **Study the publication**: Read 5-10 recent posts to match tone and style
2. **Pitch original ideas**: Do not pitch topics they have already covered
3. **Write your best work**: A guest post is your introduction to a new audience
4. **Include a relevant bio link**: Your author bio should link to your product or a relevant landing page
5. **Promote the published post**: Share it widely (publications appreciate authors who promote)
6. **Build the relationship**: Thank the editor, engage with comments, pitch again in 2-3 months

## Blog Design & UX

### Blog Page Elements

| Element | Purpose | Recommendations |
|---------|---------|-----------------|
| **Featured image** | Visual hook, social sharing thumbnail | Custom graphics or relevant screenshots. 1200x630px for social sharing. |
| **Author info** | Credibility, personal connection | Photo, name, brief bio. Link to author page with full bio. |
| **Publish date** | Freshness signal | Show month and year. Update date if content is refreshed. |
| **Reading time** | Set expectations | "5 min read" helps visitors decide to commit |
| **Table of contents** | Navigation for long posts | Clickable, sticky sidebar TOC for posts over 2,000 words |
| **In-post CTAs** | Conversion points | 1-2 contextual CTAs within the post (not just at the end) |
| **Sidebar** | Secondary CTAs, related posts | Newsletter signup, popular posts, product CTA |
| **Related posts** | Keep visitors on site | Show 3-4 related posts at the end of each article |
| **Social share buttons** | Encourage sharing | Twitter, LinkedIn, copy link. Do not include every platform. |
| **Comments** | Community engagement | Optional — only if you will moderate and respond |
| **Category/tags** | Organization, internal linking | Use consistent categories (aligned with content pillars) |

### Reading Experience

- **Max content width**: 680-720px (ideal line length for readability)
- **Font size**: 18-20px for body text on desktop
- **Line height**: 1.6-1.8x font size
- **Font choice**: Serif or clean sans-serif (avoid decorative fonts)
- **Background**: White or very light gray (avoid dark backgrounds for reading)
- **Code blocks**: Syntax-highlighted, with copy button (for technical blogs)
- **Dark mode**: Support user's system preference with `prefers-color-scheme`

### Mobile Blog UX

- Single column layout (no sidebar on mobile)
- Full-width images
- Collapsible table of contents
- Sticky social share bar (or bottom of post only)
- Large, tappable internal links
- Readable code blocks with horizontal scroll

|--------|------------------|------|--------|
| **Organic traffic** | How well your content ranks in search | GA4 | Growing month-over-month |
| **Keyword rankings** | Which queries your posts rank for | Search Console | Page 1 for target keywords |
| **Time on page** | Are people reading your content? | GA4 | 3-5 minutes (for 2,000-word posts) |
| **Bounce rate** | Do visitors engage or leave immediately? | GA4 | Under 65% |
| **Scroll depth** | How far do readers get? | GA4 / Clarity | 60%+ reaching end |
| **Email signups** | Lead generation from blog | Email platform | 2-5% of blog visitors |
| **CTA clicks** | Product interest from blog | GA4 events | Track and optimize |
| **Social shares** | Content resonance | Share counts | — |
| **Backlinks** | Authority building | Ahrefs / SC | New backlinks from posts |
