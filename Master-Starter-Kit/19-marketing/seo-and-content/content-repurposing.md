## The 1-to-10 Method

### The Core Principle

Creating original content is expensive — it requires research, writing, editing, and expertise. Repurposing multiplies the value of that investment by adapting the same ideas for different platforms, formats, and audiences. One great idea can reach 10x more people when packaged for different contexts.

### The 1-to-10 Map

Start with ONE long-form content piece and produce 10+ derivative pieces:

```
1 Blog Post (2,000-3,000 words)
│
├── 1. Twitter/X Thread (8-12 tweets summarizing key points)
├── 2. LinkedIn Post (personal narrative angle on the topic)
├── 3. Email Newsletter (summary + personal insights + link)
├── 4. Infographic (visual summary of key data/steps)
├── 5. Short Video (60-90s talking head or animated explainer)
├── 6. LinkedIn/Instagram Carousel (5-10 slide visual breakdown)
├── 7. Podcast Segment (discuss the topic on your podcast or pitch as guest topic)
├── 8. Reddit Post/Comment (answer a related question with insights)
├── 9. Quora Answer (answer a related question with a link back)
├── 10. Dev.to/Medium Cross-Post (full article with canonical URL)
│
└── BONUS:
    ├── 11. YouTube Video (5-10 min deep dive)
    ├── 12. Slide Deck (for SlideShare or presentations)
    ├── 13. Pull Quotes (individual quote graphics for social)
    └── 14. Email Sequence (3-5 email drip covering the topic in parts)
```

### Quality Priorities

Not all derivative pieces are equal. Prioritize based on where your audience is:

| Derivative Type | Effort | Reach Potential | Conversion Potential |
|-----------------|--------|----------------|---------------------|
| Twitter/X thread | Low | High | Low |
| LinkedIn post | Low | High | Medium |
| Email newsletter | Low | Medium | High |
| Short video | Medium | Very High | Medium |
| Carousel | Medium | High | Medium |
| Cross-post (dev.to/Medium) | Low | Medium | Low |
| Infographic | Medium-High | High | Low |
| Podcast segment | Medium | Medium | Medium |
| Reddit/Quora | Low | Medium | Low-Medium |
| YouTube video | High | Very High | Medium |

## Repurposing Workflows

### Workflow 1: Blog Post to Everything

**Source**: One published blog post (2,000+ words)

#### Step 1: Extract Key Elements

From the blog post, extract:
- **3-5 key takeaways** (main points that stand alone)
- **1-2 memorable quotes** (pull quotes that work as standalone graphics)
- **1 surprising stat or data point** (hook for social media)
- **The core narrative** (the before/after or problem/solution arc)
- **1 personal angle** (your experience or opinion on the topic)

#### Step 2: Create Derivatives

**Twitter/X Thread:**
```
Tweet 1 (Hook): [Surprising stat or bold claim from the post]
Tweet 2-7: [One key takeaway per tweet, condensed]
Tweet 8: [CTA — link to full post]
Optional: Add relevant images, screenshots, or data visualizations
```

**LinkedIn Post:**
```
[Opening hook — personal angle or story]
[Blank line]
[Key insight 1]
[Key insight 2]
[Key insight 3]
[Blank line]
[Conclusion with takeaway]
[Blank line]
Link to full post in comments.
```

**Email Newsletter:**
```
Subject: [Curiosity-driven subject based on the post topic]

Hey {{FIRST_NAME}},

[1-2 sentence personal intro connecting to the topic]

This week I published a post on [topic]. Here are the key takeaways:

1. [Takeaway 1]
2. [Takeaway 2]
3. [Takeaway 3]

[Personal insight or bonus tip not in the blog post]

Read the full post here: [Link]

[Sign-off]
```

**Infographic:**
```
Title: [Post title, simplified]
Section 1: [Takeaway 1 with icon/visual]
Section 2: [Takeaway 2 with icon/visual]
Section 3: [Takeaway 3 with icon/visual]
Key stat: [Highlighted data point]
CTA: [URL to full post]
Branding: [Logo + brand colors]
```

**Short Video (60-90 seconds):**
```
[0-5s] Hook: "Did you know [surprising fact]?"
[5-20s] Context: "Most people think [common belief], but..."
[20-50s] Key points: "Here are the 3 things that actually matter: ..."
[50-80s] Summary: "So instead of [old way], try [new way]"
[80-90s] CTA: "Full breakdown in my blog post — link in bio"
```

**Carousel (5-10 slides):**
```
Slide 1: Title + hook question
Slide 2: Problem statement
Slide 3: Key point 1 (with visual)
Slide 4: Key point 2 (with visual)
Slide 5: Key point 3 (with visual)
Slide 6: Summary / takeaway
Slide 7: CTA — follow for more + link to post
```

### Workflow 2: Video to Everything

**Source**: One video (webinar, YouTube video, demo, presentation) — 10+ minutes

#### Derivatives

```
1 Video
│
├── Blog Post (transcript, cleaned up and formatted)
├── Short Clips (3-5 clips of 30-60 seconds each — key moments)
├── Audiogram (audio snippet with waveform visual — for social/podcast)
├── GIFs (memorable moments or product demos)
├── Screenshots (key slides or product screens shown in the video)
├── Twitter Thread (key takeaways from the video)
├── LinkedIn Post (what you discussed and why it matters)
├── Email (summary + link to watch)
├── Blog Post Quotes (embed video clips in related blog posts)
└── Slide Deck (extract slides from the presentation)
```

#### Process

1. **Transcribe**: Use Descript, Otter.ai, or Rev to auto-transcribe
2. **Edit transcript into blog post**: Clean up filler words, add formatting, headings, and structure
3. **Identify clip-worthy moments**: Timestamp the 3-5 best moments (insights, demonstrations, reactions)
4. **Create clips**: Use Opus Clip, Descript, or CapCut to extract and format clips
5. **Add captions**: Always add captions to video clips (85% of social video is watched without sound)
6. **Create audiograms**: Extract audio from a key moment, add waveform visualization (Headliner, Wavve)
7. **Screenshot key frames**: Capture slides or screens shown in the video for static posts

### Workflow 3: Webinar to Everything

**Source**: One live webinar or workshop (30-60 minutes)

#### Derivatives

```
1 Webinar
│
├── On-Demand Recording (hosted on YouTube or your site)
├── Blog Post (key takeaways, expanded with additional detail)
├── Email Series (3-5 emails covering topics from the webinar)
├── Social Clips (3-5 highlight clips for social media)
├── Resource Page (slide deck + recording + additional resources)
├── FAQ Post (answer questions from the webinar Q&A)
├── Twitter Thread (live-tweeting key points or a post-webinar summary)
├── LinkedIn Article (expanded analysis of the webinar topic)
├── Infographic (key stats and frameworks from the webinar)
└── Follow-Up Email (to attendees with recording + key takeaways)
```

#### Process

1. **Record the webinar** (Zoom, Google Meet, or dedicated webinar platform)
2. **Post the recording** on YouTube (unlisted or public) and your website
3. **Write a blog post** based on the content (do not just paste the transcript — rewrite for reading)
4. **Extract the Q&A** and create a FAQ blog post
5. **Create an email drip** series that covers the webinar content over 3-5 emails
6. **Cut clips** of the most insightful 60-90 second moments for social sharing
7. **Build a resource page** with the slide deck, recording, and supplementary materials

### Workflow 4: Data Report to Everything

**Source**: One original research piece or data report

#### Derivatives

```
1 Data Report
│
├── Blog Post (narrative version of key findings)
├── Infographic (visual summary of data)
├── Social Posts (individual data points as standalone posts)
├── Press Release (for media pickup)
├── Twitter Thread (walk through findings one by one)
├── LinkedIn Article (analysis and implications)
├── Slide Deck (data visualizations for presentations)
├── Email to Journalists (pitch as a data source for their stories)
├── Downloadable PDF (lead magnet behind email gate)
├── Webinar (present and discuss findings live)
└── Annual Update (refresh the data each year for ongoing value)
```

## Platform-Specific Formatting

### Character Limits & Specifications

| Platform | Text Limit | Image Size | Video Length | Video Size | Notes |
|----------|-----------|------------|-------------|------------|-------|
| **Twitter/X** | 280 chars (free), 25,000 (Premium) | 1600x900px (16:9) | 2 min 20s (free), 60 min (Premium) | 512MB | Threads bypass char limit |
| **LinkedIn** | 3,000 chars (post), 125,000 (article) | 1200x627px | 10 min | 5GB | No links in post for better reach |
| **Instagram** | 2,200 chars | 1080x1080 (square), 1080x1350 (portrait) | 90s (Reels), 60 min (IGTV) | 4GB | No clickable links in captions |
| **Facebook** | 63,206 chars | 1200x630px | 240 min | 10GB | Video performs best |
| **YouTube** | 5,000 chars (description) | 1280x720px (thumbnail) | 12 hours | 256GB | SEO-optimized title and description |
| **TikTok** | 2,200 chars | 1080x1920px | 10 min | 287.6MB | Vertical video only |
| **Reddit** | 40,000 chars | Varies | — | — | Varies by subreddit rules |
| **Dev.to** | No limit | Varies | — | — | Set canonical URL to your blog |
| **Medium** | No limit | Varies | — | — | Set canonical URL to your blog |
| **Email** | No limit | 600px width | — | — | 50-125 words ideal for high engagement |
| **Pinterest** | 500 chars | 1000x1500px (2:3) | 60s | — | Vertical images perform best |

### Platform-Specific Content Adaptations

#### Twitter/X: Condensed and punchy
- Lead with the most interesting point (not background)
- Use line breaks between key points
- End threads with a CTA (follow, link, engage)
- Include 1 image per tweet for better engagement
- Avoid links in the main tweet (put in reply for better algorithm reach)

#### LinkedIn: Professional and personal
- Write in first person
- Open with a hook (bold statement, question, or story)
- Use short paragraphs (1-2 sentences)
- Use line breaks liberally (creates white space in the feed)
- Put links in the first comment (not the post — better reach)
- End with a question to encourage comments

#### Reddit: Genuine and community-first
- Do NOT post "check out my blog post" — add genuine value first
- Write a self-contained answer/insight and mention the post naturally
- Follow each subreddit's specific rules
- Engage in the comments genuinely
- Build karma before posting links

#### Email: Personal and actionable
- Subject line: Curiosity or benefit-driven (not "Newsletter #47")
- Preview text: Complement the subject line
- Keep body under 500 words
- One primary CTA per email
- Mobile-optimized layout

## Scheduling & Staggering

### Why Stagger?

If you publish the blog post, Twitter thread, LinkedIn post, email, and Reddit post all on the same day at the same time, you:
- Overwhelm followers who are on multiple platforms
- Miss opportunities to stay top-of-mind across multiple days
- Cannot learn which platform performs best (everything is simultaneous)

### The 2-Week Staggering Schedule

| Day | Platform | Content Piece |
|-----|----------|--------------|
| **Day 1** (Publish Day) | Blog | Full blog post goes live |
| **Day 1** | Twitter/X | Tweet announcing the post + link |
| **Day 1** | LinkedIn | Post with key insight (link in first comment) |
| **Day 2** | Email | Newsletter to subscribers with summary |
| **Day 2** | Discord/Slack | Share in your community |
| **Day 3** | Twitter/X | Full thread breaking down key points |
| **Day 3** | Reddit | Post in relevant subreddit (value-first) |
| **Day 4** | Dev.to | Cross-post full article (canonical URL set) |
| **Day 5** | LinkedIn | Carousel post with visual takeaways |
| **Day 5** | Hacker News | Submit (if appropriate for the content) |
| **Week 2, Day 1** | Twitter/X | Reshare with a different angle or pull quote |
| **Week 2, Day 2** | LinkedIn | Second post — personal reflection on the topic |
| **Week 2, Day 3** | Quora | Answer a related question with insights from the post |
| **Week 2, Day 4** | Medium | Cross-post (canonical URL set) |
| **Week 2, Day 5** | Email | Include in weekly/monthly roundup |

### Monthly Resharing

For evergreen content, reshare quarterly:
- Use a different hook, angle, or pull quote each time
- Update any data or examples that may have changed
- Reference the post in new related content (internal linking)

## Content Atomization Framework

### What Is Content Atomization?

Content atomization takes a large piece of content and breaks it into the smallest possible usable units. Each "atom" is a standalone piece of content that makes sense without the original context.

### How to Atomize

#### Level 1: The Full Piece
The complete blog post, video, or guide.

#### Level 2: Sections
Each major section (H2) becomes a potential standalone piece.

#### Level 3: Key Points
Each key point, stat, or takeaway becomes a social media post.

#### Level 4: Quotes
Individual sentences or phrases become pull quotes, image captions, or tweets.

### Atomization Example

**Original**: A 3,000-word blog post titled "7 Deployment Best Practices for 2026"

```
Level 1 (Full piece):
- Blog post: "7 Deployment Best Practices for 2026"

Level 2 (Sections → standalone pieces):
- Mini-post 1: "Why automated rollbacks are non-negotiable"
- Mini-post 2: "The case for feature flags in every deployment"
- Mini-post 3: "How blue-green deployments eliminate downtime"
- ... (one for each best practice)

Level 3 (Key points → social posts):
- Stat post: "Teams with automated rollbacks resolve incidents 73% faster"
- Tip post: "Feature flags let you deploy code without releasing it to users"
- Hot take: "If you're still deploying manually in 2026, you're leaving money on the table"

Level 4 (Quotes → micro-content):
- Pull quote graphic: "Deploy code, not risk."
- Tweet: "The best deployment is one you don't think about."
- LinkedIn caption: "Downtime costs an average of $5,600 per minute."
```

### Atomization Calendar

Map atoms to your posting schedule:

```
Week 1: Publish full blog post + high-level social posts
Week 2: Share section-level mini-posts
Week 3: Share individual data points and quotes
Week 4: Reshare the full post with a new angle
```

## Evergreen Content Re-Promotion

### What Is Evergreen Content?

Evergreen content remains relevant and useful long after it is published. Unlike news or trend pieces, evergreen content can be reshared and re-promoted indefinitely.

### Identifying Evergreen Content

| Evergreen (re-promote) | Time-Sensitive (do NOT re-promote) |
|------------------------|------------------------------------|
| How-to guides | News coverage |
| Best practices | Event recaps |
| Tutorials | Seasonal promotions |
| Comparison posts | Trend predictions (after the trend passes) |
| Case studies | Product announcements (become outdated) |
| FAQ / explainers | Conference talks (unless the topic is timeless) |
| Data-backed research | — |

### Re-Promotion Schedule

| Timeframe After Publish | Action |
|------------------------|--------|
| **1 month** | Reshare on social media with a new angle |
| **3 months** | Include in email newsletter / relevant email sequence |
| **6 months** | Update the post (new data, examples, links) and reshare |
| **12 months** | Major refresh (rewrite sections, update year references, new screenshots) |
| **Ongoing** | Link to from new related content (internal linking) |

### Updating Evergreen Content

When refreshing evergreen content:

1. **Update statistics and data** with the latest numbers
2. **Replace outdated examples** with current ones
3. **Fix broken links** (tools change, pages move)
4. **Add new sections** based on developments since publication
5. **Refresh screenshots** if product UIs have changed
6. **Update the publish date** (signals freshness to Google and readers)
7. **Re-submit to Google Search Console** for re-indexing
8. **Re-promote** across all channels as if it were new

### Content Decay Detection

Watch for these signs that a post needs refreshing:

| Signal | What It Means | Action |
|--------|---------------|--------|
| Traffic declining month-over-month | Competitors are outranking you | Update and deepen the content |
| Keyword rankings dropping | Content is getting stale | Refresh data, add new sections |
| High bounce rate increasing | Content may not match current intent | Rewrite intro, update for current relevance |
| Comments asking for updated info | Readers see it as outdated | Refresh the content |
| New competitors appearing in SERPs | Market is getting more competitive | Improve content quality, add unique value |

## Repurposing Checklist

### Per Content Piece

Before moving on to the next piece of content, ensure you have:

- [ ] Published the original piece on your primary platform
- [ ] Created a Twitter/X thread or tweet summarizing key points
- [ ] Written a LinkedIn post with a personal or professional angle
- [ ] Sent to your email list (newsletter or dedicated email)
- [ ] Shared in your community (Discord, Slack, forum)
- [ ] Cross-posted to dev.to and/or Medium (with canonical URL)
- [ ] Submitted to relevant Reddit subreddits or Hacker News
- [ ] Created 1-2 visual assets (infographic, carousel, or pull quote graphics)
- [ ] Scheduled re-promotion for 2 weeks, 1 month, 3 months out
- [ ] Added internal links from older related content to this new piece
- [ ] Added this piece to relevant email sequences
- [ ] Identified the top-performing derivative and created more in that format
- [ ] Tagged this piece as evergreen or time-sensitive for future planning

### Monthly Repurposing Review

- [ ] Review which derivative formats performed best this month
- [ ] Identify underperforming content that could be repurposed differently
- [ ] Update the re-promotion calendar for next month
- [ ] Refresh one evergreen piece with updated data
- [ ] Plan next month's repurposing schedule
