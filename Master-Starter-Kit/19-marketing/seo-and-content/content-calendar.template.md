## Content Calendar Structure

### Weekly View

Each week should include a mix of content types. Here is the recommended weekly rhythm:

| Day | Activity |
|-----|----------|
| **Monday** | Publish primary content piece (blog post, guide) |
| **Tuesday** | Share on social media channels, email newsletter |
| **Wednesday** | Engage with community (respond to comments, participate in discussions) |
| **Thursday** | Publish secondary content (shorter post, social-only content) |
| **Friday** | Repurpose this week's content for other platforms |
| **Weekend** | Schedule next week's social posts, plan next week's content |

### Monthly View

Each month has a theme tied to one of your content pillars. This creates focus and allows deeper exploration of topics.

```
Month 1: [Pillar 1 — e.g., "Getting Started"]
  Week 1: Foundational blog post
  Week 2: Tutorial / how-to guide
  Week 3: Case study or user story
  Week 4: Comparison or listicle

Month 2: [Pillar 2 — e.g., "Advanced Usage"]
  Week 5: Deep-dive technical post
  Week 6: Best practices guide
  Week 7: Data/research post
  Week 8: Guest post or interview

Month 3: [Pillar 3 — e.g., "Industry Insights"]
  Week 9: Thought leadership piece
  Week 10: Industry roundup / trends
  Week 11: Problem-solution post
  Week 12: Monthly recap + what's next
```

## Content Mix Recommendation

### The 40/30/20/10 Framework

| Category | Percentage | Purpose | Content Types |
|----------|-----------|---------|---------------|
| **Educational** | 40% | Teach your audience something valuable (builds trust and traffic) | How-to guides, tutorials, explainers, best practices |
| **Solution-Oriented** | 30% | Show how your product solves specific problems | Product tutorials, use cases, comparisons, case studies |
| **Thought Leadership** | 20% | Position your brand as an authority | Industry analysis, opinion pieces, trend reports, original data |
| **Promotional** | 10% | Directly promote your product or offers | Product announcements, feature launches, special offers |

### Why This Ratio Works

- **40% educational**: Attracts the widest audience. Most people searching are looking for answers, not products. This builds trust.
- **30% solution-oriented**: Connects problems to your product. These readers are further down the funnel.
- **20% thought leadership**: Builds brand authority. Gets shared by industry peers. Earns backlinks.
- **10% promotional**: Keeps promotional content from overwhelming your audience. When you DO promote, people listen because you have not been screaming "buy now" constantly.

## Distribution Plan

### Per Content Piece Distribution Checklist

For every piece of content you publish, distribute it across these channels:

#### Day 1 (Publish Day)

- [ ] Publish on website/blog
- [ ] Share on Twitter/X (2-3 tweet thread or single tweet with key takeaway)
- [ ] Share on LinkedIn (native post with key insights, link in first comment)
- [ ] Share in relevant Slack/Discord communities (if allowed and relevant)
- [ ] Share in your own community (Discord, Slack, forum)
- [ ] Email to subscriber list (if high-value content)

#### Day 2-3

- [ ] Share on Reddit (relevant subreddits, provide genuine value, do not just drop a link)
- [ ] Share on Hacker News (if technical/startup content)
- [ ] Answer a related question on Quora with a link to your content
- [ ] Post on dev.to or Medium (cross-post with canonical URL pointing to your site)
- [ ] Share in relevant Facebook groups (if your audience uses Facebook)

#### Week 2

- [ ] Reshare on Twitter/X with a different angle or pull quote
- [ ] Reshare on LinkedIn with a different insight
- [ ] Include in weekly/monthly newsletter
- [ ] Pin to relevant Pinterest boards (if visual content)

#### Month 2-3

- [ ] Reshare evergreen content on social media
- [ ] Include in relevant email sequences
- [ ] Link to from new related content (internal linking)
- [ ] Update if any information has changed

### Platform-Specific Posting Guidelines

| Platform | Best Time to Post | Format | Character Limit | Notes |
|----------|------------------|--------|----------------|-------|
| **Twitter/X** | 8-10am, 12-1pm, 5-6pm (user's timezone) | Short, punchy, thread for depth | 280 chars | Use threads for detailed content; include an image |
| **LinkedIn** | 7-9am, 12pm, 5-6pm (weekdays) | Professional, first-person, insights | 3,000 chars (post) | Put links in first comment (better reach); use line breaks |
| **Reddit** | Varies by subreddit | Genuine, helpful, not promotional | Varies | Read subreddit rules first; be a community member, not a marketer |
| **Hacker News** | 6-8am EST | Factual title, no marketing speak | ~80 chars title | Do NOT editorialise the title; focus on genuine value |
| **Dev.to** | Anytime | Full article cross-post | No limit | Set canonical URL to your blog; use their tags |
| **Medium** | Anytime | Full article cross-post | No limit | Publish in a relevant publication for more reach |
| **Email** | Tuesday-Thursday, 10am | Summary + link to full content | — | Segment your list; personalize subject lines |

## Content Batching Strategy

### What Is Content Batching?

Instead of writing one piece of content from start to finish, then starting the next, batch similar tasks together. This is more efficient because context-switching between thinking, writing, editing, and publishing is expensive.

### The Batching Workflow

#### Batch 1: Research & Ideation (1 session, 2-3 hours)

For the next 2-4 weeks of content:
- Research keywords for all upcoming pieces
- Outline all articles (headings, key points, sources)
- Identify needed visuals (screenshots, diagrams, images)
- Write titles and meta descriptions

#### Batch 2: Writing (2-3 sessions, 2-4 hours each)

- Write drafts for 2-4 articles per session
- Do not edit while writing — just get the words down
- Follow the outlines from Batch 1
- Leave [PLACEHOLDER] notes for things you need to look up later

#### Batch 3: Editing & SEO (1 session, 2-3 hours)

- Edit all drafts for clarity, grammar, and readability
- Apply on-page SEO checklist to each piece
- Add internal and external links
- Write social media copy for each piece

#### Batch 4: Visuals (1 session, 1-2 hours)

- Create or source all images, screenshots, diagrams
- Design social media graphics (use Canva templates)
- Optimize all images (compress, add alt text)
- Create featured images for each blog post

#### Batch 5: Scheduling (1 session, 1 hour)

- Schedule all blog posts in your CMS
- Schedule all social media posts (Buffer, Hootsuite, or native scheduling)
- Schedule email newsletter
- Set up distribution plan reminders

### Batching Cadence

| Team Size | Batching Frequency | Content Output |
|-----------|-------------------|----------------|
| Solo | Every 2 weeks | 1-2 pieces/week |
| 2-3 people | Weekly | 2-3 pieces/week |
| 4+ people | Weekly with overlap | 4+ pieces/week |

## Repurposing Workflow

### The 1-to-10 Method

Every long-form content piece can be repurposed into 10+ pieces of derivative content:

```
1 Blog Post (2,000 words)
  ├── 1 Twitter/X thread (key takeaways)
  ├── 1 LinkedIn post (personal angle on the topic)
  ├── 1 Email newsletter (summary + link)
  ├── 1 Infographic (visual summary of key points)
  ├── 1 Short video (60-90s talking through key points)
  ├── 1 Carousel post (Instagram/LinkedIn, 5-10 slides)
  ├── 1 Podcast talking point (discuss on your podcast or pitch as a guest topic)
  ├── 1 Reddit comment (answer a question with insights from the post)
  ├── 1 Quora answer (answer a related question)
  └── 1 Dev.to/Medium cross-post (canonical to your blog)
```

### Repurposing Schedule

Do not publish all repurposed pieces at once. Stagger them:

| Day | Repurposed Piece |
|-----|-----------------|
| Day 1 | Publish blog post |
| Day 1 | Twitter thread + LinkedIn post |
| Day 2 | Email newsletter |
| Day 3 | Reddit/HN submission |
| Day 4 | Cross-post to dev.to/Medium |
| Day 5 | Short video or carousel |
| Week 2 | Quora answer + community shares |
| Week 3 | Infographic |
| Month 2 | Reshare on social with new angle |

## Content Performance Tracking

### Metrics by Content Type

| Content Type | Primary Metric | Secondary Metrics |
|-------------|---------------|-------------------|
| **Blog post** | Organic traffic | Time on page, scroll depth, email signups |
| **Tutorial** | Organic traffic + completions | Page views, bounce rate, product sign-ups |
| **Case study** | Lead generation (downloads, demo requests) | Page views, time on page |
| **Comparison** | Organic traffic + conversions | Keyword rankings, CTR from search |
| **Data report** | Backlinks earned | Social shares, downloads, media mentions |
| **Email** | Open rate + click rate | Unsubscribe rate, conversion rate |
| **Social post** | Engagement (likes, comments, shares) | Link clicks, follower growth |

### Grading Criteria

| Grade | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| **A** | Exceeds expectations on primary AND secondary metrics |
| **B** | Meets expectations on primary metric |
| **C** | Below expectations — needs optimization (update content, improve SEO, promote more) |
| **D** | Significantly below expectations — analyze why and either update or retire |

### Actions Based on Grade

- **A content**: Promote more, create related pieces, update quarterly to keep fresh
- **B content**: Optimize (better CTAs, improved SEO, add visuals), promote in email
- **C content**: Rewrite headline, update content depth, add new keywords, promote again
- **D content**: Analyze what went wrong. If topic is wrong, retire. If execution is wrong, rewrite.

## Seasonal & Trending Content Planning

### Annual Calendar Events

Plan content around predictable events:

| Month | Events | Content Opportunity |
|-------|--------|-------------------|
| **January** | New Year, planning season | "2026 goals," annual planning guides, prediction posts |
| **February** | — | Love-themed (creative), industry trends from Q1 |
| **March** | End of Q1 | Q1 recap, tax season (if relevant), spring cleaning (digital cleanup) |
| **April** | — | Spring launches, industry conference season begins |
| **May** | — | Mid-year planning, summer prep |
| **June** | Mid-year | Half-year review, industry reports |
| **July** | Summer slowdown | Evergreen content (traffic is steady, less competition) |
| **August** | Back to school/work | Planning guides, fall prep |
| **September** | Fall, conference season | Industry analysis, event coverage |
| **October** | Q4 planning | Year-end strategy, budget planning |
| **November** | Black Friday, Cyber Monday | Deals content (if relevant), holiday prep |
| **December** | Year-end | Year in review, predictions for next year, best-of lists |

### Trending Content Strategy

- **Monitor**: Google Trends, Twitter trending, Hacker News front page, industry subreddits
- **React quickly**: Trending content has a window of 24-72 hours
- **Connect to your product**: Only jump on trends that relate to your industry
- **Provide genuine value**: Do not just mention the trend — add your unique perspective

## Post-Calendar: Next Steps

After completing the 12-week calendar:

1. **Review performance**: Which content pieces performed best? Double down on those types.
2. **Identify gaps**: What questions are your audience still asking that you have not answered?
3. **Update top performers**: Refresh your best-performing content with new data, examples, and keywords.
4. **Plan the next 90 days**: Use the same template with new topics informed by performance data.
5. **Scale up**: If solo, consider adding a freelance writer. If team, consider increasing frequency.

### Evergreen Content Maintenance

Content published in this calendar should be reviewed every 6 months:
- Update statistics and data
- Refresh examples
- Add new sections if the topic has evolved
- Fix any broken links
- Update the publish date (signals freshness to Google)
