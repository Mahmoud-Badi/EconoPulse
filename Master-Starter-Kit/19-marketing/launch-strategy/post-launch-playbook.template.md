## Day 1-3: Immediate Aftermath

The 72 hours after launch are critical. Your product is getting more attention than it ever will again (until the next major launch). Maximize this window.

### Day 1 (Launch Day +0)

You just launched. See `launch-day-checklist.template.md` for the full day-of plan. By end of day 1:

- [ ] Respond to every comment, message, and mention across all platforms.
- [ ] Fix any critical bugs that surfaced during launch.
- [ ] Note all feedback received (positive and negative) in a central document.
- [ ] Thank every person who shared, upvoted, or promoted your launch.
- [ ] Take screenshots of metrics, comments, and highlights for future content.

### Day 2 (Launch Day +1)

The "day after" is when most founders relax. Don't. Post-launch momentum is fragile.

- [ ] **Morning:** Check all platforms for overnight comments and questions. Respond to everything.
- [ ] **Post a "Day 1 results" update** on Twitter/X and LinkedIn:
  - Share key metrics (signups, traffic, upvotes — whatever you're comfortable sharing).
  - Highlight the most interesting user feedback.
  - Thank supporters by name.
  - Tease what's coming next.
- [ ] **Send a follow-up email** to launch-day signups who haven't activated yet.
  - Subject: "Quick tip to get the most out of {{PROJECT_NAME}}"
  - Include the single most important first step.
- [ ] **Continue community engagement.** Threads you started yesterday may still be active.
- [ ] **Prioritize bug fixes.** Launch day always reveals issues. Fix the ones that affect the most users first.
- [ ] **Start organizing feedback.** Create a simple system: spreadsheet, Notion database, or a tool like Canny.

### Day 3 (Launch Day +2)

- [ ] **Write a launch retrospective post** (publish later this week). Include:
  - What you launched and why.
  - Pre-launch preparation (how long, what you did).
  - Launch day timeline and results.
  - What worked, what didn't.
  - Key learnings.
  - What's next.
- [ ] **Continue responding** to all comments and messages. Engagement should stay high for 72 hours.
- [ ] **Reach out to everyone who gave detailed feedback** with a personal thank you. Offer a call if they're willing.
- [ ] **Submit to directories and listings.** Now that you're live, submit to relevant directories:
  - AlternativeTo
  - G2
  - Capterra
  - SaaSHub
  - StackShare (for dev tools)
  - Any niche-specific directories
- [ ] **Identify your most engaged early users.** These are your potential power users and advocates.

## Day 4-7: First Week Recap

### Day 4-5: Process and Publish

- [ ] **Publish your launch retrospective** on your blog, Indie Hackers, and social media. Launch stories are highly shareable and position you as transparent.
- [ ] **Create a "first week" highlight reel** for social media:
  - Best user quotes/testimonials.
  - Metrics you're comfortable sharing.
  - Product screenshots showing real usage.
- [ ] **Identify patterns in feedback.** What are the 3 most requested features? What are the 3 most common confusions?
- [ ] **Start 1-on-1 conversations** with your most engaged users. Schedule 5-10 calls this week. These conversations are gold for understanding product-market fit.
- [ ] **Send a "Week 1" email to your full list:**
  - Thank them for supporting the launch.
  - Share results and highlights.
  - Address common questions.
  - Share what's coming next.

### Day 6-7: Early Case Studies

- [ ] **Identify 2-3 users with interesting use cases.** Reach out and ask if you can feature their story.
- [ ] **Create quick case studies** (even informal ones):
  - Who they are and what they do.
  - What problem they were solving.
  - How they're using {{PROJECT_NAME}}.
  - Early results or impressions.
- [ ] **Share case studies on social media.** "Meet [User]. They're using {{PROJECT_NAME}} to [use case]. Here's their story."
- [ ] **Iterate on onboarding** based on where users get stuck. The first week of data tells you everything about your onboarding flow.

## Week 2: Content Blitz

Week 2 is about capitalizing on launch attention by flooding the market with valuable content. This is when you go from "the thing that just launched" to "a credible resource in the space."

### Blog Content (Publish 2-3 Posts)

| Post | Purpose | Template |
|------|---------|----------|
| **Launch story** | Shareability, transparency, SEO | "How We Launched {{PROJECT_NAME}} and Got [X] Signups in 24 Hours" |
| **Behind-the-scenes** | Humanize the product, build-in-public crowd | "The Tech Stack Behind {{PROJECT_NAME}}: Why We Chose [Stack]" |
| **Tutorial** | SEO, value-first content, user education | "How to [Accomplish Goal] with {{PROJECT_NAME}} in [X] Minutes" |
| **Problem-focused** | SEO, attract new users searching for solutions | "[X] Ways {{TARGET_AUDIENCE}} Waste Time on {{PAIN_POINT}}" |

### Social Media Content

- [ ] **Post 5-7 times per week** across Twitter/X and LinkedIn (minimum).
- [ ] **Content mix:**
  - User testimonials and feedback (2x/week).
  - Product tips and features (2x/week).
  - Behind-the-scenes updates (1x/week).
  - Industry commentary (1x/week).
  - Engagement posts: polls, questions, hot takes (1x/week).
- [ ] **Short-form video:** Create 3-5 short videos (TikTok, Reels, Shorts) showing product features or quick tips.

### User Stories

- [ ] **Reach out to 5-10 users** for short quotes or mini-case studies.
- [ ] **Share on social media:** "[User] just [achieved result] using {{PROJECT_NAME}}. Here's what they said: [quote]"
- [ ] **Add to your landing page.** Replace placeholder testimonials with real ones from launch week users.

### Email Content

- [ ] **Send 2 emails in Week 2:**
  1. **Educational email:** A tutorial or tip for getting more value from {{PROJECT_NAME}}.
  2. **Social proof email:** Share user stories, testimonials, and results from the first week.

## Week 3: Outreach Expansion

Week 3 is about expanding beyond your launch audience. You've validated initial interest — now reach new people.

### Guest Posts and Contributed Content

- [ ] **Pitch 5-10 relevant blogs/publications** for guest posts.
- [ ] **Offer value, not promotion.** The post should teach something useful. {{PROJECT_NAME}} can be mentioned but shouldn't be the focus.
- [ ] **Target publications your audience reads.** Industry blogs, dev communities, tech publications.
- [ ] **Repurpose existing content.** Your launch story or behind-the-scenes post can be adapted for other publications.

### Podcast Interviews

- [ ] **Identify 10-20 podcasts** in your niche. Look for indie hacker, startup, developer, or industry-specific shows.
- [ ] **Pitch yourself as a guest.** Share your launch story, a technical decision, or an industry insight.
- [ ] **Template pitch:**
  ```
  Hi [Host],

  I'm [Name], founder of {{PROJECT_NAME}}. We just launched [time ago]
  and [brief result — X signups, trending on PH, etc.].

  I'd love to come on [Podcast Name] to talk about [specific topic —
  not just "my product" but a broader theme your audience cares about].

  Some potential angles:
  - [Angle 1: e.g., "How we validated the idea before writing code"]
  - [Angle 2: e.g., "The counterintuitive marketing strategy that worked"]
  - [Angle 3: e.g., "Technical deep-dive on [architecture decision]"]

  Happy to discuss whichever fits your show best.

  Best,
  [Name]
  ```
- [ ] **Follow up once** if no response after 5-7 days.

### Partnership Conversations

- [ ] **Identify 5-10 complementary products** (not competitors). Products that serve a similar audience but do different things.
- [ ] **Reach out with partnership ideas:**
  - Cross-promotion (mention each other in newsletters, social media).
  - Integration (build a connection between your products).
  - Co-created content (joint webinar, blog post, or guide).
  - Bundle deals (offer discounts to each other's users).
- [ ] **Start small.** A cross-promotion tweet is easier to agree to than a full integration. Build the relationship first.

### Directory Submissions

If you haven't already, submit {{PROJECT_NAME}} to every relevant directory:

- [ ] AlternativeTo.net
- [ ] G2.com
- [ ] Capterra.com
- [ ] GetApp.com
- [ ] SaaSHub.com
- [ ] StackShare.io (for dev tools)
- [ ] SaaSWorthy.com
- [ ] Slant.co
- [ ] There's An AI For That (if AI-related)
- [ ] Futurepedia (if AI-related)
- [ ] Niche-specific directories for your industry
- [ ] Startup directories: Crunchbase, AngelList, F6S

## Week 4: Optimization

Week 4 is about analyzing what worked, doubling down on the best channels, and optimizing your conversion funnel.

### Analyze What Worked

- [ ] **Review all traffic data from weeks 1-3.** Which channels drove the most visitors? Which drove the most signups?
- [ ] **Calculate customer acquisition cost (if applicable)** by channel.
- [ ] **Identify your top 3 channels.** These are where you'll focus your ongoing marketing efforts.
- [ ] **Kill what didn't work.** If a channel produced zero results after 3 weeks, deprioritize it.

### Double Down on Best Channels

| If This Worked Best | Double Down By |
|--------------------|----------------|
| Twitter/X | Increase posting frequency. Start Twitter Ads. Build relationships with more influencers. |
| Product Hunt | Launch updates/new features on PH. Engage more in the community. |
| Hacker News | Write more technical content. Contribute to HN discussions regularly. |
| SEO/Blog | Publish 2-4 posts per week. Optimize existing posts. Build backlinks. |
| Email | Grow the list aggressively. Send more frequently. Segment and personalize. |
| Communities | Become a power user in 2-3 communities. Contribute daily. |
| Short-form video | Increase to daily posting. Test new formats. Collaborate with creators. |

### A/B Test Landing Page

- [ ] **Test your headline.** Try 2-3 different value propositions.
- [ ] **Test your CTA.** "Start Free Trial" vs. "Get Started Free" vs. "Try {{PROJECT_NAME}}."
- [ ] **Test with vs. without video.** Does a demo video on the landing page improve conversion?
- [ ] **Test social proof placement.** Logos? Testimonials? Metrics? Above the fold or below?
- [ ] **Test pricing page.** Simplify, reorder plans, add/remove features from display.

### Improve Onboarding

Based on 3 weeks of user data:

- [ ] **Identify where users drop off** in the onboarding flow. What's the last step they complete before abandoning?
- [ ] **Simplify the first experience.** Can you reduce time-to-first-value? Can you eliminate a step?
- [ ] **Add onboarding emails.** Automated emails triggered by user actions (or lack of actions).
- [ ] **Create help content** for the most common support questions.
- [ ] **Set up in-app guidance** if you haven't already (tooltips, checklists, progress indicators).

## Feedback Loop

### Collecting Feedback

Set up multiple channels for feedback collection:

| Channel | What It Captures | Setup |
|---------|-----------------|-------|
| **In-app feedback widget** | Contextual feedback while using the product | Canny, Userback, or simple mailto link |
| **NPS survey** | Overall satisfaction score | Sent at Day 7 and Day 30 after signup |
| **Feature request board** | What users want next | Canny, Upvoty, Nolt, or a public Notion page |
| **1-on-1 calls** | Deep qualitative insights | Schedule with power users and churned users |
| **Support tickets** | Pain points and confusion | Your help desk (Intercom, Crisp, Zendesk) |
| **Social media mentions** | Organic reactions and opinions | Monitor brand mentions |
| **Exit surveys** | Why people leave | Trigger when users cancel or downgrade |

### Organizing Feedback

Create a simple feedback database:

```
FEEDBACK CATEGORIES:
├── Bug Reports → Priority (Critical / High / Medium / Low) → Assigned To
├── Feature Requests → Vote Count → Effort Estimate → Status
├── UX/Confusion → Affected Area → Impact → Fix Status
├── Positive Feedback → Usable as Testimonial? → Published?
└── General Comments → Categorized → Actionable?
```

### Acting on Feedback

- **Week 2-3:** Focus on fixing bugs and confusion points. Don't add features yet.
- **Week 4:** Review all feature requests. Identify the top 3 that align with your vision.
- **Communicate back.** When you fix something based on feedback, tell the person who reported it: "Hey, we fixed that thing you mentioned! Thanks for the feedback."
- **Public roadmap (optional):** Share what you're working on next. This builds excitement and reduces "when will you add X?" questions.

## Early User Activation

Getting signups is half the battle. The other half is making sure those users actually **use** your product and **get value** from it.

### Activation Metrics

Define what "activated" means for {{PROJECT_NAME}}:

```
ACTIVATION DEFINITION:
A user is "activated" when they have completed [{{ACTIVATION_ACTION}}]
within [{{ACTIVATION_TIMEFRAME}}] of signing up.

Examples:
- SaaS tool: "Created their first project within 3 days"
- API product: "Made their first API call within 7 days"
- Marketplace: "Listed their first item within 7 days"
- Community: "Made their first post within 48 hours"
```

### Activation Strategy

| Timeframe | Action | Goal |
|-----------|--------|------|
| **0-1 hours** | Onboarding flow walks them through first action | Complete the core setup |
| **1-24 hours** | Welcome email with tutorial video | Reinforce the next step |
| **Day 2** | Check-in email: "How's it going? Need help?" | Catch dropoffs early |
| **Day 3-5** | Feature discovery email: "Did you know you can..." | Expand usage |
| **Day 7** | Value reinforcement: "Here's what you've accomplished" | Celebrate progress |
| **Day 14** | Social proof: "Here's what other users like you are doing" | Inspire deeper use |
| **Day 21** | Feedback request: "What would make {{PROJECT_NAME}} even better?" | Engagement + insights |
| **Day 30** | Conversion prompt (if on trial): "Your trial ends in X days" | Drive conversion |

### Re-Engaging Inactive Users

For users who signed up but never activated:

- [ ] **Day 3:** "Need help getting started? Here's a quick guide."
- [ ] **Day 7:** "We noticed you haven't [key action] yet. Here's a 2-minute walkthrough."
- [ ] **Day 14:** "Is {{PROJECT_NAME}} what you expected? Reply and let us know."
- [ ] **Day 21:** "We've added [new feature/improvement]. Want to give it another try?"
- [ ] **Day 30:** Final attempt. "We'd love to have you back. Here's what's new since you signed up."

After Day 30, stop emailing inactive users. If they haven't engaged by then, they're unlikely to without a significant product change or life event.

## Momentum Maintenance

Launch buzz fades fast. Here's how to keep the energy going beyond Day 1.

### The "Content Machine" Framework

Turn every product update, user story, and insight into content:

```
PRODUCT UPDATE (e.g., new feature shipped)
  ├── Blog post: detailed feature explanation
  ├── Twitter thread: feature announcement + use cases
  ├── Email: "What's new in {{PROJECT_NAME}}"
  ├── Short video: 30-second feature demo
  ├── Community post: share in relevant communities
  └── Changelog entry

USER STORY (e.g., customer achieves result)
  ├── Case study: detailed write-up
  ├── Social post: quote + screenshot
  ├── Email: share the story with your list
  ├── Video testimonial: record with the user
  └── Landing page update: add to social proof section

INSIGHT (e.g., interesting data or learning)
  ├── Blog post: detailed analysis
  ├── Twitter thread: share the insight
  ├── LinkedIn post: professional angle
  ├── Podcast pitch: discuss the insight as a guest
  └── Newsletter: include in your weekly/monthly update
```

### Weekly Marketing Rhythm (Post-Launch)

| Day | Activity | Time |
|-----|----------|------|
| Monday | Plan content for the week. Review last week's metrics. | 30 min |
| Tuesday | Publish blog post or long-form content. | 1-2 hours |
| Wednesday | Community engagement. Respond to all mentions. | 30 min |
| Thursday | Social media content batch (write 3-5 posts for the week). | 45 min |
| Friday | Outreach: partnerships, guest posts, podcast pitches. | 30 min |

### Maintaining Product Hunt Momentum

- When you ship a significant update, post it as a Product Hunt launch (launches 2, 3, etc.).
- Continue engaging with the PH community: upvote others, comment on launches, build relationships.
- Your Product Hunt page ranks in Google forever — keep it updated.

## "What's Next" Communication

After the launch excitement fades, your users want to know: "What's next?" Communicating your roadmap builds anticipation and reduces churn.

### Public Roadmap Options

| Approach | Pros | Cons |
|----------|------|------|
| **Full public roadmap** (Notion, Canny, Linear) | Transparency, community input, excitement | Sets expectations you must meet, competitors can see |
| **Quarterly themes** | Directional without commitment | Less exciting, less community input |
| **"Next up" teases** | Creates anticipation, flexible | Can feel vague |
| **No public roadmap** | Maximum flexibility | Users feel left in the dark |

**Recommendation for {{PROJECT_NAME}}:** Share a public roadmap with high-level items. Use a tool like Canny or a public Notion page. Let users vote on features.

### "What's Next" Email Template

Send this 2-3 weeks after launch.


## Transition From Launch Mode to Growth Mode

Launch mode is a sprint. Growth mode is a marathon. Here's how to make the transition.

### Launch Mode vs. Growth Mode

| Aspect | Launch Mode | Growth Mode |
|--------|------------|------------|
| **Timeframe** | Days to weeks | Months to years |
| **Goal** | Awareness, signups, validation | Retention, revenue, sustainable growth |
| **Marketing** | Bursts of activity (launch day, PH, HN) | Consistent daily/weekly activities |
| **Content** | Announcements, launch stories | Tutorials, SEO content, case studies |
| **Engagement** | Respond to everything immediately | Systematic engagement (daily/weekly blocks) |
| **Metrics** | Signups, traffic spikes | MRR, churn, LTV, activation rate |
| **Mindset** | "Get as many people as possible to try it" | "Get the right people to stay and pay" |

### Making the Transition

**Week 4 actions:**
1. **Define your growth channels.** Based on 4 weeks of data, which 2-3 channels drive the most value? Focus on those.
2. **Set a weekly marketing routine.** (See "Weekly Marketing Rhythm" above.) Consistency beats intensity.
3. **Shift from signups to activation.** Your most important metric is no longer "how many signed up" but "how many got value."
4. **Build systems, not heroics.** Automate what you can (email sequences, social scheduling). Document processes so they're repeatable.
5. **Set monthly goals.** Move from "launch day goals" to monthly growth targets.

### The First 90-Day Growth Plan

| Month | Focus | Key Metric |
|-------|-------|------------|
| Month 1 (Launch) | Awareness, signups, validation | Total signups, activation rate |
| Month 2 | Retention, feature development, content SEO | Active users, churn rate, organic traffic |
| Month 3 | Revenue, optimization, channel doubling-down | MRR, conversion rate, CAC |
