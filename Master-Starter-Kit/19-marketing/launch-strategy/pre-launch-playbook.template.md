## Pre-Launch Timeline Overview

The 8 weeks before launch are the most important marketing period for {{PROJECT_NAME}}. Everything you do here determines whether launch day is a quiet release or a momentum event.

```
WEEK 8 (T-56 days): Foundation
  │  Set up waitlist, social accounts, initial positioning.
  │
WEEK 7 (T-49 days): Community Seeding
  │  Join communities, start contributing value (NOT promoting).
  │
WEEK 6 (T-42 days): Content Creation
  │  Create launch content, pre-write social posts, prepare visuals.
  │
WEEK 5 (T-35 days): Build in Public
  │  Start sharing the journey. Behind-the-scenes, progress updates.
  │
WEEK 4 (T-28 days): Beta Recruitment
  │  Recruit beta users. Start collecting feedback and testimonials.
  │
WEEK 3 (T-21 days): Influencer Outreach
  │  Reach out to influencers, early advocates, and press contacts.
  │
WEEK 2 (T-14 days): Final Prep
  │  Finalize launch assets, schedule posts, prep Product Hunt.
  │
WEEK 1 (T-7 days): Launch Countdown
  │  Final testing, team alignment, launch day rehearsal.
  │
LAUNCH DAY
```

### Key Principle

**You are not "announcing" your product on launch day. You are activating a community you've already built.** The work you do in weeks 1-8 is what makes launch day successful. Without pre-launch effort, launch day is just "day one of marketing" — not a launch.

## Waitlist Strategy

A waitlist serves two purposes: it collects potential users before launch, and it creates a sense of exclusivity and anticipation.

### Waitlist Landing Page

Your pre-launch landing page should have:

1. **Headline:** Clear statement of what {{PROJECT_NAME}} does. Not clever — clear.
   - Template: "{{ONE_LINE_DESCRIPTION}} for {{TARGET_AUDIENCE}}"
2. **Subheadline:** The key benefit or differentiator.
   - Template: "{{KEY_BENEFIT}} without {{CURRENT_FRICTION}}"
3. **Email capture form:** Name + email. Nothing more. Every extra field reduces signups.
4. **Social proof (if available):** "Join 500+ developers on the waitlist" or logos of beta companies.
5. **Visual:** Product screenshot, mockup, or short demo GIF.
6. **Optional: Expected launch date.** "Launching January 2025" sets expectations.

### Referral Incentives

Turn each waitlist signup into multiple signups by adding a referral mechanism:

| Referrals | Reward |
|-----------|--------|
| 3 friends | Early access (skip the line) |
| 5 friends | Extended free trial or discount |
| 10 friends | Lifetime deal or founding member status |

**Tools for referral waitlists:**
- **Viral Loops:** Dedicated referral waitlist tool. Templates for different incentive structures.
- **Waitlist.me / Getwaitlist.com:** Simple waitlist with referral built in.
- **Custom:** Build with your email tool + unique referral links.

### Waitlist Communication

Don't let your waitlist go cold. Send updates every 1-2 weeks:

- **Week 8:** "You're on the list! Here's what we're building and why."
- **Week 6:** "Behind-the-scenes look: [feature preview or progress update]."
- **Week 4:** "We're in beta! Here's what early users are saying."
- **Week 2:** "Launch is 2 weeks away. Here's what to expect."
- **Week 1:** "7 days to launch. Be ready — you'll get first access."
- **Launch day:** "We're live! Here's your exclusive early access link."

### Waitlist Size Targets

| Launch Scale | Target Waitlist Size | How to Get There |
|-------------|---------------------|-----------------|
| Quiet launch | 100-300 | Personal network + social posts |
| Moderate launch | 500-1,000 | Build-in-public + community seeding + content |
| Big launch | 1,000-5,000 | All of the above + influencer outreach + paid ads |
| Massive launch | 5,000+ | All of the above + press coverage + viral referral mechanics |

## Build-in-Public Strategy

Build-in-public means sharing your product's development journey publicly — progress, setbacks, decisions, metrics. It's one of the most effective pre-launch strategies for indie hackers and small teams.

### What to Share

| Content Type | Example | Platform |
|-------------|---------|----------|
| Progress updates | "Week 12 update: shipped user auth, 200 waitlist signups" | Twitter, LinkedIn |
| Revenue/metrics | "Month 3: $500 MRR from beta users" | Twitter, Indie Hackers |
| Technical decisions | "Why I chose Postgres over MongoDB for {{PROJECT_NAME}}" | Twitter, blog, HN |
| Design iterations | Before/after screenshots of UI changes | Twitter, Instagram |
| Challenges/failures | "Our first deploy failed. Here's what went wrong." | Twitter, blog |
| User feedback | "A beta user said [quote]. This changed our roadmap." | Twitter, LinkedIn |
| Milestones | "Just crossed 500 waitlist signups!" | Twitter, LinkedIn |
| Decision-making | "Should I add feature X or Y first? Here's how I decided." | Twitter, newsletter |

### Where to Share

- **Twitter/X:** The primary platform for build-in-public. Daily or near-daily updates.
- **LinkedIn:** Sanitized, professional version of updates. 2-3x per week.
- **Indie Hackers:** Weekly or bi-weekly detailed updates. The community is built for this.
- **Newsletter:** Weekly digest of progress for your waitlist and subscribers.
- **Blog:** Longer-form articles on major decisions or milestones.

### Transparency Level

| Level | What You Share | Risk | Reward |
|-------|---------------|------|--------|
| **Light** | Features, milestones, screenshots | Low | Moderate interest |
| **Medium** | Revenue, user counts, decisions, challenges | Medium | Strong engagement |
| **Full** | Revenue, expenses, failures, pivots, metrics, code | Higher | Maximum engagement and trust |

**Recommendation for {{PROJECT_NAME}}:** Start at medium transparency. Share enough to build genuine interest without exposing competitive advantages or sensitive data.

### Frequency

- **Twitter/X:** 3-5 times per week minimum. Daily is ideal.
- **LinkedIn:** 2-3 times per week.
- **Indie Hackers / community posts:** 1x per week.
- **Newsletter:** 1x per week.

### Common Mistakes

- **Sharing only wins.** People connect with struggles too. Share setbacks honestly.
- **Being too salesy.** Build-in-public is storytelling, not advertising. Share the journey, not the pitch.
- **Stopping when it gets hard.** Consistency matters more than virality. Keep posting even when engagement is low.
- **Oversharing sensitive data.** Don't share security details, customer PII, or anything that gives competitors an unfair advantage.

## Beta Program Recruitment

See the dedicated `beta-program.template.md` for the full beta program guide. Here's the pre-launch summary:

### Pre-Launch Beta Goals

- **Validate product-market fit:** Do real people actually use and value {{PROJECT_NAME}}?
- **Find critical bugs:** Before launch, not on launch day.
- **Collect testimonials:** Early user quotes and stories for your launch page.
- **Build advocates:** Beta users who love the product will amplify your launch.

### Where to Find Beta Users

1. **Your waitlist:** The first place to recruit. They already expressed interest.
2. **Communities:** Subreddits, Discord servers, Slack groups, Facebook groups related to your niche.
3. **Twitter/X:** Post about your beta program. DM relevant people.
4. **Beta directories:** BetaList, BetaPage, Product Hunt Ship page.
5. **Personal network:** Friends, colleagues, former coworkers in your target audience.
6. **LinkedIn:** Post about the beta program. DM connections who fit your target user profile.

### Beta Size

- **Focused feedback:** 20-50 users. Manageable for 1-on-1 conversations.
- **Broader validation:** 100-500 users. Need structured feedback collection (surveys, analytics).
- **For {{PROJECT_NAME}}:** Start with 20-30 users. Add more as you stabilize.

## Pre-Launch Content Plan

Create content that educates your audience about the problem you solve, positioning {{PROJECT_NAME}} as the obvious solution when you launch.

### Content Calendar (8 Weeks Before Launch)

| Week | Content Piece | Purpose |
|------|-------------|---------|
| 8 | "The problem with {{PROBLEM_AREA}} today" (blog/thread) | Establish the problem |
| 7 | "How I used to handle {{TASK}} before building a solution" (personal story) | Relatability |
| 6 | "{{NUMBER}} ways {{TARGET_AUDIENCE}} waste time on {{TASK}}" (listicle) | Education + SEO |

- **Twitter threads:** Deep dives on the problem or your approach.
- **Blog posts:** Long-form content that ranks in search and establishes authority.
- **Short videos:** 30-60 second product teasers on TikTok/Reels/Shorts.
- **Screenshots and GIFs:** Product previews shared on social media.

## Email List Building

**Target: minimum 500-1,000 subscribers before launch.** This is the most reliable channel for launch day activation.

### How to Build Your Pre-Launch Email List

1. **Waitlist landing page** (see Waitlist Strategy above). Primary source.
2. **Lead magnets:** Create free resources related to your problem space.
   - Template, checklist, or guide related to {{PROBLEM_AREA}}.
   - Example: "The Ultimate {{PROBLEM_AREA}} Checklist for {{TARGET_AUDIENCE}}" — free download in exchange for email.
3. **Content upgrades:** Add email capture to your blog posts. "Get the template from this post delivered to your inbox."
4. **Social media bio link:** Point to your waitlist page from every social profile.
5. **Cross-promotion:** Guest on podcasts, write guest posts, collaborate with complementary creators — always mention the waitlist.
6. **Community engagement:** When you share valuable content in communities and someone says "this is great," invite them to your waitlist.

### Email Sequence (Pre-Launch)

Set up an automated welcome sequence for new subscribers:

```
Email 1 (Immediate): Welcome + what {{PROJECT_NAME}} is + what to expect
Email 2 (Day 3): The problem — why {{PROBLEM_AREA}} is broken
Email 3 (Day 7): The solution — your approach and philosophy
Email 4 (Day 14): Behind-the-scenes — progress update or feature preview
Email 5 (Day 21): Social proof — beta user feedback or testimonial
Email 6 (Pre-launch): "We launch in [X] days — here's how to get early access"
```

### Email Tools for Pre-Launch

| Tool | Price | Best For |
|------|-------|----------|
| Buttondown | Free up to 100 subscribers | Simple, developer-friendly |
| ConvertKit (Kit) | Free up to 1,000 subscribers | Creator-focused, easy automation |
| Mailchimp | Free up to 500 contacts | Well-known, lots of integrations |
| Loops | Startup-friendly pricing | Built for SaaS, event-based emails |
| Resend | Free up to 3,000 emails/month | Developer-first, API-based |

## Community Seeding

**Start contributing to relevant communities 60+ days before launch.** You cannot show up on launch day, drop your link, and expect support. You need to be a known, contributing member first.

### Communities to Join

| Community Type | Examples | How to Contribute |
|---------------|---------|------------------|
| **Reddit** | r/SideProject, r/startups, r/entrepreneur, r/[your niche] | Answer questions, share knowledge, upvote good content |
| **Indie Hackers** | Forum, groups | Share journey updates, comment on others' posts |
| **Discord servers** | Niche communities, dev communities | Help people, participate in discussions |
| **Slack groups** | Industry Slack communities | Answer questions, share relevant resources |
| **Hacker News** | hn.algolia.com for finding relevant threads | Comment thoughtfully on relevant discussions |
| **Twitter/X** | Build-in-public community, niche hashtags | Engage with others' content, add value in replies |
| **LinkedIn groups** | Professional groups in your industry | Share insights, comment on posts |

### The 60-Day Community Plan

| Phase | Weeks | Activity |
|-------|-------|----------|
| **Observe** | Weeks 8-7 | Join communities. Read. Understand the culture and rules. Don't post anything promotional. |
| **Contribute** | Weeks 6-5 | Start helping. Answer questions. Share knowledge. Be genuinely useful. |
| **Establish** | Weeks 4-3 | You're now a recognized contributor. Share your build-in-public journey naturally. |
| **Activate** | Weeks 2-1 | Mention your upcoming launch. Ask for feedback. People who know you will want to support you. |

### Community Rules

- **Never spam.** One promotional post where it's appropriate is fine. Ten posts across every community is spam.
- **Contribute 10x more than you promote.** For every post about {{PROJECT_NAME}}, you should have made 10 helpful comments on others' posts.
- **Read each community's rules.** Some allow self-promotion on specific days. Some don't allow it at all.
- **Be transparent.** When sharing your product, say "I built this" — don't pretend to be a random user.

## Influencer and Early Advocate Outreach

### Identifying the Right People

You don't need mega-influencers. Micro-influencers (1K-50K followers) in your niche are more effective and more accessible.

**Look for people who:**
- Create content in your problem space.
- Have an engaged audience (comments and replies, not just follower count).
- Have reviewed or discussed similar products.
- Are known for genuine recommendations (not just paid promotions).

### Where to Find Them

1. **Twitter/X search:** Search for discussions about your problem area. Who's getting the most engagement?
2. **YouTube:** Search for videos about your niche. Sort by view count or recent.
3. **Podcast directories:** Search Apple Podcasts or Spotify for shows about your topic.
4. **Blog search:** Google "[your niche] blog" or "[your niche] tools review."
5. **Product Hunt:** Look at who reviewed similar products. These makers/hunters are influential.

### The Outreach Approach

**Do NOT pitch cold.** Build a relationship first:

1. **Week 6-5:** Follow them. Engage with their content genuinely. Leave thoughtful comments.
2. **Week 4-3:** DM or email with something valuable (not a pitch). Share a resource, congratulate them on something, ask a genuine question.
3. **Week 2:** Now reach out: "I've been building {{PROJECT_NAME}}, and I'd love to give you early access. No strings attached — I just think you'd find it interesting."

### Outreach Email Template

```
Subject: Early access to {{PROJECT_NAME}} — thought you'd be interested

Hi {{INFLUENCER_NAME}},

I've been following your [content/posts/videos] about {{TOPIC}} — your piece on
{{SPECIFIC_CONTENT}} was really insightful.

I'm building {{PROJECT_NAME}}, which {{ONE_LINE_DESCRIPTION}}. We're launching
in {{WEEKS_UNTIL_LAUNCH}} weeks, and I'd love to give you early access.

No obligation to post about it — I genuinely think you'd find it useful given
your work in {{THEIR_NICHE}}. But if you do end up wanting to share your experience,
we'd obviously be thrilled.

Here's a quick preview: [SCREENSHOT OR VIDEO LINK]

Want me to set you up with an account?

Best,
{{YOUR_NAME}}
```

### What You're Offering

| Offer | Cost to You | Value to Them |
|-------|-------------|---------------|
| Early/exclusive access | Free | They get to be first. Content creators love exclusives. |
| Extended free plan | Revenue cost | They get to use it properly and form genuine opinions. |
| Feature their content | Free | Cross-promotion. They get exposure to your audience. |
| Interview for your blog | Free | They get a backlink and feature. |
| Co-marketing | Time | Mutual promotion benefits both. |

## Press and Media Preparation

### Press Kit

Create a simple press page or downloadable press kit with:

- **Company/product overview:** 2-3 paragraphs. What is {{PROJECT_NAME}}, who is it for, why does it matter.
- **Founder story:** 1-2 paragraphs. Why you built this, your background, the personal angle.
- **High-resolution images:** Logo (SVG + PNG, white and dark backgrounds), product screenshots (3-5), founder headshot.
- **Key facts:** Launch date, pricing, key features, target audience, current metrics (if impressive).
- **Press-ready quotes:** 2-3 quotable statements from the founder about the vision and problem.
- **Contact information:** Email for press inquiries (ideally press@yourdomain.com or the founder's email).

### Journalist Outreach

- **Build a list of 20-50 journalists/writers** who cover your niche. Check TechCrunch, The Verge, Hacker News, industry blogs, and newsletters.
- **Use tools like MuckRack, HARO, or manual research** to find relevant writers and their contact information.
- **Pitch timing:** Send embargoed press releases 7-10 days before launch. This gives journalists time to write.
- **The pitch email should be 3-5 sentences:** What it is, why it matters, what's unique, and an offer for more info or a demo.
- **Follow up once.** If they don't respond after one follow-up, move on. Don't spam journalists.

### Realistic Expectations

- Most startups do not get press coverage for their initial launch. That's normal.
- Press coverage matters less than you think for most products. Community, content, and direct outreach are more impactful.
- If you do get press, it creates a short-term traffic spike that fades quickly. Build sustainable channels alongside press.
- Press is more achievable if you have a genuinely unique story, impressive metrics, a notable founder, or a trending topic angle.

### 30+ Days Before Launch

- [ ] Create a Product Hunt Ship page for {{PROJECT_NAME}}.
- [ ] Write your tagline (60 characters max). Clear and specific.
