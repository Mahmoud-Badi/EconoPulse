## Product Hunt Basics

### How Product Hunt Works

Product Hunt is a daily leaderboard of new products. Every day at 12:01 AM PST, a new "day" begins. Products submitted during that day compete for upvotes (formerly called "upvotes," now just "support"). At the end of the 24-hour cycle, the top products earn badges and visibility.

**Key concepts:**
- **Products:** Launched tools, apps, websites, or services.
- **Makers:** The people who built the product. Identified on the product page.
- **Hunters:** The people who submit/post the product. Can be the maker or someone else.
- **Upvotes/Support:** Community members click to support products they like.
- **Comments:** Community discussion on the product page. Engagement matters for ranking.
- **Collections:** Curated lists of products. Being added to popular collections drives ongoing traffic.
- **Ship pages:** Pre-launch pages on PH where people can follow your upcoming product.

### Why Product Hunt Matters

- **Traffic spike:** A successful PH launch drives thousands of visitors in 24 hours.
- **SEO value:** Your PH page ranks in Google and provides a permanent backlink.
- **Social proof:** "Featured on Product Hunt" badges are widely recognized.
- **Community:** PH users are early adopters — exactly the people who try new products.
- **Press:** Journalists and bloggers monitor PH for stories. A top launch can lead to press coverage.
- **Investor attention:** VCs and angels actively browse PH for interesting products.
- **Long tail:** Your PH page continues to drive traffic for months and years after launch.

### What a Successful Launch Looks Like

| Result | What It Means |
|--------|--------------|
| **#1 Product of the Day** | Top of the leaderboard. Maximum visibility. Golden Kitty nomination potential. |
| **Top 5** | Badge earned. Strong visibility. Good social proof. |
| **Top 10** | Solid launch. Decent traffic and signups. |
| **11-20** | Average launch. Some traffic, some signups. |
| **Below 20** | Below average. Limited visibility from PH itself. |

Most launches fall in the 50-200 upvote range. Top 5 typically requires 300-600+ upvotes. #1 Product of the Day usually needs 500-1,000+ upvotes, depending on the competition that day.

## Preparation (30+ Days Before)

### Create a Ship Page (Day -60 to -30)

Product Hunt's Ship feature lets you create a pre-launch page where people can follow your upcoming product.

1. Go to producthunt.com/ship and create your Ship page.
2. Include: product name, tagline, description, and a preview image or video.
3. Share your Ship page URL on social media and in your email signature.
4. Engage with people who follow your Ship page — they're your future launch day supporters.
5. Goal: 100-500+ followers before launch day.

### Optimize Your Maker Profile

Your profile is visible on your product page. Make it credible.

- [ ] **Profile photo:** A real, professional photo. Not a logo, not an avatar.
- [ ] **Bio:** Short description of who you are and what you do. Include relevant credentials.
- [ ] **Social links:** Twitter/X (most important for PH community), LinkedIn, personal site.
- [ ] **Previous products:** If you've launched before, these appear on your profile.
- [ ] **Activity:** Comment on and upvote other products. An active profile is more credible than a dormant one.

### Build Community Relationships (Day -60 to -14)

Product Hunt is a community. People are more likely to support makers they recognize.

- [ ] **Upvote and comment on other launches.** 3-5 per week for at least a month before your launch.
- [ ] **Leave thoughtful comments,** not just "great product!" Ask questions, share insights, provide feedback.
- [ ] **Follow relevant makers and hunters.** Build genuine connections.
- [ ] **Join the Product Hunt community** on social media. Many PH regulars are active on Twitter/X.
- [ ] **Support other makers' launches.** They'll often reciprocate when you launch.

### Prepare Launch Assets

| Asset | Specs | Tips |
|-------|-------|------|
| **Logo/Icon** | 240x240px | Clear, recognizable at small sizes. PNG with transparent background. |
| **Gallery images** | 1270x760px (up to 8 images) | Show your product in action. Not just screenshots — add context, annotations, and highlights. |
| **Gallery GIF/video** | Up to 3 minutes | A 30-60 second product demo is ideal. GIFs autoplay and grab attention. |
| **Tagline** | Max 60 characters | Clear and specific. Describe what it does in plain language. Not clever — clear. |
| **Description** | 260 characters max | Expand on the tagline. What it does, who it's for, what makes it unique. |
| **First comment** | 200-400 words | See "Launch Day Execution" below. This is your most important piece of content. |
| **Topics** | Select 3-4 | Choose relevant categories from PH's topic list. |
| **Links** | Product URL + any extras | Your main product URL. Optionally: documentation, pricing page, demo. |

### Gallery Image Best Practices

1. **Image 1:** Hero image — your product's main interface or "wow moment."
2. **Image 2:** Problem/solution — show the before (problem) and after (your product).
3. **Image 3:** Key feature spotlight — your most impressive or unique feature.
4. **Image 4:** Workflow — show the end-to-end user experience.
5. **Image 5 (optional):** Testimonial or social proof — user quote overlaid on a product screenshot.

**Design tips:**
- Use consistent branding across all images (same colors, fonts, style).
- Add text annotations — don't rely on the screenshot alone to communicate value.
- Show real product data, not empty states or placeholder content.
- Make text large enough to read on mobile.
- Use a design tool (Canva, Figma) to create polished gallery images — not raw screenshots.

## Finding a Hunter

### What is a Hunter?

The "hunter" is the person who submits (posts) your product on Product Hunt. This can be you (the maker) or someone else.

### Should You Find a Hunter?

| Approach | Pros | Cons |
|----------|------|------|
| **Self-hunt (submit yourself)** | Full control of timing and messaging. No dependencies. | Slightly less reach (hunters with large followings notify their followers). |
| **Find a hunter** | Their followers get notified. Social proof of endorsement. Potential wider reach. | Less control. Dependency on someone else's schedule. |

**The honest truth:** The impact of hunters has diminished over the years. The algorithm now weighs product quality, engagement, and maker activity more than hunter follower count. Self-hunting is perfectly fine and is now the most common approach.

### If You Want to Find a Hunter

1. **Look for active PH community members** who hunt products similar to yours.
2. **Build a relationship first.** Follow them, engage with their content, support products they've hunted.
3. **Reach out personally.** DM on Twitter or PH. Be genuine, not transactional.
4. **Offer them early access** so they can genuinely evaluate your product before hunting it.
5. **Don't pay for hunting.** This violates PH guidelines and can get you banned.

### Outreach Template

```
Hi [Hunter Name],

I've been following your Product Hunt activity and noticed you've hunted
some great products in the [category] space, like [specific product they hunted].

I'm building [Product Name] — [one-line description]. We're planning to
launch on Product Hunt [date/timeframe], and I think it might be a good fit
for your hunting interests.

Would you be open to checking it out? I'd love to give you early access.
No pressure to hunt it — I'd genuinely value your feedback either way.

Here's a quick preview: [link or screenshot]

Thanks!
[Your Name]
```

## Launch Timing

### Best Day to Launch

| Day | Verdict | Notes |
|-----|---------|-------|
| Monday | Good | Competition is moderate. Not a weekend. |
| **Tuesday** | **Best** | Many successful launches happen on Tuesday. High community activity. |
| **Wednesday** | **Best** | Similar to Tuesday. High engagement. |
| **Thursday** | **Good** | Still strong. Friday weekend wind-down hasn't started. |
| Friday | Average | Some community members disengage for the weekend. |
| Saturday | Poor | Low activity. Fewer votes, fewer comments. |
| Sunday | Poor | Lowest activity day. Avoid unless you want less competition and are okay with fewer votes. |

### Best Time to Submit

- **Submit at 12:01 AM PST (Pacific Standard Time).** This gives you the full 24-hour cycle on the leaderboard.
- The "day" on Product Hunt runs from 12:01 AM PST to 11:59 PM PST.
- Submitting later in the day means fewer hours of visibility.
- If you're not in the PST timezone, set an alarm.

### Avoiding Competition

- **Check what's launching the same day.** If a major company (Google, Apple, etc.) or a well-known product is launching, consider a different day.
- **Avoid major tech events:** WWDC, Google I/O, CES. The audience is distracted.
- **Avoid holidays.** Lower community activity.

## Launch Day Execution

### Submission Checklist (12:01 AM PST)

- [ ] Submit the product with all prepared assets.
- [ ] Verify everything looks correct: images, tagline, description, links, topics.
- [ ] Post your **first comment** (maker comment) immediately.

### The First Comment (Most Important Content)

Your first comment is the first thing visitors read after your tagline and description. It's a personal message from you to the PH community.

**Structure:**

```
Hey Product Hunt! 👋

I'm [Name], the [role] behind [Product Name], and I'm thrilled to finally share it
with you all.

**Why I built this:**
[2-3 sentences about the personal story or problem that led you to build this.
Be genuine and specific. What frustrated you? What was missing in the market?]

**What [Product Name] does:**
[2-3 sentences explaining the product clearly. What does it do? For whom?
What's the "aha moment"?]

**What makes it different:**
[2-3 bullet points on your key differentiators. What can users do with your
product that they can't do elsewhere?]

**Where we are:**
[Current status — beta, launched, X users, etc. Be honest about the stage.]

**I'd love your feedback on:**
[Specific questions. This invites engagement and shows you're open to input.
Examples: "Is the pricing clear?" "Would you use feature X or Y?" "What's missing?"]

**Special offer for PH:**
[Optional — discount, extended trial, or exclusive feature for PH users.]

Thanks for checking us out — excited to hear what you think!

[Name]
```

### Social Media Push (7:00 AM+ in your audience's timezone)

After submitting at midnight, most of your audience won't be awake. Start your social push in the morning.

- [ ] **Twitter/X:** Post a launch thread. Include the PH link. Ask for support and honest feedback (NOT "please upvote me").
- [ ] **LinkedIn:** Personal post about the launch story. Include PH link.
- [ ] **Other platforms:** Indie Hackers, Reddit, Discord, Slack groups — share where appropriate.

### Email List Notification

- [ ] Send your launch email at 6-8 AM in your audience's timezone.
- [ ] Include: what launched, why it matters, PH link, ask for support.
- [ ] Template:

```
Subject: We just launched on Product Hunt! 🚀

Hi [Name],

The day is finally here — [Product Name] is live on Product Hunt!

We've been working on this for [timeframe], and I'm so excited to share it
with the world.

🔗 Check it out on Product Hunt: [PH LINK]

If you love what we're building, I'd really appreciate:
✅ Your support on our Product Hunt page
💬 A comment sharing your thoughts or feedback
🔄 Sharing with anyone who might find it useful

[Special offer for early supporters, if applicable]

Thank you for being part of this journey.

[Name]
```

### Community Engagement Throughout the Day

- [ ] **Respond to every comment** on your PH page within 5-15 minutes.
- [ ] **Be genuine and helpful,** not salesy. Answer questions honestly.
- [ ] **Update your first comment** with new information as the day progresses (e.g., "Update: We just hit 200 upvotes! Thank you! Also, based on feedback, we're adding [feature].")
- [ ] **Thank people by name** who leave detailed comments or share your launch.
- [ ] **Don't just respond — ask follow-up questions.** "Thanks for the feedback! Can you tell me more about how you'd use this?" This creates a conversation and more comments = better ranking.

### Progress Updates

Post updates on social media throughout the day:

- **Morning:** "We're live on Product Hunt! [link]"
- **Midday:** "X hours in and we're #Y on Product Hunt! Thank you for the incredible support."
- **Afternoon:** "Some amazing feedback coming in from the PH community. My favorite so far: [quote]."
- **Evening:** "What a day! [Final stats]. Thank you, Product Hunt community."

## Post-Launch

### Badges and Results

- Products in the **top 5** earn a badge (Product of the Day, #2-#5).
- **Product of the Week/Month:** Top products are further ranked for weekly and monthly awards.
- **Golden Kitty Awards:** Annual awards for the best products of the year.
- Even if you don't finish in the top 5, your PH page remains live permanently and continues to drive traffic.

### Follow-Up Content

- [ ] **Write a PH launch retrospective** (blog post). Share what worked, what didn't, and your results. The PH community loves these — share it on PH as a maker story.
- [ ] **Update your PH page** if you add significant features. You can post updates to your product page.
- [ ] **Respond to late comments.** People discover your page days and weeks after launch. Keep responding.
- [ ] **Add a "Featured on Product Hunt" badge** to your website. It's social proof.

### Converting Product Hunt Traffic

PH visitors are browsers — they check out lots of products every day. Make conversion easy:

- [ ] **Ensure your landing page has a clear CTA** visible immediately (no scrolling needed).
- [ ] **Offer a PH-specific incentive** (discount, extended trial) with a unique link to track PH conversions.
- [ ] **Fast loading page.** PH users are clicking through many products. If your page takes 3+ seconds to load, they'll bounce.
- [ ] **Mobile-friendly.** A significant portion of PH traffic is mobile.
- [ ] **Don't require a credit card for signup.** PH users are explorers — low-friction signup is essential.

### Leveraging PH Long-Term

- Your PH page ranks in Google for your product name + related keywords.
- Update your PH page periodically with new images, descriptions, and milestones.
- Engage with PH launches in your space — you'll stay visible to the community.
- When you ship major updates, launch them as new products on PH (you can launch multiple times).

## What NOT to Do

These actions can get you banned from Product Hunt or significantly hurt your launch:

### Absolute Don'ts

| Action | Why It's Bad | Consequence |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| **Buying votes** | Manipulation. PH detects unnatural voting patterns. | Product removed. Account banned. Permanent reputation damage. |
| **Using bots or fake accounts** | Same as above. PH's anti-fraud system is sophisticated. | Immediate removal and ban. |
| **Asking for upvotes explicitly** | "Please upvote my product" violates community norms. | Community backlash. Possible demotion in ranking. |
| **Vote exchange groups** | Slack/Discord groups where everyone upvotes each other. PH detects these. | Votes discounted. Potential ban. |
| **Multiple submissions** of the same product on the same day | Trying to game the system. | All submissions removed. |
| **Fake comments** from fake accounts | Easily detected. Looks desperate. | Product credibility destroyed. |
| **Spamming PH users** with DMs asking for votes | Annoying and against guidelines. | Account restricted or banned. |

### Subtle Don'ts

- **Don't ask people to upvote.** Instead, ask them to "check out your launch and share their thoughts." The difference is subtle but important.
- **Don't share direct PH upvote links** that take people straight to the vote button. Share your PH page link and let people decide on their own.
- **Don't obsess over ranking.** Check every hour, not every minute. Obsessive refreshing doesn't change the outcome and ruins your launch day.
- **Don't be defensive about criticism.** If someone points out a flaw, acknowledge it and explain your plan. Don't argue.
- **Don't launch before your product is ready.** PH users will try your product immediately. If it's broken, they'll leave negative comments.
- **Don't launch on the same day as a known major launch.** Check the scheduled launches page.

## Realistic Expectations

### Average Launch Performance

| Tier | Upvotes | Traffic (day 1) | Signups (day 1) | Likelihood |
|------|---------|-----------------|-----------------|------------|
| **Top 5** | 300-1,000+ | 3,000-20,000 visitors | 100-1,000+ | ~5% of launches |
| **Top 10** | 150-300 | 1,000-5,000 visitors | 50-200 | ~10% of launches |
| **Top 20** | 50-150 | 500-2,000 visitors | 20-100 | ~15% of launches |
| **Average** | 20-50 | 100-500 visitors | 5-30 | ~40% of launches |
| **Below average** | 0-20 | Under 100 visitors | 0-10 | ~30% of launches |

### The Truth About Product Hunt

- **Most launches don't go viral.** That's completely normal. A "failed" PH launch doesn't mean your product is bad.
- **PH traffic doesn't stick.** You'll see a spike on day 1 that quickly drops back to normal. The value is in the signups you capture, not the ongoing traffic.
- **Quality of users matters more than quantity.** 50 engaged signups from PH can be worth more than 500 casual visitors who never activate.
- **It's not make-or-break.** Many successful companies had mediocre PH launches. PH is one marketing channel among many.
- **You can launch again.** If your first launch doesn't go well, you can launch new versions, features, or pivots later.

### What Actually Drives PH Success

In order of importance:

1. **Product quality and uniqueness.** The product itself is the biggest factor. Genuinely useful and well-built products win.
2. **Maker engagement.** Responding to comments, being genuine, telling a compelling story.
3. **Gallery assets.** Great images and video make people want to learn more.
4. **Community support.** Having an existing audience that shows up on launch day.
5. **Timing.** Day of week and competitive landscape.
6. **Hunter (if applicable).** Minor factor, but a known hunter's followers get notified.

## Success Metrics Beyond Upvotes

Upvotes are the most visible metric, but they're not the most important.

### What to Actually Measure

| Metric | Why It Matters | How to Track |
|--------|---------------|-------------|
| **Signups from PH** | Direct business impact. | UTM parameter on your PH link: `?ref=producthunt` |
| **Conversion rate** | PH visitors → signups. | Analytics: filter by PH referral source. |
| **Activation rate** | PH signups → active users. | Product analytics. |
| **Revenue from PH** | If you have paid plans. | Track PH-referred users through your payment system. |
| **Press coverage** | PH launches attract journalists. | Google your product name in the days after launch. |
| **Backlinks** | SEO value from PH page + press. | Ahrefs, Moz, or Google Search Console. |
| **Newsletter signups** | Growing your owned audience. | Email tool analytics. |
| **Community growth** | Discord, Twitter followers, etc. | Platform analytics. |
| **Quality of feedback** | Insights that improve your product. | Review all comments and categorize. |
| **Connections made** | Relationships with other makers, potential partners, investors. | Your networking notes. |

### Long-Term PH Value

- Your PH page ranks in Google for months and years, driving ongoing organic traffic.
- The "Featured on Product Hunt" badge is social proof on your website.
- Relationships built with the PH community pay dividends long after launch day.
- Your next launch will benefit from lessons learned and community presence built.

## Product Hunt Launch Checklist

### 30 Days Before

- [ ] Create Ship page and start building followers
- [ ] Optimize maker profile
- [ ] Begin engaging with PH community (upvote + comment on 3-5 launches per week)
- [ ] Start preparing gallery images and demo video

### 14 Days Before

- [ ] Finalize all assets (logo, gallery images, video, tagline, description)
- [ ] Write first comment draft
- [ ] Set launch date (Tuesday-Thursday)
- [ ] Contact hunter (if using one)
- [ ] Prepare email announcement for your list
- [ ] Set up UTM tracking: `?ref=producthunt`

### 7 Days Before

- [ ] Email waitlist: "We launch on PH next [day]"
- [ ] DM supporters: personal ask for launch day support
- [ ] Prepare social media posts for launch day
- [ ] Test your product: signup flow, core features, mobile experience
- [ ] Ensure landing page loads fast and converts well

### Launch Day

- [ ] Submit at 12:01 AM PST
- [ ] Post first comment immediately
- [ ] Send launch email (6-8 AM in audience's timezone)
- [ ] Social media blitz (7-9 AM)
- [ ] Respond to every comment within 15 minutes
- [ ] Post progress updates every 3-4 hours
- [ ] Thank supporters throughout the day
- [ ] Evening: post final results and thank you

### Post-Launch (Week After)

- [ ] Continue responding to comments
- [ ] Write launch retrospective
- [ ] Add "Featured on Product Hunt" badge to website
- [ ] Analyze: signups, conversion, activation from PH traffic
- [ ] Follow up with notable PH commenters
- [ ] Plan next PH launch for future updates
